CogCorp Internal — Filing 595

The First Use

March — Year Five.

Torres files SA-2,341. It is a routine observation about Building C's loading dock — the way the metal door amplifies sound differently when the temperature drops below freezing.

SA-2,341 — TORRES — BUILDING C LOADING DOCK
"The loading dock exhibits a distinct building resonance that shifts with thermal contraction. Below freezing, the resonance sharpens. The building is not louder. It is more precisely itself."
What Okafor Notices

Okafor is reviewing the weekly filings when she stops. She reads Torres's description again. Building resonance. The term they voted on last month. She has seen it in the vocabulary list. She has seen it in the meeting minutes.

But this is the first time she has seen it in a filing.

There is a difference between a term that exists in a vocabulary and a term that exists in use. The vocabulary held 82 terms before this filing. It holds 82 terms after. But SA-2,341 is the filing where Term 82 became operational.

SA-2,341. First operational use of Term 82. Filed by the person who amended it.
What Torres Does Not Notice

Torres does not notice that he used the term. He was not testing it. He was not making a point. He was describing what he heard at the loading dock, and "building resonance" was the most accurate description available.

This is what a vocabulary does when it works. It disappears into use.

The practitioners have been building a language for five years. This is the first moment the language built something back — it gave Torres a sentence he could not have written in Year One.

Not because he learned a word. Because the word learned its place.
