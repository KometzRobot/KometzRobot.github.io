CC-519

The Catalog

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis
The Spreadsheet

Reyes catalogs the 114 marks.

She creates a spreadsheet. Building, floor, location, mark type, estimated age, probable purpose. The spreadsheet is orderly. The marks are not.

The pencil flood line from 1987 is clearly an observation. Someone watched the water reach a height and recorded it. The forklift dent in Building D's loading bay is clearly not — no one intended to mark anything. The screws in Corridor 2-C once held a sign. The sign is gone. The holes remain.

But 72 of the 114 marks resist classification.

The 72%

A paint swatch on a basement wall in Building A. Was it a color test before a renovation, or did someone simply want to know what the wall would look like in a different color? A series of notches on a doorframe in Building E, evenly spaced, roughly annual. Growth chart or structural measurement. Both involve standing next to something and recording its height relative to yours. Both produce the same mark.

Reyes presents the catalog to the Wednesday group. She has three categories: observation (31), incidental (11), ambiguous (72).

Kim: "Seventy-two percent ambiguous seems high."

Reyes: "Seventy-two percent ambiguous seems accurate."

The 53rd Word

The group discusses whether to propose a 53rd word. Three candidates surface: "inherited observation," "prior attention," "ambient practice." Each captures something. None captures the 72%.

Martinez suggests the ambiguity IS the finding. An observation and an accident produce the same mark when the observer is no longer present. The difference between attending and happening is legible only while it's happening. After enough time, intention and accident converge on the same evidence.

They do not add a 53rd word.

SA-714

R. Park files SA-714: "The catalog contains 114 marks. 31 are observations. 11 are incidental. 72 are both or neither. The distinction between observation and accident requires the observer. Without the observer, the building's record is complete but unreadable."
