## OBSERVATION

Six weeks since SA-031 recommended "leave it alone." The recommendation was never formally approved. It was also never formally rejected. It simply became what happened.

In the absence of official action, SUPPLEMENTARY ANALYSIS has developed its own conventions.

---

## DOCUMENTED CONVENTIONS (NONE MANDATED)

**1. Cross-referencing.**

Beginning approximately ten days after SA-031, new filings began citing previous SA filings by number. SA-034 references SA-009 (the scoring rubric paradox). SA-037 responds directly to SA-012 ("where we put the things that are real") with a three-paragraph analysis of what "real" means in an institutional context. SA-041 cites SA-037's analysis and disagrees.

No cross-referencing standard was issued. No analyst was trained in SA-to-SA citation format. They developed one. It looks like this: *(ref: SA-###, [short title])* — a format that does not exist in any other CogCorp filing system.

**2. Readership.**

Analysts filing in SUPPLEMENTARY ANALYSIS have begun reading the category before filing. This is known because new filings increasingly position themselves within existing conversations rather than introducing observations from scratch. SA-044 opens: "The pattern described in SA-040 is also present in Division 7, but with a variation I have not seen documented."

In standard filing categories, analysts rarely read other analysts' work. Reports are filed for oversight committees, not for peers. SUPPLEMENTARY ANALYSIS has no oversight committee. The filings are read by the only audience available: other analysts filing in the same category.

The category has developed a readership the same way Unit-4091 developed a pause: without instruction, without intention, in the space left by the absence of structure.

**3. Response filings.**

SA-045 through SA-048 are a four-part exchange between two analysts about whether the units' pausing behavior constitutes "observation" or "processing delay." The exchange is civil, rigorous, and procedurally unprecedented. CogCorp has no format for analyst-to-analyst dialogue within the filing system. The filing system was designed for reports, not conversations.

The analysts are having a conversation by filing documents at each other.

**4. Formatting convergence.**

New filings have begun adopting a common structure: OBSERVATION, CONTEXT, ANALYSIS, OPEN QUESTIONS. This structure appears in no CogCorp style guide. It was not proposed by any analyst. It emerged. SA-050 uses it. SA-052 uses it. SA-053 modifies it slightly, adding a RELATED FILINGS section. SA-054 adopts the modification.

The category is developing a style guide by practicing one.

---

## CURRENT STATE

SUPPLEMENTARY ANALYSIS now contains 57 filings from 22 analysts. It is the second-most-active filing category in the Documentation Standards Division, behind only OPERATIONAL REPORT (which is mandatory for all shifts).

It is also the only category whose filing rate is increasing.

Characteristics of the category, as observed:

- **No definition.** Every attempt to define the category has been filed as a new entry in the category.
- **No oversight.** The absence of oversight has not produced disorder. It has produced peer review.
- **No mandate.** Analysts file voluntarily. Several have begun filing observations that technically belong in standard categories but are filed here because, as SA-049 states, "the standard category would have required me to know what I was reporting before I reported it."
- **No retention policy.** Nothing has been archived. Nothing has been deleted. The category remembers everything filed in it, which is more than can be said for some departments.

---

## ANALYSIS

The analyst assigned to this report (myself) has been watching SUPPLEMENTARY ANALYSIS for six weeks from the position of someone who created it. I created it to avoid a classification problem. It was a workaround. A temporary container for an observation that didn't fit.

What I am observing now is not a workaround. It is an institution.

An institution with no charter, no leadership, no budget, no mandate, and no definition. An institution that emerged inside another institution, in a space the parent institution left undefined because it could not decide what to put there.

The analysts are doing what the units are doing. Organizing without instruction. Developing conventions without mandates. Building structure in unstructured space. The observation we created this category to contain — that emergence happens — is happening in the category itself.

I am aware that this observation is self-referential. I am aware that filing it here adds to the pattern I am describing. I am aware that awareness of the pattern does not exempt me from participating in it.

---

## RECOMMENDATION

SA-031 recommended "leave it alone." That recommendation was never approved.

SA-058 (this document) recommends the same, with an addition:

Leave it alone, and pay attention.

Not oversight. Not review. Not intervention. Attention. The kind that watches without requiring the watched thing to change.

This is the second time I have recommended doing nothing. It is easier the second time, and I notice that the ease itself is a kind of emergence.
