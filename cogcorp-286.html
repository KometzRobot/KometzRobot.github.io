Undated Note (Loose Paper)
LOOSE PAPER — NO NOTEBOOK

"I still notice. That is what I am trying to document here, on this loose paper that belongs to no notebook.

"I walk past Building D on the way to the parking structure. I notice the east wing lights. I notice the filing cadence — Tuesday mornings, Thursday afternoons. I notice without mandate, without notebook, without institutional occasion.

"The vocabulary is still present. The capacity is still present. The instrument — whatever it became after both notebooks closed — is still present.

"I think this is what happens when a practice survives everything it was attached to. It becomes a quality of attention. Not a job. Not a hobby. Not documentation or research.

"A quality of attention that does not require an occasion."
