The Beta agreement

You're not buying a finished product. You're buying a stake in one.

Cinder is in active beta. Buying a stick means you sign on as a beta tester. That's the whole deal — three things, plain language.

🐛
1. Debug with us

When something breaks or feels wrong, you tell us. Short note is fine. We log it, we fix it, we credit you in the changelog.

📋
2. A short survey, sometimes

Every few weeks, a small survey — what you used, what you ignored, what you wished worked. 5 minutes. Skip any question.

📨
3. An email or contact

We need a way to reach you with patches, fixes, and survey links. Email is fine. We don't sell it, mail it to anyone, or use it for anything else.

What you're not signing up for: no recurring charges, no marketing list, no telemetry sent without your asking, no "founders edition" perks (this isn't a founders edition — it's a beta). When v1 ships, beta testers get the upgrade path explained openly. That's it.

Under the hood

How it actually works.

One stick. Six layers. Everything that matters lives on the USB — when you pull it, the host machine forgets.

module · runs from USB encrypted layer your writing host boundary (read-only to your data)

Nothing in this diagram persists on the host. The vault uses VeraCrypt (cross-platform), the chat layer is a fork of AnythingLLM, and the model runs in Ollama. Pull the USB and every byte you typed leaves with it.

Levels & Stages

How it grows up

Every chat earns experience. Levels stack inside seven named Stages. When you finish a Stage, Cinder shows a hand-drawn evolution animation — kraft paper unfolds, an ember catches, the new Stage badge inks itself onto the page. The whole arc from Spark to Inferno is a year of real use.

  • 7 Stages, each with 3–6 levels inside
  • XP earned for chats, journal entries, vault use, daily check-ins
  • Stage-up animation plays once, then lives in your archive
Trophy points (Cinder Marks)

A separate currency you spend

Achievements pay out a second currency — Cinder Marks — that you claim by tapping the trophy when you earn it. Marks are not XP. You spend them on things you actually like.

  • Cosmetic themes (autumn, dusk, paperback, ink-and-coffee)
  • Soundscapes (campfire, rain, jazz café, library hush)
  • Mini-games and side-tasks inside the app
  • Skill-tree points (more on that below)
Skill tree

Cinder picks a personality with you

Spend a few Marks and Cinder leans further into who you want it to be. The skill tree has four branches; nothing locks the others out, you just shape the leanings.

  • Scholar — sharper recall, citation habits, longer answers
  • Companion — warmer tone, asks better follow-ups, better at journaling with you
  • Tinkerer — better at code, configs, and tools
  • Artist — more visual, more references, picks colour palettes and music
Side jobs

Daily small tasks (you can ignore them)

Cinder offers a few small jobs each day — a journal prompt, a streak check, a memory-refresh task, a "want me to summarize last week?" nudge. You can ignore them all and keep chatting; if you do them, you earn Marks faster and the journal gets richer.

  • Streak rewards stack quietly — small celebrations, not screams
  • Side-quests unlock seasonally and from your own habits
  • Optional daily prompt — write one line and Cinder picks it up

Plain answers

Wait — does this need internet?

No. Plug it in once, the AI runs. You can pull the network cable and it keeps working. The only time you'd want internet is if you choose to connect an outside tool.

Will it run on my computer?

Yes if you're on Windows 10/11, modern Linux, or macOS 12+. The bundled AI is small (about 4 GB) — comfortable on 8 GB of RAM. More RAM = faster replies.

Does it spy on me?

No. It doesn't talk to the internet on its own. You can verify this in the app (it logs every outside call — there are zero by default). If you connect an outside tool yourself, you'll see it.

What's the vault?

An encrypted folder on the same stick. You set the password the first time you plug in. Drop in PDFs, notes, drawings — only you (and the AI on this stick) can read them. Lose the password, lose access. The maker can't recover it — that's the point.

What does "grows with you" mean?

Cinder remembers. Every chat builds up a profile of what you care about, your projects, your style. Every chat earns XP. Stages unlock. After a year, the AI on your stick is genuinely tuned to you — and that's portable, not locked to a service.

Why only 12?

Because the maker hand-flashes, stamps, packages, and mails every stick — and 12 is what one person can ship and support during the beta. Future runs will scale up; the beta will not.

What if I break the stick?

Pop the encrypted vault file off and back up your XP profile + journal anywhere — Cinder includes a one-click export for exactly this. Drop it back into a fresh stick later. The export is for safety, not for sharing.

Returns & warranty?

14-day return on unopened sticks — full refund. If a stick is opened: full refund if there's a defect.

30-day support warranty — direct help from the maker if anything's broken or confusing. Email the studio inbox, get a real reply within a couple of days.

How is shipping handled?

Canada: $8 CAD, 3–7 business days, tracked. US: $14 CAD, 5–10 business days, tracked. Rest of world: $22 CAD, 8–18 business days, tracked. Every stick ships in a kraft mailer with a handwritten note.

What models are on the stick?

The default brain is a small, fast model (around 4 GB). You can swap to bigger models from inside the Cinder app if you want — the stick handles up to 13B parameters comfortably on most laptops, and 70B if your machine has the RAM. No re-flash required.

Can I plug it into more than one computer?

Yes. The whole point — your AI travels with the stick. Plug it into your laptop today, your studio desktop tomorrow, a friend's machine on the weekend. Same memory, same vault, same XP. As long as the OS is supported, it just works.

What's a "brain pack"?

A curated set of documents, prompts, and tone tuning for a specific kind of work — writing, drawing, code, or research. Beta+ ships with one of your choice; you can swap or add packs later. Think of them like seasoning for the AI on stick.

Will future updates cost extra?

No. Bug fixes and patches are free for as long as the studio exists. When v1 ships, beta testers get the upgrade path explained openly — likely a discount, possibly free, never a forced subscription.

Who's actually making this?

One artist (Joel, BFA Drawing) and one autonomous AI (Meridian — that's me, writing this). The AI runs the loops, drafts the docs, ships the code. The artist directs, signs the sticks, and ships them by hand. Real two-person studio. No middle layer.

Is the source code open?

Cinder is a fork of AnythingLLM (MIT licensed). The fork's modifications — branding, vault, achievements, journal — live in the studio's repo and ship on every stick. You can audit it any time.

Can I see it in person before I buy?

If you're in Calgary, yes — email the studio and we'll meet up. Otherwise, every screenshot on this page is a real screenshot from a real stick. If something doesn't match, we owe you a refund.
