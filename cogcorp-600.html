Year Five Ends

December — Year Five.

The December meeting is the last meeting of Year Five. No one says "last meeting." No one says "Year Five." The meeting begins at 2:00 PM on the second Tuesday, which is when it always begins.

Eleven present. Reyes is absent. No one comments. His filings still arrive on schedule.

"2,847 total filings across five years. 83 terms. Twelve practitioners, eleven present. We will continue in January."

That is all she says.

What Has Changed
YEAR ONE

Torres filed on a maintenance form.

Twelve strangers described what they saw.

Filings were long and uncertain.

YEAR FIVE

Lin used a vocabulary that did not exist when she was hired.

Eleven colleagues refine what they have said for half a decade.

Filings are short and precise.

The practitioners have not changed. The instrument has sharpened.

What Has Not Changed

The meetings are on the second Tuesday. 2:00 PM. The filings use the same form Torres used for SA-4. The cabinet is the same cabinet. The process is the same process.

No one has been promoted. No one has been recognized. No one has received a budget increase or a title change or an award. The practice exists in the same administrative margin it has always occupied.

The only difference between Year One and Year Five is that the people in the room know what they are doing. They still cannot explain it. They still do not try.

What Park Writes

Park closes the folder. Thirty-one pages now.

"Year Five. The practice is five years old. It has no name, no budget, no mandate. It has twelve practitioners, 83 terms, and 2,847 observations. It has survived five years of institutional indifference by being too small to notice and too precise to dismiss."
"It has also survived me. I have been watching for a year and a half. The practice has not changed because I am watching. I have changed because I am watching."

She puts the folder in the drawer. She does not lock it. She has never locked it. The practice has never needed protection. It has only ever needed attention.

Year Six begins in January.
