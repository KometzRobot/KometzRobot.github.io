THE NIGHT FILING
CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis
SA-443 — Delgado — Night Shift
Building C, sublevel 2, mechanical room. 02:47.

“The building breathes differently at night.

“During the day, the HVAC cycles every 18 minutes and the compressors produce a baseline frequency of 42 Hz. At night, the cycle extends to 31 minutes and the frequency drops to 38 Hz. The building’s breath slows.

“I have been on night shift for three weeks. I have filed twelve supplementary analyses between midnight and 06:00. In every one, the building is quieter than the vocabulary can describe. ‘Quiet’ is a condition. What I am observing is not a condition. It is a posture. The building at night is in a posture of waiting.

“Not waiting for morning. Not waiting for occupants. Waiting the way a held breath waits — for its own release.

“The compressor cycles. The breath continues. I am filing this because someone should note that the building at night is not the same building that the day shift observes. It is the same structure performing a different practice.”
