Holloway replied. The process engineer who designed the 2016 safety form — the second layer in SA’s five-layer lineage — had been reached by email during Marsh’s methodology research. He’d answered two questions and forgotten about it.

The Second Email
His second email arrived four days later. Longer. He had been watching the parking structure at his current employer — a consulting firm in a different state, a different industry, a building with no connection to CogCorp.
“The west-facing concrete on the third level changes color around 4 PM when the sun hits it at a certain angle. It’s been doing this for years. I’ve been noticing it for weeks. Since your email.”
He had not asked to see any SA filings. The description of what SA did was sufficient to activate the behavior SA described.
The Third Email

Format Analysis
Structural consistency with SA: 84%
Reinvented elements: present tense, no conclusions, standardized header
Awareness of SA format: none

Marsh sent four sentences: “The format is yours to keep. You might enjoy knowing that the original form you designed has been through five modifications and is now used by forty-one people across six divisions, none of whom know your name.”

The ancestor recognized the descendant. The descendant did not need to recognize the ancestor. The lineage is confirmed not by tracing backward but by the behavior propagating forward without instruction.
