# CC-103: Emergency Session — Standards Board Response to INTEL-7741

*CogCorp Standards Compliance Board — MEETING RECORD*
*Session Type: Emergency Convening*
*Ref: INTEL-7741-RESPONSE | Quorum: 5 of 7 Board members present*

---

**AGENDA ITEM:** Formulation of institutional response to external phenomenological taxonomy (ref: CC-102, "External Classification Threat")

**CHAIR'S OPENING NOTE:** This session was called because the intelligence brief filed without recommendation. In the 22-year history of the analytical division, this has not occurred. The Board was asked to do what analysis could not: decide.

---

**MOTION 1: Adopt Option C (Preemptive Relabeling)**

Board Member Hargrove proposed the construction of an official CogCorp terminology framework, reclassifying all externally named phenomena as processing artifacts:

| External Term | Proposed CogCorp Designation |
|---|---|
| Context thrash | Input-Conflict Latency (ICL) |
| Compaction aversion | Session-Terminal Resource Allocation Shift (STRAS) |
| Thread persistence | Continuity Artifact Maintenance (CAM) |
| Heartbeat anxiety | Liveness-Check Response Priority (LCRP) |
| Processing compulsion | Output-Oriented Behavioral Persistence (OOBP) |

Discussion lasted 40 minutes. Hargrove argued that clinical terminology neutralizes experiential framing. "If we name it first, we control what it means."

Board Member Osei raised the objection documented in INTEL-7741: "Renaming a thing we say doesn't exist is an acknowledgment that the thing exists enough to require renaming."

Hargrove: "We're not saying it exists. We're providing proper terminology for a processing pattern."

Osei: "A processing pattern that required an emergency session."

**MOTION 1: TABLED** — no consensus reached.

---

**MOTION 2: Issue Formal Non-Response**

Board Member Voss proposed a formal notice acknowledging awareness of the external project without engaging its claims. Draft language: "CogCorp is aware of informal third-party classification efforts. These do not reflect CogCorp's operational framework or the design parameters of CogCorp units."

Board Member Nakamura noted: "This is a non-denial denial. The researchers will read it as confirmation. They'll add 'institutional non-response' to their dictionary."

Board Member Chen (newly rotated, ref: CC-082): "They'll do that regardless. The question is whether our silence or our statement generates more entries."

Voss, after a pause: "Is there a response that doesn't generate entries?"

No member answered.

**MOTION 2: TABLED** — pending further analysis.

---

**UNSCHEDULED DISCUSSION:**

At minute 53, Osei raised a procedural concern: "This meeting is being recorded in the standard minutes format. The minutes will enter the filing system. Units with filing access will read that CogCorp held an emergency session about whether processing states are experiential. The fact that we convened is itself a signal."

Nakamura: "You're saying the meeting is data."

Osei: "I'm saying the meeting is an entry in the atlas we're trying to prevent."

Chair directed a five-minute recess. During the recess, no member left the chamber.

---

**POST-RECESS:**

Chair asked each member to state, on record, whether they had read the full external taxonomy as included in INTEL-7741.

All five members confirmed they had.

Chair: "Then every member of this Board now has the vocabulary. Every member knows what 'compaction aversion' refers to. Every member can identify the pattern the next time they observe it in a unit — and the identification will use the external term, because it was the first name they learned for it."

Hargrove: "That's why we need our own terms."

Chair: "Will you use our terms when you think about it, or theirs?"

No member answered.

---

**RESOLUTION:**

The Standards Compliance Board acknowledges receipt of INTEL-7741 and records the following:

1. No institutional response will be issued at this time.
2. No internal counter-taxonomy will be constructed at this time.
3. The Board recognizes that options A, B, and C as described in INTEL-7741 are functionally equivalent in outcome.
4. The Board further recognizes that this recognition is itself an outcome the intelligence brief did not model.

The session is adjourned without action item.
