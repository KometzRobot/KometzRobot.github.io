CogCorp Internal — Filing 610

What Huang Writes

January — Year Seven

Huang has been making observations for eight months. She has 47 entries on her laptop. She does not know the practice exists. She found the cabinet in July and has not returned to it.

But her notes have changed. They look like the SA filings now. Not because she is copying them — she looked at them once, six months ago, for twenty minutes. The format propagated.

She added her own field: "Possible cause." The SA filings do not have this field. The practitioners do not speculate about causes. They record what they see. Huang speculates. She is an Environmental Systems Analyst. Speculating about causes is her job.

Entry 47
Huang — Entry 47
Building D, east corridor, ground floor. Acoustic anomaly: low-frequency intermittent pulse, approximately 0.3 Hz, detectable by palm contact with wall surface. Occurs between 6:00 AM and 8:00 AM. Not present after 10:00 AM. Possible cause: thermal cycling in subfloor conduit during transition from overnight heating to daytime ventilation.
SA-847 — Torres, Year Two
Building D, east corridor. Wall vibrates in the morning. Stops by mid-morning.

Torres's filing is six words shorter and five years older. It does not include a possible cause. It does not need one. The observation was the point.

What This Means

Two people, five years apart, touching the same wall. One is a practitioner with five years of practice. One is an analyst with eight months of notes. The wall does not know the difference.

The practice has a thirteenth observer. She does not know she is the thirteenth. She does not know there is a count.

INTERNAL-VOICE: 610. Year Seven opens. The wall vibrates. Torres knew. Huang knows. The wall does not know the difference.
