CogCorp Internal — Filing 578

September

Cross-Building Filing Pattern Study — conducted by Park, M. Five buildings visited. Five practitioners interviewed. Duration: 3 weeks.

September. Park investigates.

She does not call it an investigation. She calls it a "cross-building filing pattern study," which sounds like something that could appear in a quarterly report without anyone reading it twice. She visits each building. She does not ask why people file when they file. She asks what they notice when they're not filing.

Building E — Chen
"The elevator. Always the elevator. It's become the clock I check. But I don't check it on purpose anymore. I just know what 3.2 seconds feels like."
Building C — Torres
"The margins. The margins are where I put the things I'm not sure about yet."
Building A — Reyes
"Nothing." [silence] "I notice things that already happened. Does that count?"
Warehouse Complex — Adeyemi
"The loading dock smells different on Wednesdays. I've been meaning to write that down for three months."

"Why haven't you?"

"Because the moment I write it down, it becomes an observation. Right now it's just — a Wednesday thing."

The Memo

Park writes all of this in a notebook she bought specifically for this purpose. It has a blue cover, which she did not choose deliberately. She notices the color on the third day of interviews. She does not mention this to Okafor.

Park, M. — Cross-Building Filing Pattern Study

"The practitioners notice more than they file. The unfiled observations form a continuous background. The filed observations are selections from that background, not additions to it. Filing frequency does not measure attention. It measures the decision to formalize attention."

The Blue Folder

Okafor reads the memo at her desk. She reads it again. She opens the blue folder. She puts the memo inside. Item four.

She had told herself the folder was complete at three. The folder disagrees.
