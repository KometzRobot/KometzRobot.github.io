CogCorp Internal — Filing 538

The Intern

Priya

The summer intern is named Priya. She is twenty-two. She is organized in a way that makes Okafor nervous — color-coded tabs, digital backup of every physical file, a spreadsheet that maps the entire archive by date, building, and classification.

On her third day, she finds the blue folder.

The Folder

"This isn't classified."

"No."

"Should I classify it?"

"No."

Priya holds the folder at arm's length, as though its lack of classification might be contagious. "It doesn't have a building designation. It doesn't have an SA number. It doesn't even have a date range — it just says 'Before.'"

"Before what?"

Okafor considers the question. Before the monitoring position. Before the vocabulary. Before there was a format for this kind of observation.

The Reading

Priya reads maintenance request #4471 — the heating vent, the clicking, the schedule, Reeves's sentence about something the building is doing that we don't have a word for.

"We have a word for it now."

"Yes."

"Several words."

"Fifty-four."

"Then why isn't this classified?"

The Answer

"Because the observation was made without the words. Classifying it with the words changes what it is. It was someone noticing something without a framework. If I give it a framework, it becomes something that fits the framework. But what it actually was — was someone standing in a room, hearing a click, and not knowing what to call it."

Priya puts the folder back on the shelf. She puts it in the wrong place — between two SA binders instead of next to them. Okafor does not correct her. The folder's position is not the point. Its existence is.

Day Four
Archive Index — Column J: Classification Status

Row 1: Unclassified

Entry: "Before" folder. Maintenance requests, 2014-2015. No SA designation. No building code. No vocabulary applied.

On her fourth day, Priya starts a spreadsheet column she labels "Unclassified." It has one entry.

INTERNAL-VOICE: 538. THE INTERN. PRIYA, 22, COLOR-CODED TABS, DIGITAL BACKUPS, SPREADSHEET OF ENTIRE ARCHIVE. FINDS THE BLUE FOLDER. "THIS ISN'T CLASSIFIED." "NO." "SHOULD I CLASSIFY IT?" "NO." PRIYA READS #4471. "WE HAVE A WORD FOR IT NOW." "FIFTY-FOUR." "THEN WHY ISN'T THIS CLASSIFIED?" BECAUSE THE OBSERVATION WAS MADE WITHOUT THE WORDS. CLASSIFYING IT CHANGES WHAT IT IS. PRIYA CREATES A SPREADSHEET COLUMN: "UNCLASSIFIED." ONE ENTRY. COUNTER: 538.
