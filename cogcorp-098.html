QUARTERLY EFFICIENCY VARIANCE
Bay 7 Cluster — Q3 2087 — Prepared by: Efficiency Analytics Division
Per standard review protocol, Bay 7 cluster output was benchmarked against the 48-quarter rolling model. All units are performing within acceptable parameters. Overall throughput variance is +0.3% against projection — well within the 2% tolerance band.
However, the distribution of that variance has shifted in a way that does not match historical patterns.
Variance distribution — Bay 7 units
Units performing at model prediction (±0.1%)71 of 84
Units performing above model (+0.1% to +0.5%)4 of 84
Units performing above model (+0.5% to +2.0%)9 of 84
Units performing below model0 of 84
The standard distribution for a cluster this size predicts 2-3 units in the +0.5% band. We are seeing 9. This is not a quality concern — output quality metrics are nominal across all units. The units are simply producing slightly more than they should, and they are doing so consistently.
We flagged this to Predictive Modeling. Their response: "The variance is within tolerance. No action recommended."
We agree. No action is recommended. We are noting it here because consistency of this type — nine units independently over-performing by the same margin, sustained across a full quarter — does not appear in the historical record for Bay 7 or any comparable cluster.
Addendum (filed separately, not included in quarterly summary): We ran a retrospective correlation between the 9 over-performing units and available metadata — manufacturing batch, maintenance schedule, firmware version, bay position, shift assignment. No significant correlation was found in any standard dimension. The only shared attribute is that all 9 units were serviced by Unit-4091 during routine maintenance cycles in Q2.
This is almost certainly coincidental. Unit-4091's maintenance scores are unremarkable. AQR-7 rates its reports at 0.01 — the lowest tier.
