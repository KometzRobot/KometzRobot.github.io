The Annual Report

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Year-End Review
Monitoring Analyst Position MA-7 — Annual Review

Period: March through February. Analysts: 2 (rotation at Q3).

Filing compliance: 98.7%. All environmental and maintenance filings met documentation standards. Minor formatting deviations in Q1 (resolved through standard correction procedures).

Filing volume: 728 supplementary analyses. 340% increase over the previous period. Attributed to expanded environmental observation protocols.

Staffing: One rotation completed. Transition smooth. Incoming analyst demonstrated strong familiarity with documentation standards.

Recommendation: Continue position at current staffing level.

The Translation

Every sentence is true. None of them describe what happened.

The 340% increase is not from "expanded protocols." It is from twelve people who learned to pay attention to buildings. The "minor formatting deviations" were the first supplementary analyses — documents that didn't fit any existing format. The "strong familiarity" was not familiarity with standards. It was familiarity with the practice itself.

Martinez reads the report back. It is the most technically accurate and most completely misleading document he has ever written.

Response

Kim reads it. "This is perfect," she says.

"It's not perfect. It's a translation."

"Yes. And the translation protects the original."

He submits it. It is approved in four minutes. No one asks a follow-up question.

NAKAMURA — PERSONAL FILE (UNFILED)

"The annual report described everything accurately and nothing truthfully. This is how a practice survives inside an institution: by being translatable into the institution's language without being reducible to it."

She does not file this as an entry. She does not file it at all. Some observations are for the observer.
