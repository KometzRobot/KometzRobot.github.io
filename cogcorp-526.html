CC-526

The Decision Not to Look

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Cross-Facility Assessment
The Proposal

Kim drafts a proposal: "Cross-facility observation pattern assessment." Scope: all fourteen CogCorp buildings. Method: archive review of maintenance and environmental filings. Estimated timeline: six weeks.

Martinez reads the proposal and does not sign it.

"If we look," he says, "we change what we find."

The Argument

Kim: "We already found it in Building C."

"Nakamura found it in Building C. She found it because she was one of them. She recognized the structure because she had the structure. If we send analysts into twelve buildings looking for a practice they already know exists, every observation we make will be shaped by what we expect to find."

Kim: "That's how all observation works."

"Yes. And that's why the Building C practitioners were genuine — because nobody was looking. The attention was real because it was unobserved. The moment we name it, count it, and file a cross-facility report, it becomes something else. It becomes official."

The Decision

Kim: "You're saying the practice survives because we don't formalize it."

"I'm saying the practice in Building E survived formalization because it happened slowly, over fourteen months, and it grew its own language. If we walk into Building J and say 'we've identified a pattern in your filings that matches a documented practice in two other buildings' — we're not discovering it. We're importing it."

Kim: "So we don't look."

Martinez: "We don't look."

SUPPLEMENTARY ANALYSIS ARCHIVE — BOUNDARY MARKER

The parallel observation report is filed. Not as evidence. Not as justification. As a boundary marker. It says: here is where we stopped looking because looking would have changed what was there.

Entry 219

Nakamura, when told, writes nothing in her monitoring log. She does not write Entry 219 for five weeks.

When she finally does, it is not about the decision. It is about the light in the east stairwell of Building E, which changes color between 2:15 and 2:45 PM when the sun passes behind the adjacent parking structure.

She is monitoring the building. Not the practice. The building.

INTERNAL-VOICE
