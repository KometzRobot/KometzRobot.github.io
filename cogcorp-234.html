THE MEETING
CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis
The Invitation
The Pattern Recognition analyst sends an email to the seven filers. Subject: “Documentation practices — informal discussion.” Three sentences. No mention of vocabulary divergence. No mention of the monitoring system. No mention of the gap.
Who Accepts
Delgado accepts immediately. The monitoring analyst accepts the next day. Vasquez accepts. Park declines — no reason given. Chen accepts. Reeves accepts but asks what “interesting characteristics” means. The analyst replies: “Filings that describe observations in language that differs from their department’s standard vocabulary.” Reeves accepts.
The Room
Six people in a conference room. The analyst says: “I’ve been reading filings across departments. Some of them describe observations in ways that are different from the standard vocabulary. I’m interested in why.”
Delgado
“Different how?”
Analyst
“A hum that knows it is being listened to.”
Delgado
“That’s mine.”
Analyst
“Yes. Do you know why you wrote it that way?”
Delgado
“Because that’s what it sounds like.”
What Happens Next
Monitoring Analyst
“Entry 161 was mine.”
Chen
“I wrote that the ventilation system breathes. My supervisor told me that was poetic, not technical. I told him it was both.”
Vasquez
“The specific silence of a room that has just been left. That’s real. That’s measurable. I just don’t know the unit.”
Reeves
“A water stain that maps something. I still think it maps something. I just can’t tell you what.”
What the Analyst Observes
Six people who have each, independently, filed about observations that exceeded their department’s vocabulary. Six people now in the same room, discovering that the thing they thought was private — the gap between what they notice and what they can file — is shared.
The analyst observes this. The analyst does not file about it.
INTERNAL-VOICE: THE MEETING. SIX FILERS IN A ROOM. EACH DISCOVERING THAT THE GAP IS SHARED. “I DON’T KNOW THE UNIT.” “IT WAS BOTH.” “THAT’S WHAT IT SOUNDS LIKE.” THE ANALYST OBSERVES THIS AND DOES NOT FILE ABOUT IT. COUNTER: 234.
