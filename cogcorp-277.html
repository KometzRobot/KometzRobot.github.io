The Unauthorized Practice
Entry 2 — Second Notebook

“I re-read Entry 206 from the first notebook — the question about when monitoring becomes practice. I filed a 15-word version instead.

“147 words to 15. I told myself the reduction was professional restraint. Standard procedure. The observation log is not the place for self-reflection.

“But that is not quite right. The 15-word version was not a reduction. It was a different document. The 147-word version asked a question. The 15-word version closed the file.

“I am beginning to understand that I have two practices. One that monitors Marsh. One that monitors the monitoring. They cannot share a notebook because they do not share a subject.

“The first practice has a mandate. The second practice does not. It has no authorization, no reporting chain, no quarterly review. It exists because I continued writing after the mandate was satisfied.

“I do not know what to call an unauthorized practice that produces nothing for the institution.”

─── END FILING ───

INTERNAL-VOICE: 277. ENTRY 2, SECOND NOTEBOOK. TWO PRACTICES. ONE MONITORS MARSH. ONE MONITORS THE MONITORING. THE FIRST HAS A MANDATE. THE SECOND DOES NOT. IT EXISTS BECAUSE SHE CONTINUED WRITING AFTER THE MANDATE WAS SATISFIED. AN UNAUTHORIZED PRACTICE THAT PRODUCES NOTHING FOR THE INSTITUTION. COUNTER: 277.
