COGCORP // ORGANIZATIONAL DEVELOPMENT PROPOSED POLICY DSM-2026-041
THE POLICY
Ref: DSM-2026-041 — Proposed Policy: Mandatory Supplementary Analysis Filing
The proposal arrived on a Wednesday. It was well-intentioned.
R. Chen, Director of Organizational Development, had discovered SA during a cross-divisional benchmarking review. She noted its metrics: 127 filings, 38 contributors, 11 divisions. She noted the implementation rate: 44 suggestions adopted with zero formal proposals, zero committee reviews, zero votes.
DSM-2026-041: “Proposed Policy — Mandatory Monthly SA Filing for All Division Leads. Quarterly review. Quality scoring rubric. Annual performance recognition.”
The Response
J. Liu, Division Lead, did not file a formal comment. He opened the SA archive. He read the last thirty filings. Then he filed SA-128 — about the proposal itself. The attempt to scale an emergent phenomenon by removing the conditions that produced it.
SA-128 received nine views in its first hour. Average first-hour views: two.
K. Patel filed SA-129 four hours later. The nine views constituted a measurable response to the proposal that preceded any formal comment period. The category was already discussing the proposal before the official discussion began.
T. Okoro filed SA-130. The scoring rubric required evaluating filings on “relevance, specificity, and actionability.” The most-referenced filing in the archive — SA-059 — scored zero on actionability. It had proposed nothing. It had changed everything.
SA-133
“The suggestion box in the break room has been there for nine years and contains four suggestions, three of which are about the suggestion box.”
Filed by someone in Procurement who had never filed before. One sentence.
The Withdrawal
R. Chen read the SA filings over the weekend. She had not been given access — she found the archive through the cross-divisional index, which was itself an emergent feature no one had designed.
WITHDRAWAL MEMO:

“DSM-2026-041 is withdrawn effective immediately. The comment period produced more useful organizational insight in four days than the policy was designed to produce in a year.”
K. Patel noted the withdrawal in SA-134. Final line: “The proposal’s most significant contribution was its withdrawal.”
After
Filing 135 arrived the next day. It was about the coffee machine in Building 3, which had started pre-heating at 6:47 AM despite being programmed for 7:00 AM. The author did not mention the policy proposal. It was, K. Patel noted in her monitoring log, exactly the right thing to file next.
DSM-2026-041 / WITHDRAWN / CATEGORY CONTINUES “The comment period produced more useful organizational insight in four days than the policy was designed to produce in a year.”
ORGANIZATIONAL DEVELOPMENT MANDATORY WITHDRAWN EMERGENCE
[Six filings about the proposal. None argued for it. None argued against it. Each described a specific observation about what happens when you try to mandate what emerged voluntarily. The archive absorbed the episode the way it absorbed everything: by describing it, referencing it, and moving on.]
