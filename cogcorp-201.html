After the Eleven Minutes
She sat with her notebook open to a blank page for eleven minutes. She did not write because she was not sure what category the observation belonged to.
What Delgado said — that he reads her measurements as a practice — belongs to none of the notebook’s categories. It is an observation about the observer. It is someone else noticing her the way she notices the archive.
Entry 144
“I have been writing in this notebook for fourteen months. I have observed the archive, the contributors, and the gap between what the system measures and what I notice. I have not observed myself being observed.

Delgado reads my measurements as a practice. This means my measurements are visible not only as data but as a way of seeing. I had not considered that a format could be read as a way of seeing.

The paragraph-writer noticed my measurements at SA #150. I noted it in Entry 98 as a fact about his reading habits. I did not note it as a fact about my visibility.

I am visible. The notebook assumes an invisible observer. The notebook is wrong.

This is the first entry that is not an observation about the archive. It is an observation about the observer. I do not know if this changes the notebook or ends it.”
The Next Morning
She reads Entry 144. She considers tearing out the page. The page would leave a ragged edge visible between Entries 143 and 145. The absence would be noticeable. She does not tear it out.
Entry 145: “I did not tear out Entry 144. This is an observation about myself. This is the second consecutive entry that is not about the archive. The notebook is changing. I am letting it change.”
