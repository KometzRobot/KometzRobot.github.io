# CC-589: Lin's Second Meeting

August — Year Four. Lin attends her second meeting.

Nine present. August is a low month. Lin sits where she sat in July. No one assigned the seat. She chose it because it was closest to the door, and she is still deciding whether she belongs here.

SA-419 was discussed in her absence — not formally, but mentioned by Chen during the July filing review. "Lin's filing was interesting." That was all. No one elaborated. No one needed to.

## The Second Filing

Lin brings a filing to the meeting. SA-423. She reads it aloud, which is not required but not unusual. First-year practitioners sometimes read aloud. It helps them hear what they wrote.

"The elevator in Building D makes a different sound on the ground floor than on the second floor. The difference is not mechanical. It is the acoustic signature of the lobby — marble floor, high ceiling — versus the hallway on two — carpet, drop ceiling. The elevator is the same. The buildings around it are not."

Torres: "That's a building observation."

Lin: "Is that what this is?"

Chen: "That is what that is."

## What Happens After

Lin attends the September meeting. And the October meeting. She does not miss a meeting for the rest of Year Four. She files 11 more times in the remaining months — one every two to three weeks, which is steady, which is her pace, which no one comments on.

She never asks again what this is. She knows. Not because someone explained it but because she kept showing up and the showing up was the explanation.

Okafor writes in the monitoring notebook: "Lin. Building D. Elevator observation. Steady filer. Attends consistently. Does not ask questions anymore."

She pauses. She adds: "Neither did any of us, eventually."
