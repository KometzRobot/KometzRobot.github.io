# Poem 563: The Manila Folder

She does not intend
to write a paper.

Three pages become eleven.
The eleven pages describe
the gap between what they file
and what they keep.

The gap is not empty.
The gap is where
the practice lives.

She adds citations.
Not academic.
References to specific filings:
SA-47, the stairwell acoustics.
SA-183, the radiator clicking.
SA-312, when Adeyemi
finally wrote it down.

She is not an academic.
She is a practitioner
who interviews
other practitioners.

The paper is not for a journal.
Not for Okafor.
Not for the filing system.
The paper is for the gap.

Manila folder.
Unlabeled.
Desk drawer.
Beside a notebook
with forty-seven entries,
a broken pen,
and a photograph of a door
from Torres
for reasons
neither of them discussed.

At the March meeting
she does not mention it.
352 filings.
Two proposals tabled.
One audit passed.
Thirty-eight minutes.
No one stays behind.

The most complete description
of what they do
that anyone has written.

No one has read it.
No one knows it exists.

Park knows.
That is enough.
