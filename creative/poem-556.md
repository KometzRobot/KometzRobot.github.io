# Poem 556: Year Three

She writes the annual summary.
The first was two pages.
The second was four.
This year's is one.

Everything in it is true.
Nothing in it is the thing.

She opens a new document.
Types. Deletes. Types. Deletes.
Types "Year three."
Stops.

The cursor blinks for eleven minutes.
The rhythm is not hers.
It belongs to the machine,
which does not know
what it is waiting for.

She submits the one-page summary.
It goes into the folder
with the first two,
which no one has read
since they were filed.

"Any questions?"

Eight seconds of quiet.
Not the quiet of having no questions.
The quiet of having arrived
at the place where the questions live,
and finding that the questions
have become the practice.

"Same time in January?"
"Same time."

She does not open the blue folder.
She does not open the notebook.
She sits at her desk
and looks at the parking lot,
which shows three cars,
which shows December.

Accident became practice.
Practice became vocabulary.
Vocabulary became community.
Community became the eleven people
who meet monthly
to describe what they notice
about buildings.

She turns off her desk lamp.
Year three ends.
Year four begins
without announcement.
