# CC-617: What Torres Tells Okafor

June — Year Seven.

Torres mentions it at the June meeting. "I saw someone in the north stairwell. Building A, between three and four. She was looking at the light on the landing."

Okafor: "Looking at it how?"

Torres: "The way we look at it."

The room adjusts. This is new information. Torres has seen the person who has been reading the cabinet filings. He did not speak to her. He does not know her name. He knows she was looking at light the way a practitioner looks at light.

Lin: "Did she file anything?"

Torres: "She was writing on a laptop."

Adeyemi: "A laptop is not a filing."

Torres: "No. But she was there."

## What The Group Knows

The group now knows: someone has been reading the filings. Someone has been in the stairwell observing light patterns. Someone writes on a laptop. The group does not know that all three facts describe the same person.

Okafor asks: "Should we do anything?"

No one answers immediately. This is not the same question as the locked cabinet. The cabinet question was about access. This question is about contact.

Reyes: "We don't recruit. We never have."

Torres: "I'm not suggesting we recruit. I'm saying she was there."

The distinction matters. There is a difference between inviting someone in and acknowledging that they arrived on their own.

## What Park Writes

Park writes: "The stairwell encounter. Torres saw someone practicing without knowing it was a practice. The group's instinct: do nothing. Not because nothing should be done, but because the practice has never expanded by intention. It expands by adjacency. The cabinet is adjacent. The stairwell is adjacent. The meeting is not adjacent to someone who does not know it exists."

She writes: "The question is not whether to include her. The question is whether the practice can include someone without the meeting."
