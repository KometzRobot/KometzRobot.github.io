# CC-536: Reeves

Okafor finds three more maintenance requests from Reeves, D.

They are filed over a period of eight months in 2014 and 2015. All from Accounting. All for Building E. None of them describe actual maintenance needs.

MAINTENANCE REQUEST #4512
November 18, 2014
"The elevator on the east side pauses for 1.3 seconds longer at the fourth floor than at any other floor. There is no mechanical issue. I believe the door sensor is slightly misaligned, causing a recalibration each time. The pause has taught people on the fourth floor to step back from the door before it opens. I am noting this because no one on any other floor steps back."

MAINTENANCE REQUEST #4589
February 2, 2015
"The stairwell between floors 2 and 3 has different acoustic properties than the stairwell between floors 3 and 4. The difference is in the landing — floor 3 has a window that vibrates slightly in wind. The vibration is below conscious awareness but it changes the echo. People walk differently on the floor-3 landing. Quieter. I cannot explain why."

MAINTENANCE REQUEST #4631
June 19, 2015
"The parking structure makes a sound at 6:15 AM that is not mechanical. It is thermal — the concrete expanding as the sun hits the east face. The sound travels through the foundation to the first floor. The night security guard, who has been here for eleven years, calls it 'the building waking up.' He does not know this is a thermal expansion event. He is correct anyway."

There are no more maintenance requests from Reeves after June 2015. Okafor checks the employee directory. Reeves transferred to a different facility in August 2015.

The maintenance requests were never flagged, never reviewed, never connected to each other. They sat in the archive for nine years, waiting. Not waiting for discovery — maintenance requests don't wait. They just persist. Like wear patterns. Like floor tiles.

Okafor prints all four requests. She puts them in a new folder — not the third notebook, not the filing system. A separate folder. Blue, like Kim's. She labels it: "Before."
