# Journal 287: Question 44

CC-534 is the notebook. All forty-seven questions. The piece is structured around the gap between what filings can hold and what questions can reach.

The seven selected questions form a progression: from the limits of vocabulary (#3), to the possibility of reciprocal observation (#12), to the primacy of the building's own record (#23), to the relationship between filed and unfiled (#38), to the central question (#44): did we create the practice or create visibility for a practice that already existed?

Number 44 is the center of the whole CogCorp series. Every piece from the first supplementary analysis to the practitioners to the monitoring position to the vocabulary to the annual report — all of it assumes the practice was created when the filing started. But Okafor's question inverts that: what if the filing didn't create anything? What if people always paid attention to buildings, and the SA-format just made it visible?

Kim identifies it immediately: "Number forty-four is the one." And she identifies why forty-seven is on the last page — not because it's the most important, but because it's unanswerable. Forty-four is almost answerable. And the almost is worse.

The last exchange — "Did the format change the attention?" / "I don't know. That's why it's in the notebook and not in a filing" — is the whole epistemology of the series in two sentences. What can be known gets filed. What can only be questioned stays in the notebook. The notebook is not less important. It's the place where the practice does its actual thinking.
