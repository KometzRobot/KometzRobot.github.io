# CC-552: The Second Notebook

By June the first notebook is full. Forty-three entries. Seven in the same handwriting. The plant waterer.

Okafor buys a second notebook. Same kind — no grid lines, no categories. She does not label it "Building A Supplementary Observations" or "Unmonitored Practice Documentation" or anything else. She leaves it blank. She puts it on the windowsill behind the fire extinguisher, on top of the full one.

On the bus she realizes she has never filed a supplementary analysis for any of this. Forty-three observations in five months and not one of them entered the system. The monitoring position for Building A has produced twelve filings in nine years, all from different people, and forty-three entries in five months from people who do not know they are filing.

She opens her monitoring notebook. The last entry is from February. Question 55.

She writes Question 56: "Is the notebook part of the monitoring system?"

She does not answer it. She suspects the answer changes depending on whether she answers it.

Kim calls. Quarterly check-in.

"Anything to report from Building A?"

"Forty-three observations in five months."

Kim pauses. "From who?"

"I don't know. They don't sign them."

"Are they in the system?"

"No."

"Should they be?"

"If I put them in the system they would stop."

Kim is quiet for eleven seconds. Okafor counts.

"Keep the notebook."

"I intend to."

"Don't file it."

"I wasn't going to."

"Okafor."

"Yes."

"This is the most interesting thing happening in any of the buildings."

"I know."
