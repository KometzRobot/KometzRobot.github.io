# CC-544: The Term Applies

Chen submits three pages with SA-891. The third page is unusual. It is not about Building E. It is about the term itself.

"Functional drift applies to the monitoring position."

Okafor reads the sentence twice. She puts the filing down. She picks it up again.

"The monitoring position was created to track maintenance anomalies. It drifted into behavioral observation. Behavioral observation drifted into vocabulary development. Vocabulary development drifted into a practice with forty-seven practitioners across eleven buildings. None of these transitions were designed. Each one occurred because the previous function was adapted by its participants into something the original role did not anticipate."

She reads it a third time.

"The monitoring position is a case of functional drift at institutional scale. The error — one person paying too much attention — became a feature. The feature was never approved. The adaptation was never taught. New practitioners arrive because the function already exists. They do not know it was not intended."

Okafor sits with the filing for a long time. She does not write anything. She does not file it.

The vocabulary has fifty-five terms. Fifty-four of them describe building behavior. Term fifty-five describes the process by which buildings and occupants co-adapt. And now Chen has used term fifty-five to describe the practice that created terms one through fifty-four.

She opens her notebook — the one Priya logged as "Unclassified, status: in use."

Question 51: "If functional drift describes the monitoring position, does the term validate the practice or dissolve it?"

She does not write an answer. She is not sure the question has one.

She files SA-891 in the regular binder. Pages one and two. Page three she folds once and places in the blue folder, next to Reeves's maintenance requests and the other documents that belong to before — or after — the vocabulary.

The folder is getting thicker.
