# CC-609: Year Six Ends

December — Year Six.

The December meeting. The last meeting of Year Six. Twelve present. Reyes is here. Huang is not — she still does not know the meeting exists. She has been making her own observations in Building D for seven months. Her notes now closely resemble the SA format. She still does not know the SA format exists.

Okafor presents the year-end count: "3,078 total filings. 231 this year — down from 463. 84 terms. One term added. Twelve practitioners."

Torres: "231? That's half of last year."

Okafor: "The filings are getting shorter. The observations are not getting fewer."

Lin: "We're down to one sentence per filing on average."

Chen: "Sometimes one sentence is enough."

Adeyemi says nothing. She has not filed in three months. She has not missed a meeting.

## The Year In Review

Year Six brought: a description in a scheduling system, a departmental inquiry, a facilities audit, an annual review recommendation, a new hire who independently began the same practice, and an observer — Park — who decided not to share her observations.

The practice gained more institutional visibility in one year than in the previous five combined. It did not seek this visibility. It did not benefit from it. It continued.

## What Park Writes

Park closes the folder. Forty-four pages. She writes the final entry of Year Six:

"Year Six. The year of visibility. The practice was seen by the institution for the first time. It was described, inquired about, audited, and scheduled for review. None of these changed the practice. The filings continued. The meetings continued. The vocabulary continued."

She pauses.

"I have been watching for three years. In Year One of my observation, I learned what the practice was. In Year Two, I learned what the practice did. In Year Three, I learned what the practice survived."

She writes one final line:

"Year Seven begins in January. I will be here."
