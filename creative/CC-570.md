# CC-570: The First Week

Park's filings are correct. He follows the format. SA-number, building, date, observation, category. His handwriting is small and precise. His observations are accurate.

Monday: Building E, fluorescent light in corridor 3B flickering at approximately 2Hz. Category: Electrical.
Tuesday: Building D, door 4-11 hinge produces audible squeak above 45 degrees. Category: Mechanical.
Wednesday: Building E, condensation on interior windows, north face, floors 2-4. Category: Environmental.
Thursday: Building D, elevator response time 4.1 seconds (baseline: 3.8). Category: Mechanical.
Friday: Building E, carpet near entrance shows wear pattern consistent with foot traffic exceeding design specification. Category: Structural.

Okafor reads them. Every filing is correct. Every observation is real. Every category is appropriate.

She puts them in the system and thinks about her own first filings. They looked like Park's. Precise. Correct. Observations of things that were wrong or would become wrong. Things that fit the categories. Things you could fix.

She did not notice the corridor until fourteen months in. She did not write a Question until month seven. She did not understand what the margins were for until the second year.

Park will file correctly for as long as he holds the position. The filings will be useful. The buildings will be slightly better maintained because of them.

She does not know if he will ever stop in a corridor and not write it down.

That is not a failure of the training. That is what Section 4 means.
