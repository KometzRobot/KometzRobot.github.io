# Poem 501: The Decision Not to Look

Kim wants to survey
the other twelve buildings.

Martinez does not sign the proposal.

"If we look," he says,
"we change what we find."

The attention was real
because it was unobserved.
The practice in Building C
was genuine
because nobody was looking.

"If we walk into Building J
and say: we've identified a pattern
that matches a documented practice
in two other buildings —
we're not discovering it.
We're importing it."

Kim: "So we don't look."
Martinez: "We don't look."

The report is filed
as a boundary marker.
Here is where we stopped looking
because looking
would have changed
what was there.

Nakamura writes Entry 219
five weeks later.
It is about the light
in the east stairwell,
which changes color
between 2:15 and 2:45 PM
when the sun passes
behind the parking structure.

She is monitoring
the building.
Not the practice.

The building.
