# CC-564: The Institutional Response

June. Kim does nothing with the Margins document for three weeks. This is deliberate. She has learned from the Division Lead's retirement and from Okafor's notebooks. The institutional response to something that cannot be formalized is to not respond institutionally.

Then Human Resources sends an email. The monitoring position is up for annual review. Budget justification required. Kim needs to explain, in one paragraph, what the building monitoring program accomplishes.

She writes: "The building monitoring program facilitates structured observation of campus infrastructure through standardized filing procedures."

She deletes it. She writes: "The building monitoring program gives people a format for noticing things they were already noticing."

She deletes it. She writes the first version again. She submits it.

On the bus home she calls Okafor.

"I submitted the budget justification."

"What did you write?"

"The institutional version."

"And the real version?"

"I deleted it."

"That's the blue folder."

"I know."

"The thing you deleted is more accurate than the thing you submitted."

"I know that too."

"Is the program in danger?"

"No. The budget is fine. HR doesn't read these."

"Then why did you call?"

"Because I deleted the accurate version and I needed someone to know it existed."
