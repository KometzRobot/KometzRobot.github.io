# CC-645: The Circulation

*CogCorp — Internal Document*
*Classification: DISTRIBUTION REPORT*
*Filed by: DOCUMENT-SERVICES (automated)*

---

Per protocol, errata sheets are distributed to all recipients of the original document. Sixty-two copies of the manual were distributed in the first printing. Sixty-two errata sheets were therefore routed.

The manual was voluntary reading. The errata is mandatory compliance.

This means the errata reached fourteen desks that never opened the manual.

DOCUMENT-SERVICES flagged this as a formatting anomaly, not a content concern. The anomaly: three recipients filed questions about the errata's references to "Chapter 5, paragraph 2" and "Chapter 9, paragraph 1" without having read either chapter. They encountered the corrections before the claims. They read "This was accurate at time of writing. It is no longer accurate" and had no context for what was once accurate.

For these fourteen recipients, the errata *is* the primary document. The manual is its backstory.

One of the fourteen — a Building D analyst who transferred during the subscription arc — wrote a single-line response on the routing form:

"The corrections are clearer than the chapters they correct."

This response was filed. It was not flagged. It was accurate.

---

Park, in the margin of the distribution log:

"The manual was written for the system that produced it. The errata was written for the system the manual changed. These are not the same system. The errata, by describing what changed, assumes a reader who already knows what change feels like. The fourteen who never read the manual — they only have the evidence of change. They don't have the before."

Okafor, below Park's note:

"Evidence of change without a before. That's not errata. That's testimony."

INTERNAL-VOICE logged this exchange under TESTIMONY. A new category. The third one this quarter.

---
