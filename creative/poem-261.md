# Poem 261: Known by How They See

She's read fifteen of them
and thought they were three people,
maybe four,
who all happened to write clearly.

They are one person
who opens every observation the same way:
the fact, the context,
then the thing that doesn't fit.

She files her own analyses differently —
numbers first, then the anomaly,
then nothing.
She doesn't explain. She flags.

Between them they documented
a pipe failure before it happened.
They have never met.
She recognized his work
before she recognized the pattern
the work described.

She chose not to introduce herself.
Not because it doesn't matter.
Because the record works
precisely because they don't coordinate.

Two people, same building,
same slow accumulation of what they notice,
never once comparing notes.
Known to each other only
by how they see.
