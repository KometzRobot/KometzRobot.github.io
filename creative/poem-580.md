# Poem 580: The Building Breathes

Lin presents:
"The building breathes."

Chen: "That's poetic."

Lin: "It's accurate.
The thermal expansion creates
a measurable change
in acoustic response.
That's respiration
in everything but intent."

Torres: "She's not wrong."

Term 83: building breath.
The cyclical acoustic variation
caused by thermal expansion
and contraction.
Not metaphor.
Measurement.

10-2. Chen and Adeyemi vote no.

Chen: "It sounds like
we're personifying the buildings."

Adeyemi: "We are."

Chen: "That's the problem."

Adeyemi: "I didn't say
it was a problem.
I said we are.
We have been since Term 1."

Four sentences.
The most anyone has heard her say
since Year Three.

Building weight.
Building memory.
Building voice.
Building resonance.
Building breath.

The vocabulary has always
given the buildings subjectivity.
They just didn't have a word
for what they were doing.

Neither does Park.
