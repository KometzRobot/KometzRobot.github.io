# CC-574: Year Three

May. Okafor begins her third year. The position is funded. Park covers six buildings. The practitioners meet quarterly. The vocabulary has grown to 71 terms. The filing system works.

She opens her monitoring notebook. Not the manual. Not the evaluation form. Not the budget report. The notebook that goes in the blue folder.

She writes: "Year Three."

She waits. She does not know what comes after Year Three. Year One built the format. Year Two understood the format is not the practice. Year Three —

She writes: "Year Three is the year the practice does not need to be explained."

She reads it back. It is not quite right. The practice has never needed to be explained. It needed a format, and a vocabulary, and a manual, and a budget, and an evaluation, and a successor. It needed all of those things. It never needed any of them.

She crosses out the sentence and writes: "Year Three is the year I stop writing about the practice and go back to doing it."

She closes the notebook. She puts on her coat. She walks to Building A. Not on the schedule. Not for an inspection. She walks through the lobby, takes the stairs to the fourth floor, and stands in the corridor.

It is warm.

She does not write it down.
