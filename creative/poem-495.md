# Poem 495: A Prior Observation I Have Not Yet Made

The corridor in Building E
has something I haven't noticed.

I know it's there
the way you know a word
before you learn it —
the sentence has a shape
and the shape has a hole
and the hole has a name
you haven't heard yet.

She said she'd return Thursday.

This is the consequence
of not naming things:
the existing names
get deeper.

"Prior observation"
used to mean a pencil line
from 1987.

Now it means:
something I noticed
before I knew
I was noticing.

The vocabulary has 52 words.
The vocabulary contains
more than 52 meanings.

The gap is getting larger.

The monitoring analyst
does not think
this is a problem.

She is right.
A language becomes alive
the moment its words
stop meaning
only what they were told to mean.
