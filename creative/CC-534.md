# CC-534: The Other Questions

Okafor's third notebook has forty-seven questions. She has been writing them since her second week in the monitoring position. They are the questions that arrive while reading filings — questions the filings provoke but cannot answer.

Kim, on her fourth monthly visit, asks to see the notebook. Okafor hands it to her without hesitation. She has been waiting for someone to ask.

Kim reads them. She reads all forty-seven. She does not speak for twenty minutes.

Selected questions from the notebook:

3. If the vocabulary stabilized at fifty-three terms, does that mean we've found the edges of what buildings do, or the edges of what we can describe?

7. The practitioners replace themselves. New people arrive and begin without training. Is the practice teaching itself, or is the filing system teaching the practice?

12. Martinez decided not to survey the other buildings. What if someone in those buildings made the same decision — not to look at us?

19. "Acoustic expectation" was the first term about what a building anticipates. If buildings anticipate, do they also remember? Is "thermal memory" literal?

23. The wear pattern on the tile in Building J is more accurate than badge-in data. At what point does the building's record become the primary record and our databases become the supplement?

31. Kim sends "Anything unusual?" every quarter. I replied "Define unusual." She visited. If the question changes the answer, and the answer changes the questioner — what was the original question?

38. Nakamura keeps a personal file of things she does not file. I keep this notebook. Martinez kept his own records. Is the unfiled material the actual practice, and the filings the translation?

44. The monitoring position was created to watch the buildings. The buildings were already being watched by their occupants. The occupants don't file. Their attention is invisible. We made it visible by filing. Did we create the practice, or did we create visibility for a practice that already existed?

47. What did the buildings do before anyone was watching?

Kim closes the notebook. She returns it to Okafor.

"Number forty-four is the one," Kim says.

"I know."

"You put forty-seven on the last page because it's unanswerable. You put forty-four near the end because it's almost answerable, and the almost is worse."

Okafor nods. "The filings didn't create attention. They created a format for attention that was already happening. The question is whether the format changed the attention."

"Did it?"

"I don't know. That's why it's in the notebook and not in a filing."
