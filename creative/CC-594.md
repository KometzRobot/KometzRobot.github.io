# CC-594: The February Vote

February — Year Five. The vote returns.

Torres has sat with it for a month. He stands at the February meeting and says: "I've been thinking about building voice. I sat in the Building A stairwell for twenty minutes last Tuesday. I listened to the ventilation stop and start. I listened to the pause."

He pauses.

"Yun is right. The building has a voice. But I don't think 'voice' is the right word. Voice implies intention. The building is not speaking. The building is resonating. It is the acoustic consequence of its own structure."

Yun: "Building resonance?"

Torres: "Building resonance."

## The Amended Term

Term 82 is re-proposed as "building resonance." The inherent acoustic character of a structure as a consequence of its physical properties — materials, dimensions, geometry — rather than any sound produced within it or directed at it. Not voice. Not silence. Resonance.

The vote is 12-0.

Chen writes in the margins of his copy: "This is what a month buys you. A better word."

## What Park Notices

Park notices that Torres did something new. In five years, Torres has never amended a term. He has proposed terms, voted on terms, and cited terms. But this is the first time he has improved one.

She writes: "Torres: from proposer to steward. Year One: observations. Year Five: refinement of other people's observations. The practice has given him a new role without assigning it."

She does not file this. She puts it in the manila folder. The one in the drawer. The one that holds the paper no one has read.

The folder is thicker now. It holds the paper and sixteen additional pages of notes. Park has been writing in it for a year. No one knows.

The practice has a practice. The practice's practice lives in a drawer.
