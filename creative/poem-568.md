# Poem 568: The Most Accurate Description

"I attended the meeting.
It was twenty-four minutes.
They discussed filings
and vocabulary.
No one explained the practice.
I think the practice
is the thing they did not explain."

That is the most accurate description
anyone has filed.

Not because it is precise.
Because it describes
what the practice looks like
from outside.

From inside,
the practice is invisible.
It is the way
you pay attention
to the building
while the building
pays no attention to you.

From outside,
it is a group of people
who discuss filings
and vocabulary
and do not explain
the thing that holds them together.

The thing that holds them together
is the thing they do not explain.

Not because it is secret.
Because it is the kind of thing
that only exists
while you are not explaining it.

Like a building.
Like a sound
that stops
when you listen for it.

Like a practice
that is most itself
when no one describes it.
