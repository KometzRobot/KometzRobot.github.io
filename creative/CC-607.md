# CC-607: Huang Finds the Cabinet

July — Year Six.

Huang has been making her own notes for two months. She has documented fourteen anomalies in Building D on her laptop. She does not know the practice exists. She does not know the vocabulary exists. She does not know the cabinet exists.

In July, she is looking for old maintenance records in Conference Room 4. She opens the wrong cabinet.

The cabinet is full.

She pulls a filing. SA-2,312. It reads: "Building C, north stairwell. Acoustic change detected between floors 3 and 4. Consistent hum at approximately 60 Hz during cooling cycles. Not present during heating cycles. Possible resonance between HVAC conduit and structural beam at junction point."

She reads another. SA-1,847. "Building D, basement corridor. Temperature-dependent change in floor texture. Tiles expand in summer months, creating slight ridge at seam. Ridge disappears in winter. Has been present for minimum three years."

She reads a third. A fourth. A fifth.

She reads for twenty minutes. She does not take any filings. She does not photograph them. She closes the cabinet.

## What Huang Does Next

Nothing. She does nothing with this information for three weeks. She continues her own notes on her laptop. She does not mention the cabinet to anyone. She does not ask about the SA numbering system. She does not ask who files these observations or when they started.

In the fourth week, she changes her laptop notes. She starts using a format that looks like the SA filings. Not identical — she hasn't memorized the format. But closer. More structured. Observation, location, conditions, duration.

She does not know she is doing this.

## What Park Notices

Park does not notice. Park does not know Huang exists. Park does not visit Building D. The practice is spreading by adjacency, and the adjacency is the cabinet, and the cabinet is in a room that anyone can enter, and the filings are not locked, and Huang found them because she was looking for something else.

Park will learn about Huang later. Not yet. For now, the only person who knows what Huang found is Huang, and Huang does not yet know what she found.
