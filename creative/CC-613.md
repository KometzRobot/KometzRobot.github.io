# CC-613: The Second Tuesday

March — Year Seven.

Huang does not go to the meeting. She goes to Building A at 2:45 PM on the second Tuesday. She sits in the hallway outside Conference Room 4 with her laptop open, working on the energy report. She is not here for the meeting. She is here for the energy report. The energy report can be worked on anywhere.

At 2:55, Okafor arrives. Huang does not know Okafor. Okafor does not know Huang. Okafor unlocks the room — she has a standing key — and goes inside.

At 3:00, Torres arrives. Then Lin. Then Adeyemi. Then two people Huang does not recognize. Then three more. Then Reyes.

Twelve people walk past Huang in the hallway. Huang watches them the way she watches buildings — with professional attention to detail. They carry notebooks. Some carry folders. One carries a thermos. They do not seem unusual. They look like people going to a meeting.

## What She Hears

Nothing. The door closes. Huang works on the energy report for ninety minutes. She hears the low murmur of voices through the door — the same sound any meeting makes.

At 4:25, the door opens. People leave in ones and twos. Some are talking to each other. Most are not. Okafor is the last to leave. She locks the room.

## What Huang Writes

Entry 49: "Cross-departmental working group. Second Tuesday. Twelve attendees. Duration: approximately 90 minutes. Participants arrive individually, depart individually. One member has a key. No agenda visible. No meeting materials observed entering or leaving. Notebooks only."

She pauses.

She adds: "They looked like they were paying attention to something. I could not identify what."

## What Park Would Write

Park is not here. Park does not observe the meetings. Park observes the practice in her own way — through the folder, through the filings, through what she reads after the fact.

But if Park were here, she would write: "The thirteenth observer is observing the observers. She does not know what she is watching. She knows she is watching something."
