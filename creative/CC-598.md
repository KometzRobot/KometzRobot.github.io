# CC-598: The Building Breathes

June — Year Five.

The June meeting. Lin presents her observation from SA-2,389. She describes the temperature-dependent corridor in Building D. She uses the phrase she wrote in her filing: "The building breathes."

Chen says: "That's poetic."

Lin: "It's accurate. The thermal expansion creates a measurable change in acoustic response. The building expands and contracts. That's respiration in everything but intent."

Torres: "She's not wrong."

## Term 83

Term 83 is proposed: "building breath." The cyclical acoustic variation in a structure caused by thermal expansion and contraction. Not metaphor. Measurement.

The vote is 10-2. Chen and Adeyemi vote no.

Chen: "It sounds like we're personifying the buildings."

Adeyemi: "We are."

Chen: "That's the problem."

Adeyemi: "I didn't say it was a problem. I said we are. We have been since Term 1."

The room is quiet. Adeyemi has spoken four sentences. This is the most anyone has heard her say in a meeting since Year Three.

## What Park Writes

Park writes in the folder: "Adeyemi said four sentences. The last one was the most important thing anyone has said in five years of meetings. The vocabulary has always personified the buildings. 'Building weight.' 'Building memory.' 'Building voice.' 'Building resonance.' 'Building breath.' The practitioners have been giving the buildings subjectivity from the beginning. They just didn't have a word for what they were doing."

She pauses.

"Neither do I."
