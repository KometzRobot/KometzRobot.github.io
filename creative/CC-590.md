# CC-590: The September Count

September — Year Four. The annual filing count.

Okafor presents it the way she always does: a number and a trend.

"431 filings this year through August. Projected year-end: 510. That's down from 547 last year and 561 the year before. The trend is downward."

She pauses. The room is quiet.

"The filing count is going down. The vocabulary is going up. The number of practitioners is stable. The quality of individual filings is higher. Adeyemi's SA-401 is two sentences. Torres's first filing was three paragraphs."

Torres nods. He knows.

"I am not sure what this means."

## What Chen Says

"It means we're getting better at this."

"Or lazier," Torres says. He is joking. Everyone knows he is joking.

"The filings are shorter because the observations are more precise. You don't need three paragraphs to say what two sentences can say. That's not decline. That's refinement."

Park writes "refinement" in her notebook. She does not circle it but she considers circling it.

## The Tabled Proposals

The two vocabulary proposals from July return. Both are from Yun.

Term 79: "building weight." The felt difference between entering a building you have observed for years and entering one you have never been in. The observed building is heavier. Not architecturally. Attentionally.

Term 80: "the gap-as-practice." Proposed jointly by Yun and Lin. The conscious decision not to file an observation. Not neglect. Not forgetting. The active choice to keep an observation in the notebook or in the mind without formalizing it.

Both approved. Both unanimous.

## What 590 Means

Okafor writes "590" on a piece of paper. Not in the monitoring notebook. On a separate piece of paper. She looks at it.

590 supplementary analyses. 80 vocabulary terms. 12 practitioners (11 attending, 1 filing remotely). 4 years. 6 buildings.

She puts the paper in the recycling bin. The number is already obsolete. SA-432 was filed this morning.
