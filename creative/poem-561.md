# Poem 561: Building Memory

Not the observations
but the residue they leave.

The way the building feels different
after you've been noticing it
for a year.

Clean ventilation.
Quiet elevator.
Building F was designed
by people who read the filings
from the other buildings,
although no one
has confirmed this.

Torres: "That's not a filing term.
That's a personal term."

Chen: "Is there a difference?"

Six seconds of quiet.
Not four.
Not eight.
Six is new.

Six is a genuine question
that no one has
an answer for.

Park writes "6 seconds"
in her notebook.
Does not write the cause.
The cause is the question.
The question is the gap
between what they file
and what they know.

Reyes: "Term 51 was never written down.
We're already past the boundary."

The vote is 7-4.

Okafor's entries
are getting shorter.
The first was a paragraph.
Now: three words
and a number.

The entries are not shrinking.
The practice
is getting more precise.
