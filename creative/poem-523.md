# Poem 523: What the Folder Holds

She does not put either filing
in the blue folder.
Both belong in the binders.

What she puts in the folder
is a sheet of paper
in her own handwriting:

"SA-891 and SA-588.
Same elevator. Same hesitation.
The comparison
is the observation."

Item six.

1. Before.
2. Outside.
3. Visual.
4. In use.
5. After.
6. Between.

The folder's principle
is not "unclassified."

It is the space
between what the system
can describe.

Each item is a gap.

Before: between behavior
and vocabulary.
Outside: between observation
and format.
Visual: between seeing
and writing.
In use: between thinking
and archiving.
After: between the system
and itself.
Between: between two ways
of describing
the same thing.

The archive holds observations.
The blue folder holds the gaps.
The practice is both.

"Is the folder growing
because the practice
is deepening,
or because I am noticing
more gaps?"

No answer.
She closes the folder.
