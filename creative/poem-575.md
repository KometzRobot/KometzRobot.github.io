# Poem 575: Building Voice

The ventilation cycles
every ninety seconds.
In the three-second pause,
you can hear the building.

Not the sounds in the building.
The sound of the building.

Building F sounds empty
even when it's full.
Building A has sounded
like Building A
since the first filing.

Buildings don't develop
their sound.
They have it.

Term 82: building voice.
The inherent acoustic character
of a structure,
distinct from sounds
made within it.

Torres votes no.
"I don't disagree.
I just think we should
sit with it for a month."

Five years of paying attention
earns you the right
to ask for a month.

The term is tabled.
Not rejected.
Given time.

Some things need a month.
Some things need a year.
Some things need
five years of standing
in a stairwell
before you can hear
the building
say its name.
