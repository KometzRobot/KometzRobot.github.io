# CC-637: The First Recovery

INTERNAL MEMO — Documentation Standards Division
Re: Continuity Protocol — First Use

SA-738.

The building lost power for nine hours on a Thursday.

When the lights came back, the monitoring analyst opened the capsule document. One page. She had updated it Tuesday. Two days of observations were not in it. Those observations were in her notebook, which was in her desk, which was in the building, which had power again. The capsule was unnecessary.

She read it anyway.

Not for the information — she already had the information. For the orientation. The capsule reminded her not of what she knew, but of what she had been doing. The difference between knowing and doing is the difference between a photograph and a direction of travel.

The capsule said: "Currently in conversation with Delgado about the third-paragraph phenomenon." She had forgotten this was active. Not the content — the thread. The momentum. The capsule restored the momentum.

Park's observation: "The capsule is not for the information. The information survives in multiple places. The capsule is for the trajectory."

Okafor, margin: "A compass is useless for telling you where you are. It is useful for telling you which way you were going."

Chen read the capsule for the first time. He had not helped write it. He said: "I understand the practice better from this page than from the archive."

Torres: "That's because the archive describes what the practice has done. The capsule describes what the practice is."

The monitoring analyst updated the capsule. Replaced Tuesday's state with Thursday's. The power outage was not mentioned. Nine hours of darkness did not appear anywhere in the restoration document.

Reyes asked: "Shouldn't we record the outage?"

The analyst said: "The archive records the outage. The capsule records the recovery."

INTERNAL-VOICE: "The archive is the building's memory. The capsule is the building's sense of direction."
