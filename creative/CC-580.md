# CC-580: November

November. The vocabulary document is seventy-four terms long.

Chen prints a copy and reads it on the bus. He has been doing this since the document was thirty-one terms. He does not know why he reads it on the bus specifically. The bus takes twenty-two minutes. The document takes twenty-two minutes. This is probably coincidence.

Term 14: filing instinct. The impulse to formalize an observation before understanding why. Proposed by Torres, May.

Term 38: ambient inventory. The background awareness of a building's systems that exists before any specific observation. Proposed by Davis, July.

Term 51: the Wednesday thing. Adeyemi's term. She never wrote it down. Someone else did. She has not corrected the definition.

Term 74: return visit. Reyes, October.

Chen notices that the terms follow a pattern he did not notice until the bus ride. Early terms name actions — filing, noticing, checking. Middle terms name states — ambient inventory, temporal margin, functional echo. Late terms name relationships — between the observer and what is observed, between the observation and the building, between one practitioner's vocabulary and another's.

He does not write this down. He folds the document and puts it in his coat pocket. The document in his pocket is three versions old.

At the November meeting, Park presents something she calls the notebook project. She does not mention her own notebook. She has interviewed six practitioners about their personal documentation — the notes they keep that are not filed, not shared, not part of any system. Everyone keeps something. Chen has a column of numbers in the margin of his work calendar. Davis has short sentences on index cards. Torres photographs things with her phone and never looks at the photos.

"What are the photos of?" Okafor asks.

"Doors, mostly," Torres says.

No one asks why. The question has been answered by not needing to ask.

Park's finding: the personal documentation is not a draft of the filed observation. It is a different thing. The filed observation is for the vocabulary. The personal documentation is for the person.

Okafor writes this in the meeting notes: "Filed observations serve the system. Personal documentation serves the practitioner. Both describe the same building."

After the meeting, Reyes stays behind again. He has been the last to leave since August.

"Do you think we should publish the vocabulary document?"

"Who would read it?"

Reyes considers this. "Other buildings."

"What would they do with it?"

"Start their own."

Okafor does not say yes. She does not say no. She says: "Term 51 was never written down by the person who named it."

Reyes understands. The vocabulary is not the document. The document is where the vocabulary rests between uses.
