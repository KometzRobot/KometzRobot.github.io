# CC-602: The Question

February — Year Six.

The question spreads.

In the first week of February, Chen is asked by a colleague in Engineering: "Do you have a recurring meeting on Tuesdays? I keep seeing you head toward the east wing at 2:00."

Chen says: "It's a standards review."

In the second week, Lin is asked by her supervisor during a one-on-one: "I noticed a recurring calendar block — second Tuesday, 2:00 to 3:00. What is that?"

Lin says: "It's an observation group. We document anomalies in building systems."

In the third week, Yun is asked by the new facilities coordinator: "Is there a regular booking for Conference Room 4? I'm trying to consolidate the scheduling system."

Yun says: "Yes. Second Tuesday. It's been booked for six years."

The facilities coordinator says: "Six years? There's no project code associated with it."

Yun says: "There's no project."

The facilities coordinator writes this down. She does not ask a follow-up question. She does not know what to do with information that has no project code.

## Three Answers

Torres called it "a working group." Chen called it "a standards review." Lin called it "an observation group." Yun said there was no project.

All four answers are accurate. None of them describe the same thing. The practice has no single description because it is not a single thing. It is twelve people, 83 terms, 2,847 filings, and a cabinet.

## What Park Writes

Park writes: "Four questions in six weeks. More questions than in the previous five years combined. The practice has not changed. The environment has changed. New facilities coordinator consolidating scheduling. New colleague in Engineering who notices patterns. A supervisor who reads calendar blocks."

She writes: "The practice was invisible because the institution was not paying attention. The institution is not paying attention now, either. Individual people are paying attention. There is a difference."

She adds: "None of the practitioners coordinated their answers. None of them lied. Each described the part of the practice they understand best. Torres described the activity. Chen described the function. Lin described the method. Yun described the administrative reality."

She adds one more line: "If anyone asked me, I would not know what to say. I have been observing for almost two years and I still do not have a single sentence."
