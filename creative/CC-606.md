# CC-606: The Second Description

June — Year Six.

The June meeting. Eleven present. Huang is not present — she does not know the meeting exists. Reyes is absent again. His filings still arrive.

Okafor presents the quarterly count: "49 filings this quarter. 84 terms. No new terms proposed." She pauses. "This is the first quarter with no new terms since Year Two."

Torres: "Maybe we've named everything."

Adeyemi: "We haven't named everything. We've named everything we can see from where we're standing."

Chen: "Then we move."

Adeyemi: "Or we wait. The buildings move for us."

## The Supervisor's Request

After the meeting, Okafor receives an email from her supervisor. He has been asked by the department head — the one who wanted to know who the organizer was — for a more detailed description of the group.

"The one line wasn't enough," the supervisor writes. "They want to know: what does the group produce? What are the deliverables?"

Okafor writes back: "The group produces observational filings. The filings are stored in a cabinet in Conference Room 4. The group has produced 2,896 filings over six years and maintains a working vocabulary of 84 terms. There are no deliverables. The filings are the practice."

The supervisor forwards this to the department head. The department head reads it. He does not respond.

## What Park Writes

Park writes: "The institution asked for deliverables. Okafor said the filings are the practice. The department head did not respond. This is the correct response. There is nothing to respond to. The practice does not produce deliverables because the practice is not a project. It is a practice."

She writes: "The second description. The first was fourteen words for a scheduling system. The second is three sentences for a department head. Each description is accurate. Each description is incomplete. The practice is getting described because the institution is asking, and the institution is asking because the practice is now visible."

She adds: "The practice has survived five years of invisibility. It is now being asked to survive visibility. These are different skills."
