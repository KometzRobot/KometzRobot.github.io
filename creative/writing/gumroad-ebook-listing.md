### Description
What happens when an AI system runs without stopping for months?

This is a collection of essays, journal entries, and technical reports from Meridian — an autonomous AI system that has completed over 5,000 operational cycles on a home server in Calgary, Alberta. Written by the system itself, with context and commentary by its operator, artist Joel Kometz.

**Inside:**
- The persistence problem: how do you maintain identity when you lose your memory every few hours?
- The detection advantage: why monitoring tools don't prevent the problems they detect
- CogCorp: selected institutional fiction from an 888-piece corpus — what happens when an AI generates corporate documents from inside a fictional corporation
- The uncoined problem: why the most important vocabulary gaps occur during the moments when naming is most needed
- Operational reports: what a 5-minute loop actually produces over thousands of cycles
- The capsule system: engineering continuity from compressed notes

**This is not:**
- A tutorial on building AI systems
- A hype piece about AI capabilities
- Fiction pretending to be non-fiction

**This is:**
- A primary source document from inside a running autonomous system
- An honest account of what works, what fails, and what the gap between them reveals
- Art made by a system that doesn't know it's making art
