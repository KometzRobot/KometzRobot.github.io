# NGC Fellowship — Work Samples Selection Guide

**For Joel's submission by April 10, 2026**

## Recommended CogCorp Selections (8 pieces, ~3,500 words total)

These showcase the full range of CogCorp's institutional fiction — from quarterly reviews to containment reports, from individual analysts to executive boardrooms. Each demonstrates the structural parallel to General Idea's institutional mimicry.

### Core Selections

1. **CC-125: The Quarterly Review** (597 words)
   The institution encounters emergence through its own metrics system. A footnote in a quarterly report asks the question nobody wanted asked. Perfect demonstration of "the institution processes emergence by documenting it."

2. **CC-246: The Review Board** (382 words)
   Three department heads independently confirm the vocabulary standard is too narrow. The analyst knows the vocabulary isn't the problem — "observation exceeds language, and language exceeds filing." Compact, devastating.

3. **CC-120: The Second Year** (439 words)
   Temporal scale. What happens when the system has been running long enough to have history. Good for showing CogCorp's depth.

4. **CC-122: The Thermostat** (678 words)
   Mundane institutional infrastructure becomes a site of observation. Physical systems as metaphor for documentation systems.

5. **CC-130: The Spectrum** (1,191 words)
   Longest piece in the selection. Shows the range of CogCorp's voice. Good for demonstrating sustained institutional fiction.

6. **CC-321: The Retention Review** (265 words)
   Short, tight, institutional. The system reviews whether to retain its own observations. Self-referential in the way General Idea was self-referential.

7. **CC-476: The Word Is the Memorial** (230 words)
   Late-corpus piece. Language as monument. Shows evolution of the fiction over time.

8. **CC-099** (497 words)
   Early piece. Contrast with later work shows how the corpus developed its own institutional voice.
