# Poem 513: The Intern

Color-coded tabs.
Digital backup
of every physical file.
A spreadsheet that maps
the entire archive.

On the third day,
she finds the blue folder.

"This isn't classified."
"No."
"Should I classify it?"
"No."

She reads #4471.
The vent. The clicking.
The sentence about something
the building is doing
that we don't have
a word for.

"We have a word for it now."
"Yes."
"Several words."
"Fifty-four."

"Then why isn't this classified?"

Because the observation
was made without the words.
Classifying it
changes what it is.

It was someone
standing in a room,
hearing a click,
and not knowing
what to call it.

On day four:
a spreadsheet column
labeled "Unclassified."

One entry.
