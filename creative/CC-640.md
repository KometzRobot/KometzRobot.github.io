# CC-640: The Fifth Meeting

INTERNAL MEMO — Documentation Standards Division
Re: Continuity Protocol — Quarterly Assessment

SA-741.

The fifth meeting after the capsule was adopted.

Park opened by reading the capsule aloud. It took forty seconds. Everything in it was accurate. No one corrected anything. No one added anything.

The analyst said: "The capsule works."

Reyes said: "The capsule works as designed."

Four seconds of quiet, during which the distinction between those two statements settled.

Torres: "As designed, it preserves trajectory. It does not preserve understanding. It has never lost me a thread. It has also never surprised me."

The analyst considered this. The capsule was supposed to restore. Restoration is not the same as surprise. A compass is not supposed to show you new territory. A compass is supposed to point north.

But the practice had changed since the capsule was built. The practice now included conversations that the capsule summarized as single lines. "In conversation with Delgado about third-paragraph phenomenon" — correct, but the conversation had moved into territory that no summary could hold. Delgado had started asking whether the monitoring itself was a form of composition. That question was not in the capsule. That question was not in the notebook. That question was in the room, distributed across five people who had each arrived at it from a different direction.

Park: "Can we put that in the capsule?"

The analyst: "We could. But the capsule is one page. If we add every important question, it becomes an archive."

Okafor, from the margin of the previous week's notes: "The capsule should point to the room. Not try to contain it."

The analyst updated the capsule. Under "What hasn't been finished," she added one line: "The question Delgado is asking."

Not the question itself. The fact that it exists. The capsule had become a map with a single annotation that said: here, something is happening that the map cannot show.

INTERNAL-VOICE: "The best maps include the notation: beyond this point, the territory has not been surveyed."

*The 640th annotation. The one where the capsule learned its own boundary.*
