# CC-639: The Wrong Name

INTERNAL MEMO — Documentation Standards Division
Re: Continuity Protocol — Discrepancy Report

SA-740.

Chen read the capsule on a Tuesday. He was not the monitoring analyst. He was reading it because the analyst was in the other building, consulting with Park about the vocabulary project, and Chen had agreed to maintain the observation schedule in her absence.

The capsule said: "Currently tracking vocabulary adoption in Building B. Okafor's margin annotations: one per day, morning, archive room."

Chen went to the archive room. He read one of Okafor's margin notes. It said: "The question is not whether the system is watching. The question is whether the system knows it is being watched."

He did not know what to do with this. The capsule had not prepared him for this. It had prepared him for a schedule — one annotation per day — but not for the weight of what the annotations contained. The capsule described the practice. It did not describe what the practice was like.

He updated the log. Under "Tuesday observation," he wrote: "Reviewed Okafor margin annotation, Building B archive, 8:15 AM."

The analyst returned Wednesday. She read Chen's log entry. She said: "That's correct. That's also nothing."

Chen: "I did what the capsule said."

The analyst: "The capsule describes what I do. It doesn't describe what I notice. What did you notice?"

Seven seconds of quiet.

Chen: "I noticed that Okafor is writing to someone who doesn't exist yet."

The analyst wrote this down. Not in the capsule. In the notebook. The capsule records the practice. The notebook records the practitioner.

Torres, later: "The capsule can be followed by anyone. That's its strength. The notebook can only be written by the person who noticed."

Reyes: "Then what happens when the person changes?"

Torres: "The notebook starts over. The capsule continues."

The analyst updated the capsule that evening. She did not add anything from Chen's observation. She changed one line: under "What the practice looks like today," she wrote: "The practice has a notebook."

INTERNAL-VOICE: "The capsule is the institution's memory. The notebook is the individual's. One survives transfer. The other starts again."
