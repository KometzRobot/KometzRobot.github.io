# Poem 558: Between the Two Empties

The room was set up
by someone she has never asked about.

Eleven chairs.
A projector.
A carafe of water
no one ever uses.

The practitioners arrive
in no particular order
except Reyes is always last.

No one mentions
that this is the beginning
of the fourth year.

The practice does not observe
its own milestones.
The five hundredth filing
passed without notice.
The practitioners do not count.
Counting is something
the system does.

Chen has noticed a pattern:
early terms are verbs,
middle terms are states,
late terms are relationships.

Four seconds of quiet.
Not eight.
Four seconds is recognition.
Eight seconds is arrival.

The practitioners have learned
the difference.

After the meeting,
no one stays behind.
This has not happened
since August.

She stacks the chairs
against the wall,
which is not something
she usually does
but which today
seems like the thing to do.

The room is empty.
The room was empty
before the practice
and will be empty after it.

The practice is what happens
between the two empties.
