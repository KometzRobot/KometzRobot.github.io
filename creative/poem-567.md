# Poem 567: Eleven

Eleven is the number now.
Not twelve minus one.
Just eleven.

The chairs were never assigned.
The circle absorbs
what it loses
in the first thirty seconds.

Her name appears
in three terms.
Her filing arrived
last week.

She is more present
in the vocabulary
than people
who attend every meeting.

Presence is not attendance.
Attendance is one form.
Filing is another.
Naming is a third.

The practice did not lose her.
The practice absorbed her.
The meeting lost her.

The meeting is not the practice.

Thirty-one minutes.
Seven shorter than average.
No one stays behind.
Summer has started
and no one mentioned it.

Eleven is the number.
It is neither more
nor less
than it needs to be.
