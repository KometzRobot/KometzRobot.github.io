# Poem 516: The Last Day

Forty-seven pages.
Every filing.
Every building.
Every term.

"This is the archive
in a format you can search."

"I can search the archive."

"You can search it
because you remember.
This is searchable
for someone who doesn't."

The Unclassified column:
still four entries.
She went through every drawer.
Only four things don't fit.

"That's not true.
There are observations
that never get filed.
Chen tells me things
at the coffee machine
that don't appear
in his filings.
Davis describes patterns
she can't articulate.
The filings are the part
of the practice
that survives the format.
They are not the practice."

"So the real Unclassified column
is everything
that's not in the archive."

"Yes."

"And there's no way to count it."

"No.
That's what makes it
unclassified."

Pencil, in the margin:
"This document describes
the visible practice.
The invisible practice
is larger."

"Can I keep this?"

"I made you two copies."
