# CC-571: Month Three

January. Park has been filing for three months. His filings remain correct. His categories are precise. He has never missed a scheduled inspection.

In December he asked Okafor: "Do the buildings feel different to you in winter?"

She said: "File what you observe."

He filed: "Building D, ambient temperature variance between floors 1 and 4 exceeds seasonal baseline by 1.3 degrees. Category: Environmental."

That is not what he was asking.

In January he stops filing for a moment in Building E. Not in a corridor. In the stairwell between floors 2 and 3. He stands still. He does not write anything.

He is listening to the building. He does not know he is listening. He would describe it as waiting for the elevator if you asked. But the elevator is on the other side of the building.

After nineteen seconds he continues up the stairs. He does not file an observation. He does not write a Question. He does not mention it to Okafor.

At the end of January he emails Okafor: "I've been thinking about Section 4. I think I understand it now."

She writes back: "What does it say?"

"You cannot be trained to notice. You can only be given enough time in enough buildings."

"What does that mean to you?"

He does not respond for three days. Then: "I think it means the manual isn't finished."

Okafor reads this twice. She saves the email in a folder she has never used before. She does not label the folder.
