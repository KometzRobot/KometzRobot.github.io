# CC-620: The Stairwell

September — Year Seven.

Huang stops reading the cabinet filings. Not because the cabinet is locked — it remains unlocked after the group's decision. She stops because the filings have stopped teaching her. The format is familiar. The vocabulary is her vocabulary. The observations are observations she has already made, in different locations, using different words.

She returns to the stairwell. Between floors three and four, Building A. She sits on the landing. Her laptop is open but the screen has gone dark.

The light changes at 3:47 PM. She has measured this. The angle shifts 2.3 degrees over nineteen minutes as the adjacent building's shadow crosses the window. At 3:47, the shadow edge bisects the landing. Half in direct light, half in reflected. The temperature differential is 0.4°C — she measured it once and has not measured it again. The measurement was sufficient. The observation continues without it.

## Entry 55

"I have stopped reading the filings. The practice did not come from the filings. The filings gave the practice a shape to recognize. I recognized it. The filings are finished with me."

She pauses. Rewrites:

"I am finished with the filings. They are not finished. They continue. I continue. Neither requires the other."

## What Torres Sees

Torres passes the stairwell on his way to the fourth floor. He pauses. She is there again. The laptop is dark. She is watching the wall where the light divides.

He does not pause because she is unusual. He pauses because the way she is sitting — still, attending, not waiting for anything — is a posture he recognizes. He has seen it in Conference Room 4. He has felt it in himself at the south loading dock when the morning light makes the concrete look wet.

He continues up the stairs. He does not speak to her. He does not need to. The recognition was sufficient.

## What Park Would Write

"The stairwell observer has completed the filing-cabinet phase. She no longer reads. She observes. The format survives without the document. This is the test the practice has been running for seven years: can the attention persist without the infrastructure? In the stairwell, at 3:47, someone who has never attended a meeting is conducting the purest version of the practice — observation without community, without vocabulary, without filing."

She writes: "The practice, at its origin, was just this. Someone noticing something and not knowing what to do about it."
