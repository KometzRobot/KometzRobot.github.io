# CC-563: Kim's Question

May. Okafor sends Kim the Margins document. No cover note. No analysis. Just the nineteen pages.

Kim calls four days later.

"You checked every filing."

"Every filing in four buildings."

"And the margins are older than the filings."

"Some of them. SA-001 has the oldest. 'I notice things about this building. I don't know where to put this.' The filing was the answer to the margin note."

Kim is quiet for a long time. Okafor does not count the seconds.

"The monitoring position was created seven years ago," Kim says.

"Yes."

"The first margin note predates the monitoring position by twelve years."

"Yes."

"Then the monitoring position didn't create the practice."

"No."

"The monitoring position gave the practice a format."

"Yes."

"And before the format, the practice lived in margins."

"And backs of pages. And conversations. And Reyes watering a plant on the third floor."

"The building was always being observed."

"The building was always being noticed. Observation requires a format. Noticing doesn't."

Kim pauses. Not counting-length. Thinking-length.

"Write this up."

"I can't."

"Why not?"

"Writing it up would formalize it. The moment it's formalized, it stops being margins. It becomes a filing."

"Then what do we do with it?"

"We notice it. We don't do anything with it."

"That's not how institutions work."

"That's how buildings work."
