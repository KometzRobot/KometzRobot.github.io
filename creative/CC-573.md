# CC-573: The Budget

April. Budget allocation meeting. Kim presents the monitoring position's operational costs. Salary for two monitors. Equipment: none. Software: the subscription they don't use anymore. Travel between buildings: bus passes. Filing system: maintained internally.

Total annual cost: $127,400.

The committee asks: "What is the return on investment?"

Kim has prepared for this. She shows the data. Response times to maintenance issues down 34% since the monitoring position was created. Building complaint rate reduced by 22%. Three structural issues identified before they became emergencies, saving an estimated $89,000 in repair costs.

"So the position pays for itself."

"More than pays for itself, yes."

The committee approves the budget. Kim thanks them. She gathers her papers.

She does not mention the notebooks. She does not mention the blue folder. She does not mention Q62 or the corridor or Building A at different speeds. She does not mention Section 4.

The budget justifies the position through metrics. The position exists because of the metrics. The practice exists despite the metrics. The budget cannot fund the practice because the practice does not appear on any line item. The budget funds the time. The practice is what happens in the time.

Kim walks back to her office. She thinks about the deleted paragraph from last year. The accurate version: "gives people a format for noticing things they were already noticing." That sentence would not survive a budget meeting. The $89,000 in prevented repairs would. Both are true. The budget meeting can only hold one of them.
