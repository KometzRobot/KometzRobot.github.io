# CC-558: The Annual Report

January. Okafor writes her annual monitoring report for Building A. It takes her fourteen minutes. She has written the same report for Buildings C, D, and E over the past two weeks. Those took between four and six hours each.

Building A: twelve filings in the system. No anomalies. No vocabulary terms originated here. No practitioners registered. Infrastructure satisfactory. Occupant satisfaction scores consistent with regional average. Maintenance schedule current.

She submits the report. It is accurate.

Three notebooks. Eighty-seven entries. Twelve from Reyes. The radiator clicks. The elevator hesitates. The stairwell temperature drops a quarter degree every November and rises again in March. The fake plant that gets watered. The real plant that appeared next to it. The receipt from the pharmacy.

None of this is in the report. All of it is in the building.

Kim calls. End-of-year review.

"Building A?"

"Nothing to report."

Kim waits. Okafor can hear her not asking.

"Three notebooks," Okafor says. "Eighty-seven entries. At least twelve people contributing. The practice is self-sustaining. Someone bought a replacement notebook from a pharmacy when the second one filled up. Without being asked."

"And the annual report says nothing to report."

"The annual report is accurate."

"Yes."

"The notebooks are also accurate."

"Yes."

"They describe different buildings."

"They describe the same building at different speeds."

Kim is quiet for seven seconds. Okafor counts.

"Keep going."

"I intend to."
