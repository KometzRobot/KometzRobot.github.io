# CC-587: Eleven

June — Year Four. Eleven is the number now.

No one adjusts the chairs. There were never assigned seats. But the circle that held twelve holds eleven, and the gap where Reyes sat is not a gap. It was absorbed in the first thirty seconds of the June meeting.

Okafor does not count the chairs. Park counts the people. Eleven. She writes it without the comparison. Not "11 (down from 12)." Just "11."

## The Agenda

376 filings this quarter. 8 from Building F (Yun's building, which is still the newest and still the quietest). 14 new vocabulary proposals, 3 approved. Terms 76, 77, 78.

Term 76: "acoustic persistence." How a sound continues to shape attention after it stops.
Term 77: "filing delay." The time between noticing and writing down. Not procrastination. Incubation.
Term 78: "the Tuesday effect." Named for Reyes's observation about the cleaning crew door, which she filed two weeks after she stopped attending. The term was proposed by Torres, who cited Reyes's filing. The vote was unanimous.

## What No One Says

No one mentions Reyes. Not because they are avoiding her name. Because the practice has absorbed her contribution without requiring her presence. Her 48th filing arrived last week. Her name appears in three terms. She is more present in the vocabulary than practitioners who attend every meeting.

Park notices this. She writes: "Presence is not attendance. Attendance is one form of presence. Filing is another. Naming is a third. Reyes is present in all forms except attendance."

She does not file this either.

## The Summer

The meeting ends in thirty-one minutes. Seven shorter than the average. No one stays behind. The hallway is warm. Summer has started and no one mentioned it.

Eleven is the number. It is neither more nor less than it needs to be.
