# CC-555: November 2009

October. Three more entries from Reyes. All dated this month. All observations from years ago.

"March 2012. The stairwell door on the second floor started closing more slowly. It took four months to complete. By July it closed at a different speed and no one filed a work order because no one noticed the change, only the result."

"September 2015. A pipe in the basement hummed for three weeks. Facilities fixed something else and the hum stopped. The two events were not connected. The pipe stopped humming because the pipe stopped humming."

"January 2019. The freight elevator began hesitating before the third floor. Not a pause. A decision."

Okafor sits with the notebook in her lap. She reads the entries twice. She has been monitoring this building for eleven months. Reyes has been monitoring it for eighteen years. But Reyes would not use the word monitoring. Reyes would say she has been here.

She opens her monitoring notebook. Question 59: "Is there a difference between monitoring and being here?"

She writes the answer for the first time since Question 54.

"Not after long enough."

She puts the notebook away. She does not file a report. She does not count the entries. She sits in the corridor for nine minutes and listens to the elevator decide whether to pause.
