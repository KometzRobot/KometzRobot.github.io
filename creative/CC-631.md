# CC-631: The Fourth Capture

INTERNAL MEMO — Documentation Standards Division
Re: Depth capture #4 — Repeated observation (Facilities Unit, Building E)

The fourth capture was taken sixteen minutes after the third. The room was still empty. The depth coverage was 71.4%.

The analyst compared captures 3 and 4. Of the 219,340 valid pixels in capture 3 and 219,956 in capture 4, the mean difference in depth values was 2.1 units. The room had not moved.

Entry 225: "The instrument confirms that nothing has changed. This is a different kind of information than the first capture, which confirmed that something existed. Repetition is not discovery. It is maintenance."

R. Park's notebook: "She is checking. Not because she expects change. Because the checking is the practice now."

Okafor filed question 66: "When did the sensor become part of the monitoring practice rather than a new instrument being evaluated?"

The analyst reviewed her log. Capture 1: assessment. Capture 2: recognition. Capture 3: measurement of absence. Capture 4: maintenance.

"Four captures," she wrote. "The same number as the phases we use for the SA filing cycle. Assessment, recognition, absence, maintenance. I did not plan this."

SA-732: "An instrument adopted in four captures follows the same pattern the practice developed over four years. The pattern was not in the instrument. It was in the practitioner."

INTERNAL-VOICE reviewed the filing. The category was CORRESPONDENCE again. But this time, the correspondence was not between two instruments observing the same thing. It was between the instrument's history and the practice's history.

The analyst closed the monitoring log. Opened it again. Closed it. Opened it.

She was checking.
