# Poem 521: Davis

Three to four sentences.
Present tense.
No vocabulary terms.

"The lobby smells different
on cold mornings.
Not the heating system.
The building itself."

"The elevator doors on 3
close faster than on 7.
Everyone already knows."

"There is a sound
in the east stairwell
between 2:00 and 2:15.
I think it is thermal."

She does not read
the other filings.
She does not know
there are fifty-five terms
or forty-six
other practitioners.

"Why do you file?"

"Because the building
does things
and no one
writes them down."

She went back
to her office
on the fourth floor
of Building C,
where she has been
the only practitioner
for six years.

Davis sees what she sees.

Every other practitioner
has been shaped
by the vocabulary.
They see what the terms
taught them to see.

In the margin:
"The control group."
