# Poem 512: Classification

Four maintenance requests
on the desk.

The vocabulary fits.
"Acoustic expectation."
"Implicit navigation."
"Temporal signature."

The terms apply cleanly
to observations made
nine years before
the terms existed.

But Reeves wrote:
"I cannot identify the source."

That uncertainty
is the observation.

If Okafor classifies it,
the not-knowing disappears.
The word makes it legible.
Legibility erases
the lived quality.

"The building waking up" —
the night guard's phrase
for thermal expansion.
"Temporal signature" is accurate.
But "waking up" contains
something the vocabulary
was designed to exclude:
the sense of a living thing.

Question 48:
Does the vocabulary validate
the observation,
or distort it?

Question 49:
What did the pre-vocabulary
observations contain
that precision
was built to remove?

Okafor does not classify.
She puts the folder
on the shelf
next to the SA binders.
Not inside them.

Between the archive
and the practice.
It belongs to before.
