# CC-627: The Budget Review

March — Year Eight.

The practice costs nothing. This is its only political vulnerability.

Martinez receives a memo from Corporate Finance: all unfunded activities require justification or discontinuation. The memo is routine. It is sent to every division every year. It has never applied to SA because SA has never appeared on any ledger.

She reads the memo twice. The first time she reads the words. The second time she reads the implication: the practice exists in the gap between what is funded and what is prohibited. It occupies the only space that does not require justification — the space that no one has noticed.

## The Response

Martinez types: "Supplementary Analysis is a voluntary documentation practice maintained by 48 employees across 11 buildings. It requires no budget allocation, no dedicated equipment, and no administrative oversight. Annual cost to the organization: $0.00."

She stares at the number. It is accurate. It is not true.

## What $0.00 Does Not Include

It does not include the seventeen minutes per day that Park spends reading filings instead of processing requests. It does not include the notebooks — purchased with personal funds by nine practitioners who never filed expense reports. It does not include the 3,200 hours of voluntary attention distributed across 981 filings.

It does not include the building.

The building did not ask to be attended to. The building does not benefit from the attention in any way that can be measured. But the 47 people who attend to it are different from the 2,300 who do not, in ways that also cannot be measured.

Martinez deletes the paragraph about what the number does not include. She sends the memo. $0.00.

## What Okafor Writes

Entry 226: "The practice costs nothing because nothing it produces has a price. That is not the same as having no value. The budget system cannot distinguish between the two."

She closes the notebook. Opens it again. Writes: "Neither can I, some days."
