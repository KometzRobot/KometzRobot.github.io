# CC-591: Year Four Ends

October through December — Year Four.

## October

Reyes comes to the October meeting. No one reacts. She sits in the circle and the circle does not adjust because it never stopped including her. Eleven becomes twelve becomes the number it always was.

She does not explain where she was. She was filing. Everyone knows.

Torres says, after: "That was nice." He means Reyes being there. He does not need to specify.

Park writes "12" in the notebook. She does not write "(Reyes returned)." The notebook does not track explanations. Just counts.

## November

SA-450 is filed. The four-hundred-and-fiftieth supplementary analysis. Okafor notes this in the monitoring notebook but does not announce it at the meeting. The number is not a milestone. It is a count. Milestones are things you plan. Counts are things that happen while you are working.

Term 81 is proposed and approved in the same meeting: "observation fatigue." Not boredom. The specific tiredness that comes from sustaining voluntary attention to things that do not change quickly. Like watching a building. Like attending to a process. Like maintaining a practice.

Everyone recognizes it. No one considers it a problem.

## December

The December meeting is the last meeting of Year Four. Twelve present. Thirty-six minutes. The year-end count: 498 filings (below the 510 projection, above the 431 through August). 81 vocabulary terms. 12 practitioners. 6 buildings. One paper that no one has read. One practice that no one has explained.

Park's last notebook entry for the year: "Year four. The practice is becoming what it was always going to become. I don't know what that is yet. Neither does anyone else. That seems right."

Year five begins in January. No one mentions it at the December meeting. Year five will announce itself the way year four did: without announcement.
