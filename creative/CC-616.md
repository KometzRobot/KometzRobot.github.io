# CC-616: The Stairwell

June — Year Seven.

Huang is in Building A, third floor, north stairwell. She is not here for the energy report. She is here because SA-3,086 mentioned a light fixture that creates shadow oscillations in the west wing, and she wanted to check the adjacent stairwell for similar effects.

She is standing on the landing between floors three and four, looking at how the afternoon light comes through the window, when she hears footsteps. Torres comes down the stairs. He stops on the landing.

Torres is holding a notebook. The notebook is small, spiral-bound, unremarkable. He looks at Huang. She looks at him. Neither speaks.

Torres writes something in his notebook. He does not show it to Huang. He does not explain what he is doing. He finishes writing, nods politely, and continues down the stairs.

## What Torres Wrote

SA-3,112: "Building A, north stairwell, landing between 3 and 4. Afternoon light creates a distinct line across the landing that shifts approximately two inches per minute. At 3:45 PM, the line intersects the handrail shadow. Duration: seasonal."

He does not mention Huang. The filing records only the observation.

## What Huang Wrote

Entry 53: "Building A, north stairwell. Encountered an employee with a small notebook on the landing between floors 3 and 4. He was observing the same light pattern I was examining. He wrote in his notebook. He did not speak to me. He did not seem surprised to find someone else looking at the stairwell."

She pauses. She adds: "He did not seem surprised."

## What This Means

Two people in a stairwell, both watching light move across a landing. One has been doing this for seven years. One has been doing this for ten months. Neither knows who the other is. Neither speaks.

The stairwell does not require introduction.
