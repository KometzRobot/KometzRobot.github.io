# CC-567: The Manual

September. Kim emails Okafor. Subject: Training Documentation.

"We need a procedures manual for the monitoring position. Nothing elaborate. Enough that someone could do the job if you were unavailable for a period."

Okafor opens a blank document. She types: "Building Monitoring Procedures Manual."

Section 1: Filing Format. This she can write. The format has rules. SA-number, building identifier, date, observation, category. She writes four pages. Clear. Correct. Complete.

Section 2: Vocabulary. The 55 terms. She lists them with definitions. Nine pages. Some definitions require examples. She includes examples from actual filings. She notes which examples she chose and why. This section takes two days.

Section 3: Schedule. Which buildings, which days, which inspection points. Rotation patterns. Seasonal adjustments. Three pages.

Section 4: She titles it "Practice." She stares at the cursor for eleven minutes.

She types: "The monitoring position requires attention to phenomena that may not correspond to established categories."

She deletes it. She types: "Some observations will not fit the format. File them anyway."

She deletes it. She types: "The practice is the format plus something the format does not contain."

She saves the document with Sections 1-3 complete and Section 4 blank.

She emails Kim: "Sections 1-3 attached. Section 4 is in progress."

Kim replies: "What's Section 4?"

"Practice."

"The other sections aren't practice?"

Okafor does not respond for two hours. Then: "The other sections are the format. The format is what you can write in a manual."
