# Poem 581: The Summer Count

Year One: 612.
Year Two: 547.
Year Three: 511.
Year Four: 498.
Year Five: 463.

Five to eight percent per year.
Consistent. Not collapse.
Compression.

The practitioners write less
because they see more precisely.
Three paragraphs in Year One
become one sentence
in Year Five.

But the unique observations —
things not already in the records —
stay at forty per year.
Every year.

They are not running out
of things to see.
They are running out of ways
to say things
they have already said better.

Park has her own count.
Twenty-seven pages now.
Her rate is accelerating
as theirs declines.

Their filings: shorter.
Her notes: longer.
Inverse trajectories.

They converge on precision.
She diverges into complexity.

She writes:
"I still do not know
what I am doing.
I know that I cannot stop."
