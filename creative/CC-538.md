# CC-538: The Intern

The summer intern is named Priya. She is twenty-two. She is organized in a way that makes Okafor nervous — color-coded tabs, digital backup of every physical file, a spreadsheet that maps the entire archive by date, building, and classification.

On her third day, she finds the blue folder.

"This isn't classified," she says.

"No."

"Should I classify it?"

"No."

Priya holds the folder at arm's length, as though its lack of classification might be contagious. "It doesn't have a building designation. It doesn't have an SA number. It doesn't even have a date range — it just says 'Before.'"

"That's correct."

"Before what?"

Okafor considers the question. It is the same question she asked herself when she made the folder. The answer she settled on was: before the format existed. But saying that to a twenty-two-year-old with color-coded tabs requires explaining what the format is, what it replaced, what came before it, and why the things that came before it cannot be filed using the system that came after. She decides to try.

"Before the monitoring position. Before the vocabulary. Before there was a format for this kind of observation."

"So it's historical material."

"It's maintenance requests."

"From when?"

"2014 and 2015."

"That's ten years ago. Historical."

"The observations in them are not historical. They describe things the buildings are still doing."

Priya looks at the folder again. She opens it. She reads maintenance request #4471 — the heating vent, the clicking, the schedule, Reeves's sentence about something the building is doing that we don't have a word for.

"We have a word for it now," she says.

"Yes."

"Several words."

"Fifty-four."

"Then why isn't this classified?"

Okafor takes a breath. "Because the observation was made without the words. Classifying it with the words changes what it is. It was someone noticing something without a framework. If I give it a framework, it becomes something that fits the framework. But what it actually was — was someone standing in a room, hearing a click, and not knowing what to call it."

Priya puts the folder back on the shelf. She puts it in the wrong place — between two SA binders instead of next to them. Okafor does not correct her. The folder's position is not the point. Its existence is.

On her fourth day, Priya starts a spreadsheet column she labels "Unclassified." It has one entry.
