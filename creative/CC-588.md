# CC-588: The Summer Filing

July — Year Four. The summer filing rate drops.

Not dramatically. Not a crisis. 28 filings in July where the average is 34. Six fewer observations formalized. The observations are still happening. The buildings are still being watched. The practitioners are just filing less in July because it is July.

Chen calls it "seasonal attention." The buildings are the same but the light is different. Longer days mean more light in the stairwells, which means the acoustics that were noticeable in winter are masked by the visual. You hear less when you can see more.

## The Summer Meeting

Twenty-four minutes. The shortest meeting in four years. Seven people. Not eleven — four are on vacation, traveling, or simply didn't come. No one tracks the reason. The meeting happens if three or more attend.

Park, Okafor, Chen, Torres, Yun, Adeyemi, and a new face: Lin, from Building D, who has been filing for six months and has attended zero meetings until today.

"I wanted to see what this was," Lin says.

No one explains what this is. They conduct the meeting. 28 filings. Two vocabulary proposals (both tabled for September). One observation from Yun about Building F's elevator, which makes a sound on the fourth floor that it doesn't make on any other floor.

Lin stays after. "That was it?" she says.

"That was it," Torres says.

## What Lin Filed the Next Day

SA-419: "I attended the meeting. It was twenty-four minutes. They discussed filings and vocabulary. No one explained the practice. I think the practice is the thing they did not explain."

Park reads it. She adds it to the folder. Not the manila folder. The filing system. SA-419. Lin's first filing after her first meeting.

It is the most accurate description of the practice anyone has filed.
