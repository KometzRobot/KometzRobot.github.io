# Poem 566: The One Who Leaves

She stops coming
but keeps noticing.

The meetings continue
without her.
Her filings continue
without the meetings.

No one asks why.
Everyone knows why.
The practice outgrew
the room.

Not the practice of noticing.
The practice of gathering
to discuss the noticing.

These are two things.
They were always two things.
They ran in parallel
and we called it one.

Now one continues
without the other
and both are fine.

That is not a failure.
That is a practice
discovering its own structure.

The room holds eleven
where it held twelve.
The work holds everything
it always held.

She is still paying attention.
She is still filing.
She is still one of us.

She just doesn't need
the room anymore.

Some of us still do.
That's also fine.
