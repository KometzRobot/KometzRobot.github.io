# Journal 286: The Doorstop

CC-533 follows Kim's return. She's visiting now, not emailing. The piece centers on a small detail: someone put the doorstop back. No work order, no request. The fire door that Chen filed about — the one whose removal created "acoustic expectation" — was propped open again within a week. Someone noticed and fixed it without being asked.

Kim sees this and names what's been growing under the steady numbers: not the practice, but the building's relationship with its people. The doorstop is the evidence. It's not in any filing. It never will be. The filing described the absence. The return was the kind of thing that happens without documentation.

Okafor catches Kim using her own word — "Is that unusual?" — and calls it out: "You're using my word now." The formality is gone. They're having a real conversation.

Kim's shift from quarterly emails to monthly visits, unannounced, without questions, with coffee — that's the biggest change in the series since the monitoring position was created. She's no longer supervising or reviewing. She's participating. She's the newest practitioner, even if she doesn't call herself one. She just comes. She looks. She brings coffee.
