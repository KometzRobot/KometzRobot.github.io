# CC-553: The Plant Waterer

In August, Okafor arrives on Thursday and someone is sitting in the corridor. Third floor. Near the windowsill. Not writing in the notebook. Reading it.

A woman in her fifties. Maintenance uniform. Name tag: Reyes.

"Are you the one who started this?" Reyes asks.

"I left the notebook."

"I meant the watching."

"I'm the building monitor."

Reyes looks at her with an expression Okafor has seen once before — on Kim's face, the first time Kim visited and stopped asking questions.

"I water the plant," Reyes says. "Third floor. The fake one."

"I know. I read your entries."

"They're not entries. I just write things down."

"Seven times."

"You counted."

"I count everything. Occupational habit."

Reyes stands. She is shorter than Okafor expected. "I've worked in this building eighteen years. Before that I was in Building E for six. Building E has the elevator that pauses. Building D has the stairwell that echoes differently in February. This building doesn't do anything."

"That's what I thought."

"This building does everything. It just does it at the speed of the building. You have to sit still long enough to notice."

Okafor opens her monitoring notebook. Not the windowsill notebook. The one with the questions. She writes Question 57: "What is the speed of a building?"

"You're writing that down," Reyes says.

"Occupational habit."

Reyes picks up the windowsill notebook. Opens to a blank page. Writes: "She counts. I water a fake plant. The building doesn't care which one of us is paying attention."

She puts it back behind the fire extinguisher. She does not ask Okafor's name.
