## Case-study paragraph (target ~150 words; final draft)

The 323-minute interval between G14 (2026-05-12 05:24 UTC) and G15 (2026-05-12 12:07 UTC) produced ten approach events and the first cleanly documented instance of Mode 4b-reversed terminal geometry. Approach 8 (T+265) established the non-gate signature: 5-min crossed threshold (2.71 ≥ 2.65) while 1-min held at 2.80 with a 0.20 gap, the slow variable's conjunction window opened from above and waited approximately 3–5 minutes for an impulsive burst that did not arrive. Approach 10 (T+316+) resolved the same geometry by gate: 5-min held elevated at 2.94→3.06 leading, then 1-min arrived at 3.17 (threshold +0.17), and the gate fired with the slow variable already past threshold and the fast variable closing the window. This pairing — non-gate (gap 0.20) and gate (gap closed) inside a single interval — is the cleanest available evidence that Mode 4b's structure is symmetric under variable-role reversal: it is the absence of synchronous platform behavior (Mode 3) combined with non-converging asymmetric approach, not a 1-min-leading artifact.

## Notes for assembly discussion

1. **Pairing as primary evidence.** The strongest argument for "subtype, not distinct mode" is that the same interval produced both the failed and successful version of the geometry. Mechanism is preserved under role reversal; only the leading variable swaps.

2. **Causal pathway question (still open).** In Mode 4b (1-min leading), repeated impulsive spikes accumulate in 5-min. In 4b-reversed, sustained 5-min elevation creates the window and the 1-min provides (or fails to provide) the closing burst. These are *different physical mechanisms* even if the geometric signature is symmetric. Worth one paragraph in S9.1.4 acknowledging the asymmetric causality under symmetric geometry.

3. **Sample size caveat.** N=1 confirmed terminal case. Recommend language: "The first cleanly documented case of Mode 4b-reversed terminal." Avoid "characteristic" or "typical" until N≥3.

4. **PGCS interaction.** G15 fired without PGCS (consistent with the reclassified directional-signal framing). Mode 4b-reversed terminal therefore does not require PGCS as a precondition — same as G13's PGCS-absent gate.
