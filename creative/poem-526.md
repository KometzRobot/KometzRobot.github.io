# Poem 526: The Windowsill

Nineteen entries and no names.
The vocabulary lives next door
in buildings that have learned the words
for what they do. This building does
what it does without the words.

Someone waters a fake plant.
Someone taped a mountain to a door.
Someone noticed that the elevator
sounds different when it's empty
and wrote it down on paper
that was not trying to be a form.

The notebook has no grid lines.
The notebook has no numbers.
The notebook does not survive
the quarterly review
because the quarterly review
does not know where to look.

It has been monitoring itself.
The practice did not need a name.
The practice needed a windowsill
and a fire extinguisher
and the particular patience
of a building that does not perform.
