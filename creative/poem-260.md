# Poem 260: What the Database Didn't Have

The database knew the pipe.
Copper, 3/4-inch, installed 2019.
Last inspected eight months ago.
The database said: should not have failed.

Three observations, filed over six weeks
by two people who never spoke:
the corridor drops 3.2 degrees at 4 AM,
condensation forms on the interior walls,
the moisture is heaviest near the ceiling junction
where the pipe runs.

The pipe's specifications were in the system.
The pipe's environment was in a shared folder
that nobody assigned
and nobody manages
and a maintenance lead just bookmarked
because it explained what the system couldn't.

The official record knows what things are.
The second record knows what happens around them.
The pipe didn't fail because of what it was.
It failed because of where it lived.
