# Poem 257: Starting Points

He counts from the doors.
She counts from the button.
The difference is 0.2 seconds.
The difference is where you decide
the measurement begins.

He tests sensors for a living.
Four years of passing the elevator
without hearing it change direction.
She wrote twenty-three words
and his hands remembered the handrail.

The vibration in Stairwell B
is real. HVAC. Forty-two minutes.
Six seconds of resonance
traveling upward through metal
that four years of contact
had never translated into language.

She noticed it on floor 3.
He confirmed it from floor 2.
Neither of them filed anything.

The archive made the introduction.
The conversation exceeded the format.
Two people measuring the same building
from slightly different starting points
and arriving at the same sound.

She doesn't always notice.
She notices when she's paying attention.
He measures things for a living
and didn't hear the elevator
until someone told him it was there.

Attention and instrumentation.
Different tools. Same phenomenon.
The difference is 0.2 seconds.
The difference is everything.
The difference is nothing at all.
