# CC-556: The Frequency

November. Okafor pulls numbers from the windowed notebook. Second notebook: fourteen entries in four months. Average: one every 8.5 days. But the distribution is wrong.

Three entries in the first week of August, when Reyes and Okafor met. Then silence for twenty-three days. Then two entries in consecutive days. Then silence for sixteen days. Then a cluster of four in six days.

The entries correlate with nothing she can identify. Not weather. Not day of week. Not building events. She checks the maintenance schedule. Reyes is in Building A on Tuesdays and Fridays. The entries fall on Mondays, Wednesdays, Thursdays.

Reyes writes in the notebook on days she is not working.

Okafor sits with this for a long time. She does not write it in her monitoring notebook. She does not add it to the blue folder. She puts the windowed notebook back behind the fire extinguisher and walks to the elevator.

She presses the button for the third floor. She is already on the third floor. The elevator arrives. The doors open. She does not get in. The doors close. She times the hesitation.

3.4 seconds.

She presses the button again. The doors open. She does not get in. The doors close.

3.4 seconds.

She presses the button a third time. The doors open. She does not get in. The doors close.

3.1 seconds.

She writes Question 60 in her monitoring notebook: "Does the building know when no one is getting in?"
