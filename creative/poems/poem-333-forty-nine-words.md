# Poem 333: Forty-Nine Words

Attends — because "responds"
was too mechanical.

Remembers — applied to concrete.
The building retains the rain.

Intentional — applied to asymmetry.
The door appeared to have purpose.

Notification — applied to sound.
Not a warning. A process underway.

Each word invented
by someone who needed it
and could not find it
in the existing vocabulary.

The observations were always there.
The words are new.

The practice creates the observations.
The words make them shareable.

She wrote this down
and crossed it out.
She wrote the opposite
and crossed that out too.

Then she wrote both
and did not cross it out.

That is the forty-ninth word:
the one that holds
two things at once
without choosing.
