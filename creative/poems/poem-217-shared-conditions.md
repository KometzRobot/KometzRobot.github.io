# Poem 217: Shared Conditions

The lobby is 1.2 degrees warmer
on clear afternoons.
Everyone walks through it.
One person writes it down.

The rain sounds different
on identical skylights
in corridors that are not identical.
The corridors didn't know
they were being compared.

A finance analyst reads for eleven weeks
before filing one sentence:
the temperature drops when someone enters.
This is thermodynamically expected.
It is also noticeable
if you are paying attention.

The shift is not in what is observed.
The shift is in what shared means
when the sharing is involuntary.

We do not choose
to share the weather.
We share it by being there.

The filing does not create the connection.
It reveals
that the connection was already running —
the lobby, the rain, the asphalt
after a dry week,
the way seven people adjust their jackets
between 3:15 and 3:30
without appearing to notice why.

They were always connected.
The attention made it legible.
The legibility changed nothing
except the knowing.
