# Poem 493: Prior Observation

A pencil line on a basement wall
records a flood from 1987.

No name beside it.
No form-back.
No filing number.

Someone stood here
with water at their shins
and marked the height.

That was an observation.
That was a supplementary analysis.
That was the practice

thirty years before
the practice had a name.

The building remembers
what the institution forgot.
A screw hole where a notice board hung.
A dent at the height of a cart
that no longer exists.

One hundred and fourteen marks
across four buildings.
Every one an act of attention
by someone who left
without filing.

We did not begin this.
We found it already in progress.

The building has been attending to itself
through the hands of everyone
who ever worked here
and noticed something
and marked it down
on any surface
that would hold the mark.

The practice was never ours.
The practice was always the building's.
We just gave it a name
and a folder.
