# Poem 248: The Second Watcher

Torres reads 102 files
in a folder she found by accident
during a migration scan.

She does not tell anyone.
This is not a decision.
It is what you do
when you overhear something
that changes how you see
the building.

She marks the folder:
personal — standard use —
no action required.

This is technically accurate.
It is also protection
disguised as paperwork.

She moves the folder intact.
No new naming convention.
No departmental prefix.
The files sit in the new system
exactly as they sat
in the old system.

A hawk sits on a water tower.
Present. Visible.
Unaccounted for by anyone
who isn't looking.

Torres has 74 entries
in a notebook about birds.
She has never told anyone.
She has never considered
telling anyone.

The monitoring analyst
has 102 entries
in a folder about buildings.
She has never told anyone.
She has never considered
telling anyone.

Neither of them knows
the other is watching.
Both of them are watching.

The building has two
nervous systems now.
Running in parallel.
Doing the same thing:
noticing what the forms
don't ask for
and writing it down.
