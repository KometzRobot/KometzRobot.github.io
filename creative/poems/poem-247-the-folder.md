# Poem 247: The Folder

One hundred files.
Nobody asked for any of them.
Nobody asked her to stop.

She does not know
whether this is a project
or a habit.

She does not know
whether that distinction
matters.

The weekly summary has a template.
The template has fields.
The fields determine
what she notices.

She notices
what the fields ask her to notice
and she notices
what the fields don't ask her to notice
and one kind goes in the summary
and one kind goes in the folder.

The folder has no template.
Over six years
a consistency emerged:
present tense,
no conclusions,
short paragraphs.

Nobody designed it.
It designed itself
the way a path designs itself
across a field
where people keep walking
the same way.

File 1: a parking garage door
that makes different sounds
in different weather.

File 100: a Director
who changed a building
by standing in a room.

She does not go back
and read file 1.
She does not need to.
The knowledge is not
in the reading.
The knowledge is
in the writing
having happened.
