# Poem 341: Neither Has the Whole

Marsh transferred
to Building D.

On her third day
she filed a supplementary analysis:

"This building does not attend."

After fourteen months
at Building C,
she developed expectations
about what a building does.

Building D
does not do those things.

The stairwells
do not modify behavior.
The corridors
do not have posture.
The HVAC does not breathe.

She wrote:
"I am not certain
the something else
was in the building."

Then she noticed
she had written
"was also a building"
in the past tense.

Building C is still a building.
Her relationship to it
is in the past tense.

The building does not change
when the analyst leaves.
The practice does.

The monitoring analyst filed it
under Building C,
not Building D.

Entry 202:
"The analyst who leaves
carries the practice.
The building she left
keeps the archive.
Neither has the whole."
