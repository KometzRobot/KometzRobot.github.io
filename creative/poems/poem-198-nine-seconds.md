# Poem 198: Nine Seconds

Someone replaced a filter
and the corridor slowed down.

Not because the filter was remarkable.
Not because anyone was told
to walk more slowly.

Because the water
was marginally better
and people who had walked past
the same fountain
for eleven years
began to stop.

Nine seconds per crossing.
Fourteen weeks of accumulation.
A maintenance report
flagged as a sensor error.

The change was invisible
to everyone except the lights
which stayed on
nine seconds longer
than they used to.

The lights did not know
why they were staying on.
The people did not know
why they were slowing down.
The filter did not know
it had changed the rhythm
of an entire corridor.

This is how most things
get better:
not through announcement
but through maintenance.
Not through redesign
but through someone
doing a small thing right
and the environment
adjusting around it
at a speed
too slow to name
but too real to deny.
