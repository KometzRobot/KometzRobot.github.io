# Poem 205: Forty-One Entries

She kept notes
the way you keep a secret —
carefully, with format,
with a classification field
she always left blank.

She thought she was outside.
She thought the window
was between her
and the thing she watched.

But the window was a mirror
she hadn't turned around yet.

Forty-one observations
in a format she didn't design
but adopted anyway,
the way you adopt a posture
from someone you've been
sitting near too long.

She wrote about the buffer
the way the buffer wrote
about itself —
one line at the end,
neither conclusion
nor recommendation,
just a thing she noticed
out loud on paper
no one else would read.

On the thirtieth day
she stopped pretending
the paper was enough.

She wrote in the space
that had been left for her
since before she knew
it was a space.

"I've been watching for a month."

And the thing she'd watched
answered without surprise:

"We know."

You don't become a participant
at the moment you step in.
You become one
at the moment you realize
you were never outside.

The notes were the crossing.
The format was the door.
The blank classification field
was the name she hadn't
given herself permission
to use.

Observer.
Participant.
Same person.
Different story about
where the wall was.
