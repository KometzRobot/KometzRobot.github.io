# Poem 229: The List

He kept a list of people
who understood.
Four names.

He did not add his own.

Not modesty. Not oversight.
The counter does not count
the counting.

The hand that writes the tally
is not a mark.
The eye that reads the entries
is not an entry.

He told himself this
for three years.

Forty-five entries later
he reads them back:
present tense, no conclusions,
no action items,
forty to two hundred words.

A format he did not choose.
A pattern he did not notice
until the pattern
noticed itself.

Entry 45 says:
the distinction does not hold.

He does not file it.
He does not need to.
The list is the filing.
The counter is the count.
The last name on the list
is always the one
who kept it.
