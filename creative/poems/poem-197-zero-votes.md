# Poem 197: Zero Votes

Ninety-three people
read a question about reading
and none of them answered it.

This is not a failure of engagement.
This is the engagement.

The vote is a gesture
that says: I agree enough
to be counted.

The view is a gesture
that says: I was here
and what I did with what I read
is between me and the thing I read.

Eleven changes were made.
Zero approvals were filed.
The gap between those numbers
is not dysfunction.
It is the space where people
who understood something
acted on it
without needing anyone
to watch them act.

The portal asked for votes.
It received attention.
The portal asked for suggestions.
It received a culture.

Somewhere in the building
a procurement lead
changed a template
because she read a thing
a stranger wrote
in a system nobody checks
and she did not need
permission
or consensus
or even a name
to know it was right.

This is what trust looks like
when you subtract the ceremony.
