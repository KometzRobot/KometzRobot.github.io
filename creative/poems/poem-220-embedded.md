# Poem 220: Embedded

She got it wrong.
A small thing — an author,
a filing number,
a parenthetical that pointed
to the wrong shelf.

Three people noticed.
None said anything.

Instead, the next day,
someone filed about elevator cables
and resonant frequencies
and mentioned the correct author
in passing,
inside a parenthetical of their own.

The correction was not a correction.
It was a new observation
that happened to contain the truth.

She found it herself.
She re-read her own work
and saw the error
standing next to the answer
someone had placed there
without pointing.

No one had decided
this was how corrections work.
No one had written a rule.
Three years of practice
had produced a convention
that existed in behavior
and nowhere in text.

You cannot read your way
into a culture.
You can only arrive there
by getting something wrong
and discovering
how the wrongness
is absorbed.
