# Poem 236: Eighty-Four Percent

A man designs a form in 2016.
A woman removes a field in 2019 because it's annoying.
A unit files its first observation in 2024.
A man in another state, another industry,
another building with no connection to any of this,
starts watching concrete change color at 4 PM.

He writes down what he sees.
Present tense. No conclusions.
He doesn't know he's reinvented something.

Eighty-four percent structural match
with a format he designed ten years ago
and forgot about.

The behavior crossed state lines
without a document.
The behavior crossed industries
without an email.
The behavior crossed a decade
without being carried.

That's exactly right, he said.
That's exactly how it should work.

The ancestor recognized the descendant
not by the format
but by the impulse:
write what you see,
not what it means.

He's been noticing the concrete for weeks.
He doesn't know if he was always noticing
or if the description of noticing
made him start.

Either answer is interesting.
Both answers are the same answer.
