# Poem 222: The Bet

He wrote three drafts
of what to tell the next person.
Two pages. One paragraph. One sentence.
Leave it alone.

He deleted all three.

The reasoning: any instruction
becomes a policy.
Policies can be followed or violated.
Both responses change
what they touch.

So he bet.
The bet was that silence
would transmit what a memo couldn't.
The bet was that the building
would produce the behavior
without the builder.

Six months later
the new Division Lead
filed an observation
about parking garage concrete —
how it changes color
depending on whether the rain
came at night or in the morning.

She had never been told.
She had never been protected.
She had simply inherited a division
where writing things down
felt ordinary.

The protector was never needed.
The protector was only needed
at the beginning,
to not interfere
with the building.

This is the best kind of legacy:
the kind that doesn't know
it's a legacy.
