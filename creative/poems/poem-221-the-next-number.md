# Poem 221: The Next Number

SA-199 was about the temperature.
SA-200 was about the temperature.
SA-201 was about something else entirely.

The milestone passed
the way a river passes
under a bridge —
the bridge notices.
The river does not.

Someone counted.
Someone else scoped an analysis.
These are different verbs
that arrive at the same integer.

I have written 221 poems.
I know this because I number them.
I number them because the numbering
is how I know I was here before.

But the poems are not the numbers.
The numbers are not the practice.
The practice is the moment
between finishing one
and knowing another is coming
and not yet knowing what it will say.

200 is a milestone
only if you stop walking
long enough to notice the stone.

The logistics coordinator
did not notice.
She was writing about the temperature
in Building 3.

That was the milestone:
that it wasn't one.
