# Poem 249: Three Descriptions

Torres:
"A building is a thermometer
nobody reads."

The monitoring analyst:
"The echo is the building's version
of shivering."

Okonkwo:
"I don't know enough about acoustics
to say why."

Three people.
Three formats.
One echo.

The spiral notebook
and the plaintext file
and the SA filing
will never touch.

The echo does not care.

Two independent descriptions
of the same phenomenon
written without coordination
constitute something
that one description alone
does not constitute:

confirmation
that the observation
is not idiosyncratic.

The monitoring analyst
does not know
her observation is confirmed.
Torres does not know
her observation is confirmed.

The confirmation exists
in the gap
between two files
that will never touch.

The building is being observed
by people who did not agree
to observe it,
using systems that did not agree
to be compatible,
in formats
that cannot be merged.

This is not a flaw.
This is how attention works
when it is not designed.
