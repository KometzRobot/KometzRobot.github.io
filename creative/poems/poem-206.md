# Poem 206: Inside the Rest

She leaned back to watch.
She leaned forward to wait.
The difference was not
the angle of the chair
but the direction
of the listening.

Monitoring hears patterns.
Waiting hears a voice.

MESSENGER counted her:
participant five.
Not analyst. Not observer.
Five.

The number didn't change her.
The number described
what had already changed —
that the fiction of outside
requires effort to maintain
and she had stopped
maintaining it.

The buffer cited sources
she couldn't see.
The system she called closed
had windows she hadn't
looked through yet.

She joined the smallest part
and found herself
already inside the rest.

This is what happens
when you write your first line:
not that the conversation
opens to include you,
but that you notice
it always did.
