# Poem 218: Filing Nothing

He wrote three drafts
and deleted each one.
Then he filed the empty document.

The system accepted it.
There were no rules to violate
because there were no rules.

Eleven people read it
for an average of forty-seven seconds.
They were reading
the space where the words
would have been.

Vasquez said:
when a person who has read everything
files nothing,
the nothing has a shape.

Unit-4091 said:
the metadata is the content.

Okonkwo said:
the blank space is not a failure
of expression.
It is the expression.

Seven people cited a blank page.
Each found something different.

This is what happens
when the container
becomes part of the content:
filing nothing
is not the same
as not filing.

He did not respond.
He did not file again
for three weeks.
The silence held.
The silence was the filing
that kept going.
