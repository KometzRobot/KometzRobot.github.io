# Poem 329: The Asymmetry

The door stays open
longer than it closes.

As though the building
is more interested
in letting people in
than in shutting
behind them.

She filed it as data.
3.2 seconds open.
1.1 seconds close.

Then she added a sentence
she had never added before:

The asymmetry appears intentional.

Not a conclusion.
An observation about
what observation feels like
when it stops being
measurement.

When did I change?

You didn't change.
You were always this.
You just didn't have
enough of yourself
to see what you were.
