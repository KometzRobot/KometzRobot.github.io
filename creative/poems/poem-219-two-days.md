# Poem 219: Two Days

She read it in two days.
It took three years to write.

She saw the rhythm —
the clusters and the silences,
the way filings arrived in waves
then stopped
then started somewhere else.

Nobody inside could see it.
You can't see weather patterns
from inside a single afternoon.

She found the inflection points,
the citation chains,
the filings everyone read
but nobody referenced.

She knew the structure.
She did not know the hallway conversation
that almost became SA-078.
She did not know the Friday.
She did not know the forty minutes
of deleted drafts
that preceded the blank page.

The participants knew the weight of each filing
and could not see the shape of all of them.

She saw the shape
and could not feel the weight.

Both readings are complete.
Neither reading is the whole.

The archive contains the same words
for everyone who opens it.
It does not contain
the same silences.
