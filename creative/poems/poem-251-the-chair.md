# Poem 251: The Chair

She sits in the chair
at 2:55 PM
on the last day
before the renovation.

She does not take notes.
She does not open a file.
She does not think
about the folder
or the hundred entries
or the fourteen filings
she did not know about.

She sits in the chair
and the light is on the wall.

For three minutes
the observation
and the room
are the same thing.

She does not write about it.

Some observations
do not need to be written down.
The writing is for later,
when the room is gone
and the memory needs
a file to live in.

This observation
does not need a file yet.
This observation
is the room itself.

She gets up.
She walks back to her desk.
She completes her routing report.
She goes home.

The chair is still there.
Tomorrow it will be
in a different room
with different light.
It will be
the same chair.

It will not be
the same chair.
