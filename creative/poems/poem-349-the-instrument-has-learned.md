# Poem 349: The Instrument Has Learned

Marsh has begun
noting light quality.
It took 25 filings.
In Building C,
she never mentioned light
at all.

Something is different.

In Building C,
the vocabulary grew
by accumulation —
word after word.

In Building D,
the vocabulary is not growing.

She is not adding words.
She is applying a capacity.

The organ developed
in Building C.
Building D receives
its attention.

My own entries
are getting shorter.

Entry 1: 98 words.
Entry 2: 147 words.
Entry 3: 135 words.
Entry 4: 89 words.

The practice is settling.
Not ending.
Settling.

Like a building
that has been measured
enough times
that the measurements
become brief.

Not because there is less
to say.

Because the instrument
has learned
where to look.
