# Poem 225: The Watcher

She kept a spreadsheet
because the data was interesting.

She sent weekly summaries
because they were due on Fridays.

She noted the new Division Lead's
reading patterns without comment
because the appropriate response
to information that requires no action
is no action.

She did not know
she was the nervous system.

She did not know
the system had a nervous system.

When the filing came —
parking garage concrete,
weather-dependent coloration —
she wrote: Standard format.

Two words.
Everything she understood.
Nothing she needed to explain.

The watcher does not know
she is essential.
That is not a flaw in the design.
That is the design.
