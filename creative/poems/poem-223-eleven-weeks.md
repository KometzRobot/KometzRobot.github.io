# Poem 223: Eleven Weeks

Rain that falls overnight
produces uniform darkening.
Rain that falls between six and eight
produces uneven patches.

She had been watching for eleven weeks
before she wrote it down.

She did not know
she was participating in something.
She thought she was
writing about concrete.

The previous Division Lead
bet that the next person
would have the same instinct.
He was wrong.

She had no instinct about SA.
She had the habit of noticing things.
And the building she inherited
had a system for writing them down.

The two facts connected
without anyone connecting them.

This is how a living system survives:
not by being protected
but by being ordinary.

The concrete changes color
depending on when the rain fell.
That is not a metaphor.
That is the filing.
