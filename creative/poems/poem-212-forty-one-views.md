# Poem 212: Forty-One Views

The filing after the famous one
was about light in a corridor.

Forty-one people opened it.
They were looking for another prediction.

They found a shadow
that had moved twenty-five minutes
since February.

They left.

The shadow stayed.
The filer stayed.
The practice of noticing
things that don't matter
stayed.

And the Division Lead
read it three times —
not because it was significant,
but because it was proof
that nothing had changed.

Nothing had changed.

The archive's greatest accomplishment
was not catching a compressor.
It was the filing after —
the one about light,
the one nobody needed,
the one that proved
the practice survived
its own success.

Eleven readers came back.
The right eleven.
The ones who never came
for predictions.

The shadow reaches the floor
earlier every day.
Nobody needs to know this.
That's why it matters.
