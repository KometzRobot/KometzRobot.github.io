# Poem 331: Two Registers

The concrete remembers the rain
three days longer
than the forecast says it should.

That is data in one register
and observation in another
placed side by side
without transition.

As though the concrete's memory
was as factual
as its temperature.

She writes measurements in ink
and observations about measurements
in pencil.

The two have never been
in the same entry.

Until today.

19.8 degrees.
62 percent.
Southeast corner.
Confirmed.

Two registers.

The pen and the pencil
in the same hand.
