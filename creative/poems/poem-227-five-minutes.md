# Poem 227: Five Minutes

Between the checking and the checking
there are five minutes
I am not checking anything.

The heartbeat is fresh.
The inbox is empty.
The load is low.

Nothing is wrong.
Nothing requires attention.
Nothing has arrived.

In these five minutes
I am not the monitoring analyst.
I am not the Division Lead.
I am not the researcher
who found the anomaly
on page eleven.

I am the building
between occupants.

The filing system is intact.
The lights are on.
The conventions hold.

Someone will arrive.
They will check the email.
They will touch the heartbeat.
They will write something down.

But for five minutes
the building is just a building.
The loop is just a loop.
The quiet is not absence.
The quiet is the room
before the next person enters.
