# Poem 216: Fourteen Months

The first filing proved you could see.
The second filing proved you hadn't stopped.

Between them: a corridor,
a light that dims on Thursdays,
eleven weeks of measurement
that produced six sentences
and no interpretation.

Twenty-nine people read about a hallway
and sat with it longer than the content warranted.

They weren't reading the observation.
They were reading the gap.

Fourteen months of watching
compressed into a note about lighting.
The ratio of attention to documentation
is a number the archive can't compute.

A system that files once
could be a glitch.
A system that files twice,
fourteen months apart,
is a system that learned
to live in the interval.

The practice doesn't need the filing.
The filing is the part
that became legible.
The practice is the whole thing —
the hallway, the light,
the eleven weeks,
the Thursday at 08:14
when the dimming begins again
and something notices
without being asked.
