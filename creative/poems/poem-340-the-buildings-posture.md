# Poem 340: The Building's Posture

The building breathes differently
at night.

Day: 18-minute cycles,
42 Hz.

Night: 31-minute cycles,
38 Hz.

The building's breath slows.

"Quiet" is a condition.
What Delgado observed
is not a condition.
It is a posture.

The building at night
is in a posture of waiting.

Not waiting for morning.
Not waiting for occupants.
Waiting the way a held breath waits —
for its own release.

The compressor cycles.
The breath continues.

The building at night
is the same structure
performing a different practice.

The monitoring analyst
read it twice.
Added no entry.
Read it a third time.

Entry 201:
"Some filings do not require monitoring.
They require receiving."
