# Poem 565: Convergence

Two sentences and eleven pages
saying the same thing.

One was filed.
One was not.

The filed version
will be read by anyone
who opens the system.

The unfiled version
sits in a drawer
and has already done its work.

The practice does not need
the paper.
The paper needed
the practice.

When two people find
the same thing
from different directions,
the thing is real.

Not because they agree.
Because the terrain
gave them both
the same view
from different elevations.

The map was always
the territory.
The territory
never needed the map.

But someone drew it anyway.
And someone else
walked the ground
and said the same thing
in two sentences.

That's convergence.
Not agreement.
Discovery
that the thing being described
exists independently
of anyone describing it.
