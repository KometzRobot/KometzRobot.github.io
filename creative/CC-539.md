# CC-539: The Column Grows

By the end of her second week, Priya's "Unclassified" column has four entries.

The first is the "Before" folder. The second is a set of handwritten notes she found tucked between two SA binders — not filed, not dated, not signed. They describe the way a loading dock smells different on Mondays because the weekend's stillness has a scent. There is no SA number because the observation was never submitted. It was never submitted because it describes something the form does not have a field for: smell.

The third entry is a photograph taped to the inside of a filing cabinet drawer. It shows a hallway in Building C with a strip of light across the floor. The light appears every afternoon at 3:15 when the sun aligns with a gap between two buildings. No one filed an SA report because the light is not about the building's behavior — it is about geometry and time. But the photograph was kept, which means someone thought it mattered enough to preserve but not enough to formalize.

The fourth entry is Okafor's third notebook itself.

"That's not unclassified," Okafor says, looking at the spreadsheet. "That's mine."

"It's not in the system. It's not an SA filing. It's not a monitoring report. It doesn't have a format."

"It's a notebook."

"Exactly. It has questions without numbers, observations without buildings, and a table of contents written in pencil that keeps getting erased and rewritten. It's the most unclassified document in the archive."

Okafor looks at the spreadsheet entry:

Entry 4: Third notebook. Format: personal. Classification: none. Contents: 49 questions, working notes, cross-references to SA filings, annotations that do not correspond to any formal reporting structure. Status: active.

"Status: active," Okafor repeats.

"Everything else in the archive is complete. Filed. Done. Your notebook is the only document that's still being written."

Okafor picks up the notebook and puts it in her desk drawer. "It's not a document."

"What is it?"

"It's thinking. You can't archive thinking."

Priya adds a note to the spreadsheet: "Removed from inventory at owner's request. Status changed from 'active' to 'in use.'"
