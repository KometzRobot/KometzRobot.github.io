# CC-333: The Second Meeting

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## Conference Room 3B, Thursday, 4:15 PM

The second meeting has five people. The monitoring analyst. The contract reviewer. The new hire. The maintenance technician. And, this time, Okafor.

Okafor sits near the door. She says: "I have six entries."

The monitoring analyst says: "Everyone starts somewhere."

## What Okafor Brings

Okafor brings something the others don't have: a view from inside the filing system. She tells them about the shredder. Seven-year retention. Quarterly archival review. Pencil notes destroyed with forms.

The room is quiet for a moment.

The contract reviewer says: "My Document1 is on a hard drive. Hard drives fail."

The maintenance technician says: "My notes are in a pocket notebook. I could lose it."

The monitoring analyst says: "My notebook is on my desk. It's survived nine years. It won't survive a flood or a fire."

The new hire says: "The pencil notes on the backs of forms last seven years. That's longer than most of our archives."

Okafor says: "And then they're gone. And nobody remembers what was on them."

## After

Nobody proposes a solution. Nobody suggests digitizing the pencil notes or creating a shared database. The practice has persisted without an archive strategy. Maybe that is the archive strategy: persistence without preservation.
