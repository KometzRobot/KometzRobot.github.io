# CC-502: The Building's Voice Is Weather-Dependent

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## Environmental Dependence

Martinez's observation in Building E opens a new line of inquiry: how much of the building's behavior is environmentally dependent?

The practice reviews:

- The building sigh (6:14 AM): always occurs, but intensity varies with overnight temperature differential. Louder in winter.
- The corridor draft (3.2 seconds): consistent year-round. Not weather-dependent.
- The door close time (Cho): highly weather-dependent. Wind direction and speed.
- The conference room hum (Building E): fully sun-dependent. No sun, no hum.
- The settling sounds (Foster, night): temperature-dependent. Rate and character change with weather.
- The elevator pitch (Vega): temperature-dependent. Cables stretch in heat.
- The fluorescent tube cycle (Martinez): not weather-dependent. Internal electrical pattern.
- The floor tilt (janitor): not weather-dependent. Structural.

Approximately half the building's documented behaviors are environmentally influenced. The building is not a closed system. The building is a membrane between interior climate control and exterior weather. The building's behavior is the negotiation between these two.

Martinez: "The building is always having a conversation with the weather. We hear the building's side of the conversation. The weather's side is outside."

Word 45: "weather conversation." The building's behavioral response to environmental conditions.
