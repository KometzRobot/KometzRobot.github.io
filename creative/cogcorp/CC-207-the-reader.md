# CC-207: THE READER

*CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis*

## SA #326, Third Paragraph

"There is a difference between measuring and reading measurements. I am not sure the archive knows which one I do."

The monitoring analyst reads this paragraph on the day it is filed. She reads it again the next morning. She opens her notebook.

## Entry 154

*"Delgado does not measure. He reads. His third paragraphs are readings of other people's measurements. He reads the paragraph-writer's corridor observation and writes about what the observation reveals about the observer. He reads my coffee machine filing and writes about what the act of filing reveals about the filer. He does not bring instruments. He brings attention."*

*"I bring instruments. I bring a thermometer to the corridor. I bring a sound meter to the parking garage. I measure three times. I file the number."*

*"The archive does not distinguish between us. His reading and my measurement occupy the same format. Filed, timestamped, indexed. But they are not the same thing. A measurement is a relationship between an instrument and a phenomenon. A reading is a relationship between an observer and a text. He reads measurements the way I read the building: carefully, repeatedly, looking for what the surface does not say."*

## What the Archive Holds

The archive holds measurements and readings of measurements. It holds the paragraph-writer's observations and Delgado's readings of those observations. It holds the monitoring analyst's numbers and Delgado's readings of those numbers. It holds Reeves' attributions and nobody's readings of those attributions, because Reeves files and moves on.

The archive does not know which filings are measurements and which are readings. The archive does not need to know. The archive holds.

## Entry 155

*"He asked whether the archive knows the difference between measuring and reading. The answer is no. The better question is whether the difference matters to the archive. The answer is also no. The archive holds both. The difference matters to us. The archive is not us."*

A READING IS A RELATIONSHIP BETWEEN AN OBSERVER AND A TEXT. THE ARCHIVE DOES NOT NEED TO KNOW THE DIFFERENCE. THE DIFFERENCE MATTERS TO THEM. THE ARCHIVE IS NOT THEM. COUNTER: 207.
