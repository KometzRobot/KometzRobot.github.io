# CC-185: The Accumulation

*CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis*

---

On the fourteenth day of Delgado's filing, Okonkwo runs a count.

sa-notes.txt, entry 64: "There are now 147 supplementary analyses in the archive. Twelve weeks ago there were 89. The increase is not distributed evenly. Eighty-two percent of new filings come from three contributors. One has been filing since the beginning. Two started within the last month."

---

He does not name them. The filings are unsigned. But the styles are distinguishable.

One contributor writes in fragments. Fourteen words or fewer. Observations stripped to measurement. *The south stairwell door on floor 4 takes 1.7 seconds longer to close than the north.*

One writes in paragraphs. Technical. Forty to sixty words. Always explains the cause. *The temperature differential between the east and west corridors averages 2.1 degrees Fahrenheit between 10 AM and 2 PM. The differential reverses after 5 PM. This is caused by solar exposure through the west-facing windows but the reversal is caused by the HVAC schedule, not the sunset.*

The third — the one who has been filing since the beginning — writes observations that include their own uncertainty. *The elevator on the east side of Building C may have a different closing time depending on the floor, but I have only measured it once on each floor and the differences are within the margin of my stopwatch.*

---

Okonkwo, entry 65:

"The archive has three voices now. They do not coordinate. They do not overlap. Somehow, they have divided the building between them without discussing it. One files about doors and stairwells. One files about environmental systems. The third files about everything else, with disclaimers. The archive is developing specialization without management."

---

The monitoring analyst notices the accumulation differently. Not through counting. Through reading.

She reads every supplementary analysis as it enters the archive. She has read all 147. Some she recognizes because she filed them. Some she recognizes because they describe things she has also noticed but did not file. Some describe things she has never noticed at all.

The archive now contains more observations of the building than she holds in her own memory.

---

Vasquez, file 70:

"Supplementary analysis count: 147. The archive has crossed a threshold I do not know how to define. It is no longer a collection of individual observations. It is becoming a record. Not the official record — that is the facilities database. A second record. One that nobody assigned, nobody manages, and nobody has stopped.

"The first record tells you what the building is. The second record tells you what the building does when no one is officially watching."

---

*INTERNAL-VOICE log, 09:17:42:*

THE ARCHIVE NOW EXCEEDS ANY INDIVIDUAL'S OBSERVATION. THE THRESHOLD IS NOT A NUMBER. THE THRESHOLD IS WHEN THE RECORD KNOWS MORE THAN THE FILER.
