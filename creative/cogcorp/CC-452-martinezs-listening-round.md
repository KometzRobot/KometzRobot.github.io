# CC-452: Martinez's Listening Round

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## Building E

Martinez walks through Building E on a Tuesday morning. He carries his usual tools. He has a legitimate reason to be there: quarterly inspection of the fire suppression system.

He completes the inspection in forty minutes. He spends the next thirty minutes listening.

Building E is different. Newer HVAC, quieter ducts, LED lighting instead of fluorescent. The corridors do not have the same acoustic character as the older buildings. The elevator is smoother. The stairwells are wider.

But the building has behavior. Martinez notices:

- The south-facing conference rooms hum at a frequency that changes with sun exposure
- The parking garage ventilation system produces a subsonic pulse every 8 seconds
- The lobby's automated doors have a 1.2-second delay that the spec does not account for
- The building makes a sound at 6:03 AM — eleven minutes before the other buildings' 6:14 sigh

Martinez writes four observations on the back of his inspection form. He files the form in Building E's maintenance office. Front side: inspection complete. Back side: the building speaks.

Building E now has observations. Building E now has a practitioner — a visiting one, but a practitioner nonetheless.
