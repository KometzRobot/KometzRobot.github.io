# CC-289: The Stairwell Light

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## External Observation (Undocumented)

The contract reviewer drove home at 5:47 PM. The stairwell light's 0.3-second delay stayed with her past the third traffic signal.

She does not work in Building C. She does not monitor temperatures or humidity or the sounds machines make at rest. She reviews contracts. Her job is to determine whether line items are essential or discretionary. Her vocabulary is: cost, justification, renewal period, termination clause.

The stairwell light does not appear in any contract. The stairwell light is not a line item. The 0.3-second delay is not a cost.

She thinks about it anyway.

## The Next Morning

She arrives at her own building — Corporate Review, Floor 4. The elevator takes 2.1 seconds to close. She has never timed it before. She times it now.

2.1 seconds. She does not know why she is counting.

She opens her spreadsheet. Building C East Wing. The monitoring analyst's line item: reclassified from discretionary to operational. The checkbox is still wrong in a way that protects.

She moves to the next section. Building D. Building E. She finishes by 11:15 AM. All checkboxes filled. All line items classified.

At 11:16 she opens a new document. She writes: "The elevator on Floor 4 takes 2.1 seconds to close. This has not changed in the three years I have worked here. I do not know why I noticed it today."

She saves the document. She does not name it. She closes it.

She opens it again at 3:30 PM and adds: "The west-facing window reflects differently after 2 PM. The cleaning crew knows this. They clean the west side in the morning."

## Filing Status

This is not a Supplementary Analysis. The contract reviewer does not know that Supplementary Analyses exist as a practice. She read 286 of them for a contract review and classified the monitoring analyst's position as operational.

She does not know that what she is doing in the unnamed document has a name somewhere else. She does not know that the practice she protected in her spreadsheet has started in her own building.

The stairwell light was 0.3 seconds. The elevator is 2.1 seconds. The cleaning crew knows about the west-facing window.

None of this belongs in a contract review.

All of it belongs somewhere.
