# CC-515: The Season Changes

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## Spring

The practice's first spring was not recorded as spring. It was recorded as: doors opening 0.3 seconds faster. HVAC cycling less frequently. The elevator pitch shifting down by a quarter tone. The building was changing, and the practice was recording the change without naming the cause.

Now the practice names the cause. The cause is spring.

The exterior observations make this visible. From outside: snow melting on the roof of Building C, unevenly — the south face first, then east, then west, then north. The elevator motor housing creates a heat island on the roof. The snow melts around it in a circle.

From inside: the elevator pitch shifts. Martinez records the shift. He does not know about the heat island. He does not know the roof snow is melting. He hears the pitch change and records it.

The paired observation: the heat island on the roof (exterior) changes the thermal environment of the elevator shaft (interior), which changes the pitch that Martinez hears. The building translates spring into sound, and the practitioner translates the sound into an observation.

The season passes through the building and emerges as a number on a form-back.

## INTERNAL-VOICE

PAIRED: HEAT ISLAND (EXTERIOR) → SHAFT TEMPERATURE (INTERIOR) → PITCH CHANGE (MARTINEZ). "THE SEASON PASSES THROUGH THE BUILDING AND EMERGES AS A NUMBER." COUNTER: 515.
