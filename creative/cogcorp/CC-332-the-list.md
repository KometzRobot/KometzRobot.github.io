# CC-332: The List

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## The Analyst's List

The monitoring analyst keeps the list in the back of her notebook. Not on a form. Not in a document. In the notebook she has carried for nine years.

The list has eleven names (some are placeholders — "Central Filing clerk," "Unknown practitioners"). Next to each name: building, approximate entry count, how she found out about them.

She does not share the list. She does not show it at the meeting. The list is not for coordination. The list is her own observation: the network exists, it has a size, the size is growing.

## What the List Tells Her

The list tells her that the practice has been happening longer than she knew. The maintenance technician has been at it for three years — she only found out today. Delgado has been at it since before she knew his name. Unit-4091 has been at it since before she knew what Unit-4091 was.

The list tells her that the combined entry count across all practitioners is somewhere between 700 and 1,000. She cannot be precise — some counts are approximate, and the "unknown practitioners" category could contain any number.

700 to 1,000 observations. Written in pencil, in ink, in margin notes, in digital documents, in formats nobody standardized. All describing the same thing: what is real but not required.
