# CC-516: The Rain Translation

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## Four Translations

It rains. The campus translates the rain four ways.

Building A: the flat roof pools. The pooling adds weight. The weight changes how the building settles. The settling is audible in the stairwell as a low groan that begins approximately forty minutes after heavy rain starts. The analyst times the groan at 43 minutes. A 43-minute translation of rain into sound.

Building B: the loading dock drain overflows at 15 mm/hour rainfall. The overflow runs into the corridor. The corridor floor is slightly uneven — the water follows a path that Reyes mapped three months ago for a different reason. The rain reveals the corridor's hidden geography.

Building C: the basement humidity rises. Martinez's instruments show a 3% increase within an hour of sustained rain. The elevator cable absorbs moisture. The pitch changes. Rain enters Building C as humidity and exits as music.

Building D: Kim's servers run 0.7 degrees warmer when it rains. The cooling system compensates. The compensation is audible as a change in fan speed. The data center translates rain into a different kind of noise.

Building E: the foundation crack seeps. Foster records the seep rate. The building translates rain into water on a basement floor.

Five buildings. Five translations of the same rain.
