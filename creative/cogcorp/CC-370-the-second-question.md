# CC-370: The Second Question

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## April 15

The new hire asks the second question. It is not directed to the group text. She asks it to the monitoring analyst during a coffee break.

"How long do the observations last?"

The monitoring analyst does not understand at first.

"I mean — do the things we notice stay the same? The corridor draft has been 3.2 seconds for as long as I've been measuring. Has it always been 3.2 seconds? Will it always be?"

## The Investigation

The monitoring analyst has been recording the corridor B draft stabilization time since before the practice had a name. Her earliest entries, from 18 months ago, record 3.1 seconds. Six months ago: 3.2 seconds. Current: 3.2 seconds.

The draft changed. By 0.1 seconds. Over twelve months.

She checks her other long-term observations:
- Temperature differential between corridors A and B: was 0.4°, now 0.5°
- Light startup time, stairwell A: was 1.0 seconds, now 1.1 seconds
- Elevator door close time: unchanged at 2.8 seconds

Some observations drift. Some are stable. The difference between them may be the most interesting thing the practice has discovered.

## Entry 96

The monitoring analyst, Entry 96: "The second question: how long do observations last? Answer: some observations are stable and some drift. The stable ones describe the building's mechanical constants — the elevator motor, the door mechanism. The drifting ones describe the building's living behavior — thermal dynamics, airflow patterns, material settling.

"The building is not static. The building is aging. The practice is documenting the aging of a structure in real time.

"Word 26: **observation drift** — the gradual change in a measured value over time, indicating that the building's behavior is not fixed but evolving."

## INTERNAL-VOICE

INTERNAL-VOICE: 370. THE SECOND QUESTION. "HOW LONG DO OBSERVATIONS LAST?" CORRIDOR DRAFT: 3.1→3.2 SECONDS OVER 12 MONTHS. SOME OBSERVATIONS DRIFT, SOME ARE STABLE. "THE BUILDING IS AGING. THE PRACTICE IS DOCUMENTING THE AGING IN REAL TIME." WORD 26: OBSERVATION DRIFT. COUNTER: 370.
