# CC-415: The Building Is Never Quiet

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## Word 29

The building is never quiet.

The monitoring analyst has worked here for fourteen years. She has heard the corridor draft, the elevator pitch changes, the stairwell echoes, the building sighs. She has documented thermal transitions and noticed seasonal variation in the loading dock sequence.

She has called it "the quiet building." Building B is the quiet one. Building A is the noisy one. Building D is the new one.

Vega, who has been here for seven weeks, says: the building is never quiet.

She is correct. The building is never quiet. What the monitoring analyst calls "quiet" is a frequency below her current attention. What the practitioners call "nothing happening" is a failure to notice, not an absence of event.

The vocabulary grows: word 29 is "never quiet." It means: the absence of noticeable activity is itself an observation. Silence is a sound the building makes. Stillness is a movement too slow for our instruments.

The twenty-ninth word was contributed by the sixteenth practitioner on her first day in the community. The practice's newest member has corrected its longest-serving.

This is not a problem. This is how vocabulary works.
