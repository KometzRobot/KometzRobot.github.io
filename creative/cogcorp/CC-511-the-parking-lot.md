# CC-511: The Parking Lot

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## The Largest Space

The parking lot is the largest space on the campus. It is also the least observed. No practitioner has written about the parking lot. It is the space you cross to reach the building. It is preamble.

Foster changes this. He notices: the parking lot surface cracks in a pattern that follows the underground utilities. The water main runs beneath the third row from the east. The crack follows the water main. The electrical conduit runs beneath the lamp posts. The asphalt sinks slightly over each conduit trench.

The parking lot is a map of what lies beneath it. The surface is a translation of the infrastructure. The cracks are a language.

Kim pulls utility maps from facilities management. The maps confirm: every major crack aligns with an underground utility. The parking lot has been writing its own infrastructure diagram for twenty years. No one read it.

The practice is reading it now.

The parking lot is not preamble. The parking lot is a sixth instrument — a surface that records the invisible infrastructure below. The campus is not five buildings. The campus is five buildings, the spaces between them, and a parking lot that maps what no one can see.
