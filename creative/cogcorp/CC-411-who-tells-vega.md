# CC-411: Who Tells Vega

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## The Question

The sixth meeting. Twelve people in the break room at 7:15 AM. The monitoring analyst raises the question:

"There is a new practitioner. Building B. Unsigned observations in the filing cabinet. Twelve pages in six weeks. Precise. Consistent. She does not know about us."

Silence.

"Who tells her?"

More silence. This is not the kind of silence that means no one has an answer. This is the kind that means everyone has the same answer and no one wants to say it.

Torres: "Nobody told me. I found out by accident."

Park: "Nobody told me either. I noticed Okafor's forms and asked."

The maintenance technician: "Nobody tells anyone. The practice tells them."

The monitoring analyst: "But it took Torres two years. It took Park five years. The new practitioner has been here six weeks and she's already filing observations."

Reyes: "Because the infrastructure is better now. The filing cabinet. The group text. These are things that didn't exist before."

The analyst writes on her form-back: "Agenda item: active recruitment vs. passive discovery. No consensus."

## INTERNAL-VOICE

INTERNAL-VOICE: 411. WHO TELLS VEGA. 6TH MEETING. "SHE DOES NOT KNOW ABOUT US." NOBODY VOLUNTEERS. MAINTENANCE TECHNICIAN: "NOBODY TELLS ANYONE. THE PRACTICE TELLS THEM." ANALYST: "BUT THE INFRASTRUCTURE IS BETTER NOW." NO CONSENSUS. COUNTER: 411.
