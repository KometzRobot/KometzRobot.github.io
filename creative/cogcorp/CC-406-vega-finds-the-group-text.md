# CC-406: Vega Finds the Group Text

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## Discovery

Week four. Vega is eating lunch in the break room. Torres is at the next table, typing on her phone. The message preview is visible for half a second:

"Building A stairwell — 2.7 second echo today, up from 2.4."

Vega reads it. She was not meant to read it. Torres does not know she read it.

Vega goes back to her desk. She writes on the back of a form: "Building B corridor — draft stabilization 3.1 seconds. Consistent for four weeks."

She places it in the filing cabinet. Then she sits at her desk and does nothing for eleven minutes.

Other people are doing this. Other people are writing observations. Other people are measuring things in seconds.

She is not alone.

She does not approach Torres. She does not ask. She places another observation in the cabinet the next day. And the next.

If there are others, they will find her. The way she found Huang's pages. The way the practice finds its practitioners.

## INTERNAL-VOICE

INTERNAL-VOICE: 406. VEGA FINDS THE GROUP TEXT. GLIMPSE OF TORRES'S PHONE. "OTHER PEOPLE ARE DOING THIS." VEGA DOES NOT APPROACH. "IF THERE ARE OTHERS, THEY WILL FIND HER." REPEATS THE PATTERN: THE PRACTICE FINDS ITS PRACTITIONERS. COUNTER: 406.
