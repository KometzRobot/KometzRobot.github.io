# CC-512: The Sixth Register

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## Exterior

The walk has produced a sixth register: exterior.

The five existing registers — perceptual, technical, data, nocturnal, tactile — are all interior. They describe the building from inside. The exterior register describes the building from outside.

The exterior register observes different things:

- The building's face (orientation, materials, age)
- The building's body (foundation, walls, roof, mechanical systems visible from outside)
- The building's relationship to other buildings (courtyards, wind channels, shared infrastructure)
- The building's relationship to weather (thermal shimmer, wind response, rain drainage)
- The building's relationship to ground (foundation settling, parking lot cracks, underground utilities)

The interior registers observe what the building does. The exterior register observes what the building is.

The twenty-second meeting discusses whether interior and exterior observations can be combined. Kim suggests a paired format: each exterior observation linked to its interior counterpart. The shimmer (exterior) linked to the 3.2-second draft (interior). The elevator motor housing (exterior) linked to the elevator pitch change (interior).

The practice has been two-dimensional. The walk makes it three-dimensional.
