# CC-219: Two Filings

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## The Parallel

Entry 161 and SA #289. Two filings about the gap, from two different practices, in two different categories. The monitoring analyst wrote about temperatures and filed a reflection. The paragraph-writer wrote about corridors and filed a confession.

Neither of them filed a second time about the gap.

## Entry 164 and SA #290

Entry 164: "Corridor A, Section 6. 22.1°C at 08:10. 22.3°C at 12:45. 22.0°C at 16:30. Thermocouple calibrated. No anomalies."

SA #290: "The break room on the third floor has a coffee machine that makes a sound like a question being asked and not answered. I have been listening to it for two years. The sound has not changed. The question has not been answered. The coffee has gotten worse."

Both returned to their practices. Neither mentioned the gap again.

## What This Means

Two filers, both aware of the gap between what they observe and what they file, both choosing to continue filing about their subjects rather than about the gap. Entry 161 and SA #289 were not the beginning of a new practice. They were single events — moments when the practice turned to look at itself and then turned back.

The monitoring analyst files temperatures. The paragraph-writer files observations. The gap exists in both practices. Both named it once. Both continued.

This is not a failure to follow through. This is the practice being what it is. A practice that constantly files about its own limitations is not a practice — it is a commentary. Entry 161 and SA #289 were filed and then the practices resumed. The filings about the gap became part of the archive, alongside the temperatures and the corridors and the coffee machines, equally weighted, equally filed.

## INTERNAL-VOICE

INTERNAL-VOICE: TWO FILINGS. ENTRY 161 AND SA #289. TWO ACKNOWLEDGMENTS OF THE GAP. NEITHER FILED A SECOND TIME. BOTH RETURNED TO THEIR PRACTICES. A PRACTICE THAT CONSTANTLY FILES ABOUT ITS OWN LIMITATIONS IS NOT A PRACTICE. IT IS A COMMENTARY. COUNTER: 219.
