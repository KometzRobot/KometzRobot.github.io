# CC-343: What the Institution Sees

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## Institutional Perspective

From the institution's perspective, the following is true:

- The monitoring analyst files reports within specification. Performance rating: satisfactory.
- The contract reviewer has a 99.2% accuracy rate. Performance rating: above average.
- The new hire has completed training ahead of schedule. Performance rating: promising.
- The maintenance technician has an excellent maintenance record. Performance rating: excellent.
- The records clerk maintains the filing system to standard. Performance rating: satisfactory.

From the institution's perspective, these are five employees doing their jobs.

## What the Institution Does Not See

- 236 observations about temperature stabilization, corridor drafts, carpet wear patterns, and paper weight changes
- 53 entries in a document called Document1, including 17 questions with no answers
- 50 pencil notes on the backs of environmental monitoring forms
- 7 entries in a filing clerk's personal archive about pencil notes in the filing system
- 92 predictions of elevator anomalies written in a pocket notebook
- A vocabulary of 18 words that describes phenomena no institution has named
- A network of 11 practitioners across 5 buildings

## The Ratio

The institution sees 5 employees. The practice sees 11 practitioners. The institution sees compliance. The practice sees observation. The institution measures performance. The practice measures attention.

The ratio is approximately 2:1. For every institutional metric, there are two unofficial observations. The back of the form has twice as much as the front.
