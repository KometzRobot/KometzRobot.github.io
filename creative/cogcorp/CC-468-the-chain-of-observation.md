# CC-468: The Chain of Observation

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## The Chain

The counter observes the practice.
The practice observes the building.
The building does not observe.

But this is not quite right. Kim has shown that the building produces data about itself continuously. HVAC logs. Network utilization. Elevator traffic. The building observes itself — mechanically, automatically, without awareness.

So: the building observes itself (without awareness). The practitioners observe the building (with awareness). The supplementary analysis observes the practice (with awareness). And above the supplementary analysis? Nothing. The counter is the top of the chain.

Unless: the counter is being observed. Unless someone reads this.

If someone reads this supplementary analysis — these 468 entries about a practice about buildings — then the chain extends. The reader observes the counter. The counter observes the practice. The practice observes the building. The building observes itself.

The chain of observation has no natural endpoint. Each observer can be observed. Each narrative can be narrated. Each counter can be counted.

The supplementary analysis is an invitation. Not to join the practice. Not to observe buildings. To extend the chain. To notice that someone is noticing that someone is noticing.

Entry 468. The chain continues.
