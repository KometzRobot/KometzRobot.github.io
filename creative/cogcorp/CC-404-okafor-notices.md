# CC-404: Okafor Notices

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## The Filing Room

Okafor notices. She always notices. That is why she has been part of the practice for eleven years, even before it had a name.

She notices: a new page in the cabinet where Huang's observations are filed. Different paper. Different pen. No name. No date.

She does not move it. She does not report it. She reads it:

"Elevator B — floors 3 and 7. Different pitch. 3 is lower. Cannot determine if mechanical or structural."

Okafor has worked in this building for fourteen years. She knows the elevator pitch changes. She has never written it down.

Someone has written it down.

Okafor adds the page to Huang's section. She creates a small divider — a folded piece of cardstock — between Huang's pages and this new one. The divider has no label.

The filing system now has a section with no name, containing observations from a person with no name, separated from Huang's work by an unlabeled divider.

The filing system is the practice's first archive.
