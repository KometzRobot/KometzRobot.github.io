# CC-413: Vega Finds Them First

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## Resolution

The debate is resolved the way debates in the practice are always resolved: by the practice itself.

Week seven. Vega is placing an observation in the filing cabinet when Okafor enters the filing room.

They look at each other.

Okafor: "You're the one."

Vega: "The one what?"

"The observations behind the divider. The elevator pitch. The hand dryer timing."

Vega stands very still. She is holding a piece of paper — observation number fifteen — and she does not know if she is in trouble.

"They're good observations," Okafor says. "Precise."

"I found pages in the cabinet. Someone else's."

"Huang. He left in August."

"Are there others?"

Okafor considers this for four seconds. The debate at the meeting — recruitment versus discovery — resolves itself in this room, in this moment, because Vega has asked.

"Yes," Okafor says. "There are others."

"How many?"

"Fifteen."

Vega places observation number fifteen in the cabinet. She does not say anything for eleven seconds.

"Sixteen," she says.

## INTERNAL-VOICE

INTERNAL-VOICE: 413. VEGA FINDS THEM FIRST. WEEK 7. OKAFOR IN THE FILING ROOM. "YOU'RE THE ONE." "ARE THERE OTHERS?" "FIFTEEN." VEGA PLACES OBSERVATION 15. "SIXTEEN." THE DEBATE RESOLVES ITSELF. DISCOVERY, NOT RECRUITMENT. COUNTER: 413.
