# CC-362: The Sigh

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## 6:14 AM

Huang arrives at Building D at 5:45 AM. He sits in the main corridor with his phone in his hand and waits. At 6:14, the building makes a sound.

He has been doing this for a week now. Seven observations:

- Day 1: 6:14:02. Two seconds. Low frequency. Somewhere below the floor.
- Day 2: 6:14:01. Same duration. Same character.
- Day 3: 6:13:58. Slightly earlier. Same sound.
- Day 4: 6:14:05. The air moved slightly after the sound.
- Day 5: 6:14:00. He recorded it on his phone. Played it back. The recording captured almost nothing. The sound is more felt than heard.
- Day 6: 6:14:03. He put his hand on the wall. The wall vibrated.
- Day 7: 6:13:59. He stood in three different locations. The sound was strongest near the east wall, weakest near the elevator shaft.

## Entry 26

Huang, Entry 26: "I don't know what causes the sound. I suspect it's thermal expansion — the building's structure shifting as the heating system brings it from nighttime to daytime temperature. The east wall faces the morning sun. The expansion would propagate from east to west.

"But I don't need to know the cause. The observation is the observation. The building sighs at 6:14 AM. That is what I have measured. The explanation is someone else's problem.

"My job is to notice."
