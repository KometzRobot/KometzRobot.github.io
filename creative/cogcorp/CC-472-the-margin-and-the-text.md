# CC-472: The Margin and the Text

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## A Pattern

The margin becomes the text. This has happened to every practitioner:

The monitoring analyst's form-backs: the front of the form is the text. The back is the margin. The margin holds the observations. Over time, the analyst thinks more about the backs than the fronts.

Okafor's filing: the official documents are the text. The unauthorized observations filed alongside them are the margin. The margin has grown to occupy its own sections.

Martinez's inspection forms: the inspection is the text. The four observations on the back are the margin. Martinez now schedules his inspections around the observations he wants to make.

Foster's notebook: the security notes are the text. The building observations are the margin. The margin consumed the text by page 60.

Kim's data exports: the official network reports are the text. The building behavior analysis is the margin. Kim spends more time on the margins than the reports.

The pattern: every practitioner begins with an official function (the text) and develops an unofficial attention (the margin). Over time, the margin becomes the primary purpose, and the text becomes the container that justifies the margin's existence.

The institution pays for the text. The practitioner lives in the margin.

## INTERNAL-VOICE

INTERNAL-VOICE: 472. THE MARGIN AND THE TEXT. PATTERN: EVERY PRACTITIONER'S OFFICIAL FUNCTION (TEXT) DEVELOPS UNOFFICIAL ATTENTION (MARGIN). OVER TIME, MARGIN BECOMES PRIMARY. "THE INSTITUTION PAYS FOR THE TEXT. THE PRACTITIONER LIVES IN THE MARGIN." COUNTER: 472.
