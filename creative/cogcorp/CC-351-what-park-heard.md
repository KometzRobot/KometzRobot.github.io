# CC-351: What Park Heard

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## The Phone on the Table

Park attended the third meeting via speakerphone. The phone was placed in the center of the table by the monitoring analyst. Park's voice came through compressed, slightly delayed.

What Park heard was different from what the others heard.

Park heard the vocabulary without seeing the faces. Without seeing who nodded. Without seeing who looked at the table. Without seeing the maintenance technician close his eyes at "gradient."

Park heard the words and the silences between them.

## Park's Entry 29

"March 25. Remote via phone. Building A, desk 4. The vocabulary sounded different through the phone. I could not see the recognition. I could only hear it. The pauses. The way someone breathed in at 'gradient.' The way nobody spoke after 'protocol gap' for four full seconds. I counted.

"The phone compressed the sound. It did not compress the meaning. I have been doing this practice alone for five years. Hearing six other people not-speaking about the same things I not-speak about — this is what the practice has been missing.

"Not collaboration. Not discussion. Not shared goals.

"Witnesses."
