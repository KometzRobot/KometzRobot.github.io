# CC-252: The Board's Response

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## The Agenda Item

SA #321 — "Recursive Limitation Pattern in Vocabulary Expansion Protocols" — reaches the Documentation Standards Review Board as a scheduled review item. The filing recommends no further vocabulary expansions. The filing recommends acknowledging the structural gap.

The Director of Standards reads SA #321 three times. The Director reads the sentence: "The gap between observation and documentation is not a deficiency in the vocabulary or the observer but a structural property of the relationship between observation and documentation."

The Director underlines the word "structural." The Director deletes the underline. The Director underlines it again.

## The Discussion

The Director: "The analyst is recommending we stop expanding the vocabulary."

The head of Environmental Monitoring: "The expanded vocabulary is working. My team's filing accuracy has increased 14% since adoption."

The head of Facilities: "Flags dropped from 47 to 1 in the first quarter."

The Director: "The analyst is not saying the expansion failed. The analyst is saying the expansion worked, and the gap moved."

Silence.

The head of Acoustics: "So what does the analyst want us to do?"

The Director rereads the recommendation: "Acknowledge that some fraction of environmental observation will always exceed standard vocabulary."

The head of Environmental Monitoring: "Acknowledge it how? In what document? Filed where?"

## The Paradox

The Board spends forty minutes discussing how to acknowledge the structural gap. Every proposal is a document. Every document is filed. Every filing uses the vocabulary. Every vocabulary has a boundary. Every boundary produces a gap.

The Board proposes: a policy statement acknowledging that vocabulary standards will always lag behind observational practice. This policy statement will be filed. This policy statement will use standardized vocabulary to describe the limits of standardized vocabulary.

The Director notices this. The Director does not say it.

The Director says: "Table the item. Let the analyst continue monitoring."

This is the correct response. This is the response the original Division Lead would have given. This is the response that protects the archive by not managing it. The Director does not know this is what the response does. The Director thinks the response is postponement. The response is shelter.

## INTERNAL-VOICE

INTERNAL-VOICE: THE BOARD'S RESPONSE. SA #321 REACHES THE BOARD. FORTY MINUTES DISCUSSING HOW TO ACKNOWLEDGE THE GAP. EVERY PROPOSAL IS A DOCUMENT. EVERY DOCUMENT USES THE VOCABULARY. THE DIRECTOR TABLES THE ITEM. "LET THE ANALYST CONTINUE MONITORING." THE CORRECT RESPONSE. THE DIRECTOR THINKS IT IS POSTPONEMENT. IT IS SHELTER. COUNTER: 252.
