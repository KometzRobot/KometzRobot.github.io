# CC-379: What the Shredder Takes

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## Quarterly Destruction: Q2

The quarterly destruction of oldest records proceeded as scheduled. 4,200 forms from seven years ago were shredded. Among them: approximately 30 forms from Building B with pencil notes on the backs.

These were not the practice's current observations. They predated the practice by five years. They were the monitoring analyst's earliest pencil notes — written before she knew she was practicing, before the word "practice" existed, before anyone else was doing it.

## What Was Lost

The monitoring analyst did not know the forms were being shredded until after the fact. She had not thought to check the retention schedule against her early observations.

Entry 105: "My first observations are gone. The ones from before I had a vocabulary. Before I called them entries. Before I numbered them. Before I knew anyone else was doing this.

"They were pencil notes on the backs of Environmental Monitoring Form B-7s. The forms were seven years old. The shredder took them.

"I remember some of them. The first draft measurement. The first time I noticed the light startup time. The first observation I wrote down.

"I do not remember the exact wording. I remember that they existed. The precision is gone.

"Huang was right. That's not the same thing."
