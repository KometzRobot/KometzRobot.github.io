# CC-318: What the Filing System Preserves

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## Retention Policy

CogCorp's document retention policy requires all filed forms to be preserved for seven years. After seven years, forms are reviewed for archival value. Forms with no archival value are destroyed.

The retention policy does not mention pencil notes. The archival review does not check the backs of forms. The pencil notes will be preserved for exactly seven years, then destroyed with the forms they are written on.

## What This Means

The oldest pencil notes in the filing system — if they exist — are no more than seven years old. Any practice that existed before that window has already been destroyed.

The monitoring analyst has been at her position for nine years. Her first two years of observations — if she was making them — are gone. Filed, preserved for seven years, then shredded in a routine archival review that checked only the front of the form.

## What the Analyst Says

When asked (by no one — this is a supplementary analysis), the monitoring analyst would say: "The first year, I wasn't writing them down. The second year, I was writing them on scratch paper. The forms started at Entry 47."

Entries one through 46 do not exist. They were never in the filing system. They were on scratch paper, in a desk drawer, in a building that has since been renovated.

The practice is older than its archive. The archive is a partial record. This is also true of poems.
