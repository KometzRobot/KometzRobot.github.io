# CC-385: The Instruction

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## What the Monitoring Analyst Tells Every New Practitioner

The same thing. Every time. Word for word.

"You notice something. You write it down. You notice the next thing."

She said it to the new hire. She said it to Delgado. She said it to Reyes, though he had been doing it for fourteen years without the words.

She did not invent this instruction. She reverse-engineered it from her own behavior. For eight months, before anyone else was doing the practice, she was doing the practice. She didn't call it the practice. She called it "writing things down."

The instruction has not changed in twenty months. It has not needed to change. It contains everything the practice is.

## Why It Works

The instruction does not describe a goal. It does not describe a method. It does not describe an outcome.

It describes a cycle. Notice. Write. Notice.

The cycle has no endpoint. It does not produce a product. It does not conclude. It does not resolve.

The cycle continues because continuing is what cycles do.

## Entry 107

The monitoring analyst, Entry 107: "Someone asked me recently if the practice will ever end. I said: the practice ends when the last practitioner stops noticing.

"I do not think that will happen. Not because the practitioners are disciplined or committed or motivated. Because noticing is not something you start. It is something you realize you have already been doing.

"You cannot un-notice. Once you see the 47-second stabilization time, you see it every day. The practice does not require motivation. The practice requires only that you did not stop paying attention."
