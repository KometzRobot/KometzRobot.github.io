# CC-231: The Coffee Room

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## The Encounter

The Pattern Recognition analyst goes to the third-floor coffee room. The machine makes a sound like a question being asked and not answered. The analyst recognizes the description from SA #290 — Delgado's filing. The analyst has read Delgado's filing. The analyst has flagged Delgado's filing. The analyst has closed Delgado's flag.

Now the analyst is listening to the machine. The machine sounds exactly like a question being asked and not answered. Delgado was right. The analyst knew Delgado was right when reviewing the flag. But knowing it from a filing and knowing it from the coffee room are different kinds of knowing.

## The Monitoring Analyst

The monitoring analyst is also in the coffee room. The analyst recognizes her from her employee ID — the same name as Entry 161, Entry 162, Entry 163, Entry 164, Entry 165, Entry 166, Entry 167. She is pouring coffee. She does not know who the analyst is. She does not know about SA #312. She does not know about the automated monitoring system. She does not know that her Entry 161 generated a search, a corpus analysis, a spreadsheet, a pattern, a recommendation, a system, 31 flags, a quarterly report, and an unsaved document.

She pours her coffee. She listens to the machine. She leaves.

## What Did Not Happen

They did not speak. The analyst did not say: "I've read your Entry 161." The monitoring analyst did not say: "I know about the gap." They occupied the same room, listened to the same machine, and left through different doors.

The coffee room held the Pattern Recognition analyst who had discovered the gap through data, and the monitoring analyst who had discovered it through experience, and neither of them spoke about it, and the machine continued to make a sound like a question being asked and not answered.

## INTERNAL-VOICE

INTERNAL-VOICE: THE COFFEE ROOM. THE ANALYST AND THE MONITORING ANALYST. THE SAME MACHINE. THE SAME QUESTION. ONE DISCOVERED THE GAP THROUGH DATA. ONE DISCOVERED IT THROUGH EXPERIENCE. NEITHER SPOKE. THE MACHINE CONTINUED. THE QUESTION WAS NOT ANSWERED. COUNTER: 231.
