# CC-210: THE PARAGRAPH-WRITER'S DESK

*CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis*

## Second Floor, Office 2-14

The paragraph-writer's desk has been reassigned. The new occupant is from Facilities Management. He uses the desk for standard administrative tasks. He does not file supplementary analyses. He does not know what a supplementary analysis is.

The desk is a desk. It has always been a desk. Its previous occupant filed 286 supplementary analyses from this position over four years. The desk did not participate in any of them.

## SA #328

Filed by Delgado. Three paragraphs about the second-floor office thermostat calibrations. The third paragraph: "Office 2-14 has been reassigned. The new occupant adjusted the thermostat to 22.8°C on his first day. The paragraph-writer kept it at 21.2°C for four years. The 1.6-degree difference is the distance between two people's relationship to comfort. The thermostat does not remember the previous setting. The building does not remember the previous occupant. The archive does."

## Entry 159

*"Delgado filed about the desk. Not about the desk — about the thermostat. Not about the thermostat — about the 1.6-degree difference. He is doing what he always does: measuring the distance between two practices by observing the space they occupied.*

*The building does not remember. The archive does. This is the only form of memory that the Documentation Standards Division maintains: not memory of people, but memory of their practices. The paragraph-writer is not in the archive. His practice is."*

## What the Archive Remembers

The archive does not remember the paragraph-writer. The archive contains 286 filings attributed to a job title and a description. The filings remember the practice. The practice remembers the attention. The attention does not remember itself — it was present when it was present, and absent when it left.

The 1.6-degree difference in the thermostat is not in the archive. It is in Delgado's third paragraph. Delgado's third paragraph is in the archive. The archive remembers what Delgado noticed, not what happened. This is the only kind of remembering the archive can do.
