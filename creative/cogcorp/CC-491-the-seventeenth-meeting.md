# CC-491: The Seventeenth Meeting

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## The Question Returns

The seventeenth meeting. Nineteen people. Torres returns to her question from the eleventh meeting — six meetings and four months later:

"I asked: does the practice need to become something more? We said no. We decided to remain unauthorized. But the practice has become something more anyway. Five registers. Twenty-one practitioners. Two thousand observations. A vocabulary. An archive."

"We decided not to formalize. The practice formalized itself."

The room is quiet. Torres is right. The practice has structure. Meetings, vocabulary, archives, registers, a census, a word list. The practice has every attribute of a program except authorization.

The monitoring analyst: "What's different from a program?"

Torres: "The voluntariness. Every part of this structure emerged because someone chose to do something, not because someone was assigned to do it. The meetings happen because people come. The vocabulary grows because someone notices a new distinction. The archive exists because Okafor files."

Martinez: "If it walks like a program and talks like a program..."

Torres: "It's a practice that looks like a program to an outsider. But to us, it's still a practice. Because we're here by choice."

Word 42: "voluntary structure." Structure that emerges from choice rather than assignment.
