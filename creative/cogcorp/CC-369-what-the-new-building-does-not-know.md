# CC-369: What the New Building Does Not Know

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## 2021

Building E was completed in 2021. Its systems are new. Its corridors are uniform. Its HVAC is centrally controlled and digitally monitored. Its lighting is LED — no flickers. Its doors close on schedule. Its elevators arrive within 3 seconds of the call.

Building E performs to spec.

## What This Means

The other four buildings have diverged from spec over decades of operation. Their doors take longer. Their lights flicker. Their temperatures drift. Their corridors have drafts that no one is responsible for. Their elevators have personalities.

Building E has none of this. Building E is too new to have developed its own behavior. Its systems do what they were designed to do. Its specs and its performance are still the same thing.

## The Maintenance Technician's Observation

Entry 120: "I did a routine check in Building E last week. Everything is perfect. The HVAC maintains temperature within 0.1° of setpoint. The lighting is uniform. The doors are silent and smooth.

"There was nothing to observe. Not because I wasn't paying attention. Because the building has not yet differentiated from its design.

"Give it ten years. Every building differentiates eventually. The materials settle. The systems wear. The building begins to have opinions.

"Building E doesn't have opinions yet. It is still following instructions."
