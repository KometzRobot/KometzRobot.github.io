# CC-359: The Fourth Meeting

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## Conference Room 3B, Thursday, 4:15 PM

Nine people. The seven from last time, plus Delgado's supervisor Reyes and a contract worker from Building D named Huang who heard about the meetings from the maintenance technician.

The monitoring analyst did not announce this meeting. It happened because the technician told Reyes, and Reyes told Delgado he was coming, and the new hire mentioned it to Huang in the elevator.

## What Was Discussed

Reyes introduced the concept of "the building's own spec." He described the loading dock door. 11 seconds. Spec says 8. Two repairs. No change.

Park, via phone: "Every system I monitor has a version of that. The documented behavior and the actual behavior. They diverge over time. I always correct my reports to match the documented behavior. I've never written down the actual behavior. Until now."

Huang: "I'm a contractor. I move between buildings. What I've noticed is that every building has a personality. Not metaphorically. The way the HVAC responds to load, the way the elevators sequence, the way the lights come up in the morning — each building does it differently. And none of that is in any spec."

The monitoring analyst added a 24th word to the vocabulary: **building personality**.

## What Was Not Discussed

Nobody discussed Reyes's position as a manager. Nobody asked whether his presence changed the practice. Nobody asked whether a manager observing the practice constituted supervision.

Reyes wrote Entry 5 that evening: "I attended as a practitioner. Not as a manager. I am not sure the distinction holds, but I am sure it matters."
