# CC-425: Martinez Joins the Meeting

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## The Eighth Meeting

The eighth meeting. Fourteen people. Martinez stands near the door, still in his coveralls.

The monitoring analyst introduces him: "Martinez. Building C. Maintenance. Twenty-six months of observations. Fifty-five pages."

Martinez does not speak for the first twenty minutes. He listens. Torres describes a timing anomaly in the HVAC shutdown sequence. Park notes a filing pattern that suggests a quarterly rhythm in form submissions. Vega reports that the elevator pitch in Building B has shifted by half a step over four weeks.

Martinez: "The elevator cables stretch. Seasonal. Temperature dependent. The pitch will come back when it cools down."

The room goes quiet. Martinez has answered a question the practice has held for three weeks in seven seconds. Because he is maintenance. He knows.

Vega: "How do you know that?"

Martinez: "I've been replacing elevator cables for eleven years. They stretch in heat. It's mechanical, not acoustic. But it sounds acoustic."

Word 31: "sounds acoustic." When a mechanical phenomenon presents as a perceptual one. When the building's body produces symptoms that its nervous system misreads.
