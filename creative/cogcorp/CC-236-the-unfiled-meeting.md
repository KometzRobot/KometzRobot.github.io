# CC-236: The Unfiled Meeting

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## No Record

The meeting lasted forty-seven minutes. No minutes were taken. No report was filed. No supplementary analysis was generated. The analyst scheduled the meeting through their personal calendar, not through the departmental system. There is no institutional record that six filers sat in a conference room and described observations that exceed the archive's capacity.

This is the largest gap in the archive's history. Not because the meeting contained important data — although it did. But because the gap is now collective. Six people chose not to file about the same thing. Previously, the gap was individual: each filer alone with the distance between experience and record. Now the gap is shared, acknowledged, and still unfiled.

## Why the Analyst Did Not File

The analyst considered filing. The analyst opened a new document — the same document format that produced the unsaved "observation is not actionable." The analyst typed the date, the room number, the attendees. Then stopped.

Filing the meeting would convert it into data. The meeting was not data. The meeting was six people discovering that they share something the archive cannot hold. Filing that discovery would demonstrate the very gap it describes — the meeting would be reduced to attendees, timestamps, and quotes stripped of the silence between them.

The analyst closed the document. This time, the analyst did not type four words and delete them. The analyst simply closed the document and left it empty.

## What Exists Instead

Six people who now know that the gap is shared. Six people who will continue to file temperatures, corridor assessments, supplementary analyses, and facilities reports. Six people who will occasionally file divergently, and whose divergent filings will be flagged, and whose flags will be resolved with "no action required."

Nothing has changed in the archive. Everything has changed outside it.
