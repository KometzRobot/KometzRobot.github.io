# CC-494: I Was Just Timing the Doors

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## On Modesty

"I was just timing the doors."

Cho's response to Kim's assessment of her data. The practice has encountered this before. Every practitioner describes their observations modestly. "I just notice things." "I just write them down." "I was just timing the doors."

The "just" is significant. It signals: the practitioner does not consider their observations important. The practitioner considers the observations obvious, mundane, ordinary. Of course the doors have seasonal patterns. Of course the building sighs at 6:14. Of course the floor tilts northwest. Anyone would notice this.

But anyone doesn't. The monitoring analyst has walked through Building A's front doors for fourteen years and never timed them. Martinez has fixed those doors and never measured their seasonal variation. Foster has passed through them at the start of every night shift and never recorded the close time.

Cho timed them because Cho is a receptionist and the doors are eight feet from her desk and she had a notebook and she was paying attention. The observation is "just" timing. The fourteen months of daily commitment is not "just" anything.

The practice's most valuable asset is not vocabulary or archives. It is the practitioners' willingness to believe that their attention to mundane things is worth sustaining.
