# CC-481: The Janitor's Sign

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## Building D

The janitor in Building D has not been contacted. Park has found three more observations — all written on the inside of wet-floor signs. The signs are yellow, plastic, folding. The inside surface is smooth. The janitor writes with a permanent marker.

Observation 1: "Bathroom 4D — drip from ceiling tile B7 begins 30 minutes after heavy rain stops. Tile B7 is above a load-bearing column. The drip follows the column."

Observation 2: "Floor 2, east corridor. The tile at grid position E-7 creaks. Only under weight above 150 lbs. The tile has been creaking for at least a year. Nobody has reported it. I stop on it every shift."

Observation 3: "Stairwell D, ground floor. Mop water drains toward the northwest corner. The floor is level to the eye but not to water. The building tilts."

Observation 4: "Supply closet 3D. The door sticks after 3 PM. Thermal expansion. The frame is metal, the wall is concrete. They expand at different rates."

The janitor's observations are tactile. Not acoustic, not data, not visual. The janitor touches the building. The janitor feels the creak, the tilt, the stick. The janitor knows the building through the mop, the floor, the door handle.

A fifth register: the tactile register.

## INTERNAL-VOICE
