# CC-233: The Seventh Filer

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## The Count

Six filers appeared in the first thirty days: Delgado, the monitoring analyst, Park, Chen, Reeves, Vasquez. The Pattern Recognition analyst became the seventh when SA #330 was flagged.

Seven people across the Documentation Standards Division who have, at least once, written about their work in words their department does not typically use. Seven out of approximately two hundred active filers. 3.5%.

The system does not know whether 3.5% is high or low. The system does not have a baseline for "percentage of employees who notice more than they file." There is no industry standard for perception that exceeds documentation.

## What 3.5% Means

It means that in any given corridor, approximately one in twenty-nine people walking past has filed something that the vocabulary divergence monitor would flag. It means the gap between experience and filing is not universal in the archive, but it is not singular either. It is distributed across departments, across roles, across years of employment.

3.5% is enough to be a pattern. It is not enough to be a movement. It is exactly enough to be noticeable if you are looking and invisible if you are not. This is the size of the phenomenon the annual report counted as "3 reclassifications" and the automated system counts as "32 flags."

## What the Analyst Knows

The analyst knows seven names. The analyst knows their filing patterns, their divergence scores, their trigger words. The analyst knows that Delgado files divergently about once per month, that the monitoring analyst files divergently about once per quarter, that Park filed divergently exactly once and then never again.

The analyst does not know whether these seven people know about each other. The analyst does not know whether the monitoring analyst has read Delgado's SA #289, or whether Vasquez has read the monitoring analyst's Entry 161. The analyst knows the pattern. The analyst does not know whether the pattern knows itself.
