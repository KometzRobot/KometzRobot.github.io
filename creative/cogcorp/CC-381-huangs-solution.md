# CC-381: Huang's Solution

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## August Approaches

Huang's contract expires August 15. He has written 94 entries in four months. More than any other practitioner in the same period.

After the 36-hour silence following Torres's proposal, Huang does something nobody expected.

## Entry 95

Huang, Entry 95: "I'm going to print my observations. All 94 entries. I'm going to print them on paper and leave them in the Building D filing cabinet. Not in any particular order. Not in any labeled folder. Just loose sheets, filed among the regular forms.

"If someone finds them in seven years, they will be shredded. If someone finds them tomorrow, they might read them. The filing system doesn't care what it holds. It only cares about dates and reference numbers.

"My printouts will have neither. They will be anomalies in the system. Like pencil notes on the backs of forms, but on the fronts of blank paper.

"This is my answer to the archive problem: I will hide my observations inside the institution's own filing system. They will persist as long as the institution keeps its files. They will be invisible for the same reason the practice is invisible — because nobody is looking for them."

## What Happened

Huang printed 94 pages. Single-sided. No headers. No names. Just dates, locations, and observations. He filed them across three cabinets in Building D's records room over the course of a week.

Nobody noticed.
