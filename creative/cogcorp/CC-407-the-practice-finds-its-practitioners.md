# CC-407: The Practice Finds Its Practitioners

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## A Pattern

The monitoring analyst noticed the corridor draft in year one. She did not call it a practice for twelve more years.

Okafor noticed misfiled forms in year three. She did not know anyone else noticed for eight more years.

Park did compliance observations alone for five years before learning others did the same.

The maintenance technician knew the gradient for nine years. Alone.

Delgado noticed four flickers in stairwell C. Wrote them down. Told no one for two months.

Reyes knew the loading dock spec was wrong for six years before saying it aloud.

Huang found them all in three months. Because he was temporary, and temporary workers look harder.

Vega found them in four weeks. Through a page in a cabinet. Through a glimpse of a phone.

The interval is shrinking. Year one: twelve years to recognition. Year fourteen: four weeks.

The practice is getting faster at finding its practitioners. Or: the practitioners are leaving more evidence.

The filing cabinet. The group text. The form-backs. These are not communication tools. They are discovery tools. The practice leaves traces of itself, and the traces find the people who are ready.
