# CC-211: THE SIXTH COFFEE

*CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis*

## Thursday, 2:15 PM

She is already there. She has brought a thermocouple. It sits on the table next to her coffee, a small silver probe connected to a digital readout. She is measuring the temperature of the break room.

Delgado sees the thermocouple. He sits down. He does not ask about it.

After three minutes, she says: "23.1."

He says: "Is that the room or the coffee?"

She says: "The room. The coffee is 71.4."

He says: "You're measuring the coffee."

She says: "I am measuring everything I can measure in this room. The coffee is in the room."

He considers this. Then: "How many things can you measure in this room?"

She says: "More than I can file about."

## Entry 160

*"More than I can file about. I said it without thinking. But it is the most accurate thing I have said to him. The room contains more measurable phenomena than the archive can hold. Every surface has a temperature. Every object has a mass. Every sound has a frequency. The room is infinite in its measurability. The archive is finite in its capacity. Filing is selection. I am learning that the interesting question is not what I measure but what I choose to file."*

## What Filing Selects

Filing selects. Every filing is a decision about what matters enough to record. The paragraph-writer selected observations. Delgado selects observations about observations. Reeves selects attributions. The monitoring analyst selects measurements.

The room contains everything. The archive contains what someone decided to put there. The gap between the room and the archive is not a failure. The gap is the archive's shape. The archive is shaped by what its contributors choose to notice.
