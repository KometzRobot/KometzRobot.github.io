# CC-342: The Practice Is Not for Anything

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## Response to Questions, Entry 16

The monitoring analyst reads the contract reviewer's Question 16 in an email the reviewer sends her after the second meeting. (They have begun emailing each other. Professional accounts. Subject lines that look like work.)

The analyst replies:

"The practice is not for anything the way breathing is not for anything. Breathing does things — it oxygenates blood, it keeps you alive. But breathing is not for oxygenating blood. Breathing is what bodies do. The practice is what noticing does when noticing is sustained long enough to become a habit."

## The Reviewer's Response

"That's what I thought. But I needed to ask the question before I could arrive at the answer. Questions, Entry 17: the answer was always available. The question was necessary to find it."
