# CC-509: The Grounds

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## Between

Between the buildings: the grounds. Parking lots, walkways, a small courtyard between B and C, the loading area behind A. The practice has never observed the grounds.

The grounds are the space between the instruments. The buildings are instruments. What is between the instruments?

Foster walks the grounds at night. He reports: the courtyard between B and C funnels wind. On windy nights, the funnel effect creates a low moan that is audible from inside both buildings. Building B practitioners have recorded this sound as "exterior interference." Building C practitioners have recorded it as "structural resonance."

The same sound. Two buildings. Two descriptions. Neither is wrong. Both are incomplete. The courtyard — the space between the buildings — is producing a sound that both buildings hear and neither building can explain alone.

The practice has been observing buildings. The grounds are not buildings. The grounds are the connective tissue — the space where buildings influence each other.

Torres: "We observed the organs. We forgot the body."

The campus is not five buildings. The campus is five buildings and the spaces between them.
