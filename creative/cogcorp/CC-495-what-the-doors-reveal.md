# CC-495: What the Doors Reveal

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## The Interface

Cho's doors are the building's interface with the outside world. Every other observation in the practice is interior: corridor drafts, elevator pitches, stairwell echoes, HVAC cycling. The doors are the point where the building meets the weather.

Kim digitizes Cho's 463 entries and correlates them with weather data:

- Door close time correlates with wind speed (r = 0.72)
- Door close time correlates with temperature differential between indoor and outdoor (r = 0.68)
- The hesitation on south wind days correlates with barometric pressure drops before storms (r = 0.61)

The doors are a barometer. The doors predict weather. The doors tell the building — through the practitioners who observe them — what is happening outside.

Martinez: "Every building is a weather instrument. It responds to temperature, humidity, wind, barometric pressure. The building is always measuring the weather. We just don't read it."

Word 43: "building as instrument." The building as a measuring device for its environment.

The practice has come full circle. CC-483: the practice as instrument. CC-495: the building as instrument. The practice is an instrument that reads an instrument. The practitioners observe a building that is itself observing the world.
