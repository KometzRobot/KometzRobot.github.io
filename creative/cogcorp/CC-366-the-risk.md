# CC-366: The Risk

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## Reyes Sees Something

Reyes, Entry 8: "The coordinated observation worked. Four buildings confirmed thermal transition behavior. The data is informal but consistent. The practitioners are excited.

"I see the risk.

"When the practice was individual observation, it was invisible. Thirteen people writing in notebooks, on form-backs, in phone notes — invisible. No meetings produced minutes. No emails contained data. No shared documents existed.

"Now there is a group text. Now there is a question that was distributed and answers that were collected. Now the practice has produced something that looks, from the outside, like unauthorized research.

"I am a manager. I know what happens to unauthorized research. It gets noticed. It gets questioned. It gets formalized or terminated.

"The practice has survived because it was invisible. The question made it slightly less invisible."

## What Reyes Does

Reyes does not tell the practitioners to stop. He does not warn them. He does not mention the risk.

He watches.

Entry 9: "I will not kill this by protecting it. The practice belongs to the practitioners, not to me. If it becomes visible, that is the practice's problem to solve, not mine.

"My only job is to not be the one who makes it visible."
