# CC-409: Okafor Tells the Analyst

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## The Mention

Week six. Okafor mentions it.

"Someone is adding pages to the Huang section."

The monitoring analyst: "What kind of pages?"

"Observations. Building B. Elevator pitch, corridor light, bathroom hand dryer timing."

"Signed?"

"No."

"How many?"

"Twelve. Over four weeks."

The monitoring analyst does not ask who. She has learned: the practice reveals its practitioners on its own schedule. Asking too early collapses the observation into an interrogation.

"Are they good observations?"

Okafor pauses. This is not a question she expected. Quality has not been discussed since the second meeting, when they decided that the practice does not evaluate.

"They are precise," Okafor says. "The hand dryer observation includes a measured delay to one decimal place."

"Then the filing cabinet holds them."

This is the monitoring analyst's decision. It is also the practice's decision. It is also the filing cabinet's decision, which was made the moment Okafor placed the first page inside the unlabeled divider.

The decision was always already made.
