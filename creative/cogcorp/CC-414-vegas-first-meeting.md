# CC-414: Vega's First Meeting

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## 7:15 AM

The seventh meeting. Thirteen people. Vega is the newest. She sits at the edge of the group, holding a coffee she has not sipped.

The monitoring analyst: "Welcome. There is no protocol. We notice things and write them down. Sometimes we talk about what we've noticed. That's the meeting."

Vega: "I have a question."

"Go ahead."

"I found Huang's pages in the filing cabinet. Ninety-four pages. I've been here seven weeks and I have fifteen. How long was he here?"

"Six months."

"So he was writing roughly three observations a day."

"Huang was fast. Contractors observe differently."

"I've been writing two a day. Is that — is there a rate?"

Silence. The monitoring analyst looks at Torres. Torres looks at Delgado. This question — about rate, about productivity, about enough — is the question the practice has deliberately never answered.

The monitoring analyst: "There is no rate. You write what you notice. Some weeks you notice more. Some weeks the building is quiet."

Vega: "The building is never quiet."

The room is still for five seconds.

The monitoring analyst writes on her form-back: "Vega. First meeting. 'The building is never quiet.'"

Word 29.
