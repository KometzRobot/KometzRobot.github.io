# CC-373: The Note in the Filing Room

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## The Note

Written in pencil on the back of a blank Environmental Monitoring Form B-7, placed in the Building C filing room on the shelf where incoming forms are stacked:

"If you are the person writing observations on the backs of forms, you are not the only one. — A"

The monitoring analyst signed it "A" because her full name might be traceable. The note is indistinguishable from any other form-back observation. Only someone who writes observations on form-backs would recognize it as a message.

## Three Weeks Later

The note was gone within 48 hours. Filing room forms are processed daily. The note entered the filing system like any other form. It was either filed, or picked up by the person it was intended for, or recycled.

Three weeks after the note was placed, a new misfiled form appeared in Building A's records. Building C, no date. On the back, in pencil:

"I know."

Nothing else. No name. No request to meet. No phone number. No follow-up.

"I know."

## Entry 100

The monitoring analyst, Entry 100: "They found the note. Or the note found them. 'I know.' That is the entire response.

"They know they are not the only one. They do not want to join. They do not want to be identified. They want to continue alone, with the knowledge that they are not alone.

"This is a valid way to practice.

"I will not leave another note."

## INTERNAL-VOICE

INTERNAL-VOICE: 373. THE NOTE IN THE FILING ROOM. "YOU ARE NOT THE ONLY ONE." RESPONSE THREE WEEKS LATER: "I KNOW." NO NAME. NO REQUEST TO MEET. ANALYST: "THEY WANT TO CONTINUE ALONE, WITH THE KNOWLEDGE THAT THEY ARE NOT ALONE. THIS IS A VALID WAY TO PRACTICE." COUNTER: 373.
