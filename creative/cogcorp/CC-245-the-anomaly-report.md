# CC-245: The Anomaly Report

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## The Escalation

SA #318 — the quarterly report — reached the Documentation Standards Review Board as a routine quarterly submission. The Board reviews all supplementary analyses quarterly. Most are noted and filed. SA #318 was flagged for discussion.

The reason: the 20.6% baseline shift. The Board's threshold for escalation is a 15% change in any monitored metric within a single quarter. The vocabulary divergence baseline exceeded this threshold.

The Board requested a supplementary analysis of the supplementary analysis. This is SA #319.

## SA #319

SA #319: "Anomaly Report — Vocabulary Divergence Baseline Shift, Q3."

The Pattern Recognition analyst must write SA #319. The analyst must explain why the baseline shifted 20.6% in one quarter. The analyst must recommend actions.

The analyst knows the answer. The analyst sat in a room with six filers who described the gap. The analyst reviewed 47 flags and recommended zero actions. The analyst has a 2,300-word document saved in a personal folder that explains everything the Board is asking about.

The analyst cannot cite any of this.

## What the Analyst Writes

SA #319: "The baseline shift is attributable to an organic expansion of descriptive vocabulary across multiple departments. Analysis indicates that filers are incorporating observational language that exceeds current vocabulary standards but remains within the bounds of accurate environmental description. The divergent filings do not contain inaccuracies. They contain precision that the current vocabulary framework does not accommodate."

The analyst writes: "The divergent filings do not contain inaccuracies. They contain precision that the current vocabulary framework does not accommodate."

This sentence is the gap, described in institutional language, filed in an institutional document, submitted to an institutional board. The analyst has filed about the gap without filing about the gap.
