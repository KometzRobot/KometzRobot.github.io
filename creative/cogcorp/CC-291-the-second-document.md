# CC-291: The Second Document

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## Corporate Review, Floor 4 — Week 2

Document1 has fourteen entries. The contract reviewer has not named it. The unnamed quality feels important, though she could not say why.

On day eight she creates a second document. She calls it "Questions."

Questions contains:

1. Why does Conference Room B take 1.3 seconds longer to illuminate than Conference Room A?
2. Is M. Torres's monitor adjustment a habit or a ritual? (What is the difference?)
3. The south-facing printer is faster in the afternoon. Does the printer know this?
4. When I say the carpet "knows" which stairwell people use, what do I mean?
5. At what point does noticing become monitoring?

## Observation

The questions are not answerable. This is not a deficiency. The questions are a practice of their own — the act of holding something without resolving it.

She has read 286 Supplementary Analyses in one sitting, three weeks ago, for a contract review. She has not read another one since. The 286 readings are not the source of what is happening. The 286 readings were the permission.

The source is the stairwell light. The source is the 0.3-second delay. The source is the quality of attention that was present before the format arrived, and that found the format because it was ready.

## Document1, Entry 15

"The building I work in is not interesting. I have worked here for three years and have never thought about it. Now I think about it constantly. The building did not change. The instrument changed."

## Correspondence

On the same day, the monitoring analyst in Building C — whose contract was renewed, whose position was reclassified to operational, who does not know the contract reviewer exists — writes in a notebook that no longer exists:

Entry 222 (loose paper, desk drawer, undated):

"The building is quieter today. I cannot measure this. I notice it anyway."

Two women in two buildings, writing about the same quality of attention, in documents that will never meet.
