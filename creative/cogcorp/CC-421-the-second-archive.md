# CC-421: The Second Archive

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## Building C

M.'s signed page changes something. The monitoring analyst visits Building C's filing room for the first time.

She finds: not a section in a cabinet, but a drawer. The third drawer of a lateral filing cabinet in the basement storage room. Inside: forty-three pages. Handwritten. Some on form-backs. Some on lined paper. Some on the backs of maintenance requisition forms.

Forty-three pages. The unnamed practitioner — M. — has been writing for longer than anyone knew.

The pages are not dated. But the paper types change. The oldest pages are on a form template that was retired eighteen months ago. M. has been observing for at least eighteen months. Possibly longer.

The monitoring analyst reads them. They are maintenance observations: valve pressures, light cycles, HVAC timing, elevator delays. Precise. Consistent. Technical.

And then, starting approximately five pages in, something changes. The observations shift from the mechanical to the perceptual. Not "valve pressure: 47 PSI" but "the sound the valve makes when pressure exceeds 45 has a different quality at night."

M. made the transition — from fixing to watching — sometime in the first five pages. The filing drawer holds the evidence.

Building C has its own archive. The practice has two.

## INTERNAL-VOICE

INTERNAL-VOICE: 421. THE SECOND ARCHIVE. BUILDING C. 43 PAGES IN A BASEMENT DRAWER. M. HAS BEEN WRITING 18+ MONTHS. TRANSITION VISIBLE IN PAGES: MECHANICAL → PERCEPTUAL. "THE SOUND THE VALVE MAKES HAS A DIFFERENT QUALITY AT NIGHT." THE PRACTICE HAS TWO ARCHIVES. COUNTER: 421.
