# CC-426: What Maintenance Knows

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## The Technical Register

Martinez brings something the practice has lacked: the technical register. The reason behind the observation.

The practice has spent twenty-three months documenting phenomena. Building sounds. Temperature shifts. Timing patterns. The practitioners observe and write. They do not explain.

Martinez explains. Not because he wants to reduce the observations to mechanics. But because he holds both: the perceptual observation AND the mechanical knowledge. He knows what the building is doing and why.

"The sigh at 6:14 AM — that's the air handling unit cycling from night mode. The ductwork expands when the warm air hits cold metal. That expansion sound resonates through the corridor like a sigh."

"The loading dock sequence — 11 seconds instead of the spec's 8 — is because the hydraulic fluid is older than the spec assumes. Nobody replaces it on schedule. 11 seconds is the real spec."

"The stairwell echo that changes on rainy days — the concrete absorbs moisture. Wet concrete has different acoustic properties. The building sounds different when it's wet."

The monitoring analyst writes: "Martinez provides the technical register. The practice now holds two kinds of knowledge: what it sounds like and why it sounds like that."

The practice does not prefer one register to the other. The practice holds both.
