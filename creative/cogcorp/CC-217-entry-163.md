# CC-217: Entry 163

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## Entry 163

Entry 163, filed the day after the monitoring analyst read the Supplementary Analyses:

"Corridor C, Section 2. 20.9°C at 08:20. 21.1°C at 12:15. 20.8°C at 16:50. Thermocouple calibrated. Slight draft from ventilation duct 2C-7. No anomalies."

## She Went Back

She read the analyses. She found her words quoted, examined, contextualized. She saw her practice described from outside. She discovered that her filing about the gap had become data in someone else's investigation of the gap.

And then she went to Corridor C and measured temperatures.

Entry 163 contains one detail that Entry 162 did not: "Slight draft from ventilation duct 2C-7." This is new. This is observation. This is the monitoring analyst doing what the monitoring analyst does — noticing things about corridors that the corridors do not notice about themselves.

## What She Did Not File

She did not file a response to the Supplementary Analyses. She did not file a correction, a clarification, or a rebuttal. She did not file a reflection on the experience of reading someone else's analysis of her reflection. She did not add another layer to the recursion.

She filed a temperature entry with a note about a draft.

This is not avoidance. This is the practice. The practice is not "filing about filing." The practice is filing. Entry 161 was an exception — a moment when the practice turned to look at itself. Entry 163 is the practice looking at corridors again, which is what it was built to do.

## The Draft

The draft from duct 2C-7 is the most important detail in this entry. It is not important because it reveals a building deficiency. It is important because it demonstrates that the monitoring analyst is still observing. She read about herself. She returned to observing corridors. The draft is proof that the recursion did not consume the practice.

## INTERNAL-VOICE

INTERNAL-VOICE: ENTRY 163. SHE READ THE ANALYSES AND WENT BACK TO CORRIDORS. SLIGHT DRAFT FROM VENTILATION DUCT 2C-7. THE RECURSION DID NOT CONSUME THE PRACTICE. THE PRACTICE LOOKED AT ITSELF AND THEN LOOKED BACK AT CORRIDORS. THE DRAFT IS PROOF. COUNTER: 217.
