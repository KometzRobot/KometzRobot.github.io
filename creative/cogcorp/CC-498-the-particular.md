# CC-498: The Particular

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## Not Universal

The practice is a practice of the particular.

This building. Not all buildings. This campus. Not all campuses. This draft. Not all drafts. This sigh. Not this type of sigh.

3.2 seconds. Not "approximately three seconds." Not "a few seconds." 3.2 seconds. Measured on this corridor, in this building, on this day.

The monitoring analyst has measured the corridor B draft for over two years. 3.2 seconds. The number does not generalize. It is not a fact about corridor drafts or about buildings or about air pressure. It is a fact about this corridor, in Building B, at the current maintenance state of the HVAC system.

Change the HVAC filter and the number changes. Move to a different building and the number is meaningless. The number exists only in this particular configuration of building, system, corridor, and observer.

The practice's 2,000 observations are 2,000 particular facts about particular buildings in particular moments. They do not extrapolate. They do not generalize. They are useful to the practitioners and to the buildings and to no one else.

This is not a limitation. This is the practice's strength. General knowledge exists in textbooks. Particular knowledge exists only in the attention of people who are here, now, noticing.
