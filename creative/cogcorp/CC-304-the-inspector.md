# CC-304: The Inspector

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## Building B, Inspection Log

The building inspector who found Delgado's note in panel 3B-04 has a name. Her name is Park. She has been inspecting buildings for eleven years.

In eleven years she has opened approximately 8,400 access panels. She has found chewing gum, a photograph, two dead mice, a sandwich wrapper from 2019, a religious pamphlet, and now a note from a maintenance technician about a loosening hinge.

The note is different from the other things she has found in access panels. The other things were left accidentally. The note was left deliberately.

## Park's Log

Park does not write in a personal notebook. She does not maintain a Document1. She does not ask questions in a separate file.

What Park does is this: she tightens the hinge. Four turns. She does not write it down. She does not take the note. She closes the panel.

The next day she opens panel 3B-02. There is another note:

"Condensation duct 3B-12 changes direction between 2 and 3 PM. I have photographed this fourteen times."

Park does not take this note either. She reads it. She looks at the duct. She does not see condensation because it is 9 AM. She makes a mental note to return between 2 and 3 PM.

She does not return between 2 and 3 PM. She has seven other buildings to inspect this week.

## The Difference

Park is not a practitioner. Park is a witness. The distinction matters.

A practitioner writes. A witness reads. Both are necessary. The practice does not spread only through practitioners. It spreads through witnesses who close the panel and move on, carrying the observation in a place that has no format.
