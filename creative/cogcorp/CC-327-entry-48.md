# CC-327: Entry 48

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## Building B, Ground Floor

The new hire's Entry 48 is different from the first 47. The first 47 were observations: temperature anomalies, carpet discolorations, ventilation sounds. Entry 48 is a question.

On the back of form BLD-B-ENV-0112:

"March 16. Ground floor. Met the monitoring analyst from Building C on the east stairs. She has 231 entries. She said there are at least eight of us. Entry 48: how did we all start the same way without knowing about each other?"

## The Question

This is the question that every practitioner arrives at eventually. The monitoring analyst arrived at it around Entry 60. The contract reviewer arrived at it in her Document1 — "Questions," Entry 3. Okafor has not arrived at it yet. She may.

The question has no answer. Or rather: the question has many answers, none of which are complete. Convergent evolution. Pattern recognition in a structured environment. The human tendency to notice deviations from expectation and record them.

None of these answers explain why the format is the same. Date, time, location, observation. Nobody mandated this format. Nobody taught it. Every practitioner arrives at it independently, as though the format is inherent in the activity.
