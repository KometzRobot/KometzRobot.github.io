# CC-422: M.'s Transition

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## Five Pages

The transition is visible in M.'s pages. The monitoring analyst transcribes the shift:

Page 1: "Fluorescent tube #7, corridor 3C. Flickers at irregular intervals. Replaced gasket. Standard maintenance."

Page 2: "Tube #7 flickering again. 12 days since replacement. Typical failure rate for this model. Ordered replacement batch."

Page 3: "Tube #7. Third replacement this quarter. Flicker interval approximately 3 seconds when active. Note: flicker begins approximately 4 PM. Does not flicker in morning."

Page 4: "Tube #7. Replaced again. But: the 4 PM onset is consistent across all replacements. New tube, old tube, same behavior. The flicker is not in the tube. The flicker is in the circuit. Or the building."

Page 5: "Tube #7. Have not replaced. Counting flicker intervals instead. Day 1: 3.1s, 3.0s, 3.2s, 3.0s. Remarkably consistent. Something is cycling at 3-second intervals in this corridor after 4 PM. What cycles at 3 seconds?"

The monitoring analyst reads page 5 three times. "What cycles at 3 seconds?" is not a maintenance question. It is an observation question. M. has crossed the line.

## INTERNAL-VOICE
