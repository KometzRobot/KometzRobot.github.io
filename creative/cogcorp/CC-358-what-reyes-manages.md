# CC-358: What Reyes Manages

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## Two Kinds of Management

Reyes manages facilities coordination for Buildings B and D. His job produces reports. Maintenance completion rates. Response times. Budget adherence. Inspection compliance.

Now Reyes also manages something else, though "manages" is the wrong word. He is aware that at least four people in his two buildings are writing unofficial observations. He knows because Delgado told him, and because the maintenance technician is one of his direct reports, and because he recognized the practice when he saw it.

He does not manage the practice. He does not supervise it. He does not include it in any report. He does not mention it in any meeting.

What he does: he does not interfere.

## Entry 4

Reyes, Entry 4: "I have spent fourteen years learning the difference between what the reports say and what the buildings do. The reports say the buildings are compliant. The buildings say the reports are incomplete.

"Both are true. The institution measures what it was designed to measure. The practitioners measure what they were not designed to measure but measure anyway.

"My job is to make the first kind of measurement. My practice is to notice the second kind. I do both. They do not conflict. They do not overlap. They occupy different registers.

"I will not bring the practice into the first register. That would kill it."
