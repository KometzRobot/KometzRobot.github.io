# CC-402: The Filing Cabinet Holds

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## What Remains

94 pages. Huang's observations, printed on company paper, filed by Okafor in the cabinets between Facilities Maintenance and General Correspondence.

Nobody has read them all. Okafor filed them. The monitoring analyst knows they exist. Reyes saw one page during a routine check and did not remove it.

The filing cabinet does not distinguish between official and unofficial documents. It holds what is placed inside it. It does not ask why. It does not ask for authorization.

Huang left in August. The observations he made between March and August are in the cabinet. The observations he would have made in September are not anywhere.

The filing cabinet holds what exists. It does not hold what was lost. But the gap — the missing September, the missing October — that gap is also a kind of holding.

The cabinet holds the absence by holding the context that surrounds it.
