# CC-423: The Analyst Visits M.

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## Contact

The monitoring analyst goes to Building C. She waits near the third-floor corridor at 4 PM. The fluorescent tube flickers. She counts: 3.1 seconds. 3.0. 3.1.

At 4:22 PM a man in maintenance coveralls comes through the corridor with a cart. He slows near tube #7. He looks up. He does not stop. He does not write anything down. But his pace changes — slightly slower, slightly more attentive — as he passes under the flickering tube.

The analyst follows at a distance. The man enters the basement storage room. He opens the third drawer of the lateral filing cabinet. He adds a page.

She waits three minutes. Then she enters.

"M.?"

He turns. He is approximately fifty. Gray at the temples. Badge reads: MARTINEZ. He looks at her for four seconds.

"You found the drawer," he says.

"I found the drawer."

"I thought someone would, eventually. The pages accumulate."

"Forty-three pages. At least eighteen months."

"Twenty-six months," Martinez says. "The first twelve are in my locker. I moved them here when the locker got full."

The practice has a name for its unnamed practitioner. Martinez. Building C. Twenty-six months. Fifty-five pages. Alone until now.
