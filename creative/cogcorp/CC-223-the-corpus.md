# CC-223: The Corpus

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## Digitization

Before digitization, the archive was a room. Filing cabinets. Paper. The only way to find a filing was to know where it was — which department, which date range, which drawer. You could not search the archive. You could only navigate it.

Digitization changed what the archive is. It is no longer a collection of individual filings stored in a known order. It is a corpus. A searchable body of text. Every word in every filing is now adjacent to every other word in every other filing through the search index.

Corpus means body.

## What the Body Contains

14,247 filings. 2.1 million words. 847 instances of "observe." 312 instances of "notice." 89 instances of "gap." 23 instances of "experience" within ten words of "file" or "record" or "archive."

These numbers did not exist before digitization. The paper archive contained the same words, but it did not contain the counts. The counts are a property of the corpus, not of the individual filings. No filer wrote "847 instances of observe." The corpus generated that number by being searchable.

## Entry 161 in the Corpus

In the paper archive, Entry 161 was a single page in a drawer in the Environmental Monitoring section. It was surrounded by Entry 160 and Entry 162. Its context was temperatures.

In the corpus, Entry 161 is connected to every filing that shares a word with it. It is connected to SA #289 through "observe." It is connected to the annual report through "reclassified." It is connected to the quarterly review through "non-standard." It is connected to 847 other filings through "observe" alone.

The paper filing had neighbors. The digital filing has a network.

## What the Analyst Sees

The Pattern Recognition analyst does not see a filing in a drawer. The analyst sees a node in a corpus. The analyst sees keyword frequencies, proximity clusters, co-occurrence patterns. The analyst sees Entry 161 as a statistical anomaly — a filing whose vocabulary diverges from its department's baseline by 2.3 standard deviations.

The monitoring analyst wrote about the gap between experience and record. The corpus turned her filing into data about vocabulary divergence. The gap she described is happening to her description.
