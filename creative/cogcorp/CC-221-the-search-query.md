# CC-221: The Search Query

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## The Interface

The archive was digitized three years ago. The search interface is simple: a text field, a date range, a department filter. It returns results ranked by relevance, which means ranked by keyword density, which means ranked by how often a word appears in a filing relative to its length.

Someone in the Pattern Recognition Group typed a word into the search field. The word was "observe."

## 847 Results

The archive contains 847 filings that use the word "observe." This is not surprising. The Documentation Standards Division employs people whose job is to observe things and file reports about them. The word "observe" appears in temperature logs, corridor assessments, equipment inspections, personnel evaluations, and supplementary analyses.

847 results. Sorted by relevance. The filing at the top of the list is not a temperature log.

## Entry 161

Entry 161 appears as result #1. Not because it is the most important filing about observation, but because the word "observe" and its variants appear five times in a filing that is only 73 words long. The keyword density is 6.8%. The next closest filing has a density of 2.1%.

The search algorithm does not know that Entry 161 is about the gap between observation and filing. The search algorithm knows that Entry 161 contains the word "observe" at a frequency that exceeds the corpus average by a factor of 3.2.

## What Search Does

Search finds things you asked for. It does not find things you should have asked for. The Pattern Recognition analyst searched for "observe" and found 847 filings. Entry 161 appeared at the top because of keyword density, not because of significance. The algorithm surfaced it for the wrong reason and the analyst read it for the right one.

The analyst read Entry 161 three times. Then the analyst modified the search query.
