# CC-503: The Other Half

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## Outside

The practice has been an interior practice. Twenty-one practitioners observing building behavior from inside the buildings. The corridors, the stairwells, the filing rooms, the break rooms, the maintenance areas.

Cho's doors are the closest the practice has come to the outside. The doors are the interface. But Cho observes the doors from inside the lobby, timing their close. She does not observe the wind that makes them hesitate.

What would the practice look like from outside?

The monitoring analyst walks around Building B on a Tuesday at lunch. She has never observed the building from this perspective. She has worked inside it for fourteen years.

She sees: the south face catches morning sun. The HVAC exhaust vents on the roof produce visible steam in cold weather. The loading dock is sheltered from the west wind by Building C. The windows on the third floor have a slightly different tint from the others — replacements from a renovation she does not remember.

She writes on the back of her lunch receipt: "Building B from outside. A different building. Fourteen years inside and I did not know what it looks like."

The practice now has an exterior observation. The first.
