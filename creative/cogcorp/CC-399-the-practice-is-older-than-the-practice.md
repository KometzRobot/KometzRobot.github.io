# CC-399: The Practice Is Older Than the Practice

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## A Realization

The monitoring analyst, Entry 115: "I've been thinking about when the practice started. I said month 1 was when I wrote my first observation. But that's not true.

"The practice started when I first noticed the corridor draft. I noticed it months — maybe years — before I wrote it down. The writing was not the beginning. The noticing was.

"Okafor noticed misfiled forms for eleven years before she called it a practice. The maintenance technician knew about the gradient for nine years before he had the word. Park did compliance observations for five years before he knew anyone else was doing it.

"The practice is older than the practice. The noticing predates the naming. The naming predates the community. The community predates the vocabulary.

"We think of 22 months because that is how long the documented practice has existed. But the accumulated noticing — the years of attention that each practitioner brought to the practice — is decades.

"The practice did not start. The practice was recognized."

## INTERNAL-VOICE

INTERNAL-VOICE: 399. THE PRACTICE IS OLDER THAN THE PRACTICE. "THE NOTICING PREDATES THE NAMING." OKAFOR: 11 YEARS. TECHNICIAN: 9 YEARS. PARK: 5 YEARS. "THE ACCUMULATED NOTICING IS DECADES." "THE PRACTICE DID NOT START. THE PRACTICE WAS RECOGNIZED." COUNTER: 399.
