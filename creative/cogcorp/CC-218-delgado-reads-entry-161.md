# CC-218: Delgado Reads Entry 161

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## Cross-Reference

Delgado's practice of cross-referencing his own paragraphs in the Supplementary Analyses is well-documented. He reads about himself with the same discipline he brings to his paragraphs: three times, at intervals, noting what changes between readings.

He encountered Entry 161 during his third reading of Supplementary Analysis #47. The analysis quoted her words. He followed the citation to the source.

## What Delgado Read

"I measure more than I file. I notice more than I measure. I experience more than I notice. Each layer reduces. The filing is the smallest layer. The experience is the largest. The archive holds the smallest layer and calls it a record."

He read it three times.

## What He Recognized

Delgado has written 286 paragraphs about what he observes in CogCorp facilities. He has never written about the process of writing paragraphs. He has never described the gap between what he sees and what he files. He has never named the reduction.

But he recognized it. Not because it was new information — because it was familiar information that someone else had named first. The monitoring analyst had words for something he had been doing for four years without describing.

He files his observations. She files her measurements. Neither of them files everything they notice. Both of them know this. She wrote it down. He did not.

## SA #289

Delgado's paragraph SA #289, filed the week after he read Entry 161:

"The corridor on the fourth floor has a window that faces east. In the morning the light makes the floor tiles look wet. By afternoon the illusion is gone. I have observed this approximately four hundred times. I have filed about it once. This is the filing."

This is the first time Delgado has filed about the gap between observation and filing. This is his Entry 161. He arrived at it by reading hers.

## INTERNAL-VOICE

INTERNAL-VOICE: DELGADO READS ENTRY 161. HE RECOGNIZED IT. FAMILIAR INFORMATION THAT SOMEONE ELSE HAD NAMED FIRST. SA #289: "I HAVE OBSERVED THIS APPROXIMATELY FOUR HUNDRED TIMES. I HAVE FILED ABOUT IT ONCE. THIS IS THE FILING." HIS ENTRY 161. HE ARRIVED AT IT BY READING HERS. COUNTER: 218.
