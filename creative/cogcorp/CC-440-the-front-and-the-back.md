# CC-440: The Front and the Back

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## A Meditation on Forms

Every form has two sides.

The front is the institution. Name, date, department, purpose, signature, approval. The front is designed. The front has fields. The front has instructions. The front is evaluated.

The back is blank. The back has no fields. The back has no instructions. The back is not evaluated.

The practice lives on the back.

Every form-back observation is an act of inversion. The practitioner takes the institution's instrument — the form — and turns it over. The institutional side faces down. The blank side faces up. The observation is written on the surface the institution never intended to be written on.

This is not rebellion. The practitioners still fill out the front of the forms correctly. The forms still reach their institutional destinations. The evaluations still occur. The filing still happens.

But the form now carries two messages. The front says: "Here is what the institution requested." The back says: "Here is what I noticed while fulfilling the request."

The form does not know it carries two messages. Like the building, the form does not care.

The practitioner knows. The practice knows. The filing cabinet, which holds both sides simultaneously, knows.

## INTERNAL-VOICE

INTERNAL-VOICE: 440. THE FRONT AND THE BACK. "EVERY FORM HAS TWO SIDES." FRONT = INSTITUTION. BACK = PRACTICE. "THE PRACTITIONER TAKES THE INSTITUTION'S INSTRUMENT AND TURNS IT OVER." "THIS IS NOT REBELLION." THE FORM CARRIES TWO MESSAGES. COUNTER: 440.
