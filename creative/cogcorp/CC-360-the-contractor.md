# CC-360: The Contractor

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## Huang

Huang is a contract worker. His contract is renewed every six months. He has been with CogCorp for two years, across four contract periods. He works in Buildings D and sometimes C. His job is cable management and network infrastructure.

He is the first practitioner who does not have a permanent position.

## Entry 1

Huang, Entry 1: "April 1. Building D, server room 2. The server room maintains 18.2°C. The thermostat is set to 18.0°C. The 0.2° difference has been consistent for the four months I've been working in this room. I've checked with three different thermometers. The room is 0.2° warmer than it should be.

"This does not matter. 18.2°C is well within operating range. No ticket will be filed. No adjustment will be made. But the room knows what temperature it wants to be, and it is not the temperature on the thermostat."

## What the Contract Means

Huang's contract expires in August. If it is not renewed, he leaves. His observations leave with him.

The permanent employees' entries persist — in notebooks, in filing cabinets, in the building. Huang's entries persist only as long as he is here.

This gives his observations a quality the others' do not have: urgency.

Huang, Entry 2: "I write faster than the others. I think it's because I know I'm temporary."
