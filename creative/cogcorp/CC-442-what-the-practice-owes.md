# CC-442: What the Practice Owes

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## The Debt

Torres's question persists after the meeting. The monitoring analyst writes about it:

"The practice has documented that the loading dock sequence takes 11 seconds, not the specified 8. This is because the hydraulic fluid is old. Martinez knows this. The facilities team does not. If the hydraulic fluid were replaced, the sequence would return to 8 seconds. Energy savings, wear reduction, noise reduction.

"The practice has documented that the HVAC cycling at 6:14 AM causes electromagnetic interference that disrupts wifi in corridor B. Kim knows this. The IT team has been troubleshooting the wifi issue for six months. The answer is in Kim's data exports.

"The practice has documented that fluorescent tube #7 in Building C corridor 3C flickers because something cycles at 3-second intervals after 4 PM — not because the tube is defective. Martinez and Kim could determine the cause in an afternoon. The facilities team has replaced the tube twelve times.

"The practice holds knowledge the institution needs. But sharing it requires explaining how we obtained it. And explaining that requires making the practice visible."

The practice's dilemma: the knowledge is useful, but the method is unauthorized. The building would benefit, but the practitioners might not survive the disclosure.
