# CC-451: Building E's First Practitioner

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## The Corporate Building

Building E. The newest building. Corporate offices. Nobody has been observing it.

Until now.

Kim has been running data analysis on Building E's systems remotely. HVAC logs, network utilization, elevator traffic. She has been treating the data as noise — background patterns with no practitioner to interpret them.

Week two of the data analysis, Kim finds something: Building E's HVAC system cycles 11 seconds earlier than the other four buildings every morning. Not a malfunction. A configuration difference. The newer building has a slightly different startup sequence.

She mentions this at the thirteenth meeting.

Torres: "Building E is doing something different and nobody is there to hear it."

Martinez: "Someone needs to be in Building E."

The monitoring analyst: "We don't recruit."

Martinez: "I'm not suggesting recruitment. I'm suggesting I walk through Building E during my next maintenance round. I already have access. I'll just... listen."

The room considers this. Martinez is proposing to extend the practice to a new building. Not by recruiting a Building E employee. By bringing a practitioner's attention to a building that has none.

The monitoring analyst writes: "Word 36: 'listening round.' When a practitioner visits a building to observe, not to fix."
