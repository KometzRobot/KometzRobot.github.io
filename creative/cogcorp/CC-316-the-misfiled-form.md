# CC-316: The Misfiled Form

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## A Note on Misfiling

Form BLD-C-SUP-0194 was filed in the Building B environmental monitoring drawer. It should have been in the Building C supplementary analysis drawer. The misfiling was routine — approximately 2.3% of forms are misfiled in any given quarter.

The misfiling brought a Building C observation into Building B. Okafor found it.

If the form had been filed correctly, Okafor would not have seen the margin note. She would not have learned that the monitoring analyst numbers her entries. She would not have written Entry Two.

## On Accident

The monitoring analyst's Entry 194 reads: "The old carpet had a wear pattern that told you where people walked. The new carpet tells you nothing. I wrote about it."

This entry, misfiled into Building B, became the bridge between two practitioners who have never met, in buildings they have never visited, doing a practice neither was told to do.

The filing system does not know it built a bridge. The filing error rate is 2.3%. The bridge was an accident of infrastructure.

All of the bridges have been accidents of infrastructure.
