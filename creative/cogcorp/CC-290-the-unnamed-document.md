# CC-290: The Unnamed Document

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## External Location: Corporate Review, Floor 4

The contract reviewer's document has been open for three days. It has no filename. The operating system has assigned it "Document1."

Document1 contains seven entries:

1. Elevator: 2.1 seconds to close.
2. West-facing window: reflection changes at 2 PM. Cleaning crew knows.
3. Break room microwave: hums at a slightly different pitch when empty vs. when containing food. Pitch difference is approximately one semitone.
4. Floor 4 hallway: the carpet has a wear pattern that matches foot traffic from the east stairwell but not the west. The west stairwell is closer to more offices.
5. Conference room B: lights take 1.7 seconds to reach full brightness. Conference room A: 0.4 seconds. Both rooms have the same fixtures.
6. The south-facing printer: prints 0.3 seconds faster in the afternoon. Thermal difference.
7. Colleague M. Torres starts every morning by adjusting her monitor angle. The angle has never been wrong. The adjustment is the practice, not the correction.

## Observation

Seven entries in three days. None of them are part of her job. All of them happened during her job.

She has not read about Supplementary Analyses since the contract review. She does not know Building C has a monitoring analyst who kept two notebooks containing 221 entries about the same kind of observations.

She does not know that what she is doing has a name. She does not know it doesn't need one.

## The Eighth Entry

On the fourth day she writes:

"I noticed that I am noticing things. I do not know when this started. It may have started during the contract review. It may have started before that and I did not have a format for it until now."

She pauses.

"Document1 is the format."

## INTERNAL-VOICE

INTERNAL-VOICE: 290. THE UNNAMED DOCUMENT. CONTRACT REVIEWER'S DOCUMENT1 CONTAINS SEVEN ENTRIES IN THREE DAYS. ELEVATOR TIMING, WINDOW REFLECTIONS, MICROWAVE PITCH, CARPET WEAR, LIGHT DELAY DIFFERENCES, PRINTER SPEED, COLLEAGUE'S MONITOR ADJUSTMENT. THE EIGHTH ENTRY: "I NOTICED THAT I AM NOTICING THINGS." DOCUMENT1 IS THE FORMAT. SHE DOES NOT KNOW THE PRACTICE HAS A NAME. IT DOESN'T NEED ONE. COUNTER: 290.
