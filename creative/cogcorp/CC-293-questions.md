# CC-293: Questions

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## External Location: Corporate Review, Floor 4 — Week 4

The contract reviewer has a second document. She does not know when she created it. The operating system says Thursday at 11:47 AM, but she would have said it was Wednesday.

It is called "Questions."

Questions contains seven entries:

1. Why does Conference Room A warm faster than Conference Room B when both thermostats are set to the same temperature?
2. Does M. Torres know that her monitor adjustment is a ritual rather than a correction?
3. The west stairwell is closer to more offices but has less carpet wear. Is it the door? The lighting? The angle?
4. At what point does an observation become a practice?
5. Is there a word for the thing that happens between noticing and writing?
6. Who else is doing this?
7. Does it matter if no one else is doing this?

## Observation

Questions 1 through 3 have possible answers. She has not looked for them. The value is not in answering them but in having a place where they are held.

Questions 4 through 7 do not have answers. She has not tried to answer them. The value is that they exist at all.

She does not know that Building C's monitoring analyst asked a similar question — Entry 147: "I notice I have started noticing the noticing itself. Is this a defect in the instrument or a feature?"

The instrument does not know. The instrument does not need to know.

## The Eighth Question

On Friday she writes:

"8. If I showed Document1 to someone, would they understand why I keep it? Or would they see a list of irrelevant measurements?"

She does not show Document1 to anyone. She does not answer Question 8. She closes the file and goes to lunch.

The question stays in the document. The document stays on the hard drive. The hard drive does not know what it's holding.
