# CC-184: The Practice

*CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis*

---

Delgado starts filing supplementary analyses in week three.

His first is SA-408: "The fluorescent light in Conference Room B-12 has a 0.3-second flicker every 47 minutes. The flicker is not visible to most people but is audible as a faint click in the ballast. The click is louder on the north side of the room."

He does not tell the monitoring analyst. She reads it on Wednesday morning, the way she reads all supplementary analyses — as part of the archive. She does not recognize his writing. The filing is unsigned, as all supplementary analyses are. She recognizes the precision.

---

SA-409, two days later: "The ventilation system in Building C produces a low-frequency hum that varies between floors. Floor 1: 58 Hz. Floor 2: 61 Hz. Floor 3: 63 Hz. The variation is caused by ductwork length. The difference between floors is not noticeable in isolation but is noticeable when descending stairs quickly."

She replies to the filing. Not through the system — there is no reply mechanism for supplementary analyses. She sends an email. "SA-409 is the first filing that describes a phenomenon you can only notice in motion."

He replies: "I noticed it while testing a sensor on the stairwell. I was moving between floors and the pitch changed. You can't hear it standing still."

---

Okonkwo, entry 63:

"Two people are now filing supplementary analyses who did not file them before. One of them is the monitoring analyst. One is someone in Applied Research who measures environmental sensors. Their filings are different. Hers describe what she notices. His describe what he measures. Both are precise. Hers are shorter."

---

SA-410 is hers. Fourteen words: "The south stairwell door on floor 4 takes 1.7 seconds longer to close than the north."

SA-411 is his. Forty-one words: "The temperature differential between the east and west corridors in Building C averages 2.1 degrees Fahrenheit between 10 AM and 2 PM. The differential reverses after 5 PM. This is caused by solar exposure through the west-facing windows but the reversal is caused by the HVAC schedule, not the sunset."

She does not know he filed SA-411. He does not know she filed SA-410. They are filing in parallel, in the same archive, about the same building, from different instruments.

---

On Friday, he sends her an email that is not about a measurement.

Subject: "Practice."

Body: "I think I'm doing what you do now. Not measuring — that's my job. Noticing. I catch myself counting things I used to walk past. The stairwell door. The ventilation pitch. The way the parking garage echoes differently at 7 AM versus 5 PM. Is this what it's like for you? Or is it different?"

She takes a day.

"It's the same. It's always the same. You notice something once and then you can't stop noticing it. The filing is what happens after. The noticing is what happens before. The practice is the part between noticing and filing, when you're just holding the observation and haven't decided what to do with it yet."

---

Vasquez, file 69:

"Delgado is filing now. His filings are technical. Hers are observational. Together they are building a record of the building that the facilities database does not contain. The facilities database describes what the building is. The supplementary analyses describe what the building does. Neither knows they are collaborating. The archive knows."
