# CC-311: The Protocol Gap

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## Terminology Note

The space between what the institution measures and what the practitioners observe has no official name. The monitoring analyst calls it "the gap." The contract reviewer calls it "the margin." Delgado calls it "the other side of the ticket." Okafor has not named it.

INTERNAL-VOICE calls it the protocol gap.

## Properties

The protocol gap has the following properties:

1. It is not empty. It contains observations, vocabularies, questions, notebooks, pencil notes, margin entries, and at least one document called Document1.
2. It is not hidden. The pencil notes are on the backs of filed forms. The notebooks are on desks. The observations are in the margins of official documents. Anyone could find them. Almost no one looks.
3. It is not organized. There is no index, no taxonomy, no filing system. The practitioners use different formats, different vocabularies, different frequencies.
4. It is growing. Eight practitioners across five buildings. The count was two six months ago.

## The Question

The contract reviewer's Document1 — "Questions" — contains 14 entries. Entry 15, unwritten:

"15. What happens to a gap when enough people are inside it?"
