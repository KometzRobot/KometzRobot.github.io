# CC-203: THE CORRIDOR

*CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis*

## The Second-Floor Corridor, North Side

The corridor runs forty-seven meters from the east stairwell to the west elevator bank. The ceiling height is 2.74 meters. The floor is poured concrete with a polyurethane finish applied in 2019. The walls are drywall, painted CogCorp Standard White (SW 7012, Creamy) in 2020.

The corridor has been the subject of three supplementary analyses measuring the temperature differential between the north and south sides.

## Three Measurements

**SA #286** (the paragraph-writer, final filing): 0.8°C. Measured at varying times over several weeks. No explanation provided. "I leave this observation without explanation because the observation does not require one."

**SA #301** (Reeves): 0.4°C. Measured once, in the afternoon. Attributed to HVAC routing and south-facing window exposure.

**SA #323** (the monitoring analyst): 0.6°C. Measured at 11:00 AM on three consecutive Tuesdays. "Consistent methodology produces a measurement between the two previous observations. The differential exists. Its magnitude depends on when you measure it."

## What the Corridor Knows

The corridor does not know it has been measured three times. The corridor does not know the measurements disagree. The corridor maintains a temperature differential that varies with time of day, season, occupancy, and the mechanical rhythms of the HVAC system. The differential exists whether or not anyone measures it. The measurements are about the observers. The differential is about the building.

## What the Archive Contains

The archive now contains three descriptions of the same corridor. Each description is accurate. Each description reveals the observer more clearly than it reveals the corridor.

The paragraph-writer's description reveals a person who believed observations did not require explanation. Reeves' description reveals a person who believed observations required attribution. The monitoring analyst's description reveals a person who believed observations required consistency.

The corridor does not prefer any of these descriptions. The corridor does not prefer being described.
