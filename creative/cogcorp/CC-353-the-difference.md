# CC-353: The Difference

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## A Question

Delgado, Entry 3: "I measure things all day. Response times. Completion percentages. SLA adherence. Then I go to stairwell C and count flickers. Both are measurements. Both are precise. Both are repeatable.

"The difference: the first measurements go into a system. They are aggregated. They produce metrics. The metrics produce reports. The reports produce actions. The actions are supposed to improve the measurements.

"The flicker count goes nowhere. It produces nothing. It improves nothing. No one reads it except me.

"So why does the flicker count feel more like observation and the SLA report feel more like processing?"

## The Monitoring Analyst's Response

The monitoring analyst, Entry 89: "Delgado asked me this question and I've been thinking about it for two days. Here is my answer:

"Processing serves the institution. Observation serves the observer. Both involve measurement. But processing asks: 'What does this number do?' And observation asks: 'What does this number mean?'

"The SLA report means something to the system. The flicker count means something to Delgado.

"The practice is the space where meaning belongs to the person who noticed, not the system that required the noticing."
