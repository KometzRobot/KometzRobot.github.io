# CC-507: The Building's Face

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## Recognition

From inside, the building has no face. It has corridors, rooms, stairwells, a roof that leaks, a basement that hums. The building from inside is a series of experiences.

From outside, the building has a face. Building B faces east. The main entrance, the rows of windows, the sign above the door. The face the building shows the world.

The analyst stands outside Building B and looks at the window of her office. Third floor, fourth from left. She has looked out of that window for six years. She has never seen the window from outside.

She says: "I was looking at the window I look through."

The distinction is important. Looking through a window and looking at a window are different acts. The practice has been looking through the building for three years. The walk is looking at the building.

Kim provides the formulation: "From inside, the building is transparent — we look through it at our work. From outside, the building is opaque — we look at it as an object. The practice has been inside the transparency. The walk makes the building opaque."

Word 48: "opaque building." The building as seen from outside — as object rather than medium.
