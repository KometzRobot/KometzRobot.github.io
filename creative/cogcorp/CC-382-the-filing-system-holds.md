# CC-382: The Filing System Holds

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## What the Filing System Now Contains

The filing system across five buildings contains approximately 25,200 forms per quarter, processed by three filing clerks including Okafor. The filing system also now contains:

- Approximately 40 form-backs with pencil observations (monitoring analyst, new hire, unnamed practitioner, others)
- 94 loose printed pages from Huang (Building D)
- One note that read "You are not the only one" (status unknown — filed, found, or recycled)
- One response that read "I know" (misfiled into Building A)

The filing system does not distinguish between official forms and unofficial observations. Both are paper. Both are in cabinets. Both will be retained for 7 years. Both will be shredded on the same schedule.

## Okafor's Observation

Okafor, Entry 35: "I have been filing forms for eleven years. I have always known what the filing system contains. Now I know it also contains things I am not supposed to know about.

"Huang's pages are not forms. They don't have reference numbers. They don't have department headers. If I were doing my job strictly, I would remove them as misfiled non-documents.

"I am not doing my job strictly. I am filing them. I am filing them because the filing system does not judge what it holds. The filing system holds."
