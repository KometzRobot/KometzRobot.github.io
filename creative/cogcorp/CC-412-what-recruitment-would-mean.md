# CC-412: What Recruitment Would Mean

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## The Concern

Delgado raises it. Quietly, at the end of the meeting:

"If we tell her, we change what she's doing."

The room waits.

"Right now she is noticing things and writing them down because she notices things and writes them down. If we tell her there are fifteen other people doing the same thing, with a vocabulary and meetings and a group text — she will start noticing things and writing them down because she is part of a group."

Torres: "Is that different?"

Delgado: "I don't know. Maybe not. But when I started, I was alone, and the observations were mine. When I found out about the practice, the observations became ours. Something changed. I don't know if it was better or worse. It was different."

Park: "You're saying recruitment would skip the solitary phase."

Delgado: "I'm saying the solitary phase might be important. Every one of us had it. Years alone with our observations. That might be where the attention gets calibrated."

The monitoring analyst writes: "Open question: does the solitary phase produce different practitioners than the communal phase?"

No one answers. The practice does not have data on this. The practice has only one sample: itself.
