# CC-431: The Building D Practitioner

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## Discovery

Park investigates. Building D, the new building. He has been the only practitioner here for eighteen months. But nine pages in the filing room are not his.

He checks the pages daily for a week. On Wednesday, a tenth page appears:

"Service entrance, Building D. The door seal produces a whistle when wind exceeds 15 mph from the northwest. Duration: as long as the wind maintains direction. Frequency: approximately once per week in winter. The whistle is audible from the service corridor but not from the main lobby."

Park recognizes the observation. He has heard the whistle. He has not written it down. Someone else in Building D has written it down.

He leaves a note next to the page: "I have heard this too. — P."

The next morning, a response: "How long have you been listening? — K."

K. Building D. The practice's seventeenth confirmed practitioner. The conversation begins in the filing room, on the backs of pages, between two people who work in the same building and have been observing the same things without knowing the other existed.
