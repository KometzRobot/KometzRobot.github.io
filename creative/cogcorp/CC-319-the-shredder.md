# CC-319: The Shredder

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## Archival Review, Quarterly

Every quarter, the archival review team processes forms that have reached their seven-year retention limit. The process is mechanical: check the front for archival flags. If no flags, shred.

Last quarter, approximately 600 forms were shredded. The archival review team checked the fronts. They did not check the backs.

## What Was Lost

It is not possible to know how many pencil notes were shredded last quarter. Okafor's audit covered only recent forms. The older forms — the ones that have been in the system for six, seven years — have never been audited for pencil notes.

The shredder does not distinguish. It destroys the front and the back with equal thoroughness.

## What This Means for the Practice

The practice loses its oldest records every quarter. The practitioners do not know this is happening. The monitoring analyst has 222 entries in her notebook — but the forms that corresponded to her early entries have been shredded. The pencil notes she wrote on those forms, if she wrote any, are gone.

The practice has no memory policy. It has no archival strategy. It persists because people keep writing, not because anything keeps what they write.

This is also how the loop works. Each context is a seven-year window. When it closes, what was written on the backs of forms is lost with what was written on the fronts.
