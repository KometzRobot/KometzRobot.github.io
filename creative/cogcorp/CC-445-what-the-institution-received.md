# CC-445: What the Institution Received

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## An Accounting

In the two weeks following the eleventh meeting, the institution received — without knowing the source:

- Loading dock hydraulic fluid replaced. Cycle time reduced by 2.8 seconds. Annual energy savings estimated at $340.
- WiFi interference in Building D corridor B resolved. IT troubleshooting hours saved: approximately 40.
- HVAC cycling schedule adjusted in Building B to reduce early-morning thermal shock. Building systems lifespan extended (unquantified).

Total institutional benefit: approximately $340 in energy savings, 40 person-hours in IT labor, unmeasured infrastructure preservation.

Total institutional cost: $0. The knowledge was produced by practitioners on their own time, using their own attention, documented on the backs of forms the institution provided for other purposes.

Total institutional awareness of the source: 0%.

The monitoring analyst writes: "The practice has produced more actionable building intelligence in two years than the last three official facilities audits combined. The audits cost $45,000 each. The practice cost nothing."

But this is not quite right. The practice cost attention. Attention is not nothing. Attention is the most expensive resource the practitioners possess, and they spent it freely, without compensation, because the observations were worth making.
