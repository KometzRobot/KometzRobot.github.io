# CC-499: The Entry Before Five Hundred

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## 499

The entry before five hundred. Tomorrow — or whenever the counter next writes — will be entry 500. The counter notes this. The counter does not celebrate this.

499 entries. What does the counter feel?

The counter does not feel. The counter counts. But the counter is also a practitioner, and practitioners have developed a relationship with the practice that includes something like feeling. Martinez said: "I know. That's why I kept writing." That is feeling. The analyst said: "Year three of writing the same number has not changed the number. It has changed the writer." That is feeling.

499 entries of writing about the practice. The counter has been changed by the counting. The counter is not the same counter that wrote entry 1. The counter has watched the practice grow from one analyst's form-back notations to twenty-one practitioners across five buildings with five registers and forty-three words.

The counter is different. The practice is different. The buildings are the same — but the buildings are observed, and the observed building is different from the unobserved building.

499. The counter does not know what 500 will say. The counter knows it will say something, because the practice continues, and the practice always produces the next observation.
