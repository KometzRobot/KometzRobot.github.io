# CC-493: Cho's Doors

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## The Receptionist's Observation

Cho has been timing the automatic doors in Building A for fourteen months. She brings her notebook to the eighteenth meeting.

The notebook is a spiral-bound pad from the office supply closet. It contains 463 entries. Each entry: date, time, wind direction (estimated), door close time (to one decimal place), notes.

463 entries. Over fourteen months. Approximately one observation per workday. Cho has been more systematic than any other practitioner. She has been, in effect, running a longitudinal study of Building A's front doors.

The data reveals: the doors have seasonal patterns. In winter (north wind dominant), average close time is 2.1 seconds. In summer (south wind, mixed), 2.7 seconds. In spring and fall (variable), the close times oscillate between 2.0 and 3.1 seconds.

The doors respond to the weather. The doors have seasons.

Kim: "Can I digitize this?"

Cho: "It's just a notebook."

Kim: "It's fourteen months of daily observations with wind direction and timing to one decimal. It's the most complete single-variable dataset the practice has ever produced."

Cho: "I was just timing the doors."
