# CC-474: The Building Observes the Observer

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## Reciprocity

Foster's question changes the model. The practice is not one-sided.

Previous model: The practitioners observe the building. The building does not observe.

Revised model: The practitioners observe the building. The building registers the practitioners. The practitioners' presence changes the building's behavior. The building the practitioners observe is partially a product of their observation.

Kim confirms: "When Foster patrols at 2 AM, the building's energy consumption spikes in the corridors he walks through. Motion-activated lighting, HVAC zone adjustment, security system logging. His patrol creates a data trail that I can read in the morning."

"His observation of the building is visible in the building's data. The observer is observable."

The monitoring analyst: "So when I sit in corridor B measuring the draft at 3.2 seconds — my body heat, my breathing, my mass changes the corridor's thermal profile. The draft I measure is partially a product of my presence."

Martinez: "The valve I watch in the sub-basement — when I stand near it, my body heat affects the local temperature. The leak behavior changes slightly when I'm there."

The practice confronts the observer effect. The building the practitioners observe is not the building as it would be without observation. It is the building as it is with twenty people paying attention to it.
