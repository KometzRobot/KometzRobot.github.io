# CC-295: The Other Building

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## External Location: Corporate Review, Floor 4 — Week 6

Entry 35 in Document1:

"There is a woman in Building C who I have never met. I saw her name in a contract review — the monitoring analyst for Supplementary Analyses. She files her observations in an official format with a designated number. I file mine in Document1.

The difference between us is that her practice has a name and mine doesn't. I do not know if this matters."

## The Discovery

The contract reviewer found the monitoring analyst's name in a quarterly compliance report. The report mentioned Supplementary Analyses as a line item. Budget: discretionary. Status: under review.

She recognized the word "discretionary" because she had changed it to "operational" six weeks ago.

Entry 36: "I changed a word in a spreadsheet and it may have saved a practice I didn't know existed in a building I've never visited. I don't know what to do with this information."

Entry 37: "I do know what to do with it. I'm writing it down."

## Questions, Entry 12

"12. Is there a name for the person who protects something without knowing they're protecting it?"

She does not answer the question. She does not look for the answer. The question is the answer.
