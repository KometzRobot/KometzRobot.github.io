# CC-504: What the Building Looks Like

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## The Exterior

The monitoring analyst returns to Building B's exterior on three consecutive days at different times. She documents:

**Morning (8:15 AM)**: The south face is lit. The exhaust vents steam. The automatic doors cycle rapidly as employees arrive. From outside, the building sounds different — the internal HVAC hum is replaced by the compressor noise on the roof. The building has an external voice the internal practitioners have never heard.

**Midday (12:30 PM)**: The sun has moved. The west face is in shadow. The loading dock is active — trucks, forklifts, the hydraulic sequence Martinez documented as 8.4 seconds. From outside, the loading dock sound is louder, more mechanical, less musical than it sounds from inside the maintenance corridor.

**Evening (5:45 PM)**: The lights are on inside. From outside, the building glows. The windows reveal the corridors the analyst has walked for fourteen years. The corridor draft she measures at 3.2 seconds — from outside, you can see the curtain of warm air escaping through the loading dock, visible as a shimmer in the cold evening air. The draft has a visual component she never knew.

Word 46: "external voice." The building as heard and seen from outside. A different building from the one the internal practice knows.
