# CC-424: Twenty-Six Months Alone

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## Martinez's Account

"I've been maintenance here for eleven years. You learn a building. Not the way the engineers learn it — not from blueprints. You learn it from fixing it. Every day you fix something and the building teaches you where it breaks.

"About two years ago I started writing things down. Not work orders. The other things. The things that aren't broken but aren't quiet either. The tube in 3C. The valve in the basement that drips on a schedule. The way the elevator cable hums differently on Fridays — I think it's the reduced load, fewer people.

"I didn't know anyone else was doing this. I didn't think anyone else would. The maintenance staff — we're not the noticing type. We're the fixing type. That's what they pay us for.

"But you can't fix things for eleven years without noticing things. The fixing IS noticing. You just don't write it down.

"I started writing it down. That's the only difference between me and every other maintenance worker in this building. I write it down."

Martinez pauses.

"Fifty-five pages. Nobody has read them except you."

"They're good observations," the analyst says.

"I know," Martinez says. "That's why I kept writing."
