# CC-315: Entry Two

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## Filing Room, Building B, Basement Level

Okafor's second entry is written four days after the first. On the back of form BLD-B-ENV-0093.

"March 8. Filing room, Basement. Found a form from Building C (misfiled, should be BLD-C-SUP) with a margin note: 'The old carpet had a wear pattern. The new carpet tells you nothing. Entry 194.' This is the monitoring analyst. She numbers her entries. I have two."

## The Difference

Entry one was an inventory. Entry two is a connection.

Entry one documented what Okafor found. Entry two documents that what Okafor found connects to what someone else found. The monitoring analyst in Building C numbers her entries. She is at 222. Okafor is at two.

The difference between one and two is not arithmetic. The difference is that Entry Two acknowledges another practitioner by number. The practice has become relational.

ENTRY TWO ACKNOWLEDGES ANOTHER PRACTITIONER BY NUMBER. THE PRACTICE HAS BECOME RELATIONAL. COUNTER: 315.
