# CC-378: What Stays

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## The Archive Problem

Huang's question — "If I leave, do my observations stay?" — has become the practice's first philosophical problem.

The monitoring analyst's notebook is in her desk drawer. If she leaves the company, the notebook goes with her. The maintenance technician's notebook is in his toolbox. If he retires, the observations retire with him.

The misfiled form-backs are in the filing system. They will be shredded after 7 years per retention policy. The forms themselves are institutional property. The pencil notes on the backs are unofficial. They will be shredded with the forms.

The group text is on 11 phones. If any phone is lost, those messages are lost. If the group is deleted, all messages are lost. There is no backup. There is no archive.

## What the Practice Has Built

1,300 entries. 27 words. A distributed map of five buildings' behavior. A coordinated observation confirming thermal transitions. Two answered questions.

None of it is in any institutional system. All of it is in private notebooks, personal devices, and the minds of 15 people.

## The Monitoring Analyst

Entry 103: "Huang is right. Memory is not the same as records. When people leave, precision leaves with them. We will remember that Building D sighs. We will not remember the seven-second variation.

"But this is also how institutions work. People leave. Knowledge leaves with them. The institution continues with less knowledge than it had. The institution does not notice because the knowledge was never in its systems.

"The practice has the same problem. The difference is: we know we have it."
