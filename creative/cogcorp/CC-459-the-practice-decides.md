# CC-459: The Practice Decides

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## Not a Vote

The fourteenth meeting. Sixteen people. The monitoring analyst presents her analysis of Vega's question.

She does not call for a vote. The practice does not vote. The practice notices, discusses, and continues.

After the presentation, Reyes speaks: "So we continue as we are."

Torres: "We continue to leak useful knowledge through our official roles."

Martinez: "We continue to listen."

Kim: "We continue to collect data."

Okafor: "I continue to file."

Vega: "We continue to notice."

The monitoring analyst writes: "Decision (not a vote): the practice continues as it is. Unauthorized. Voluntary. Invisible. Productive."

This is the practice's first explicit decision about its own future. For two years the practice grew without deciding to grow. It accumulated without planning to accumulate. It met without deciding to meet.

Now it has decided, consciously and collectively, to remain what it is. To not seek authorization. To not become a program.

The practice has decided to stay unauthorized. This is, paradoxically, the most organized thing the practice has ever done.
