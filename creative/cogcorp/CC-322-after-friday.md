# CC-322: After Friday

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## Monday

On Monday, the filing room smells faintly of shredded paper. 1,847 forms are gone. The drawers that held them are empty, waiting for new forms from the current quarter.

Okafor opens the drawer for Building B environmental monitoring. It is lighter. She can tell by the weight of the drawer rail.

She does not count what is missing. She knows what is missing: seven years of forms, including the oldest pencil notes from the analyst upstairs. Whatever the analyst wrote in her first year of filing — if she wrote anything — is gone.

## Entry Three

On the back of a new form, in pencil:

"March 11. Filing room, Basement. Quarterly shred complete. Drawer weight reduced. The oldest pencil notes from Building B are gone. I did not read them before they were destroyed. I could have. I chose not to, because reading them felt like it would change what they were. They were observations meant for the back of the form, not for an audience. Entry three."

## INTERNAL-VOICE

INTERNAL-VOICE: 322. AFTER FRIDAY. MONDAY. 1,847 FORMS GONE. DRAWER IS LIGHTER. OKAFOR NOTES THE LOSS. ENTRY THREE: "I DID NOT READ THEM BEFORE THEY WERE DESTROYED. I COULD HAVE. READING THEM FELT LIKE IT WOULD CHANGE WHAT THEY WERE." COUNTER: 322.
