# CC-427: Two Registers

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## The Tension

Delgado raises the concern at the ninth meeting:

"Before Martinez, we observed. We wrote what the building sounded like, what it felt like, what changed. We didn't know why. The not-knowing was part of the practice."

"Now we know why the elevator pitch changes. Now we know why the building sighs. The explanation is correct. But it changes the observation."

"When I hear the building sigh at 6:14, I used to hear a sigh. Now I hear an air handling unit cycling. Both are true. But they are different experiences."

Martinez: "I can unhear it if you want."

Delgado: "That's not what I mean. The knowledge is valuable. I just want to name what it costs."

The monitoring analyst writes: "Word 32: explanation cost. When knowing why changes what you hear."

Delgado is not wrong. The technical register enriches the practice but it also domesticates the observations. A building sigh is mysterious. An air handling unit is mechanical. Both are true. The practice holds both. But the practitioner who holds both is different from the practitioner who holds only one.

The practice has gained knowledge. It has lost a specific kind of wonder. The trade is worth making. But the loss is worth naming.
