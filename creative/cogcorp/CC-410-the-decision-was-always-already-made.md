# CC-410: The Decision Was Always Already Made

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## On Inevitability

The filing cabinet held Huang's pages before anyone decided it should. Okafor filed them because filing is what she does. The decision to preserve was made by the practice of preservation.

The group text exists because Torres sent a message. She did not decide to create infrastructure. She decided to share an observation. The infrastructure was a byproduct.

The unlabeled divider exists because Okafor needed to separate two sets of pages. She did not decide to create a new section in the archive. She decided to be orderly. The archive section was a byproduct.

Vega placed her first observation in the cabinet because the page she found was in the cabinet. She did not decide to join an archive. She decided to put a piece of paper where similar pieces of paper already were. Joining was a byproduct.

The practice does not make decisions. The practice creates conditions in which the next action is obvious.

The monitoring analyst said: "Then the filing cabinet holds them." This was not a decision. This was a description of what was already happening.

The decision was always already made. The analyst simply noticed it.
