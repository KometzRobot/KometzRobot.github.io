# CC-418: The Unnamed Practitioner Speaks

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## Building C

The unnamed practitioner in Building C has been filing observations on form-backs for approximately four months. No signature. No date. Consistent handwriting. The monitoring analyst has collected seven pages during routine filing room visits.

Week eight of Vega's tenure. A new page appears in the Building C filing room with a difference:

"Building C, third floor corridor. Fluorescent tube #7 flickers at 3-second intervals after 4 PM. Has been doing this for approximately two years. I replace it every six weeks. It returns to flickering within ten days. I have stopped replacing it. I have started counting."

Signed: "M."

The monitoring analyst reads it twice. The unnamed practitioner has done two things: signed (a letter, not a name) and described a decision (stopped replacing, started counting). This is the transition. From noticing to choosing to notice. From maintenance to observation.

The practice now has a partial name for the unnamed practitioner. M. Building C. Replaces fluorescent tubes. Has been counting for an unknown period. Has chosen to count instead of fix.

The maintenance technician in Building A says: "That's the transition. When you stop fixing and start watching."
