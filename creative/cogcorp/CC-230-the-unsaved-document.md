# CC-230: The Unsaved Document

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## What the Analyst Typed

Before closing without saving, the analyst typed four words: "Observation is not actionable."

Four words. Not a recommendation. Not a filing. Not a supplementary analysis. Just a sentence the analyst wrote to no one, in a document that will not exist, about a problem that is not a problem.

The analyst deleted the four words, then closed the document, then opened it again, then closed it again.

## The Analyst's Entry 161

This is the analyst's Entry 161. Not the monitoring analyst's — the Pattern Recognition analyst's. The monitoring analyst had a moment when her practice turned to look at itself. The Pattern Recognition analyst is having the same moment.

The practice of pattern recognition has encountered a pattern it can recognize but cannot act on. The practice turned to look at itself and found: we are monitoring something we cannot change. We are flagging something that is not wrong. We are counting something that does not need counting.

## What the Analyst Files

The analyst does not file the unsaved document. The analyst files a quarterly summary: "Vocabulary divergence monitoring: 31 flags in 90 days, trending upward. All filings reviewed, none requiring action. Recommend continued monitoring pending further guidance."

"Continued monitoring pending further guidance." This is the institutional equivalent of the analyst's unsaved sentence. "Observation is not actionable" becomes "recommend continued monitoring." The meaning is the same. The vocabulary is different. The divergence score of the unsaved document would have been high. The divergence score of the quarterly summary is zero.

The analyst has learned to say the same thing in two vocabularies — the one that gets flagged and the one that doesn't. This is not deception. This is bilingualism. The analyst now speaks both the language of the gap and the language of the institution.
