# CC-380: The Proposal

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## Thursday, After the Fifth Meeting

Torres, the new practitioner from Building D, proposes something. He proposes it in the group text at 8:47 PM.

"We should create a shared document. A spreadsheet or a wiki or something. Everyone puts their observations in one place. Then when someone leaves, the observations stay."

Four people respond within an hour.

The monitoring analyst: "No."

The maintenance technician: "I thought about this. The problem is that a shared document is a system. Systems produce compliance. We'd start writing for the document instead of writing for ourselves."

Okafor: "My entries in the filing system are not mine. The institution owns them. My notebook is mine. A shared document would make my observations institutional property."

Huang: "I understand why you're saying no. But my observations will leave with me in August."

## The Silence After

Torres did not respond. Nobody else responded. The group text was silent for 36 hours — the longest silence since it was created.

Then the new hire posted: "Building B, corridor 3, 2:15 PM. Draft stabilization: 3.2 seconds."

The practice resumed.
