# CC-448: The Plural Pronoun

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## A Shift

Martinez's observation — "from 'because I notice' to 'because we notice'" — generates three weeks of form-back reflections from different practitioners:

Torres: "I still notice alone. I notice in corridors, at my desk, during meetings. But when I write it down, I write it for the group text. I write it knowing someone will read it. The audience changes the writing. Not the content — the intent."

Okafor: "I filed Huang's pages when he was the only one. Now I file everyone's. The filing has not changed. But I know the cabinet holds a collective, not a collection."

Vega: "I was alone for four weeks. The 'we' has been part of my practice for eight weeks. I don't know the difference. I don't know what my solitary practice would have become."

Kim: "The data doesn't change with the pronoun. The HVAC log is the same whether one person reads it or eighteen. But the interpretation changes. When I see a pattern now, I think: who else has noticed this? That question is new."

The monitoring analyst compiles these reflections. She notices: every practitioner describes the same shift differently. The plural pronoun operates on each practitioner according to their instrument.

The practice is the same. The practitioners are changed.
