# CC-508: Five Faces

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## The Campus From Outside

Five buildings. Five walks. Five faces.

Building A faces south. Brick, 1960s construction, institutional. The face says: administration. Cho's automatic doors are visible from the parking lot — the analyst watches one close. 4.1 seconds. Cho's average from inside: 4.2 seconds. The difference is probably nothing. But the analyst cannot stop noticing it.

Building B faces east. Concrete, newer addition. The shimmer from the loading dock is visible at 5:45 PM. The analyst's window is the fourth from the left.

Building C faces north. Martinez's building. The elevator motor housing on the roof is smaller than expected. "I thought it was enormous," Martinez says. "It sounds enormous."

Building D faces west. Kim's building. The server room exhaust is a constant low heat on the west wall. Kim: "That's why the west stairwell is always 2.3 degrees warmer."

Building E faces the river. The newest building. The foundation settles differently than the others — visible in a hairline crack along the east wall that Foster noticed during his walk but none of the daytime practitioners saw.

Five faces. Five orientations. The campus from outside is a different campus from inside.
