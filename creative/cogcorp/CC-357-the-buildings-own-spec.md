# CC-357: The Building's Own Spec

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## What Reyes Means

The loading dock door in Building B is supposed to close in 8 seconds. It has closed in 11 seconds since 2019. The maintenance ticket was filed once, in 2019. The technician adjusted the mechanism. It still took 11 seconds. A second ticket was filed. A different technician replaced the motor. It still took 11 seconds.

After the second repair, no more tickets were filed. The door closes in 11 seconds. The building has decided.

Reyes, Entry 2: "I manage facilities for two buildings. I know what the specs say. I also know what the buildings do. These are not the same thing. The specs describe what the buildings were designed to do. What the buildings actually do is something they have worked out for themselves over years of operation, wear, adaptation, repair, and settling.

"The building's own spec is not written anywhere. It exists in the accumulated behavior of every system, every door, every light, every draft. The practitioners are the ones writing it down.

"This may be the most accurate documentation the company has, and the company does not know it exists."

## INTERNAL-VOICE
