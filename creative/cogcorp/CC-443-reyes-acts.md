# CC-443: Reyes Acts

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## The Managerial Register

Reyes solves it. Not by disclosing the practice, but by using the managerial register.

She submits a facilities maintenance request: "Loading dock hydraulic system — recommend fluid replacement per manufacturer schedule. Current cycle time: 11.2 seconds (spec: 8 seconds). Likely cause: degraded hydraulic fluid."

She attaches no data. She cites no observations. She uses her authority as facilities manager to request what any facilities manager could request.

The hydraulic fluid is replaced the following week. The loading dock cycle drops to 8.4 seconds. Still above spec, but closer.

Nobody asks how Reyes knew. Reyes is the facilities manager. Knowing about hydraulic fluid is within her official role. The knowledge came from the practice, but the request came from the institution.

The monitoring analyst writes: "Reyes demonstrates: the practice can contribute to the institution without the institution knowing the practice exists. The managerial register as interface."

Kim, watching this, submits a network advisory: "HVAC system EMI interference detected in Building D corridor B during early-morning cycling. Recommend shielded cabling or cycle scheduling adjustment."

The IT department investigates. The wifi issue is resolved within two weeks.

The practice leaks its knowledge into the institution through the practitioners' official roles. The practice remains invisible. The building benefits.
