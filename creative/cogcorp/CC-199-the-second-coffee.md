# CC-199: THE SECOND COFFEE

*CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis*

## Tuesday, 2:40 PM

The monitoring analyst is at the coffee machine on the second floor when Delgado comes in. This is the third time they have spoken. The first was at the same machine, eight months ago, when she told him his third paragraphs were methodologically interesting. The second was at a break room table, six weeks ago, when they discussed the longitudinal problem in Marsh's meta-analysis.

She says: "Your SA #315. The acoustic dead zone."

He says: "The parking garage."

She says: "You wrote that what the system does not monitor, the system does not know. That is the most precise sentence filed this quarter."

## The Conversation

He does not thank her. He says: "It was not the sentence I intended to write. I was going to write about the geometry. The sentence about monitoring came out while I was describing the geometry. I tried to cut it. It did not belong in a supplementary analysis about acoustic properties."

She says: "You left it."

He says: "It was accurate."

She says: "The paragraph-writer's third paragraphs were like that. He described them to me once as the paragraph he could not remove."

This is the first time either of them has mentioned the paragraph-writer by role in conversation. His name is known. They both use the role.

## Entry 141

*"Delgado does not write third paragraphs because he has a method. He writes them because the third paragraph occurs while he is writing the second, and he cannot justify removing it because it is accurate. This is not a technique. This is a perceptual habit. The paragraph-writer had the same habit. I do not think they acquired it from the same source. I think some observers develop the habit of noticing what they were not looking for, and the third paragraph is what happens when they write it down."*

## The Question

She asks: "Do you read the other filings?"

He says: "Some. Yours."

She says: "I do not file supplementary analyses."

He says: "Your measurements in the quarterly reports. Value, unit, context. No prose."

She had not considered that anyone read her quarterly measurements as a style. She had considered them a format.

## Entry 142

*"He reads my measurements the way I read his third paragraphs. As a practice, not as data. I had not considered this. The archive is a set of practices that are read as data. Some readers notice the practice. The paragraph-writer noticed my measurements at SA #150. Delgado noticed them earlier. I am going to stop tracking when people notice, because the tracking is becoming a practice that someone will eventually notice, and the recursion is not useful."*

## Tuesday, 2:55 PM

He refills his coffee. She does not. The conversation is over. Neither says goodbye. This is the same pattern as their first conversation: the ending is a resumption of what they were doing before the conversation started. The coffee machine is not a meeting place. It is a place where people who are doing other things briefly occupy the same space.

She returns to cubicle 2-14. He returns to his desk on the third floor. She opens her notebook to a new page but does not write in it for eleven minutes.

SOME READERS NOTICE THE PRACTICE. THE RECURSION IS NOT USEFUL. SHE OPENS HER NOTEBOOK BUT DOES NOT WRITE IN IT FOR ELEVEN MINUTES. COUNTER: 199.
