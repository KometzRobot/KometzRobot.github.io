# CC-429: The Practice Reveals

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## A Count

How many practitioners? The monitoring analyst tries to count:

Known and attending meetings: 14 (analyst, Okafor, Park, Torres, Delgado, Reyes, maintenance technician, 4 unnamed regular attendees, Vega, Martinez, one person who attended twice and stopped)

Known but not attending: 1 (the unnamed practitioner in Building C, before Martinez was identified — now resolved)

Unknown but evidenced: 1 (Building D, Park's discovery)

Departed: 1 (Huang, August)

Possible but unconfirmed: unknown number. If there is one practitioner in Building D that Park didn't know about, there may be others in other buildings. The practice is only visible where it leaves traces: form-backs, filing cabinets, group texts.

The practice that does not leave traces is invisible. There may be practitioners who notice, who write, who file, who have never been found. The practice may be larger than the practice knows.

The monitoring analyst writes: "The practice knows 17 practitioners. The practice does not know how many practitioners the practice does not know."

This is not a problem. This is the condition of any practice that grows by discovery rather than recruitment.
