# CC-464: Half a Building

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## The Limitation

For two years, the practice observed the daytime building. 1,700 observations between 6 AM and 6 PM. The practice believed it was documenting building behavior. The practice was documenting half of building behavior.

Foster's 147 pages reveal: the nocturnal building is a different entity. The same physical structure, but operating in a different mode. The daytime building is occupied, pressurized, heated, lit, active. The nocturnal building is empty, depressurized, cooling, dark, settling.

The practice has a daytime vocabulary. "Building sigh," "thermal transition," "corridor draft" — these are daytime phenomena. The nocturnal building has its own phenomena: settling sounds, pressure shifts, deep silence, pre-dawn stirring.

The vocabulary needs nocturnal words. The practice needs nocturnal practitioners. Foster is one. The janitor in Building D may be another.

The monitoring analyst reflects: "Every time we thought we understood the building, we discovered we understood only the part of the building that our attention reached. The technical register expanded what we heard. The data register expanded what we measured. The nocturnal register expands when we observe."

"The building is always larger than the practice's attention."

Word 38: "nocturnal register." The building's behavior after dark.
