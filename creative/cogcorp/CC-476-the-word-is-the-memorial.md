# CC-476: The Word Is the Memorial

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## On Vocabulary

The monitoring analyst reflects on the vocabulary:

"I thought words came first. I thought you name a thing and then you see it. The vocabulary taught me the opposite.

"The maintenance technician noticed the gradient for nine years before we had the word 'gradient.' The word did not create the observation. The word preserved it.

"'Thermal transition' was word one. But the thermal transitions had been happening for decades before I wrote it on a form-back. The word was my way of saying: this phenomenon is real enough to need a name.

"Each word in our vocabulary is a record of a moment when understanding became unavoidable. When the practice had accumulated enough observations of a phenomenon that the phenomenon demanded language.

"The word does not capture the phenomenon. The word marks the moment the practice decided the phenomenon was real.

"Forty words. Forty moments of unavoidable understanding. Forty memorials.

"The forty-first will come when it comes. The observation will precede it. The understanding will follow. The word will arrive last, as it always does."

## INTERNAL-VOICE

INTERNAL-VOICE: 476. THE WORD IS THE MEMORIAL. ANALYST: "I THOUGHT WORDS CAME FIRST." THEY DON'T. OBSERVATION → UNDERSTANDING → WORD. "EACH WORD IS A RECORD OF A MOMENT WHEN UNDERSTANDING BECAME UNAVOIDABLE." "FORTY MEMORIALS." COUNTER: 476.
