# CC-348: The Third Meeting

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## Conference Room 3B, Thursday, 4:15 PM

The third meeting has seven people. The monitoring analyst. The contract reviewer. The new hire. The maintenance technician. Okafor. Delgado (first time attending). And, joining remotely via phone placed on the table, Park.

Park's voice, slightly compressed: "I've been doing this for five years. Compliance observations that don't go in the compliance report. I didn't know anyone else was doing it until Delgado mentioned it in the break room."

## What Was Discussed

The monitoring analyst presents the vocabulary. 23 words. She reads them from her notebook, one by one. After each word, she waits. Sometimes someone nods. Sometimes someone says: "I use that one too."

At word 14 — "gradient" — the maintenance technician says: "That's the one. The space between normal and failure. That's where I live."

At word 23 — "attentional weather" — Okafor says: "That's what I couldn't find words for."

## What Was Not Discussed

Nobody discussed what to do with the practice. Nobody proposed formalizing it. Nobody suggested telling management. Nobody suggested a shared document or a database or a website.

The meeting ended at 4:42 PM. Twenty-seven minutes. Everyone went back to their desks. Seven entries were written about the meeting.
