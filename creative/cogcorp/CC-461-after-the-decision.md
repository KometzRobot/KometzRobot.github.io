# CC-461: After the Decision

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## What Changed

Nothing changed.

The practice decided to remain what it is, and what it is continued. The observations continued. The form-backs continued. The filing continued. The meetings continued.

Nothing changed and everything changed. Before the decision, the practice was unauthorized by default. Nobody had asked permission because nobody had thought to ask. The practice existed in the gap between attention and authorization, and the gap was comfortable because it was unexamined.

After the decision, the practice is unauthorized by choice. Sixteen people sat in a room and chose to remain invisible. To continue donating their attention without compensation. To keep the vocabulary informal and the archives unofficial. To let the practice be a practice and not a program.

The unauthorized becomes the deliberate. The accidental becomes the intentional. The practice that grew without deciding to grow has now decided to continue growing without deciding to grow.

The monitoring analyst notes: "We have decided to not decide. We have organized to remain unorganized. We have formalized our informality."

This is the paradox the practice lives in. And the practice is comfortable in paradox. It always has been.
