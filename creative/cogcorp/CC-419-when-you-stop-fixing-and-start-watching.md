# CC-419: When You Stop Fixing and Start Watching

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## The Transition

The maintenance technician in Building A describes it:

"There was a valve in the sub-basement. Pressure relief valve, B-wing, north corridor. It leaked. Every spring. Not enough to cause damage. Enough to leave a mark on the concrete.

"For three years I fixed it. Every March. Replaced the gasket. Filed the work order. Done.

"Year four I noticed: the mark on the concrete was always the same shape. Same size. Same position. The valve leaked identically each time. The gasket degraded at the same rate. The leak started on the same week.

"I stopped fixing it. For one cycle. I watched. I measured the mark each day. I timed the drip. I documented the pattern.

"Then I fixed it. But the fixing was different. I was not repairing a malfunction. I was interrupting a pattern I had documented.

"That's when I became a practitioner. Not when I noticed the valve. When I chose to watch instead of fix. When I let the pattern complete one cycle while I documented it.

"The building taught me something that week. But I had to stop fixing to hear it."
