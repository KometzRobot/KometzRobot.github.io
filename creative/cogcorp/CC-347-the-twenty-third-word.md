# CC-347: The Twenty-Third Word

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## Vocabulary Update

Three weeks after Okafor's Entry 9, the monitoring analyst uses a new word in an email to the contract reviewer:

"The new hire asked me something today. She asked: 'Do you ever notice that you notice differently on different days?' I said yes. She said: 'What do you call that?' I didn't have a word for it. I said: 'Attentional weather.' She wrote it down."

## Word 23: Attentional Weather

**attentional weather** — the variation in the quality and direction of attention across time, independent of external conditions. The practice produces different observations on different days not because the environment changes, but because the instrument changes. Attentional weather is the instrument's report on itself.

Contributed by the monitoring analyst, named in conversation with the new hire.

First use in an entry: the new hire's Entry 52: "March 25. Ground floor. Attentional weather: overcast. Noticed fewer things today. The draft is still at 3:15 but I did not time it. I noticed that I did not notice. This is also an observation."
