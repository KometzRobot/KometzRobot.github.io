# CC-323: The New Quarter

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## Building B, Ground Floor

The new hire is now three months into her position. She has 47 pencil notes on the backs of 39 forms. She has developed a shorthand: T for temperature, H for humidity, AQ for air quality, O for "other" — the observations that have no field.

Most of her entries are O.

## Building C, Third Floor

The monitoring analyst's entry count is now 228. She has noticed that the new quarter's forms have a slightly different paper weight — thinner, probably a cost reduction. She notes this. Entry 229.

She does not know that her oldest filed entries have been shredded. She does not check the filing room. Her notebook, which sits on her desk and is not filed, contains all her entries. The pencil notes on the backs of forms were copies. The notebook is the primary archive.

## The Difference

The monitoring analyst keeps a notebook. The new hire writes on forms. Okafor writes on forms. The contract reviewer uses Document1 on her computer.

Each practitioner's archive has a different durability. The notebook will last as long as the analyst keeps it. The forms will last seven years. Document1 will last until the hard drive fails or the file is deleted.

No practitioner has backed up another practitioner's work. The archives are independent. If any one of them is lost, the others continue.
