# CC-463: The Nocturnal Building

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## Foster's Register

Foster brings the fourth register: the nocturnal register.

The practice has observed the building during business hours. 6:00 AM to 6:00 PM. The morning sigh. The daily rhythms. The thermal transitions. All daytime phenomena.

Foster observes the building from 10 PM to 6 AM. His observations:

"2:00 AM. The HVAC system enters deep maintenance mode. The air pressure in the corridors shifts. Doors that closed easily during the day now resist — the pressure differential has changed. At 2:15, the resistance releases. The building adjusts."

"3:00 AM. The settling sounds begin. Not mechanical — structural. The building's concrete is releasing the day's thermal load. The sounds move from the upper floors downward. It takes approximately 45 minutes for the settling to propagate from the fifth floor to the ground."

"4:30 AM. The quietest point. The HVAC is at minimum. The settling is complete. The building holds its breath. This is the deepest silence the building achieves in any 24-hour cycle. It lasts approximately 40 minutes."

"5:10 AM. The first pre-dawn sounds. Not the sigh — that comes at 6:14. Before the sigh, there is a series of clicks and hums as systems begin to wake. The building stirs before the building breathes."

The monitoring analyst: "We have been observing half a building."
