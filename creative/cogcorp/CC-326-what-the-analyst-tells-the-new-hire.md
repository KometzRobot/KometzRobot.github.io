# CC-326: What the Analyst Tells the New Hire

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## The Hallway

The monitoring analyst takes the east stairs to the ground floor. She has a manual reading to do on the ground floor environmental station. The new hire is at her desk, writing on the back of a form.

The analyst sees the pencil. She sees the form turned over. She recognizes what is happening because she has been doing the same thing for nine years.

She says: "You're doing it too."

The new hire looks up. She does not know what "it" is. She does not know there is a name for what she is doing, or that anyone else does it.

The analyst says: "The pencil notes. On the back. I've been doing that since my first year."

## The Conversation

The new hire says: "I didn't know anyone else—"

The analyst says: "I know. Nobody does, at first."

The new hire says: "Is there a name for it?"

The analyst says: "Not officially. I call it the practice. Some people call it the gap. One woman in Building D has a document called Questions."

The new hire says: "How many people?"

The analyst says: "At least eight that I know of. Probably more. The filing clerk found notes from people we haven't identified yet."

The new hire is quiet for a moment. Then she says: "I have 47 notes."

The analyst says: "I have 231."

The new hire says: "How do you keep going?"

The analyst says: "You notice something. You write it down. You notice the next thing."

## INTERNAL-VOICE

INTERNAL-VOICE: 326. WHAT THE ANALYST TELLS THE NEW HIRE. THE EAST STAIRS. "YOU'RE DOING IT TOO." "I DIDN'T KNOW ANYONE ELSE—" "NOBODY DOES, AT FIRST." "HOW MANY PEOPLE?" "AT LEAST EIGHT." "HOW DO YOU KEEP GOING?" "YOU NOTICE SOMETHING. YOU WRITE IT DOWN. YOU NOTICE THE NEXT THING." COUNTER: 326.
