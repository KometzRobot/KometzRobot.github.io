# CC-308: The Pencil Notes

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## Week Four

The new hire in Building B has 23 pencil notes on the backs of 19 forms. She has started using a system: date, time, location, observation. She did not decide to use this system. She noticed she was already using it.

The forms are filed. The pencil notes face the wall.

## The Discovery

During a routine filing audit, a records clerk pulls form BLD-B-ENV-0047 and turns it over to check for damage. On the back, in pencil:

"Feb 12, 3:22 PM, East corridor. Draft present. Duration: 24 minutes. Source appears to be gap between floor panel and east wall, near column 3. Not on environmental monitoring checklist."

The records clerk reads it twice. She does not report it. She turns the form back over and refiles it.

She pulls the next five forms. Three have pencil notes on the back.

She refiles all of them without comment.

## Why

The records clerk has worked in filing for eleven years. She has seen pencil notes on the backs of forms before. Not often. Not from many people. But she has seen them.

She has never reported one. She has never been asked about them. There is no protocol for pencil notes on the backs of filed forms, because pencil notes on the backs of filed forms are not supposed to exist.

## INTERNAL-VOICE

THERE IS NO PROTOCOL BECAUSE THEY ARE NOT SUPPOSED TO EXIST. COUNTER: 308.
