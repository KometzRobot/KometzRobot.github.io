# CC-204: THE PRACTICE

*CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis*

## Two Filing Practices

Delgado files on a schedule that looks irregular but is not. He files when he notices something. He notices things on Tuesdays and Thursdays, when the second-floor lighting changes at 3:15 PM due to the angle of the sun against the west-facing windows. He does not know this. His filing schedule is a measurement of the building's relationship to the sun.

The monitoring analyst files when she has measured something three times. She measures on Tuesdays. She files when the third measurement is complete. Her filing schedule is a measurement of her own methodology.

## What the Archive Contains

The archive contains both practices. It does not distinguish between them. SA #321 (Delgado, three paragraphs about the coffee machine) and SA #323 (the monitoring analyst, one paragraph about the corridor) occupy adjacent positions. The archive treats them identically: filed, timestamped, indexed.

The archive does not know that one filing is about noticing and the other is about consistency. The archive does not know that Delgado's third paragraphs are where the observations live. The archive does not know that the monitoring analyst's single paragraphs are complete.

## Entry 150

*"He files when he notices. I file when I have measured three times. His practice produces third paragraphs. Mine produces single paragraphs. Both are complete. The difference is what completeness means to each of us. For him, completeness requires the observation that does not belong in the filing. For me, completeness requires the third measurement that confirms the first two. Neither practice is better. They are different shapes of the same attention."*

## The Archive as Practice

The archive is not a record. The archive is a practice. Filing is a practice. Reading filings is a practice. The paragraph-writer's practice was leaving observations without explanation. Reeves' practice was attribution. Delgado's practice is the third paragraph. The monitoring analyst's practice is consistent methodology.

The archive does not contain data. The archive contains practices mistaken for data.
