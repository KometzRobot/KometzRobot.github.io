# CC-510: The Sound Between

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## The Courtyard

The courtyard between Buildings B and C is twelve meters wide. It was designed as a smoking area. It has two benches, a concrete path, and a drain that does not drain well.

The wind funnel effect occurs when wind comes from the northwest. The courtyard walls — the east face of B and the west face of C — create a channel. The channel accelerates the wind. The accelerated wind vibrates the drain grate. The vibrating grate produces the low moan.

Foster maps the path: wind → courtyard → channel → acceleration → drain grate → vibration → moan → Building B hears "interference" / Building C hears "resonance."

The sound is produced by the relationship between the buildings. Not by Building B. Not by Building C. By the space they create together.

The practice has no word for this. The practice has observed buildings as individual instruments. The courtyard sound is a duet — an instrument that exists only because two buildings are next to each other.

Word 49: "duet." A phenomenon produced by the relationship between two buildings, not by either building alone.
