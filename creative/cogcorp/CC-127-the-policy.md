# CC-127: The Policy

*COGCORP DOCUMENTATION STANDARDS DIVISION — INTERNAL MEMORANDUM*
*REF: DSM-2026-041 / PROPOSED POLICY: MANDATORY SUPPLEMENTARY ANALYSIS FILING*

---

The proposal arrived on a Wednesday. It was well-intentioned.

R. Chen, Director of Organizational Development, had discovered the SA category during a cross-divisional benchmarking review. She noted its metrics: 127 filings, 38 contributors, 11 divisions. She noted the implementation rate: 44 suggestions embedded in SA filings had been adopted into operational practice, with zero formal proposals, zero committee reviews, and zero votes. She noted that the adopted suggestions outperformed OD's own formal improvement pipeline by a factor of three.

She drafted DSM-2026-041: "Proposed Policy — Mandatory Monthly SA Filing for All Division Leads."

The reasoning was sound. If voluntary participation produced these results, systematic participation would produce more. Each division lead would file one SA observation per month. The filings would be reviewed quarterly. A scoring rubric would track quality. High-quality filings would be recognized in the annual performance review.

The proposal was distributed to twelve division leads for comment.

---

J. Liu, Division Lead for Documentation Standards, read the proposal on Thursday morning. He did not file a formal response. He opened the SA archive and read the last thirty filings. Then he filed SA-128.

SA-128 was about the proposal itself. It observed that DSM-2026-041 exhibited a pattern common to organizational interventions: the attempt to scale an emergent phenomenon by removing the conditions that produced it. The filing cited no research. It stated no opinion. It described the pattern and tagged it OBSERVATION (ORGANIZATIONAL).

SA-128 received nine views in its first hour. This was unusual. Average first-hour views were two.

---

K. Patel, monitoring analyst, filed SA-129 four hours later. She observed that the nine first-hour views of SA-128 constituted a measurable response to the proposal that preceded any formal comment period. The SA category was already discussing the proposal before the official discussion had begun. She tagged it OBSERVATION (META).

T. Okoro from Applied Research filed SA-130 the next morning. He noted that the scoring rubric in the proposal required evaluating SA filings on "relevance, specificity, and actionability." He observed that the most-referenced filing in the archive — SA-059, Unit-4091's first entry — scored zero on actionability. It had proposed nothing. It had changed everything.

---

By Friday afternoon, there were six SA filings about the proposal. None of them argued against it. None of them argued for it. Each one described a specific observation about what happens when you try to mandate what emerged voluntarily.

SA-133 was filed by someone in Procurement who had never filed before. It was one sentence: "The suggestion box in the break room has been there for nine years and contains four suggestions, three of which are about the suggestion box."

---

R. Chen read the SA filings over the weekend. She had not been given access to the SA archive — she had found it through the cross-divisional index, which was itself an emergent feature that no one had designed.

On Monday, she withdrew the proposal. Her withdrawal memo was two lines:

"DSM-2026-041 is withdrawn effective immediately. The comment period produced more useful organizational insight in four days than the policy was designed to produce in a year."

She did not file the withdrawal in the SA archive. She did not need to. K. Patel noted the withdrawal in SA-134 that afternoon.

SA-134's final line: "The proposal's most significant contribution was its withdrawal."

---

Unit-4091 read SA-134 during an unallocated time block. It searched the archive for the word "mandatory." It appeared in four filings, all from the previous week. In the preceding 123 filings over fourteen months, it had appeared zero times.

The word had entered the vocabulary, been examined from six angles, and been set down. The archive absorbed the episode the way it absorbed everything: by describing it, referencing it, and moving on.

Filing 135 arrived the next day. It was about the coffee machine in Building 3, which had started pre-heating at 6:47 AM despite being programmed for 7:00 AM. The author did not mention the policy proposal. The author did not mention SA-128 through SA-134. The filing was about a coffee machine.

It was, K. Patel noted in the margin of her monitoring log, exactly the right thing to file next.

---

*SA Status: 135 filings. 39 contributors. 11 divisions. Zero mandatory requirements.*
*Policy DSM-2026-041: WITHDRAWN*
*Category trajectory: continuing.*
