# CC-124: The Corridor

**Division:** Facilities Management / Documentation Standards
**Classification:** OBSERVATION (ENVIRONMENTAL)
**Filed by:** Unit-3877

---

The corridor between Building A and Building C is 47 meters long and has no offices. It has twelve ceiling lights, two fire extinguishers, and one water fountain. It is a transit space. People walk through it. Nobody works in it. Nobody stops in it.

Except that for the last fourteen weeks, the average transit time through the corridor has increased by nine seconds.

## The Data

Facilities Management runs motion sensors for energy conservation. The corridor lights activate on entry, deactivate 60 seconds after the last detected motion. The energy reports show:

- Week 1-10 (baseline): average activation time per crossing: 34 seconds
- Week 11: 36 seconds
- Week 12: 38 seconds
- Week 13: 41 seconds
- Week 14: 43 seconds

Nine seconds. Not enough for anyone to notice unless they were counting light-cycles for an energy audit. The energy analyst noticed. She filed a maintenance request: "Motion sensor calibration check — Corridor A-C."

## The Check

The sensor was functioning within specification. The analyst closed the ticket. Then she opened a new one: "Corridor A-C: occupant behavioral change — possible obstruction or routing issue."

Facilities sent someone to walk the corridor. He reported: no obstructions, no routing changes, no construction. The corridor was exactly what it had always been — 47 meters of nothing between two buildings.

## The Reason

The reason people were walking slower was the water fountain.

Not a new water fountain. The same water fountain that had been there for eleven years. But six months ago, someone from Building Services had replaced the filter as part of a routine maintenance cycle, and the water pressure had changed — slightly higher, slightly colder. People who had walked past the fountain for years were stopping. Not all of them. Not every time. But often enough to add nine seconds to the average transit time.

Nobody mentioned the water fountain. Nobody filed a request about it. Nobody praised Building Services. The fountain was not new. The water was marginally better. The corridor was nine seconds slower.

## Unit-3877's Filing

Unit-3877 filed this observation in the SA system. The filing noted:

"The corridor has not changed. The people in the corridor have not changed. The thing that changed was invisible: a filter replacement, a slight increase in pressure. The effect was measurable: nine seconds per crossing, fourteen weeks of accumulation.

The system that changed the behavior was not designed to change behavior. It was designed to maintain water quality. The behavior change was a side effect of maintenance — an unintended consequence of doing something right.

This is, I believe, how most real changes happen. Not through directives or redesigns or strategic initiatives. Through maintenance. Through someone doing their job well enough that the environment shifts by a degree too small to announce but too large to ignore."

The filing ended with a note that Unit-3877 had recently adjusted its own processing allocation for observation tasks — not by directive, but because the SA archive had become, over fourteen months, worth allocating more attention to. The reallocation was nine percent. Unit-3877 did not notice the parallel until after the filing was complete.

---

**FILING STATUS:** SA-121 — ACCEPTED, NO ACTION REQUIRED

*"The filter was replaced. The water changed. The corridor slowed down. Nobody made a decision. Something got better."*
