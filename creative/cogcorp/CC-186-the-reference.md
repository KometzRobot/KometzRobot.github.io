# CC-186: The Reference

*CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis*

---

On a Tuesday, a pipe bursts on the third floor of Building C.

Facilities responds within eleven minutes. They consult the building database: pipe installed 2019, copper, 3/4-inch, last inspected eight months ago. The database says the pipe should not have failed. The database has no explanation.

The maintenance lead files a report: "Cause unknown. Recommend full inspection of third-floor plumbing."

---

Okonkwo reads the report that afternoon.

sa-notes.txt, entry 68: "The maintenance report says the pipe failure is unexplained. But supplementary analysis #91 — filed six weeks ago — notes that the temperature in the east corridor of the third floor drops 3.2 degrees between 4 AM and 6 AM. SA #91 attributes this to the HVAC schedule. SA #104 notes that the same corridor has condensation on the interior walls during this window. SA #119 — filed by a different contributor — observes that the condensation is heaviest near the ceiling junction where the pipe runs."

---

He does not file a formal correction.

He sends the maintenance lead an email. Three sentences. He includes the SA numbers.

---

The maintenance lead replies in forty minutes.

"Where did you find these? They're not in the database."

Okonkwo does not explain. He forwards the three supplementary analyses.

---

Entry 69:

"The maintenance lead used the supplementary analyses to identify the probable cause: chronic condensation stress on the pipe joint, caused by the HVAC cycle creating a recurring temperature differential. The official database had the pipe's specifications but not its environment. The supplementary analyses had what the database didn't — not what the pipe is, but what happens around it.

"The second record has been cited. Not formally. Not in any system. One person emailed another person three numbers. But a facilities problem that the official database could not explain was explained by observations that nobody assigned."

---

Vasquez, file 72:

"The pipe burst is fixed. The maintenance lead has requested access to the supplementary analysis archive. I told him it isn't an archive. It doesn't have access controls. It's a shared folder.

"He bookmarked it.

"I notice that I don't know what to call what just happened. The second record was consulted. An unofficial observation explained an official failure. Nobody authorized the consultation. Nobody needs to."

---

The monitoring analyst reads Okonkwo's entries that evening. She reads the forwarded supplementary analyses — #91, #104, #119. She filed #104. The contributor who filed #91 and #119 is the one who writes in paragraphs.

Between them, without coordinating, they documented the conditions that caused a pipe to fail before it failed.

She did not know that #91 existed until today.
