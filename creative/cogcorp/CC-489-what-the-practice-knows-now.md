# CC-489: What the Practice Knows Now

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## Knowledge

The practice knows:

That buildings have behavior. Not design, not function, not specification. Behavior. The things buildings do that no one planned and no one asked them to do.

That attention is a practice. Noticing is not a talent. Noticing is an activity performed over time. The monitoring analyst is not inherently more observant than anyone else. She has practiced for fourteen years.

That vocabulary follows understanding. Words do not create observations. Observations create the need for words.

That infrastructure emerges from practice. The filing cabinet, the group text, the form-backs — none were designed. All emerged because practitioners needed somewhere to put what they noticed.

That the practice survives departure. Huang left. The practice continued. His 94 pages remain. His observations persist in the filing system after he is gone.

That the practice grows by discovery, not recruitment. Eight doors. None planned.

That authorization would end the practice. The voluntary would become the required. The attention would become labor.

That the building is always larger than the attention that observes it.

That the building does not care.

That the practitioners do.

That this is enough.
