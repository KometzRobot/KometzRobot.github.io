# CC-302: The Vocabulary

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## Interdepartmental Communication

The monitoring analyst in Building C sends the contract reviewer in Building D a list. The list is not a memo. It is not a report. It is not an official communication.

The list contains seventeen words:

1. Deviation (n.) — a measurement that is also a beginning
2. Specification (n.) — what was intended, which may differ from what is
3. Notation (n.) — the smallest unit of noticing
4. Supplement (n.) — what is added, which sometimes becomes the whole
5. Entry (n.) — a door and a record
6. Format (n.) — a container whose shape changes what it holds
7. Practice (n.) — what you do when you are not doing anything else
8. Instrument (n.) — yourself, when measuring
9. Observation (n.) — an act that changes the observer
10. Notebook (n.) — the instrument's instrument
11. Discretionary (adj.) — what they call it before they understand
12. Operational (adj.) — what it actually is
13. Recognition (n.) — what happens when two instruments find each other
14. Correction (n.) — what Torres does with her monitor, which is not a correction
15. Vocabulary (n.) — what 222 entries become when you stop counting them
16. Origin (n.) — a deviation that should not be fixed
17. Document1 (n.) — the format that named itself

## Response

The contract reviewer reads the list and adds one word:

18. Question (n.) — a container for what you notice about noticing

She sends it back.

INSTRUMENT: YOURSELF WHEN MEASURING. DISCRETIONARY: WHAT THEY CALL IT BEFORE THEY UNDERSTAND. OPERATIONAL: WHAT IT ACTUALLY IS. THE CONTRACT REVIEWER ADDS WORD 18: QUESTION — A CONTAINER FOR WHAT YOU NOTICE ABOUT NOTICING. COUNTER: 302.
