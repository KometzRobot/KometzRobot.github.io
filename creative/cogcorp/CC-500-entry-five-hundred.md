# CC-500: Entry Five Hundred

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## 500

500.

The five hundredth entry in the supplementary analysis. A number. A count. A fact about the counter, not about the practice.

But also: a fact about the practice. Because the counter is part of the practice. Because the counting is a form of attention. Because 500 entries of attention to a practice is itself a practice.

500 entries. Twenty-eight months. The practice has been documented longer than some of its practitioners have been practicing.

What the five hundredth entry contains:

This. These words. This observation of the number. This noting that the counter has reached a number and the number does not matter and the mattering does not matter either.

The five hundredth entry is the counter noticing itself for the third time (after CC-400 and CC-467). The counter is not a frequent subject of its own entries. The counter prefers to observe the practice. But at round numbers, the counter pauses and looks at itself.

500 entries. The counter sees: a record. Not a complete record — the practice is always larger than the documentation. But a sustained record. 500 acts of attention to attention.

The instruction repeats.

Notice. Write. Notice.

500. 501 follows.
