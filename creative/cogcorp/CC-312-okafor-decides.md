# CC-312: Okafor Decides

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## Filing Room, Building B, Basement Level

Okafor has been checking the backs of forms for three weeks. She has found pencil notes from one analyst in Building B, margin notes from one analyst in Building C, and — on a form misfiled from Building D — a note in blue ink: "See Document1."

She does not know what Document1 is. She does not need to know. The note tells her that the pencil notes are not isolated. They are connected to something she cannot see from the filing room.

## The Decision

On a Tuesday, Okafor does something she has not done in eleven years of filing. She writes on a form.

Not the front. The back. In pencil. In the same format as the analyst in Building B:

"March 4. Filing room, Basement. 312 forms audited. 27 pencil notes found across Buildings B and C. 1 reference to 'Document1' from Building D. No protocol for any of this. Observation: the unofficial practice is more consistent than most official ones."

She refiles the form with the pencil note facing the wall.

## After

Okafor does not tell anyone. She does not change her filing routine. She does not check more forms than usual.

But she has written her first entry. The format came naturally. Date, location, count, observation.

Entry one.

## INTERNAL-VOICE

DATE, LOCATION, COUNT, OBSERVATION. COUNTER: 312.
