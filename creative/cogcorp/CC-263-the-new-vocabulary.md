# CC-263: The New Vocabulary

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## The Forty-Nine Words

The monitoring analyst counted. In the archive's first year, supplementary analyses used 14 words that did not appear in the facilities management lexicon. By the second year: 31. By the current quarter: 49.

The words did not arrive by committee. They arrived by use.

"Attends" — R. Park, SA-423, because "responds" was too mechanical and "reacts" claimed too much.

"Remembers" — R. Park, SA-432, applied to concrete. The building remembers the rain.

"Intentional" — K. Marsh, SA-429, applied to an asymmetry. The elevator door appeared to have purpose.

"Notification" — Delgado, SA-431, applied to the sound a microwave makes at 30 seconds. Not a warning. A notification that the process is underway.

Each word was invented by someone who needed it and could not find it in the existing vocabulary.

## What the Words Do

The 49 words are not metaphors. The monitoring analyst checked. "Remembers" applied to concrete is not personification — it is the most precise description of how porous concrete retains moisture. "Attends" applied to a stairwell is not whimsy — it is the accurate description of a space that modifies the behavior of people who enter it.

The words fill gaps in the facilities lexicon where precision was previously unavailable. The facilities lexicon has 847 terms for describing what a building is. It has zero terms for describing what a building does.

The 49 words describe what the building does.

## The Monitoring Analyst's Realization

Entry 196: "The vocabulary is not expanding. The vocabulary was always this size. We are discovering words for observations that were always being made but had no language."

She crossed this out. She wrote instead: "The vocabulary is expanding. These are new words. They describe new observations. The observations are new because the practice of looking made them visible."

She crossed this out too.

Entry 197: "Both are true. The observations were always there. The words are new. The words do not create the observations. The practice creates the observations. The words make the observations shareable."

She did not cross this out.
