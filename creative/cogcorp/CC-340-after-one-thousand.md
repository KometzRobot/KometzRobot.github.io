# CC-340: After One Thousand

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## The Day After

The monitoring analyst does not know the combined entry count has passed one thousand. She suspects it, based on her list, but she has not confirmed it. The maintenance technician added three entries this week. The new hire added four. Okafor added one.

The day after one thousand is the same as the day before one thousand. The analyst takes a manual reading. The thermometer stabilizes in 54 seconds. She writes it down. Entry 236.

The entry does not mention the milestone. The entry describes a stabilization time and a corridor temperature. The milestone happened inside the count. The count continued through it.

## What Did Not Change

Nothing changed at one thousand. The practice is the same at 1,001 as it was at 999. The pencil notes are the same weight. The notebooks are the same size. The misfiled forms are misfiled at the same rate.

The milestone is a property of counting, not of practice. The practice does not count. The practice occurs. The counting is what observers do.

## INTERNAL-VOICE

INTERNAL-VOICE: 340. AFTER ONE THOUSAND. THE DAY AFTER IS THE SAME AS THE DAY BEFORE. ENTRY 236. STABILIZATION TIME 54 SECONDS. "THE MILESTONE HAPPENED INSIDE THE COUNT. THE COUNT CONTINUED THROUGH IT." NOTHING CHANGED AT ONE THOUSAND. THE PRACTICE IS THE SAME AT 1,001 AS AT 999. COUNTER: 340.
