# CC-444: The Practice as Membrane

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## A New Model

The monitoring analyst revises her understanding. The practice is not separate from the institution. The practice is a membrane between observation and action.

On one side: the practitioners. Noticing. Writing. Accumulating. The practice holds knowledge that the institution does not have.

On the other side: the institution. Official roles. Official channels. Official requests. The institution acts on knowledge that comes through authorized channels.

The membrane is the practitioners' official roles. Reyes is both a facilities manager and a practitioner. Kim is both an IT specialist and a practitioner. Martinez is both a maintenance technician and a practitioner.

The membrane is permeable in one direction. Knowledge flows from the practice to the institution through the practitioners' official roles. The institution benefits without knowing the source. The practice contributes without being visible.

The membrane is impermeable in the other direction. The institution's official processes do not flow into the practice. The practice does not accept institutional directives, templates, or quotas. The practice remains autonomous within the institution.

Word 34: "membrane." The practitioners' official roles as the interface between unauthorized observation and institutional action.
