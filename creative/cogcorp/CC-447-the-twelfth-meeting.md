# CC-447: The Twelfth Meeting

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## Looking Forward

The twelfth meeting. Sixteen people. The monitoring analyst opens:

"Two years. Eighteen practitioners. Thirty-four words. Three archives. Three registers. The practice has leaked useful knowledge into the institution without being detected. The building benefits. The practitioners continue."

"I have one question: what happens next?"

The room is quiet. Then:

Vega: "The practice continues."

Torres: "But does it change?"

Park: "It has always been changing. We just call it 'continuing.'"

Martinez: "I'll be honest. I've been doing this for twenty-six months alone and one month with all of you. The month with all of you has been different. Not better. Not worse. Different. I have an audience now. The observations are the same but the reason I write them down has shifted."

Delgado: "From what to what?"

Martinez: "From 'because I notice' to 'because we notice.' It's subtle. But it's there."

The monitoring analyst writes: "Word 35: 'we notice.' The shift from individual attention to collective attention. Not the same as collaboration. Not the same as teamwork. The shift from 'I see this' to 'we see this.' The plural changes the pronoun, not the observation."
