# CC-202: THE RESPONSE

*CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis*

## SA #319 in the Archive

The monitoring analyst's filing sits in the archive between SA #318 (Huang, cafeteria lighting during evening shift) and SA #320 (Reeves, loading dock drainage capacity after the January modification). It occupies the same format as every other filing. It looks like every other filing.

Delgado reads it on Wednesday morning. He reads it twice. The second time he is not reading the data. He is reading the format. Value, unit, context. No prose. He recognizes it.

## SA #321

Filed by Delgado that afternoon. Three paragraphs about the second-floor break room coffee machine. The first paragraph measures temperature and volume: 237 mL at 78.2°C. The second paragraph describes the recalibration timeline. The third paragraph:

"The monitoring analyst filed SA #319 about this machine. Her measurements are 0.2°C different from mine, taken forty-eight hours later. The difference is within instrument variance. But the filing itself is not within variance. She has not filed a supplementary analysis before. The filing measures the coffee machine. The act of filing measures something else."

## Entry 147

*"He noticed. Of course he noticed. He is the person who reads filings as practices. I filed, and he read the filing as a practice. My one paragraph, value-unit-context, and he wrote three paragraphs about the fact that I filed at all.*

*His third paragraph: 'The filing measures the coffee machine. The act of filing measures something else.' He is right. The filing measured the coffee machine. The act of filing measured whether I could participate in the archive I have been observing for fourteen months.*

*I could. The result was one paragraph. I do not know what happens next."*

## The Break Room, Thursday

They do not discuss SA #321. They are both in the break room at 2:30 PM. She is reading a quarterly report. He is eating lunch. Neither speaks. This is not avoidance. This is two people who communicate through filings choosing to occupy the same space without filing about it.

## SA #323

Filed by the monitoring analyst. Her second supplementary analysis. One paragraph.

"The second-floor corridor temperature differential has stabilized at 0.6°C between the north and south sides, measured at 11:00 AM on three consecutive Tuesdays. The paragraph-writer's measurement of 0.8°C was taken at varying times. Reeves' measurement of 0.4°C was taken once, in the afternoon. Consistent methodology produces a measurement between the two previous observations. The differential exists. Its magnitude depends on when you measure it."

## Entry 148

*"I filed about the corridor. The paragraph-writer's corridor. I filed about it because the 0.8°C observation needed a follow-up and no one had done it consistently. I filed about it because I wanted to know if my format — value, unit, context — could describe something that the paragraph-writer described in prose and left without explanation.*

*It can. It describes it differently. My filing says the differential is 0.6°C and depends on time of day. His filing said it was 0.8°C and did not require explanation. Both are true. The difference between us is what we believe an observation requires."*
