# CC-505: The Shimmer

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## Seeing the Draft

The shimmer. The monitoring analyst has measured the corridor B draft at 3.2 seconds for over two years. She has felt it on her skin. She has timed it with a watch. She has written the number on hundreds of form-backs.

She has never seen it.

From outside Building B, at 5:45 PM on a cold evening, the warm air escaping through the loading dock is visible. It shimmers — a heat distortion effect, like the air above a road in summer but vertical, curling upward from the dock opening.

The draft she measures at 3.2 seconds is the internal manifestation of this warm air escaping. The shimmer is the external manifestation of the same phenomenon. Inside: a timed draft. Outside: a visible distortion.

The same event. Two perceptions. The practice has been holding one for two years. Now it holds both.

The analyst reports this at the nineteenth meeting. The room is quiet. Every practitioner has experienced something similar: knowing the building intimately from inside and not knowing it from outside.

Vega: "I hear the elevator pitch change. I never thought about what the elevator looks like from outside."

Martinez: "The elevator motor is on the roof. From outside, you can hear it. A different sound from the cable hum inside."

The building has an inside and an outside. The practice has been inside. The outside is a new country.
