# CC-238: The Corridor at 3 AM

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## Who Is There

The monitoring analyst is in Corridor E at 3 AM. This is part of her rotation — nighttime temperature checks, monthly. She has been doing this for six years. The corridor is empty. The fluorescent lights make the sound that Delgado described: a hum that knows it is being listened to.

She has read Delgado's description. She heard him confirm it in the meeting. Now she is standing in Corridor E at 3 AM and the lights are making that sound and Delgado's words are in her ears and her thermocouple is in her hand and the temperature is 20.4 degrees and the temperature has never been the point.

## What She Files

Entry 169: "Corridor E, Section 1. 20.4°C at 03:10. Thermocouple calibrated. No anomalies."

## What She Does Not File

That the corridor at 3 AM is a different place than the corridor at 3 PM. That the lights sound different. That the air feels different — not in temperature, which she has measured, but in density, in presence, in the quality of being the only person in a space designed for hundreds. That she can feel the building around her in a way she cannot feel at 3 PM, when the building is full of people and the building's presence is diluted by theirs.

That Delgado was right. That the hum knows. That the word "knows" is not metaphor — it is the most precise word available for the quality of that sound, and the fact that it is not a measurement term does not make it less precise.

## The Two Practices

The monitoring analyst has two practices now. The first is filing temperatures. The second is noticing things she does not file. The first practice is visible in the archive. The second practice is visible only to her, and now to six other people who have their own second practices.

Entry 161 was the moment the second practice became visible in the archive — the one time it surfaced. Entry 168, Entry 169 — these are the first practice reasserting itself. The second practice continues underneath, unfiled, shared, real.

The thermocouple measures temperature. Nothing measures the second practice. Nothing needs to.
