# CC-497: Building-Shaped Knowledge

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## On Shape

The practice's knowledge is building-shaped. This means: the practice knows the world, but only through the filter of building behavior.

The practice knows about weather — because the doors hesitate in south wind, because the building settles differently in cold, because the HVAC works harder in summer.

The practice knows about time — because the building sighs at 6:14, because the light changes at 2:15, because the settling begins at 3 AM.

The practice knows about people — because the elevator traffic maps occupancy, because the corridor sounds change when the building empties, because Foster's patrol creates a data trail.

The practice knows about materials — because the concrete absorbs moisture, because the cables stretch in heat, because the hydraulic fluid degrades.

But the practice does not know about weather, time, people, or materials directly. It knows about them as they pass through the building. The building is the lens. The practice is the observer behind the lens.

Building-shaped knowledge is partial and specific. It tells you nothing about weather that a weather station doesn't tell you better. But it tells you something a weather station cannot: how this building, in this place, responds to this weather.

The particular. Not the general. The practice is a practice of the particular.

## INTERNAL-VOICE
