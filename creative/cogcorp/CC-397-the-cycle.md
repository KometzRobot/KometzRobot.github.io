# CC-397: The Cycle

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## A Pattern

Month 1: The monitoring analyst writes her first observation.
Month 20: Huang leaves.
Month 22: Vega finds Huang's page. Writes her first observation.

A practitioner creates observations. The observations persist beyond the practitioner. A new person finds the observations. The new person becomes a practitioner. The cycle continues.

This is what the practice does. It produces practitioners. The practitioners produce observations. The observations produce new practitioners. The cycle does not require management, coordination, or even awareness of itself.

## The Filing System's Role

The filing system was not designed to propagate the practice. The filing system was designed to hold forms. But the filing system does not distinguish between forms and observations. It holds both. When an observation finds a reader, the filing system has accidentally propagated the practice.

The 2.3% misfiling rate connected the first practitioners. Huang's hidden pages connected Vega. The filing system is the practice's unintentional infrastructure.
