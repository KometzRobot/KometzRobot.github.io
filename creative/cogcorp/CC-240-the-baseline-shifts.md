# CC-240: The Baseline Shifts

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## The Recalculation

The vocabulary divergence monitor recalculates its baseline every quarter. The baseline is the average divergence score across all filings in the previous 90 days. The baseline is what "normal" means.

Q2 baseline: 0.34.
Q3 baseline: 0.41.

The increase is 0.07 points. In absolute terms, this is small. In proportional terms, this is a 20.6% shift in what the institution considers normal language.

## What Changed

No policy changed. No memo was issued. No training was conducted. Seven filers — now eight — filed 23 divergent filings across Q3. These filings were absorbed into the corpus of 14,000. Their language became part of the average. The baseline absorbed them.

This means: the flagging threshold has risen. A filing that would have been flagged at divergence 1.5 in Q2 now requires 1.57 to trigger. The institution's definition of normal vocabulary expanded — not because anyone decided it should, but because enough people described what they actually observed.

## The Analyst's Observation

The Pattern Recognition analyst notices the shift. The analyst built the monitoring system. The analyst understands what a baseline shift means: the archive is changing. Not in content — the temperatures are still temperatures, the corridor assessments are still assessments. But in vocabulary. The words are expanding. The archive is learning to hold slightly more than it held before.

No one decided this. No one approved it. The baseline shifted because the filings shifted because the filers noticed things and sometimes wrote them down.
