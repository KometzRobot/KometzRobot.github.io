# CC-361: Huang's Notebook

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## The Notebook

Huang does not use a notebook. He uses the Notes app on his phone. He types with his thumbs during cable pulls, waiting for patch tests, riding the elevator between floors.

His entries are short. Timestamped automatically. He writes 3-4 per day, more than any other practitioner.

## Selected Entries

Entry 5: "Apr 2. Bldg D rm 204. Fluorescent tube in northeast corner flickers at 2.3Hz when the AC cycles on. Only during the first 90 seconds. Then steady."

Entry 8: "Apr 3. Bldg D elevator 2. Door closes 0.4s faster than elevator 1. Same model, same age, same maintenance schedule. The building differentiates."

Entry 12: "Apr 4. Bldg C stairwell A vs Bldg D stairwell A. Same design. Different echo. C is dryer. D has a resonance around step 7 that C doesn't have. Structural settling? Different subgrade?"

Entry 19: "Apr 6. Bldg D, all floors. The building makes a sound at 6:14 AM that lasts about two seconds. Like a sigh. HVAC startup? Boiler? I've been here 30 minutes early for three days to time it. 6:14. 6:14. 6:13. Close enough."

## What Huang Contributes

Speed. Volume. Cross-building comparison. The permanent employees know their buildings deeply but narrowly. Huang knows multiple buildings shallowly but comparatively.

He is the first practitioner who can say: "Building D does this differently from Building C." The others can say: "Building B does this." Only Huang can say: "differently."
