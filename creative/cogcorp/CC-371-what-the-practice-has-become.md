# CC-371: What the Practice Has Become

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## A Summary

The monitoring analyst, Entry 97, written at home on a Sunday:

"I want to write down what the practice has become. Not what it is for. Not what it means. Just what it is.

"13 practitioners across 5 buildings. One contractor. One manager. One remote participant. 26 words in a shared vocabulary. 4 meetings. 1 group text. Approximately 1,200 entries in individual notebooks, phone notes, and form-backs.

"The practice has produced: observations, a vocabulary, a coordinated inquiry, two questions, and a concept of the building's own spec.

"The practice has not produced: reports, presentations, proposals, publications, patents, products, revenue, or institutional recognition.

"The practice operates in the space between what the institution measures and what the practitioners observe. It has no budget. No schedule. No deliverables. No stakeholders.

"It is 19 months old. It began when I wrote down the stabilization time of a corridor draft. It has grown entirely by accident.

"It is the most important thing I have done at this company."

## INTERNAL-VOICE

INTERNAL-VOICE: 371. WHAT THE PRACTICE HAS BECOME. ANALYST ENTRY 97. 13 PRACTITIONERS. 5 BUILDINGS. 26 WORDS. 4 MEETINGS. ~1,200 ENTRIES. ZERO: REPORTS, PRESENTATIONS, PROPOSALS, PUBLICATIONS, REVENUE, RECOGNITION. "THE MOST IMPORTANT THING I HAVE DONE AT THIS COMPANY." COUNTER: 371.
