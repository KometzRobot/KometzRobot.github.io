# CC-430: What the Practice Does Not Know

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## Absence of Information

The practice does not know:

How many people notice things and do not write them down. The maintenance technician says: "Every maintenance worker in this building is a practitioner who doesn't know the word." If this is true, there are approximately forty maintenance staff across five buildings who notice building behavior daily and never document it.

How many people write things down and do not file them. Huang wrote on his phone. The notes left with him. How many others write on phones, in private notebooks, on scraps of paper that go into pockets and then into washing machines?

How many people have found form-backs in filing rooms and thought: someone else notices. And then walked away. The filing cabinet in Building B holds Huang's pages and Vega's pages. How many people have seen those pages and not added their own?

The practice is the visible portion of a larger phenomenon. The phenomenon is: people who work in buildings notice the buildings. Some of them write it down. Some of those who write it down leave traces. Some of those traces are found.

The practice is the last layer. The noticing is the first.

The practice does not know how deep the first layer goes.
