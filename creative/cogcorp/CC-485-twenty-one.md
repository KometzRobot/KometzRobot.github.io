# CC-485: Twenty-One

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## The Count

Twenty-one practitioners. The monitoring analyst has stopped trying to predict the number. Every time she thinks the practice has found its natural size, another practitioner appears.

Cho is different from the others. She did not discover the practice through traces (form-backs, filing cabinets, group texts). She discovered it through conversation. Diana — one of the four unnamed regular attendees — mentioned it over lunch.

This is the first time a practitioner has been brought to the practice by another practitioner through direct invitation. Not recruitment — Diana did not convince Cho to observe buildings. Diana mentioned that other people notice building behavior, and Cho said: "I do that too."

The practice's discovery mechanisms:

1. Solitary accumulation (the original practitioners, years alone)
2. Trace discovery (Vega finding Huang's pages)
3. Accidental encounter (Okafor meeting Vega in the filing room)
4. Filing room correspondence (Park and Kim, leaving notes)
5. Data analysis (Kim finding patterns remotely)
6. Notebook discovery (Foster's notebook left on the table)
7. Artifact reading (wet-floor signs)
8. Direct mention (Diana bringing Cho)

Eight discovery mechanisms. The practice finds its practitioners through eight doors. More doors than the practice designed. Every door was opened by someone who was already practicing.
