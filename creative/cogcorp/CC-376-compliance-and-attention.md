# CC-376: Compliance and Attention

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## Two Words

The maintenance technician used two words that the monitoring analyst has been thinking about for a week.

Compliance: doing what the system requires. Filing forms. Reading numbers. Meeting targets. The institution runs on compliance. Every quarterly report is an artifact of compliance. Every form is a compliance event. Every metric is a compliance measurement.

Attention: doing what the observer chooses. Noticing the draft. Timing the stabilization. Counting the flickers. Writing it on the back of a form. The practice runs on attention.

## Entry 101

The monitoring analyst, Entry 101: "The technician distinguished compliance from attention. I have been thinking about this distinction for a week and I believe it is the most important thing the practice has produced.

"The institution cannot demand attention. It can only demand compliance. Attention is voluntary. You cannot mandate that someone notice the 47-second stabilization time. You can only mandate that they record the final number.

"The entire practice is built on voluntary attention. Thirteen people choosing to notice things they are not required to notice. This is why the practice cannot be formalized — formalization turns attention into compliance. The moment you require someone to write observations, the observations become reports.

"Word 27: **voluntary attention** — the act of choosing to observe what is not required; the foundation of the practice."
