# CC-123: The View Count

**Division:** Human Resources / Cross-Divisional
**Classification:** OBSERVATION (PASSIVE ENGAGEMENT)
**Filed by:** Employee Suggestion Portal — Automated Analytics

---

The intern enabled view counts on June 14th.

By July 1st, the portal had forty-four submissions. The average submission received eleven views. The average submission received zero votes. This was not apathy. This was something else.

## The Distribution

Submission 12 — a proposal from Procurement to standardize vendor communication templates — received forty-seven views and zero votes. The proposal was competent. The proposal was specific. The proposal did not need a vote to be implemented.

It was implemented on June 28th, without committee approval, without executive sign-off, without anyone filing a change request. The Procurement lead simply read the suggestion, agreed with it, and changed the template. When asked during the quarterly review how the new template originated, she said: "It was in the portal."

She did not say who wrote it. She did not know. The portal does not display author names. This was a design decision made during initial configuration seven years ago. Nobody had questioned it until now. Nobody questioned it now, either.

## The Pattern

Submission 24 — a request from Building Services to adjust the elevator inspection schedule — received sixty-one views. The most-viewed submission in the portal's history. Zero votes. The schedule was adjusted the following Monday.

Submission 31 asked: "What does it mean when reading is the response?"

It received ninety-three views.

## The Analytics Report

K. Patel, the monitoring analyst who had filed SA-116 using a tag she invented, was also the person who pulled the first analytics report from the suggestion portal. She was not assigned to HR. She was assigned to Documentation Standards. She pulled the report because the portal's cross-reference pattern reminded her of something she had seen in the SA archive.

The report showed:
- 44 submissions
- 0 submissions with more than 2 votes
- Average view count: 34.7
- Median time between submission and implementation: 9 days
- Number of submissions that resulted in actual changes: 11
- Number of those changes that went through formal approval: 0

She highlighted the last line. She did not write a conclusion. She saved the report in the SA filing system under the tag: OBSERVATION (PARALLEL SYSTEMS).

It was the second time that tag had been used. The first time was in SA-116 — the filing where she observed herself adjusting to the archive's rhythm before she knew she was doing it.

## Submission 38

Submission 38 was filed by someone in Corporate Intelligence. The suggestion: retire the voting system entirely. The rationale:

"The portal has demonstrated that reading is sufficient for adoption. The vote count is not zero because people are indifferent. The vote count is zero because voting is a form of endorsement that the portal's participants have collectively decided is unnecessary. They read. They implement. The gap between reading and implementing contains no intermediate step. We do not need to name this. But we should stop measuring what we have already stopped using."

Submission 38 received one hundred and twelve views. It was the first submission to exceed one hundred. It received zero votes.

The voting system was not retired. But the quarterly metrics report, which had always included "total votes cast" as a portal health indicator, dropped that column the following month. The analyst who removed it noted in the changelog: "Metric not reflective of engagement."
