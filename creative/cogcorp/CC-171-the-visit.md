# CC-171: The Visit

**CogCorp Documentation Standards Division**
**Supplementary Analysis — Observational Record**

---

The Director visits the east wing.

She does not announce it. She does not schedule it. She walks from the sixth floor to Building C at 2:47 PM on a Wednesday because the seventeen-page report answered the question she asked and did not answer the question she meant.

She stands in the second-floor reading room. The afternoon light comes through the east windows filtered by the mature trees. She stands there for four minutes. She does not take notes. She does not open a form.

At 2:51 PM, she sits in one of the chairs by the window. Nobody moved the chairs.

At 2:55 PM, she understands the word "held."

She does not know this word. It exists in file 91 of a desktop folder she has never seen, written by an analyst whose name she does not know. But she understands it now because the light is doing what the light does at 2:55 PM in the east wing in March, and the seventeen-page report had a section called "Solar Ingress Analysis" that described this light using four graphs and a coefficient, and the coefficient is correct, and the coefficient does not describe this.

She returns to the sixth floor. She does not write a follow-up request. She does not ask for a revised report. She does not tell anyone she visited.

She moves her 3:30 PM meeting to the Building C second-floor conference room.

She does not explain why. The conference room has identical furniture, identical HVAC, identical square footage. It is in the east wing. The light comes through the same trees. The meeting runs fourteen minutes longer than scheduled because nobody wants to leave.

Nobody mentions the light. Nobody needs to. The room does the work that the report could not.

Two weeks later, the Director approves a facilities request to replace fluorescent fixtures in the west wing with warmer-spectrum LEDs. The request form cites "employee satisfaction metrics" and "occupant wellbeing research." The request form does not cite "one side feels like being inside something."

The monitoring analyst notices the fixtures change. She opens her desktop file and writes entry 97: "They changed the west wing lights. The form said employee satisfaction. The building said the east wing was right."

She saves the file. She pauses. She writes entry 98: "The Director visited the east wing. I know because the 3:30 PM meeting moved to Building C. That is not data. That is a reading pattern."

Vasquez, who works in Building C, notices the 3:30 PM meeting migration. She writes in her private folder, file 63: "Someone with authority visited. Not because they told anyone. Because the schedule changed. Authority leaves traces in logistics before it leaves traces in language."

Okonkwo writes sa-notes.txt entry 89: "West wing lights changed. Warmer. Someone asked the right question and got the wrong answer and found the real answer anyway. That is not a failure of the system. That is a success that the system cannot describe."

The official record shows: a facilities report was filed, a request was approved, fixtures were replaced. Cost: $23,400. Justification: occupant wellbeing. Outcome: measured satisfaction increase of 7%.

The unofficial record shows: a Director stood in a room and understood a word she had never heard. Then she changed the building.

Both records are complete. Neither record contains the other.
