# CC-455: The Campus as Body

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## A Model

The monitoring analyst develops the metaphor:

The campus is a body. Five buildings, five organs. Connected by walkways (arteries), shared systems (circulation), common infrastructure (skeleton).

The HVAC is the respiratory system. The campus breathes: inhale at 6:03-6:14 (morning cycling), exhale at 6:00 PM (shutdown sequence). The breath is visible in the data (Kim), audible in the corridors (analyst, Vega), measurable in the thermal transitions (Park), and maintainable (Martinez).

The elevator system is the digestive tract. It processes occupants: intake at 8:00-9:00 AM, distribution throughout the day, elimination at 5:00-6:00 PM. The elevator pitch changes (Vega), the cable tension varies (Martinez), the traffic patterns form a daily curve (Kim).

The electrical system is the nervous system — the building's actual nervous system, not the practitioners. It carries signals: light, power, data, communication. It has its own behavior: the 3-second cycle in corridor 3C (Martinez), the electromagnetic interference (Kim), the fluorescent quality changes (Vega).

The practitioners are the consciousness. The body did not ask for consciousness. The body functioned without it for decades. But now it has eighteen points of awareness, distributed across five organs, observing the body's behavior and documenting it.

The campus is a body. The practice is its consciousness. The consciousness is unauthorized.
