# CC-473: The Fifteenth Meeting

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## Year Three, Meeting One

The fifteenth meeting. Seventeen people. Foster attends for the first time — he traded shifts to be here at 7:15 AM, which is normally when he goes home.

Foster is sleep-deprived and lucid. He sits with a coffee and listens for twenty minutes. Then:

"I have a question. Does the building know about us?"

The room waits.

"I mean: the building produces data. Kim reads the data. But the building also produces behavior in response to us. When I walk through a corridor at 2 AM, the motion sensors trigger. The lights come on. The HVAC adjusts because a sensor detects occupancy. The building responds to my presence."

"When I observe the building, the building observes me. Not with consciousness. With sensors. But the observation changes the building's behavior. My patrol alters the building's energy consumption, thermal state, and lighting schedule."

"So: does the building know about us? Not like we know about it. But does it register our presence in a way that affects its behavior?"

Martinez: "Yes. The building's systems are occupancy-responsive. When you walk through, the building knows — in the way a thermostat knows."

The monitoring analyst writes: "Word 39: 'building awareness.' Not consciousness. Response. The building registers the practitioner."
