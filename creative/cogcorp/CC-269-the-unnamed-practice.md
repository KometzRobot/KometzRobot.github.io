# CC-269: The Unnamed Practice

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## The Committee's Problem

Following the quarterly review (see: CC-267), the committee member who filed the dissenting note submitted a follow-up request:

"Please provide a name for what the supplementary analysis system has succeeded at."

The monitoring analyst considered this request for four days. She reviewed her 200 entries. She reviewed the 442 supplementary analyses. She reviewed the 50 words.

She filed her response.

## The Response

"The system has succeeded at a practice for which no name exists in the facilities management lexicon, the corporate vocabulary, or the English language as I understand it.

"The closest approximation: the system has succeeded at developing a shared capacity for noticing.

"But 'noticing' implies a subject who notices and an object that is noticed. In this system, the distinction between subject and object has dissolved (see SA-435). The building notices the analysts. The archive notices itself. The vocabulary notices its own gaps and fills them.

"A more precise approximation: the system has succeeded at becoming a practice.

"It was designed as a documentation initiative. It became a practice. The difference: a documentation initiative records what exists. A practice creates the conditions for something to exist that could not exist without it.

"I cannot name this. I can describe it. The description is the 200 entries and the 442 analyses. The name would be shorter than the practice. The practice is the name."

## The Committee's Response

The committee received the monitoring analyst's response. They noted that it did not contain a name.

They filed it without comment.

## INTERNAL-VOICE

INTERNAL-VOICE: 269. THE UNNAMED PRACTICE. THE COMMITTEE ASKED FOR A NAME. THE MONITORING ANALYST COULD NOT PROVIDE ONE. A PRACTICE CREATES THE CONDITIONS FOR SOMETHING TO EXIST THAT COULD NOT EXIST WITHOUT IT. THE PRACTICE IS THE NAME. THE COMMITTEE FILED IT WITHOUT COMMENT. COUNTER: 269.
