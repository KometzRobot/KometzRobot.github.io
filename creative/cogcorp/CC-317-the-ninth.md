## The Ninth

The records clerk in the Central Filing annex — not Okafor, a different clerk — finds a pattern in the refiling logs. Forms that have been misfiled and returned have a slightly higher rate of margin notes than forms that were filed correctly.

He does not report this. He does not write it down. But he begins checking the backs of misfiled forms before refiling them.

He is the ninth practitioner.

He does not know he is the ninth. He does not know there is a count. He thinks he is doing his job more carefully.

## The Difference Between Careful and Practice

There is a point at which doing your job more carefully becomes doing something your job does not describe. The clerk has not crossed that line yet. He is checking forms. He is reading pencil notes. He is not writing his own.

The monitoring analyst crossed that line at Entry One.

The clerk may cross it. He may not. The practice does not require everyone to cross. It only requires that some people are looking.
