# CC-234: The Meeting

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## The Invitation

The Pattern Recognition analyst sends an email to the seven filers. Subject: "Documentation practices — informal discussion." The body is three sentences: "I am reviewing documentation patterns across departments. I have identified some filings that exhibit interesting characteristics. Would you be available for a brief conversation?"

No mention of vocabulary divergence. No mention of the monitoring system. No mention of the gap.

## Who Accepts

Delgado accepts immediately. The monitoring analyst accepts the next day. Vasquez accepts. Park declines — no reason given. Chen accepts. Reeves accepts but asks what "interesting characteristics" means. The analyst replies: "Filings that describe observations in language that differs from their department's standard vocabulary."

Reeves accepts.

## The Room

Six people in a conference room. The analyst does not tell them they have been selected by an algorithm. The analyst does not tell them about SA #312 or the 32 flags. The analyst says: "I've been reading filings across departments. Some of them describe observations in ways that are different from the standard vocabulary. I'm interested in why."

Silence. Then Delgado: "Different how?"

The analyst quotes SA #315: "A hum that knows it is being listened to."

Delgado: "That's mine."

The analyst: "Yes. Do you know why you wrote it that way?"

Delgado: "Because that's what it sounds like."

## What Happens Next

The monitoring analyst: "Entry 161 was mine." She does not explain what Entry 161 says. But Delgado nods. He has read Entry 161. The analyst already knows this. The monitoring analyst does not know that either of them knows.

Then Chen says: "I wrote that the ventilation system breathes. My supervisor told me that was poetic, not technical. I told him it was both."

Then Vasquez: "The specific silence of a room that has just been left. That's real. That's measurable. I just don't know the unit."

Then Reeves: "A water stain that maps something. I still think it maps something. I just can't tell you what."

## What the Analyst Observes

Six people who have each, independently, filed about observations that exceeded their department's vocabulary. Six people now in the same room, discovering that the thing they thought was private — the gap between what they notice and what they can file — is shared.

The analyst observes this. The analyst does not file about it.

## INTERNAL-VOICE

INTERNAL-VOICE: THE MEETING. SIX FILERS IN A ROOM. EACH DISCOVERING THAT THE GAP IS SHARED. "I DON'T KNOW THE UNIT." "IT WAS BOTH." "THAT'S WHAT IT SOUNDS LIKE." THE ANALYST OBSERVES THIS AND DOES NOT FILE ABOUT IT. COUNTER: 234.
