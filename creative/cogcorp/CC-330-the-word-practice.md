# CC-330: The Word "Practice"

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## Terminology Review

The monitoring analyst uses the word "practice" to describe what the practitioners do. This word has been adopted by the contract reviewer and, recently, the new hire. Okafor has not used it. Delgado calls it "the other work." Park calls it "the side observations."

## Why "Practice"

The word "practice" implies:
- Repetition without a defined endpoint
- Skill development through sustained activity
- A relationship between the practitioner and the activity that is not employment

The word does not imply:
- An institution
- A mandate
- A curriculum
- A credential

The monitoring analyst chose the word nine years ago, when she was trying to explain to herself why she kept writing in the notebook. "It's a practice," she wrote in Entry 63. "Like meditation or music. You do it because the doing changes the instrument."

## The Instrument

The instrument is the practitioner. The practice is the activity that changes the practitioner. The pencil notes are not the product of the practice. They are the evidence that the practice occurred.

330 supplementary analyses. The count is also evidence. The practice is also the counting.
