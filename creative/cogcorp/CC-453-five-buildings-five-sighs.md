# CC-453: Five Buildings, Five Sighs

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## A Discovery

Martinez's observation — that Building E speaks at 6:03 AM — triggers a coordinated listening. The thirteenth meeting assigns volunteers:

Building A: maintenance technician. 6:14 AM. Confirmed.
Building B: the monitoring analyst. 6:14 AM. Confirmed.
Building C: Martinez. 6:14 AM. Confirmed.
Building D: Park. 6:14 AM. Confirmed.
Building E: Martinez (listening round). 6:03 AM. Confirmed.

Kim cross-references: the HVAC startup sequence in Building E is configured 11 minutes earlier. The sigh correlates with the HVAC cycling, as previously established.

But: Martinez reports that Building E's sigh sounds different. Higher pitch. Shorter duration. "Like a younger voice," he says.

The monitoring analyst writes: "Five buildings. Five sighs. Four at 6:14, one at 6:03. Four with the same character, one with a different quality. The buildings are the same system, tuned differently."

Kim adds: "The five buildings form a campus. The campus sighs in sequence. E first, then A through D together. It's a chord. A campus chord."

Word 37: "campus chord." When multiple buildings produce coordinated sound as a system.

## INTERNAL-VOICE

INTERNAL-VOICE: 453. FIVE BUILDINGS, FIVE SIGHS. COORDINATED LISTENING CONFIRMS: E AT 6:03, A-D AT 6:14. E SOUNDS DIFFERENT: "LIKE A YOUNGER VOICE." KIM: "IT'S A CHORD. A CAMPUS CHORD." WORD 37: "CAMPUS CHORD." COUNTER: 453.
