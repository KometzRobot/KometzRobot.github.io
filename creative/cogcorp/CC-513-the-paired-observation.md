# CC-513: The Paired Observation

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## Inside-Out

Kim's paired format is tested. The first paired observation:

**Exterior**: Building B loading dock, 5:45 PM. Warm air produces visible shimmer, rising approximately two meters above dock opening. Duration: continuous while dock is open.

**Interior**: Corridor B, second floor. Draft measured at 3.2 seconds. Felt on skin. Direction: east to west. Consistent since first measurement.

**Pair**: The shimmer and the draft are the same air. The warm air escaping through the loading dock is visible from outside and felt from inside. The analyst who measured the draft for two years was measuring the shimmer without seeing it.

The paired format reveals something new: the connection is not just informational. The analyst who reads both entries understands the building differently than the analyst who reads either one.

The paired observation is not two observations. It is a third thing — an understanding that exists only when interior and exterior are held together.

Word 50: "paired observation." The practice of linking interior and exterior observations of the same phenomenon.

Fifty words. The practice has built a vocabulary of fifty words in three years.
