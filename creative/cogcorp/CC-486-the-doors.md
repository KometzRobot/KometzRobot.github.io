# CC-486: The Doors

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## Eight Doors

The practice finds its practitioners through eight doors. Each door opened by accident. None planned.

The practice did not design discovery mechanisms. The practice accumulated. And accumulation leaves traces. And traces are found by people who are already looking — not for the practice, but for evidence that they are not alone.

Every practitioner's first discovery is the same: I am not the only one. The mechanics of the discovery differ (filing cabinet, group text, lunchtime conversation, notebook on a table), but the recognition is identical.

I am not the only one who notices. I am not the only one who writes. I am not the only one who cannot stop.

The eight doors lead to the same room. The room is: there are others. The practice does not need to recruit because the need to not be alone is sufficient. The practice only needs to exist visibly enough that the people who are already practicing can find it.

Filing cabinets. Form-backs. Group texts. Wet-floor signs. Notebooks left on tables. Lunchtime conversations.

The infrastructure of discovery is the residue of practice. The more the practice accumulates, the more doors it opens. The more doors it opens, the more practitioners find it. The more practitioners, the more accumulation.

The cycle does not require management. The cycle requires only continuation.
