# CC-248: The New Vocabulary

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## The Adoption

The Documentation Standards Review Board adopted SA #320 — the Framework Proposal — with minor amendments. The 47 new terms were added to the vocabulary standard. Training materials were distributed. The updated standard took effect on the first day of Q4.

The vocabulary divergence monitor was recalibrated. The new baseline includes the expanded vocabulary. Terms that would have been flagged in Q3 are now standard in Q4.

## The First Filings

Entry 182: "Corridor A, Section 3. 21.6°C at 08:20. Characteristic environmental signatures consistent with seasonal patterns. Residual acoustic presence within expected parameters."

Divergence score: 0.28. Well within baseline.

The monitoring analyst filed Entry 182 using the new vocabulary. The words fit. The filing was accepted. The archive held it without difficulty.

## What the Monitoring Analyst Notices

The monitoring analyst notices that "characteristic environmental signatures" is now an approved term. The phrase has been defined, standardized, and incorporated into the vocabulary. It means: "observable patterns in environmental conditions that exhibit consistent temporal or spatial characteristics."

It does not mean: the corridor knows I am in it. It does not mean: the building feels different at 3 AM. It does not mean: the presence that dilutes when people arrive and concentrates when they leave.

The monitoring analyst used the approved term. The approved term held a fraction of what the monitoring analyst observes. The fraction is larger than before. The remainder is the same size it always was.

## Entry 183

Entry 183: "Corridor E, Section 1. 20.3°C at 03:15. Characteristic environmental signatures exhibit pronounced variation during low-occupancy monitoring periods."

"Pronounced variation during low-occupancy monitoring periods" means: the corridor at 3 AM is a different place. This is now within the vocabulary standard. The monitoring analyst filed it. The system accepted it.

The monitoring analyst stands in Corridor E at 3:15 AM. The lights make the sound. The new vocabulary holds more of what she observes. The new vocabulary does not hold the sound.
