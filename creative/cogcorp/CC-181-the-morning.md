# CC-181: The Morning

*CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis*

---

The monitoring analyst arrives at 7:48 AM. She does not go to the south wing. She does not check the east wing renovation schedule. She sits at her desk and opens sa-notes.txt.

Entry 103: "The light at my desk at 7:52 AM has a yellow quality I have not noticed before. It comes through the east-facing window and hits the top left corner of my second monitor. Duration: approximately four minutes before the angle changes."

She files it as SA-403.

It is the shortest filing she has submitted. Seven sentences about light on a screen. No interpretation, no cross-reference, no context about the renovation or the room she lost or the south wing that didn't hold her.

Vasquez reads it at 8:14 AM. Files nothing.

Okonkwo reads it at 8:31. Adds entry 58 to sa-notes.txt: "SA-403 is the first filing submitted from outside a dedicated observation space. The practice has left the room."

The monitoring analyst does not know that Okonkwo wrote this. She will never see sa-notes.txt. She will never learn that her shift was documented the morning it happened.

On Wednesday she files SA-404: the heating vent under her desk cycles every fourteen minutes. A clicking sound, then warm air, then silence. She has sat at this desk for six years without counting the interval.

Thursday, SA-405: the maintenance crew's schedule creates a twenty-minute quiet window between 10:40 and 11:00. This is when she does her best analytical work. She has known this without knowing she knew it.

Friday, SA-406: the coffee machine in the shared kitchen produces a sound at exactly 7:55 AM, two minutes before the first person uses it. Warm-up cycle. Nobody waits for it. They arrive and it is ready and they do not think about the two minutes of preparation that happened without them.

By Friday she has filed four observations from her desk. All are about her immediate environment. All are shorter than her average. None reference the east wing.

R. Park reads all four during her lunch break. Pauses her cursor over the filing location field. Every previous filing from the monitoring analyst listed an observation room. Building C East Wing. South Wing Corridor. Conference Room 3B.

Now it reads: "Cubicle 2-14, Floor 2, Main Building."

The practice did not need the room. The room was where she learned what the practice was. The room was training, and she did not know she was being trained, and now the training is complete and the training ground is gone and the skill remains.

Park does not file a filing about this. She closes the archive and looks at her own desk. The pen holder has seven pens. Three work. She has never counted them before.

She counts them now.
