# CC-224: The Spreadsheet

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## The Export

The analyst exported all filings with vocabulary divergence exceeding 1.5 standard deviations from their department baseline. The export produced a spreadsheet with 73 rows. 73 filings out of 14,247 whose word choices diverge significantly from what their department typically files.

Each row contains: filing ID, department, date, word count, divergence score, and the three words contributing most to the divergence.

## Row 23

Row 23: Entry 161, Environmental Monitoring, filed [date redacted], 73 words, divergence score 2.31, top divergent words: "experience," "layers," "reduces."

No temperature data appears in the row. No mention of corridors or thermocouples. The spreadsheet contains the statistical shadow of Entry 161 — the measurements that the filing's divergence from its context produced.

## Row 31

Row 31: SA #289, Supplementary Analysis, filed [date redacted], 62 words, divergence score 1.87, top divergent words: "observed," "approximately," "illusion."

Delgado's filing about the corridor light — the one he wrote after reading Entry 161 — appears eight rows below hers. In the archive, they are in different departments, different cabinets, different categories. In the spreadsheet, they are sorted by divergence score and they are almost adjacent.

The spreadsheet created a proximity that the archive never contained.

## What the Spreadsheet Strips

The spreadsheet does not contain the text of the filings. It contains metadata about the text. The analyst can see that Entry 161 diverges from Environmental Monitoring's baseline. The analyst cannot see why. The spreadsheet reports that "experience" is a divergent word for that department. It does not report what the filer experienced.

This is a reduction that the monitoring analyst would recognize. She wrote about the gap between experience and filing. The spreadsheet is the gap between the filing and its analysis. Each layer reduces. She said so. The spreadsheet proves it by being another layer.

## The Sort

The analyst sorted the spreadsheet by department. Environmental Monitoring: 4 filings. Supplementary Analysis: 11 filings. Facilities Assessment: 8 filings. Pattern Recognition: 0 filings.

The Pattern Recognition Group, whose job is to identify anomalies, has never filed an anomalous filing. Their vocabulary never diverges. They describe patterns in the language of pattern description. They are, by their own metrics, perfectly calibrated.

The analyst noted this and did not file a report about it.
