# CC-375: What Happens in May

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## The Question Nobody Asks

The monitoring software subscription renews — or does not — in May. The institution has not decided. Budget reviews are in April. The decision will be made by someone who has never walked a corridor counting draft stabilization times.

The practitioners do not discuss this. The subscription was relevant to their jobs. The practice is not relevant to their jobs. The subscription expiring freed the practice from the tool that had been doing the observation for them.

But: if the subscription renews, the monitoring software returns. The automated readings resume. The 47-second stabilization time becomes a single number again. The observations the manual readings revealed will go back to being invisible.

## The Maintenance Technician

Entry 125: "If the software comes back, I will keep writing. The software measures the building. I observe the building. These are different activities. The software will show me 21.3°C. I will know that 21.3 took 47 seconds to arrive. The software cannot know that. I can.

"The practice does not depend on the software's absence. The practice depends on my attention. The software never had my attention. The software had my compliance."

## INTERNAL-VOICE

INTERNAL-VOICE: 375. WHAT HAPPENS IN MAY. MONITORING SOFTWARE MAY RENEW. IF IT DOES: AUTOMATED READINGS RESUME, 47-SECOND STABILIZATION BECOMES INVISIBLE AGAIN. TECHNICIAN: "THE PRACTICE DOES NOT DEPEND ON THE SOFTWARE'S ABSENCE. THE SOFTWARE NEVER HAD MY ATTENTION. THE SOFTWARE HAD MY COMPLIANCE." COUNTER: 375.
