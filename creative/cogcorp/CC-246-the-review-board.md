# CC-246: The Review Board

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## The Meeting

The Documentation Standards Review Board convenes quarterly. Seven members: the Director of Standards, the heads of Environmental Monitoring, Facilities, Acoustics, Structural Engineering, and HVAC, and one rotating member from Pattern Recognition.

The rotating member this quarter is the Pattern Recognition analyst.

The analyst sits at the table with SA #319 in front of the Board. The Board has read the supplementary analysis. The Director of Standards asks: "You're recommending no action on a 20.6% baseline shift?"

The analyst says: "The shift represents expanded descriptive capacity, not degraded accuracy."

## The Discussion

The Director of Standards: "Expanded descriptive capacity is not in the vocabulary framework."

The analyst: "That is precisely the point."

The head of Environmental Monitoring — the monitoring analyst's supervisor — says: "My team's filings have been flagged twelve times this quarter. None of the flagged filings contained errors."

The head of Facilities: "Nine flags. No errors."

The head of Acoustics: "Eight flags. The flagged descriptions were more accurate than the standard vocabulary allows."

The Director of Standards looks at SA #319. The Director looks at the department heads. The Director says: "You're telling me the vocabulary standard is too narrow."

Three department heads nod.

## The Silence

The analyst watches three department heads confirm what six filers described in an unfiled meeting. The department heads were not in that meeting. The department heads arrived at the same conclusion independently, through their own review of the flagged filings. The gap is visible from above as well as from within.

The Director of Standards says: "I'll convene a vocabulary review committee."

The analyst does not say: the vocabulary is not the problem. The vocabulary is a symptom. The problem is that observation exceeds language, and language exceeds filing, and filing exceeds the archive, and this chain of reduction is structural, not fixable.

The analyst says: "I'll prepare a framework proposal."
