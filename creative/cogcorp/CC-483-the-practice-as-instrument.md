# CC-483: The Practice as Instrument

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## Calibration

The monitoring analyst reflects:

"In the beginning, I thought the practitioners were the instruments. Each person a sensor. Each observation a reading. The practice was the network that connected the sensors.

"This was wrong. Or incomplete. The practitioners are not instruments. The practice is the instrument. The practitioners are the practice's components.

"A thermometer is not a bead of mercury. A thermometer is the interaction between the mercury, the glass, the scale, and the reader. Remove any component and it stops being a thermometer.

"The practice is the interaction between twenty people, five buildings, five registers, forty-one words, three archives, and the instruction that repeats. Remove any component and the practice changes. It does not stop — we have lost practitioners and gained them, and the practice continued — but it changes.

"The practice is a self-calibrating instrument. When Martinez joined, the practice recalibrated to include technical knowledge. When Kim joined, it recalibrated for data. When Foster joined, it recalibrated for night. When the janitor's observations appeared, it recalibrated for touch.

"Each new practitioner does not add a sensor to a network. Each new practitioner recalibrates the instrument. The practice after Foster is not the practice before Foster plus a night-shift guard. It is a fundamentally recalibrated practice."
