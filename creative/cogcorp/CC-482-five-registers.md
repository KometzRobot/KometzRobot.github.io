# CC-482: Five Registers

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## The Senses of the Practice

Five registers. The practice has five senses:

**Perceptual** (the monitoring analyst, Vega, the original practitioners): what the building sounds like, looks like, feels like to the unaided human. The corridor draft. The light change at 2:15. The building sigh.

**Technical** (Martinez, the maintenance technician): why the building does what it does. The mechanical explanation. The system behind the phenomenon. The air handling unit behind the sigh.

**Data** (Kim): what the building looks like in numbers. HVAC logs. Network utilization. Elevator traffic. The building's self-documentation, read by a practitioner who can hear its frequency.

**Nocturnal** (Foster): what the building does when no one is there. The settling sounds. The deep silence. The pre-dawn stirring. The building's private behavior.

**Tactile** (the janitor): what the building feels like to touch, to walk on, to push against. The creak, the tilt, the stick. The building as a physical object, known through the body.

Five registers. Each reveals a building the others cannot perceive. The practice holds all five. No single practitioner holds more than two.

The practice is the only instrument capable of observing the whole building. And even the practice observes only a fraction.

Word 41: "five senses." The practice's perceptual apparatus.
