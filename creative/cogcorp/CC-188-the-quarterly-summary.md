# CC-188: The Quarterly Summary

*CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis*

---

Corporate Records requests a summary of the supplementary analysis archive for the Q1 infrastructure review. One page. Due Friday.

Okonkwo receives the request on a Tuesday.

---

Entry 72:

"I have been asked to summarize the supplementary analysis archive. Corporate Records wants a one-page overview for the quarterly review. They want to know: how many filings, how many contributors, what categories, any trends.

"The archive has 237 supplementary analyses. I can count them. I can categorize them: environmental (91), structural (64), procedural (47), observational (35). I can estimate contributors: between 12 and 18, based on writing style. I can identify trends: filings have increased 40% over the past quarter.

"This is all accurate. I can fit it on one page."

---

He writes the summary on Wednesday. It says:

*The supplementary analysis archive contains 237 filings from an estimated 12-18 contributors, spanning environmental, structural, procedural, and observational categories. Filing frequency has increased approximately 40% quarter-over-quarter. The archive has been informally consulted by facilities maintenance (one documented instance) and is maintained in a shared folder with no formal access controls.*

---

Entry 73:

"The summary is accurate. It describes the archive the way a census describes a city: population, area, density, median income. These numbers are true. They do not describe what it is like to live there.

"What the summary does not say: that the monitoring analyst recognized the paragraph-writer by observation style across fifteen analyses. That SA #91, #104, and #119 — filed independently by two contributors who have never met — described the conditions that caused a pipe failure before it happened. That nobody assigned any of it. That the archive works because contributors observe independently, and coordination might reduce it.

"The summary compresses 237 observations into six lines. The compression is lossy. What survives: counts, categories, percentages. What does not survive: the texture of independent observation accumulating without direction."

---

Vasquez, file 74:

"Okonkwo showed me the summary before submitting it. It is correct. I have no objections.

"But I notice something: the summary is itself a supplementary analysis. It observes the archive the way the archive observes the building. And the summary's format — one page, quarterly, requested by management — compresses away exactly what the archive's format preserves. The archive has no page limit. No deadline. No requester. Each observation exists on its own terms.

"The summary converts 237 separate acts of noticing into a single act of reporting. The conversion is not reversible. You cannot reconstruct the monitoring analyst's recognition of the paragraph-writer from the number '12-18 contributors.' You cannot reconstruct the pipe failure prediction from '237 filings.' The information that made the archive valuable is the information that does not survive the summary format.

"I am not suggesting the summary is wrong. I am suggesting that the format selects for what it can carry and discards the rest. The archive carried texture. The summary carries structure. The quarterly review will read the structure and never know about the texture."

---

The monitoring analyst reads Okonkwo's summary when it is posted to the quarterly review folder. She reads: "12-18 contributors." She is one of them. The paragraph-writer is another. Their parallel observation of the same corridor — a pattern she recognized by style, a recognition she chose not to act on — is now a fraction of a number in a six-line document.

She does not object. The summary is accurate.

She files SA #238 that evening. It observes that the east corridor temperature differential has shifted 0.4 degrees since the pipe repair. Nobody asked her to.
