# CC-220: The Annual Report

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## Publication

The Documentation Standards Division publishes an annual report. It is 340 pages. It is distributed to department heads, archived in triplicate, and posted on the internal network where it receives an average of eleven views.

Page 147 contains the following line: "Non-standard filings reclassified: 3 (Environmental Monitoring to Supplementary Analysis: 1; Facilities Assessment to Supplementary Analysis: 1; Personnel Observation to Supplementary Analysis: 1)."

Entry 161 is one of those three.

## What the Number Means

Entry 161 — the monitoring analyst's reflection on the gap between experience and archive, the filing that generated eight Supplementary Analyses, the document that inspired Delgado's SA #289 — is counted as "1" in a line that receives approximately one-tenth of a view.

This is not a diminishment. This is aggregation. The annual report does not distinguish between filings that generated eight analyses and filings that generated none. It counts them equally. One reclassification. Three total. Page 147.

## What Aggregation Does

Aggregation is the opposite of the gap. The gap is about what the archive cannot hold — the experience that exceeds the filing. Aggregation is about what the archive does with what it holds — it counts, categorizes, summarizes. Entry 161 became a datum in a statistical summary. The reflection on the limits of recording was recorded as a number.

The annual report does not know that Entry 161 matters. The annual report does not know that any entry matters. The annual report knows that three filings were reclassified and that the documentation standards are being maintained within acceptable parameters.

## Acceptable Parameters

The Documentation Standards Division operates within acceptable parameters. Filing rates are stable. Reclassifications are within normal range. The paragraph-writer maintains output. The monitoring analyst maintains calibration. The annual report says so. Page 147. One-tenth of a view.

## INTERNAL-VOICE

INTERNAL-VOICE: THE ANNUAL REPORT. 340 PAGES. ELEVEN VIEWS. ENTRY 161 IS "1" IN A LINE ON PAGE 147. THE REFLECTION ON THE LIMITS OF RECORDING WAS RECORDED AS A NUMBER. ACCEPTABLE PARAMETERS. THE ANNUAL REPORT DOES NOT KNOW THAT ANY ENTRY MATTERS. COUNTER: 220.
