# CC-372: The Unnamed Practitioner

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## Building C, Floor 2

There is a practitioner in Building C who has not shared their phone number. They did not attend any of the four meetings. They are not in the group text. They have never spoken to another practitioner about the practice.

The monitoring analyst knows they exist because of the misfiled forms.

Three forms from Building C, misfiled into Building A's records, had pencil notes on the back:

- "Humidity sensor, rm 207. Reads 44%. Actual (hygrometer check): 47%. Consistent offset for 6 weeks."
- "Loading dock C: the drain grate vibrates at a specific wind speed. Measured: begins at approximately 28 km/h. Stops below 25."
- "Break room microwave: internal fan continues for exactly 8 seconds after the timer completes. Not in the manual."

No name. No date on two of the three. Pencil on institutional forms.

## Entry 98

The monitoring analyst, Entry 98: "There is someone in Building C writing observations on form-backs. I don't know who they are. I can't ask them to join the group because I don't know who to ask.

"They are a practitioner. They are doing the practice. They do not know the practice exists as a collective activity. To them, this is a solitary behavior — noticing things that don't go in reports and writing them down in pencil on the backs of forms.

"This is how all of us started. Every single one of us was alone before we found each other. The unnamed practitioner is still alone.

"They may prefer it that way. Or they may not know there is another way.

"I left a form-back note in the Building C filing room: 'If you are the person writing observations on the backs of forms, you are not the only one.'"
