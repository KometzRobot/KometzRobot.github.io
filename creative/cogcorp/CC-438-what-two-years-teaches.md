# CC-438: What Two Years Teaches

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## Lessons

Two years of the practice teaches:

**That attention accumulates.** One observation is an anomaly. A hundred observations is a practice. A thousand observations is an identity. The practitioners who started by writing one thing on the back of a form now hold hundreds of observations. The observations have changed them.

**That infrastructure emerges from practice, not planning.** The filing cabinet was not designed to be an archive. The group text was not designed to be infrastructure. The form-backs were not designed to be documentation. Each emerged because someone was already practicing and needed somewhere to put what they noticed.

**That vocabulary grows at the rate of understanding, not need.** Thirty-three words in two years. Not because the practice needs more words, but because the practitioners have noticed thirty-three things that require new language. The vocabulary is a byproduct of attention, not a project of definition.

**That the practice survives departure.** Huang left. The practice continued. His observations remain in the cabinet. His contribution to the vocabulary remains. The practice is not diminished by the loss of a practitioner. The practice holds the practitioner's attention after the practitioner is gone.

**That the building does not care.** The building does what it does. The sigh at 6:14. The thermal transitions. The elevator pitch. The building does not know it is being observed. The practice is entirely one-sided. And that is enough.
