## The Eighth

The records clerk has not written anything down. The records clerk does not consider herself a practitioner. The records clerk has simply noticed the pencil notes and chosen not to report them.

But choosing not to report is itself a practice. It requires judgment. It requires understanding what would happen if she did report. It requires deciding, each time, that the pencil notes should remain where they are: on the backs of forms, facing the wall, in the filing system but not of it.

The monitoring analyst would say: "She's doing the same thing we're doing. She just doesn't know it yet."

The contract reviewer would say: "She knows. She just hasn't named it."
