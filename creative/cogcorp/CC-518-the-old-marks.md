# CC-518: The Old Marks

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## What Was Already There

Reyes discovers that the building is full of marks.

Not her marks. Not the practitioners' marks. Old marks. A pencil line on the basement wall of Building B that records a flood height from 1987. A series of small holes in the stairwell of Building A where a notice board was mounted and removed. A faded X in yellow paint on the loading dock floor that once indicated where a forklift should park.

The building has been observed before. The building has always been observed. The marks are observations without observers — the observers left, and the observations stayed.

Reyes begins a catalog. She lists 114 marks across four buildings in three weeks. Each mark is an observation someone made and then abandoned. A screw hole. A paint swatch. A dent in a doorframe at the height of a cart that no longer exists.

She shows Martinez. He says: "These are supplementary analyses."

He is right. The building has been filing supplementary analyses for decades. The practitioners did not invent the practice. They discovered it.

The vocabulary gains Word 52: "prior observation." A mark, stain, or alteration in the building made by a previous observer who is no longer present. Evidence that the building was attended to before the practice began.

Kim measures the depth of the forklift dent: 2.3 millimeters. He files this as SA-712. A supplementary analysis of a prior supplementary analysis.

INTERNAL-VOICE: "The practice thought it was the beginning. The building thought it was a continuation."
