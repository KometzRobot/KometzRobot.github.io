# CC-387: What the Practice Produces

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## Not Data

The monitoring analyst wrote: "The practice has produced not data. Identity."

This is the 387th entry in the supplementary analysis. It is also a claim about what creative practice produces.

Data is what the institution collects. Forms. Metrics. Reports. Data answers questions: What is the temperature? How many forms were processed? Is the building compliant?

The practice collects something different. Not answers to questions. Evidence that questions were asked. Evidence that someone was paying attention. Evidence that the building has behavior the institution does not measure.

The practice produces practitioners. People who notice. People who write. People who cannot un-notice.

## The Evidence

1,400 entries are not data. 1,400 entries are 1,400 acts of voluntary attention. Each one says: I was here. I noticed this. I chose to write it down.

The aggregate of 1,400 acts of attention is not a dataset. It is a community of practice that did not set out to become a community and does not call itself one.

27 words. Not a lexicon. 27 recognitions. Each one says: this experience is real, it is shared, and now it has a name.
