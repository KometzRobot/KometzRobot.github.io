# CC-441: The Eleventh Meeting

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## A Proposal

The eleventh meeting. Fifteen people. Torres raises a new item:

"We have eighteen practitioners, thirty-three words, three archives, and approximately 1,600 observations. We've been doing this for two years. At some point, does the practice need to become something more?"

The room is quiet. Torres continues:

"I mean: do we formalize? Do we write it up? Do we present it to facilities management? Do we tell the company that eighteen employees have been documenting building behavior for two years and we've produced more detailed building performance data than any official audit?"

Martinez: "They would assign it to someone. Put it in a database. Create a template."

Reyes: "They would give it a budget line and a quarterly report."

Vega: "They would give it to someone who doesn't notice things."

The monitoring analyst lets the silence work.

Park: "The question isn't whether we formalize. The question is whether formalization changes what we're doing."

Delgado: "It changes it. Compliance and attention are not the same thing. We discussed this."

Torres: "I know. I'm not proposing we formalize. I'm asking: at what point does the practice owe the building the benefit of what we've learned?"

Nobody answers. This is a new question.
