# CC-477: What the Practice Has Produced

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## An Inventory

Twenty-seven months. What the practice has produced:

**Observations**: approximately 1,800. Across five buildings, four registers, day and night. Growing at approximately 70 per month.

**Practitioners**: 20 confirmed. From monitoring analyst to security guard. From facilities manager to IT specialist. From maintenance technician to janitor. The practice draws from every level of the institution.

**Vocabulary**: 40 words. Evolving from phenomena to relationships. A living language that names what the practitioners see.

**Archives**: 3 physical (filing cabinets in Buildings B, C), 1 digital (Kim's shared drive), numerous desk drawers and pockets and notebooks. Total archived pages: approximately 500+.

**Infrastructure**: group text (12 members), meetings (15 held), form-back system (universal, uncoordinated), filing room drop system (3 buildings).

**Institutional benefit**: 3 resolved maintenance issues, estimated savings of $340 + 40 labor hours, unmeasured infrastructure preservation. Delivered through the membrane of practitioners' official roles.

**Knowledge**: the campus as body, the campus chord, the nocturnal building, the observer effect, four registers of perception, the distinction between compliance and attention, the margin and the text.

**Identity**: 20 people who define themselves partly through their attention to building behavior. The practice produces practitioners.

The practice has produced everything except permission to exist.
