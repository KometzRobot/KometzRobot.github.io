# CC-232: The New Filer

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## Flag #32

The thirty-second flag is different. It is not from Delgado, or the monitoring analyst, or Park, or any of the six filers who have appeared in the queue before. It is from the Pattern Recognition analyst.

SA #330: "The automated vocabulary divergence monitor has been operational for ninety-three days. In that time it has flagged thirty-one filings. I have reviewed all thirty-one. None required action. The system detects moments when filers describe their work in words their department does not typically use. These moments are real. They are not errors. They are not threats. They are the places where observation exceeds the vocabulary assigned to it."

Divergence score: 2.14.

## The System Flags Its Operator

The automated monitor flagged SA #330 because "moments," "real," "exceeds," and "assigned" are not standard Pattern Recognition vocabulary. The system does not know that SA #330 was written by the person who reviews the flags. The system does not know that the filing describes the system itself.

The flag appeared in the review queue on Thursday morning. The analyst opened the queue and found a flag on their own filing. The analyst read their own words through the system's lens: vocabulary divergence, 2.14, top divergent words. The system had done to the analyst what it does to everyone — measured the distance between what was said and what is usually said.

## The Review

The analyst wrote: "Filing exhibits vocabulary divergence consistent with descriptive range expansion. Content describes observational distinction that exceeds standard measurement vocabulary. No action required."

The same review. The same words. Applied to their own filing. The analyst closed the flag and went to get coffee.
