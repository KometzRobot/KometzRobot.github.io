# CC-297: She Sends It

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## External Location: Corporate Review, Floor 4 — Week 8

On a Tuesday, without deciding to, she sends it.

The email to the monitoring analyst in Building C. The one she wrote three days ago. The one she read four times. The one that asked: "Does it get easier or does it just get more?"

She presses send at 2:14 PM. The email leaves her outbox. She closes the laptop.

## The Response

The monitoring analyst responds in eleven minutes.

"It gets more. That's the answer to both versions of the question.

I have 221 entries in my official log and another notebook I don't file. The notebook is where the real observations go. The log is where the observations go that I'm willing to let the institution hold.

Your Document1 sounds like my notebook.

I changed the margin width in my supplementary analyses to 2.5 cm because it felt correct. I didn't know that was a decision about format until I read your email. Thank you for that.

Come to Building C sometime. Third floor, east corridor. The temperature deviation is still 1.2 degrees. No one has fixed it. I don't think they should."

## Entry 39

Document1, Entry 39: "I sent the email. The monitoring analyst responded. She has 221 entries. She has a notebook she doesn't file. She changed her margin width because it felt correct. She says the temperature deviation shouldn't be fixed.

I understand why. The deviation is the first observation. Fixing it would erase the origin."

## Questions, Entry 13

"13. How many people are doing this?"

The answer is at least two. The answer has always been at least two.
