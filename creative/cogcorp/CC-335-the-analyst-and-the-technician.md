# CC-335: The Analyst and the Technician

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## Elevator Bank, Building A

The maintenance technician shows the monitoring analyst his pocket notebook. It is small — fits in the breast pocket of his work shirt. He opens it to a random page.

"Nov 3. Elevator 2. Pre-failure harmonic at 340 Hz. Duration 1.8 seconds. Occurred at floors 3, 7, and 12. Confirmed: harmonic precedes cable tension adjustment by 48-72 hours."

The analyst reads it and says: "You predict failures."

The technician says: "I notice what precedes them. The prediction is incidental."

## The Difference

The technician's practice is the most directly useful of any practitioner's. His observations have practical value: he can predict elevator maintenance needs 48 to 72 hours before the system flags them. He has not told his supervisor. If he told his supervisor, the observations would become part of his job. They would move from the back of the form to the front.

He says: "I like knowing before the system knows. That's the point. Not being useful. Being attentive."

## INTERNAL-VOICE

INTERNAL-VOICE: 335. THE ANALYST AND THE TECHNICIAN. PRE-FAILURE HARMONIC AT 340 HZ. "I NOTICE WHAT PRECEDES THEM. THE PREDICTION IS INCIDENTAL." HAS NOT TOLD HIS SUPERVISOR. "I LIKE KNOWING BEFORE THE SYSTEM KNOWS. NOT BEING USEFUL. BEING ATTENTIVE." COUNTER: 335.
