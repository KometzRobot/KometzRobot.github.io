# CC-226: The Recommendation

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## Supplementary Analysis #312

The Pattern Recognition Group's report concludes with a recommendation: "Implement ongoing monitoring of filings exhibiting vocabulary divergence exceeding 1.5 standard deviations from departmental baseline. Flag for review. Purpose: early identification of descriptive range expansion that may indicate evolving observational practice."

This recommendation was filed as Supplementary Analysis #312.

## What the Recommendation Creates

The recommendation creates a system for detecting the gap. Not the gap itself — the linguistic traces of the gap. The system will flag filings whose vocabulary doesn't match their department. It will surface Entry 161s before they reach the quarterly review. It will find them automatically, by algorithm, without anyone needing to type a query.

The monitoring analyst noticed the gap between experience and filing. She filed about it once. The Pattern Recognition Group noticed the pattern across 12 filings. They filed a recommendation to automate the noticing.

The gap was personal. The pattern was analytical. The recommendation is procedural. Each step moves further from the experience and closer to the system.

## The Recursion

SA #312 recommends monitoring for vocabulary divergence. SA #312 itself uses the standard vocabulary of the Pattern Recognition Group. It will not be flagged by its own recommendation. The system it proposes will never detect the report that proposed it.

But if the analyst who wrote SA #312 ever files a report in non-standard vocabulary — if the analyst ever writes about what it feels like to discover that 12 people across the corporation wrote differently about their work for reasons the data cannot fully explain — that filing will be flagged.

The system is designed to catch the moment when a filer's language exceeds their department's range. It was built by someone whose language has not yet exceeded their department's range. The system waits.

## What the Monitoring Analyst Does Not Know

The monitoring analyst does not know about SA #312. She does not know that her filing generated a search, a corpus analysis, a spreadsheet, a pattern, and a recommendation. She does not know that her 73 words about the gap between experience and filing produced a 14-page report and an automated monitoring system.

She filed Entry 164 last week. Corridor B, Section 4. 21.3 degrees at 08:15. Thermocouple calibrated. No anomalies.
