# CC-321: The Retention Review

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## Quarterly Retention Review — Minutes

Present: Filing Supervisor, Archival Coordinator, Records Management (remote)

Agenda: Standard quarterly review of forms reaching seven-year retention limit.

## Discussion

Filing Supervisor: "1,847 forms flagged for review this quarter. Standard distribution across buildings. No archival flags on any of them."

Archival Coordinator: "Recommendation is bulk shred. Any objections?"

Records Management: "None from my end. Standard procedure."

## Note Not in Minutes

The filing supervisor noticed, while preparing the quarterly count, that approximately 3% of the flagged forms had pencil marks on the back. He did not mention this in the meeting. It was not relevant to the agenda.

He has been filing supervisor for four years. He has always noticed the pencil marks. He assumed they were scratch calculations — someone using the back of a form as scrap paper. He has never read them.

## After the Meeting

The 1,847 forms are moved to the shredding queue. The shredding will occur on Friday. After Friday, whatever was written on the backs of those forms will not exist.

The filing supervisor goes back to his desk. He does not think about the pencil marks. He has a meeting at 2 PM about the new filing software migration.
