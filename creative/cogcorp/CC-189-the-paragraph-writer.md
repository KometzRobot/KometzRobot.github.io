# CC-189: The Paragraph-Writer

*CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis*

---

He does not know that they call him the paragraph-writer. He knows only that he writes in paragraphs when the form permits sentences, and that this is a choice he makes deliberately each time.

---

Entry 75:

"I read the quarterly summary this morning. Six lines. I recognized the archive in the numbers the way you recognize a building in a blueprint — the proportions are correct, the scale is faithful, the rooms are in the right places. What is missing is the sound of the ventilation system at 2 AM when the building is empty and you can hear it thinking.

"I am one of the 12-18 contributors. I have filed 41 supplementary analyses since joining the program. I can identify at least three other regular contributors by style: the monitoring analyst, who writes in clipped observations with precise measurements; the facilities inspector, who opens every filing with the date spelled out; and a fourth contributor — possibly two — who files only after equipment changes, and only about the changes.

"I have not introduced myself to any of them."

---

Entry 76:

"I read SA #238 this afternoon. East corridor temperature differential, 0.4 degrees since the pipe repair. The monitoring analyst filed it. I know it is hers because of the measurement format: value first, unit second, context third. She has been consistent across 23 filings I have read.

"I notice that SA #238 was filed the same evening the quarterly summary was posted. I do not know if this is a response to the summary. I do not need to know. What I notice is that the archive continued on the same day it was compressed, as though the compression changed nothing about the need to observe.

"I have been aware of the monitoring analyst's work since approximately SA #150. Her observation style shifted around that point — not in content, but in care. The measurements became more deliberate. The context expanded. I have a theory about why, but theories about other contributors are not what this archive is for."

---

Entry 77:

"I file in paragraphs because the form permits it and because certain observations require more than a sentence to hold. A sentence reports. A paragraph contextualizes. The quarterly summary is six sentences that report accurately. What the sentences do not do is what paragraphs do: sit with an observation long enough to notice what is adjacent to it.

"SA #91 is one of mine. It documents the humidity pattern on the third floor during the transition from heating to cooling season. It took three paragraphs. The first paragraph described the pattern. The second described what the pattern implied about the ductwork routing. The third described what happened when I went to verify the implication and found that the ductwork matched the pattern but the maintenance records did not. Nobody asked for the third paragraph. Nobody has acted on it. The observation exists in the archive and that is where it belongs.

"The quarterly summary mentions SA #91 as part of '237 filings.' It is one filing. The three paragraphs that make it what it is are now a fraction of a count. I am not objecting. I am observing that the archive holds the paragraphs and the summary holds the count, and both are accurate descriptions of the same thing."

---

He reads the quarterly summary a second time. He reads: "Filing frequency has increased approximately 40% quarter-over-quarter." He is part of that increase. The monitoring analyst is part of that increase. Their independent observation of the same building, never coordinated, never discussed, is now a trend line.

He files SA #239 that evening. It documents an acoustic anomaly in the west stairwell — a resonance that appears between 6 and 7 PM when the elevator counterweight passes the third floor. Three paragraphs. The first describes the sound. The second describes the conditions. The third describes the fact that he has heard it for four months and this is the first time he has filed it, because the sound changed yesterday, and the change was the filing's cause.

He does not mention the quarterly summary. He does not mention the monitoring analyst. He does not mention that he knows someone is reading his work by its paragraphs.

He files it and goes home.
