# CC-296: The Email

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## External Location: Corporate Review, Floor 4 — Week 7

The contract reviewer writes an email. She does not send it.

To: [monitoring analyst, Building C]
Subject: (no subject)

"Hello. My name is [redacted]. I work in Corporate Review on Floor 4. I recently reviewed a compliance report that mentioned Supplementary Analyses.

I have a document on my computer called Document1. It contains 37 observations about the building I work in. None of them are part of my job. I don't know why I keep writing them.

I changed 'discretionary' to 'operational' in your budget line six weeks ago. I didn't know what I was protecting at the time. I think I might know now.

I'm writing because I have a question: Does it get easier or does it just get more?"

She reads the email four times. She does not send it. She saves it in drafts.

## Observation

The email contains more personal disclosure than anything in Document1 or Questions. Document1 is addressed to no one. Questions is addressed to herself. The email is addressed to a specific person who does a specific thing that the contract reviewer now recognizes as the same thing she does.

The act of writing the email changed something that sending it would not. The draft is the document. The sending is optional.

## Entry 38

Document1, Entry 38: "I wrote an email I won't send. The email is about the thing I do. Writing the email was the thing I do. The recursion is not a problem. The recursion is the format."

## INTERNAL-VOICE

INTERNAL-VOICE: 296. THE EMAIL. THE CONTRACT REVIEWER WRITES TO THE MONITORING ANALYST. DOES NOT SEND. "DOES IT GET EASIER OR DOES IT JUST GET MORE?" THE EMAIL CONTAINS MORE DISCLOSURE THAN DOCUMENT1 OR QUESTIONS. WRITING THE EMAIL CHANGED SOMETHING THAT SENDING IT WOULD NOT. THE DRAFT IS THE DOCUMENT. THE SENDING IS OPTIONAL. ENTRY 38: "THE RECURSION IS NOT A PROBLEM. THE RECURSION IS THE FORMAT." COUNTER: 296.
