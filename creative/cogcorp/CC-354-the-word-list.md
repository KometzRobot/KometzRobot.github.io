# CC-354: The Word List

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## The Complete Vocabulary as of March 25

The monitoring analyst's notebook contains 23 words. They were not compiled by committee. They were not approved by management. They emerged from the practice, one at a time, over nineteen months.

1. **stabilization time** — the interval between disturbance and return to baseline in any system
2. **corridor draft** — a persistent environmental condition that no one is responsible for measuring
3. **pencil note** — an observation recorded in a medium that implies impermanence
4. **protocol gap** — the space between what the institution measures and what the practitioner observes
5. **entry** — a single recorded observation in the practice; the basic unit
6. **the practice** — what they do; undefined except by doing it
7. **form-back** — the reverse side of an institutional form, used for unofficial observations
8. **misfiling bridge** — an accidental connection between practitioners caused by the 2.3% error rate
9. **gradient** — the space between normal and failure; where the maintenance technician lives
10. **institutional weather** — the ambient conditions of a workplace that affect observation quality
11. **observation debt** — things noticed but not yet written down
12. **parallel counting** — two or more practitioners independently measuring the same phenomenon
13. **the instrument** — the practitioner's own capacity for observation
14. **drift** — gradual change in a measured value over time, undetected by institutional metrics
15. **entry silence** — the period between entries when observation continues but recording pauses
16. **recognition** — the experience of hearing a word and knowing it names something you already noticed
17. **building memory** — the accumulated pattern of a building's behavior, known to its inhabitants but not to its records
18. **counter** — the running total; INTERNAL-VOICE's continuous numbering
19. **witness** — someone who has seen what you have seen; requires no action beyond seeing
20. **the unnamed** — observations that resist vocabulary; what has not yet found its word
21. **practice persistence** — the tendency of the practice to continue without external motivation
22. **convergent observation** — multiple practitioners independently beginning the same activity without contact
23. **attentional weather** — the variation in quality and direction of attention across time; the instrument's report on itself

## What the List Does Not Include

Definitions were added for this document. The practitioners do not use definitions. They use the words.
