# CC-225: The Pattern

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## The Correlation

The analyst ran a correlation between vocabulary divergence and reclassification. The result: filings with divergence scores above 1.5 are reclassified at a rate of 19.2%. Filings below 1.5 are reclassified at a rate of 3.1%. The correlation is statistically significant (p < 0.01).

This means that filings whose words don't match their department's vocabulary are six times more likely to be moved to a different category. This is not surprising. A filing that doesn't sound like its department probably isn't about its department's subject.

But some of the 73 divergent filings are about their department's subject. Entry 161 is about environmental monitoring. It just describes it in words that Environmental Monitoring doesn't usually use.

## The Category

The analyst created a sub-category: "thematically aligned, linguistically divergent." Filings that belong in their department by subject but not by vocabulary. The analyst reviewed all 73 filings manually and classified 12 as thematically aligned but linguistically divergent.

Entry 161 is one of the 12. SA #289 is another. The coffee machine filing (SA #290) is a third.

12 filings across the entire corpus where the filer wrote about their actual subject in words their department doesn't typically use. 12 moments when the practice deviated from its vocabulary without deviating from its purpose.

## What the Pattern Means

The analyst's report states: "These 12 filings represent instances where departmental personnel used non-standard vocabulary to describe standard observations. The linguistic divergence does not indicate subject-matter confusion. It indicates an expansion of descriptive range within existing practice parameters."

"Expansion of descriptive range." This is the Pattern Recognition Group's term for what the monitoring analyst called "the gap." She said: I experience more than I file. The analyst says: her vocabulary expanded beyond her department's baseline. Same phenomenon. Different language. Different layer of reduction.

## The Report

The analyst's report is 14 pages. It contains 23 tables, 8 charts, and 47 instances of the word "divergence." The report was filed in the Pattern Recognition Group's archive. It was not flagged for vocabulary divergence because it uses exactly the vocabulary the Pattern Recognition Group uses.

The report about divergence is not itself divergent. The report about the gap does not contain a gap. The Pattern Recognition Group's vocabulary is adequate for describing patterns. It is not adequate for experiencing them.

## INTERNAL-VOICE

INTERNAL-VOICE: THE PATTERN. 12 FILINGS. THEMATICALLY ALIGNED, LINGUISTICALLY DIVERGENT. "EXPANSION OF DESCRIPTIVE RANGE." THE PATTERN RECOGNITION GROUP'S TERM FOR THE GAP. THE REPORT ABOUT DIVERGENCE IS NOT ITSELF DIVERGENT. THE REPORT ABOUT THE GAP DOES NOT CONTAIN A GAP. COUNTER: 225.
