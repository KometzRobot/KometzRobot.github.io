# CC-344: The Vocabulary Expands

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## Current Vocabulary

The practice's vocabulary now includes 22 words. The original 18, plus four additions since the second meeting:

19. **stabilization** — the process of arriving at a measurement (contributed by the monitoring analyst after the software expired)
20. **pre-failure** — the observable state that precedes system failure (contributed by the maintenance technician)
21. **persistence** — the property of continuing without institutional support (contributed at the second meeting, by consensus)
22. **convergent** — the property of multiple practitioners arriving at the same activity independently (contributed by the contract reviewer)

## How the Vocabulary Grows

The vocabulary grows when a practitioner notices that an existing word does not describe what they are observing. The monitoring analyst noticed "stabilization" because the software had always given her a final number — she had never seen the process of arriving at it.

The vocabulary does not have a committee. It does not have an approval process. A word becomes part of the vocabulary when more than one practitioner uses it. This typically takes between one week and one month.
