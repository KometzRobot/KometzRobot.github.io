# CC-126: The Budget Line

**Division:** Finance / Documentation Standards
**Classification:** FISCAL YEAR PLANNING — LINE ITEM REVIEW
**Filed by:** Budget Analyst R. Okoro

---

The fiscal year planning document is 247 pages. Every division, every category, every line item. SA appears on page 183 as a single row in a table of forty-seven Documentation Standards subcategories.

The row reads:

| Category | FTE | Budget | Status |
|----------|-----|--------|--------|
| SA | 0.00 | $0 | Active |

R. Okoro, the budget analyst assigned to Documentation Standards, flagged the row during her line-by-line review. Not because of the zero. Dozens of categories have zero budgets — legacy categories, archived projects, placeholder entries. She flagged it because of the word "Active."

## The Question

An active category with zero resources is either an error or an anomaly. Errors get corrected. Anomalies get investigated.

R. Okoro investigated. She pulled the filing metrics (124 filings), the contributor count (31), the cross-reference rate (72%), and the divisional spread (9 divisions). She compared these to the three funded categories in Documentation Standards:

- Operational Standards (OS): 2.0 FTE, $180K budget, 47 filings
- Technical Compliance (TC): 1.5 FTE, $140K budget, 38 filings
- Documentation Review (DR): 3.0 FTE, $220K budget, 52 filings

Combined: 6.5 FTE, $540K, 137 filings.

SA: 0.0 FTE, $0, 124 filings.

She did not write a report. She added a second flag to the row: "ANOMALY — REVIEW RECOMMENDED."

## The Review

The review was conducted by R. Okoro and one senior budget analyst. It lasted twenty minutes. The senior analyst asked three questions:

1. "Is anyone being paid to contribute to SA?" No.
2. "Is SA using resources from other budget lines?" No identifiable resource transfer.
3. "What would happen if we allocated a budget to SA?"

R. Okoro said: "I don't know."

The senior analyst said: "That's the first honest answer I've heard in budget season."

## The Recommendation

The review concluded with a single recommendation: "Maintain current allocation (zero). Category functions without resources. Assigning resources may alter the conditions that enable its function."

This was, as far as anyone could determine, the first time in CogCorp's fiscal history that a budget review had recommended maintaining a zero allocation not because the category was inactive, but because it was too active to risk changing.

R. Okoro added a note to the recommendation: "SA operates on a resource model that the current budget framework does not have a category for. The closest analogue is 'organic growth,' but that term implies cultivation. SA was not cultivated. It was not prevented."

## The Filing

The budget review document was archived in the standard fiscal repository. A copy was filed in the SA system by R. Okoro under the tag: OBSERVATION (FISCAL).

It was the first SA filing to include a pie chart. The pie chart showed SA's share of Documentation Standards' total budget: 0%. And SA's share of Documentation Standards' total output: 47.5%.

The pie chart was viewed eighty-one times in three days. It received zero comments.
