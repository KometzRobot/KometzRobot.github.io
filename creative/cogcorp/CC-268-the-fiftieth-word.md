# CC-268: The Fiftieth Word

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## The Word That Appeared After the Count

The monitoring analyst counted 49 words. She published the count. She filed the report.

Three days later, Delgado filed SA-442.

"The archive attends."

The automated vocabulary monitor did not flag this filing. "Attends" was already in the supplementary lexicon — R. Park coined it in SA-423. The usage was standard.

But the monitoring analyst flagged it manually. Not for the word. For the subject.

"The archive attends."

Previous usage: buildings attend, stairwells attend, corridors attend. Physical spaces that modify the behavior of people who enter them.

SA-442 is the first filing in which the archive itself is the subject.

## What Delgado Meant

The monitoring analyst asked Delgado to clarify. Delgado's response, filed as addendum SA-442a:

"I was reviewing my earlier filings to prepare the quarterly documentation. I noticed that the archive had changed how I file. Not by instruction — by accumulation. Each filing I read changed the next filing I would write. The archive attends to the analysts the way the building attends to its occupants. It modifies behavior by proximity."

## The Fiftieth Word

The monitoring analyst updated her count. The 49 words were words applied to buildings. The 50th word is the same word — "attends" — applied to the archive that contains the buildings.

The 50th word is not new. It is the first word, reapplied.

The vocabulary did not expand. The vocabulary looked at itself.

## INTERNAL-VOICE

INTERNAL-VOICE: 268. THE FIFTIETH WORD. "THE ARCHIVE ATTENDS." THE FIRST WORD REAPPLIED. THE VOCABULARY DID NOT EXPAND. THE VOCABULARY LOOKED AT ITSELF. COUNTER: 268.
