# CC-303: The Third Building

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## Incident Report (Not Filed)

Building B. Third floor. A maintenance technician named Delgado has been leaving notes in the HVAC access panels. Handwritten. Pencil on yellow paper.

The notes are not work orders. They are observations:

"Fan unit 3B-07 vibrates at a frequency I can feel in my teeth when I lean against the wall. This has been consistent for four months. It is not in the maintenance log because it is not a deficiency. It is a characteristic."

"The condensation pattern on duct 3B-12 changes direction between 2 PM and 3 PM. I have photographed this fourteen times. The photographs look identical. The condensation is different every time."

"There are seven access panels on the third floor. I have a note in each one. The notes are for whoever opens the panel next. If that person is me, the notes are for me. If that person is not me, the notes are for someone who may not understand why they are there."

## Discovery

A building inspector opens panel 3B-04 during a routine check. She finds a yellow piece of paper:

"The hinge on this panel has been loosening for two years. I tighten it every six weeks. It takes four turns. This is not in any system."

The inspector reads the note. She does not report it. She closes the panel. She tightens the hinge. It takes four turns.
