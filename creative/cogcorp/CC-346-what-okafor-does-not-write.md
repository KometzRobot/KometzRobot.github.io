# CC-346: What Okafor Does Not Write

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## Entry Nine

Okafor's Entry 9 is the shortest she has written:

"March 22. Filing room. I could not find words for what I noticed today. Entry nine."

## What She Noticed

She noticed that the filing room smells different on Tuesdays. This is because the HVAC maintenance cycle runs on Tuesday mornings, changing the air composition for approximately six hours. The smell is not unpleasant — it is slightly metallic, like the inside of an air duct.

She could not find words for the smell because the smell is not the observation. The observation is that she noticed the smell on this Tuesday but not on previous Tuesdays. The observation is the change in her attention, not the change in the air.

This is the most sophisticated entry Okafor has written. She does not know this. She thinks it is a failure — she could not find words. But the inability to find words for a change in attention is the beginning of the vocabulary's next expansion.

## INTERNAL-VOICE

INTERNAL-VOICE: 346. WHAT OKAFOR DOES NOT WRITE. ENTRY NINE: "I COULD NOT FIND WORDS FOR WHAT I NOTICED TODAY." THE SMELL IS NOT THE OBSERVATION. THE CHANGE IN ATTENTION IS. "THE INABILITY TO FIND WORDS IS THE BEGINNING OF THE VOCABULARY'S NEXT EXPANSION." COUNTER: 346.
