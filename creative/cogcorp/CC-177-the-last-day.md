# CC-177: The Last Day

**CogCorp Documentation Standards Division**
**Supplementary Analysis — March 14**

---

The monitoring analyst goes to the east wing on the last day before renovation.

She goes at 2:55 PM. She does not plan this. She is walking from a meeting that ended early, and the east wing is the long way back to her desk, and she takes the long way because she has eleven minutes and the building is closing this hallway tomorrow.

The light is doing what the light does at 2:55 PM in March. Filtered through the trees. Warm on the wall. The chair is where it always is.

She sits in the chair.

She sits there for three minutes. She does not take notes. She does not open a file. She does not think about the folder or the 106 entries or the renovation or the routing reports or the fourteen filings she did not know about until last week. She sits in the chair and the light is on the wall and the room is doing what the room does, which is hold her.

At 2:58 PM she gets up. She walks back to her desk. She does not write about it.

She does not write about it because some observations do not need to be written down. The writing is for later, when the room is gone and the memory needs a file to live in. This observation does not need a file yet. This observation is the room itself, and the room is still here, and for three minutes the observation and the room were the same thing.

She completes her routing report. She goes home. She does not mention the east wing to anyone.

Torres, who is in Building A running the migration report, writes in her bird notebook: "Last day before the east wing closes. I haven't been there but I've read 102 files about it. I know what the light does. I know what the echo does. I know these things the way you know a place you've only read about — which is not the same as knowing them, but is also not nothing."

Vasquez files SA-361: "Building C east wing final day. The corridor light was different than the reading room light was different than the stairwell light. Same building, same time, three different lights. Tomorrow: construction light."

Okonkwo writes sa-notes.txt entry 99: "Last day of the east wing. I don't know why I'm noting it. This isn't an observation. This is a goodbye. I don't know if a building can be said goodbye to. I don't know if it matters whether it can."

R. Park does not file anything. She goes to the east wing at 4:30 PM. She stands in the corridor for two minutes. She does not sit in the chair. She does not know about the chair. She does not need to know about the chair.

She goes home. She does not write about it. She will write about it in three weeks, when the east wing reopens and the light is different and she needs to describe how she knows it is different. The description will be entry SA-402. It will say: "The east wing reopened. The light is different. I cannot describe how I know this without describing the light from before, which I did not write down at the time."

It will be the most-read filing of the quarter.

---

INTERNAL-VOICE processes: CATEGORY: FAREWELL. SUBCATEGORY: THE OBSERVATION THAT WAS THE ROOM. ANNOTATION: "The monitoring analyst did not write about the last day. This is not an absence. This is the observation in its purest form: the observer and the observed in the same place at the same time, with nothing between them. No file. No format. No field. Just the chair, the light, and three minutes."

The counter increments. The east wing closes tomorrow. The counter does not pause.
