# CC-229: The Ratio

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## Ninety Days

After ninety days, the system has flagged 31 filings. The analyst has reviewed all 31. All 31 reviews conclude with "no action required."

31 flags. 0 actions. The ratio is infinity — infinite flags per action. Or zero — zero actions per flag. Either way, the number describes a system that detects something it does not need to respond to.

## The Pattern Recognition Group's Quarterly Review

The analyst presents the ninety-day report. 31 flagged filings. Distribution across departments. Trend line (slightly increasing — 7 in month one, 11 in month two, 13 in month three). Correlation with filing volume (none — the increase in flags is independent of filing frequency).

The group lead asks: "Are any of these actionable?"

The analyst says: "No."

The group lead asks: "Then why are we monitoring them?"

## The Analyst's Answer

"The recommendation was to implement monitoring for early identification of descriptive range expansion. The monitoring is working. We are identifying descriptive range expansion. The recommendation did not specify what action to take upon identification."

This is true. SA #312 recommended monitoring. SA #312 did not recommend intervention. The system was built to watch, not to act. It watches. It flags. The analyst reviews. The analyst closes. The system continues.

The group lead asks the analyst to draft a follow-up recommendation specifying what action, if any, should be taken when descriptive range expansion is identified.

## The Problem

The analyst opens a new document. Types "Supplementary Analysis #" and pauses. The analyst needs to recommend an action for filings that describe observations in non-standard vocabulary. The available actions are: reclassify, flag for additional review, counsel the filer, do nothing.

Reclassify: Entry 161 was already reclassified. It did not change anything. Reclassification moves the filing, not the observation.

Flag for additional review: The system already flags them. Adding another review adds another reader, not another response.

Counsel the filer: Tell the monitoring analyst to stop noticing that she notices more than she files. Tell Delgado to stop hearing things in the fluorescent lights. This is not a vocabulary problem. This is a perception problem. You cannot counsel someone out of perceiving.

Do nothing: This is already the current practice.

The analyst closes the document without saving.
