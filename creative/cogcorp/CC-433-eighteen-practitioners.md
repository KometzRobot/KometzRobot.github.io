# CC-433: Eighteen Practitioners

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## An Update

K. attends the tenth meeting. Her name is Kim. She works in Building D's IT department. She has been observing building behavior for seven months while running network diagnostics.

"The network traffic patterns correlate with the building sounds," Kim says. "When the HVAC cycles at 6:14 AM, there's a brief electromagnetic interference spike that affects the wifi signal in corridor B. I noticed the wifi disruption first. Then I heard the sigh."

The room absorbs this. Kim has bridged the technical register and the perceptual register. She noticed the data anomaly. Then she noticed the sound.

The monitoring analyst: "You came to the building through the data."

Kim: "I come to everything through the data. Then I look up from the screen and notice that the data has a sound."

Eighteen practitioners. Across five buildings. With 32 words, three archives, a group text, ten meetings, approximately 1,500 documented observations, and one practice that does not end.

The instruction repeats: notice, write, notice.
