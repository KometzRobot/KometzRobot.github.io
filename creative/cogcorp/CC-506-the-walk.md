# CC-506: The Walk

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## Around

The analyst walks around Building B. It takes eleven minutes. She has worked in Building B for six years and has never walked around it.

The walk reveals: a loading dock she did not know existed. A ventilation shaft on the north face. Three windows that belong to the archive room she uses every Tuesday. A crack in the foundation, south corner, approximately four meters long.

She reports: "I knew the building from inside. I did not know the building."

The practice schedules a walk. Every practitioner walks around their own building. Twenty-one practitioners. Five buildings. The walk takes one Saturday morning.

Martinez walks around Building C in nine minutes. He identifies three things from outside that he monitors from inside: the elevator motor housing (roof), the stairwell window (east face), the HVAC exhaust (west face). He says: "The stairwell sigh has a chimney. I can see the chimney."

The walk produces 127 new observations in one morning. More observations than any single meeting has ever produced. The outside is not a supplement to the inside. The outside is a second building — the same structure, observed from a position the practice has never occupied.

Word 47: "the walk." The practice of observing the building from the exterior.
