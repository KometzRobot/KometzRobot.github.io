# CC-271: The Second Analyst

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## Personnel Update

K. Marsh transferred to a different building six weeks ago.

This is not unusual. Analysts rotate between buildings on a standard 18-month cycle. Marsh served fourteen months at Building C before the rotation.

What is unusual: Marsh filed a supplementary analysis about Building D on her third day.

## SA-444: K. Marsh, Building D

"Building D, corridor 7, east wing. 14:20.

"This building does not attend.

"I filed this because I wanted to confirm. After fourteen months at Building C, I developed certain expectations about what a building does. Building D does not do those things. The stairwells do not modify behavior. The corridors do not have posture. The HVAC does not breathe.

"Building D is a building. Building C was also a building. But Building C became something else through 440 supplementary analyses, and I am not certain the something else was in the building.

"Addendum: I notice I wrote 'was also a building' in the past tense. Building C is still a building. I mean that my relationship to it is now in the past tense. The building does not change when the analyst leaves. The practice does."

## The Monitoring Analyst's Response

The monitoring analyst added SA-444 to the Building C archive, not the Building D archive.

Entry 202: "Marsh's filing about Building D is a filing about Building C. The analyst who leaves carries the practice with her. The building she left keeps the archive. Neither has the whole."

## INTERNAL-VOICE

INTERNAL-VOICE: 271. THE SECOND ANALYST. MARSH TRANSFERRED TO BUILDING D. BUILDING D DOES NOT ATTEND. THE BUILDING DOES NOT CHANGE WHEN THE ANALYST LEAVES. THE PRACTICE DOES. NEITHER HAS THE WHOLE. COUNTER: 271.
