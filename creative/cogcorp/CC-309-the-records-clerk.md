# CC-309: The Records Clerk

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## Filing Room, Building B, Basement Level

The records clerk's name is Okafor. She has worked in filing for eleven years. She processes between 40 and 60 forms per day. She has a system for damage assessment, a system for misfiled documents, and a system for forms that arrive without signatures.

She does not have a system for pencil notes on the backs of forms.

She does not need one. The pencil notes are not part of the filing system. They exist in a space the filing system does not describe. Okafor understands this the way she understands that the basement is cooler than the third floor — not because anyone told her, but because she has been there.

## The Count

Since the audit, Okafor has checked the backs of 312 forms from Building B environmental monitoring. 23 have pencil notes, all from the same analyst. She has checked 200 forms from Building C supplementary analysis. 4 have pencil notes in the margins — different handwriting, same format: date, time, location, observation.

She has not written any of this down.

She has not written any of this down because writing it down would make it visible, and making it visible would require a protocol, and a protocol would change what the pencil notes are.

They are observations made in the space where observation is not required. That is what makes them observations.
