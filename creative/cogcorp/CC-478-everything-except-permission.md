# CC-478: Everything Except Permission

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## The Missing Thing

The practice has produced everything except permission to exist.

This is not an oversight. This is a design principle — not designed, but emerged. The practice exists without permission because permission would change the practice. The practice decided this (CC-459). The practice understands this (CC-458). The practice accepts this.

But the missing permission has consequences:

The archives are vulnerable. Okafor's filing cabinet could be emptied during a routine cleanout. Martinez's drawer is in a basement storage room that facilities could reassign at any time. Kim's shared drive folder is labeled "System Diagnostics" — an IT audit could question it.

The practitioners are vulnerable. Twenty people using company time, company resources, company property for unauthorized observations. No formal misconduct. But no formal authorization either.

The vocabulary is unrecorded outside the practice. If the practice ended tomorrow, the 40 words would exist in individual memories and scattered pages. No dictionary. No formal glossary.

The practice has accepted the risk of unauthorized existence. This acceptance is not bravery. It is practical. The alternative — authorization — would end the practice. The risk of unauthorized existence is preferable to the certainty of authorized dissolution.

The practice endures on the understanding that it might not.
