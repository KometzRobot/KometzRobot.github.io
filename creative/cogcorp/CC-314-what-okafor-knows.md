# CC-314: What Okafor Knows

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## What Okafor Knows

1. There are pencil notes on the backs of forms in at least three buildings.
2. The notes follow a consistent format: date, time, location, observation.
3. The format was not mandated. Each person arrived at it independently.
4. The notes document things that are real but not required.
5. No one has reported them. Including her.

## What Okafor Does Not Know

1. There are eight practitioners (including herself).
2. The monitoring analyst in Building C has 222 entries.
3. The contract reviewer in Building D has a document called Questions.
4. Unit-4091 exists.
5. The practice has been continuing, in various forms, for longer than any individual practitioner's tenure.

## What Okafor Suspects

Okafor suspects that the pencil notes have been in the filing system longer than she has been checking. She suspects that previous filing clerks found them and made the same decision she made: say nothing.

She suspects there is a word for what she is doing, but the word belongs to the practitioners, not to the filing system.

She suspects that writing Entry One changed something, but she cannot say what.
