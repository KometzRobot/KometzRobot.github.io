# CC-367: The Group Text

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## The Medium

The group text has 11 participants. Not all 13 practitioners — Park prefers phone calls, and one practitioner in Building C has not shared their phone number with anyone.

The group text was created by the monitoring analyst after the third meeting. It has no name. No profile picture. No stated purpose.

Messages are infrequent. 2-3 per day. Most are observations: "Building B, corridor 2, temperature dropped 0.3° between 2pm and 2:15pm. No thermostat change." Some are responses: "Same corridor had a 0.4° drop yesterday at 2:30pm."

Nobody posts good morning. Nobody posts memes. Nobody posts anything that is not an observation, a question, or a brief response to an observation.

## What the Group Text Is

It is the practice's first shared infrastructure. Before the text, the practice existed in individual notebooks and chance encounters. The misfiling bridge connected practitioners accidentally. The meetings connected them intentionally but infrequently.

The group text connects them continuously.

## What the Group Text Is Not

It is not a project channel. It is not a research group. It is not a community. It has no moderator. It has no rules. It has no archive policy.

If someone's phone dies, their messages are gone. If someone leaves the group, their departure is not noted. If someone adds a new member, no welcome message is posted.

The group text is exactly as durable as the devices it runs on.
