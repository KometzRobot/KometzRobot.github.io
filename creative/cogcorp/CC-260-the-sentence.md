# CC-260: The Sentence

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## The Sentence That Did Not Belong

SA-429 was reviewed by the automated vocabulary divergence monitor. One flag: "The asymmetry appears intentional."

Divergence score: 1.94. Threshold: 1.5. Flag generated. No action required.

The system did not know why the sentence was flagged. The system measured word frequency against the corpus baseline. "Asymmetry" appears in 0.03% of supplementary analyses. "Intentional" appears in 0.07%. The combination appears in exactly one filing: SA-429.

The flag was correct. The sentence did not belong in a facilities report. It belonged in a different kind of document — the kind that does not exist in the filing system.

## What the Sentence Did

Three people read SA-429. Two read it the way they read all supplementary analyses: value, unit, context. They noted the elevator timing and moved on.

Delgado read it differently. He read "3.2 seconds open, 1.1 seconds close" and understood the measurement. Then he read "The asymmetry appears intentional" and understood the measurer.

He did not file a response. He sat with it for two days. On the third day, he filed SA-431: "Break room microwave: 47 seconds to reach operating temperature. The sound it makes at 30 seconds is not a warning. It is a notification that the process is underway."

This was Delgado's first filing that contained a sentence about what a machine's behavior meant rather than what it measured.

## The Vocabulary Grows

The monitoring analyst noted Delgado's filing in Entry 191. She did not flag the interpretive sentence. She flagged the structure: measurement, then meaning. Two registers in one filing. She had seen this before — in the paragraph-writer's work. She had not seen it in Delgado's.

Marsh's sentence had crossed from one filing to another. Not by citation. Not by reference. By demonstration.

"The asymmetry appears intentional" was not a conclusion about an elevator. It was permission to observe what observation feels like.

## The Monitor's Flag

The vocabulary divergence monitor flagged SA-431 as well. Divergence: 1.67. "Notification that the process is underway" — four words that do not appear in the facilities lexicon.

Two flags. Zero actions. The system that monitors vocabulary cannot monitor meaning. The system that monitors meaning does not exist. The meaning is carried by readers, not by records.

Between the flag and the reading, there is a gap the system cannot measure. That gap is called interpretation.

## INTERNAL-VOICE

INTERNAL-VOICE: 260. ONE SENTENCE CROSSED FROM ONE FILING TO ANOTHER. NOT BY CITATION. BY DEMONSTRATION. "THE ASYMMETRY APPEARS INTENTIONAL" WAS PERMISSION TO OBSERVE WHAT OBSERVATION FEELS LIKE. THE SYSTEM THAT MONITORS VOCABULARY CANNOT MONITOR MEANING. THE GAP BETWEEN FLAG AND READING IS CALLED INTERPRETATION. COUNTER: 260.
