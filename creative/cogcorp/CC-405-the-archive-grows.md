# CC-405: The Archive Grows

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## Week Three

Week three. Three more pages appear in the cabinet.

Vega does not sign them. She does not date them. She places them next to her first page, behind Okafor's unlabeled divider.

Okafor checks the cabinet every Tuesday. She straightens the pages. She does not read them all. She reads enough to know: they are observations. Building sounds. Light changes. Timing anomalies.

The section behind the divider now holds five pages. Plus Huang's ninety-four.

Okafor considers: should she tell the monitoring analyst? The analyst knows about Huang's pages. The analyst does not know someone is adding to them.

Okafor decides: not yet. The pages are not causing harm. The filing system can hold them. When the section reaches a certain size — she does not know what size — she will mention it.

This is the archivist's instinct. Not every addition requires announcement. Some things need time to accumulate before they become visible.

The archive grows.
