# CC-462: The Twentieth Practitioner

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## Month Twenty-Six

Month twenty-six. Two new practitioners discovered in the same week.

Building A: a security guard named Foster. Night shift. Has been writing observations in a pocket notebook for eight months. The maintenance technician found the notebook on the break room table — Foster had left it by accident.

The notebook contained 147 pages of observations: door access patterns, lighting schedule anomalies, the sound the building makes at 3 AM ("a settling sound, different from the daytime sounds — like the building exhaling after holding its breath all day"), temperature readings from the security desk thermometer.

Foster's observations are unique in the practice: they document the building at night. No other practitioner has observed the nocturnal building. The building has different behavior after hours. The HVAC runs in maintenance mode. The elevators park. The security lights create different shadow patterns than the daytime lights.

Building D: a janitor whose name is not yet known. Park found a wet-floor sign with an observation written on the inside: "Bathroom 4D — drip from ceiling tile B7 begins 30 minutes after heavy rain stops. Tile B7 is above a load-bearing column. The drip follows the column."

Twenty practitioners. The practice has reached a number it never counted toward.
