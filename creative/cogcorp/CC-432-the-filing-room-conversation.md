# CC-432: The Filing Room Conversation

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## In Pages

The conversation between Park and K. takes place over eleven days, on pages left in the filing room:

P: "I have been listening for eighteen months. I am part of a group. Sixteen people across five buildings."

K: "Sixteen? What do they listen for?"

P: "Everything. Building sounds. Temperature patterns. Timing anomalies. Whatever the building does that isn't in the specifications."

K: "I have been doing this for seven months. I thought I was the only one."

P: "Everyone thinks that at first."

K: "Is there a name for it?"

P: "We call it 'the practice.' There is a vocabulary. Thirty-two words."

K: "What's the first word?"

P: "Thermal transition."

K: "I don't know that one. I know the service entrance whistle. I know the elevator delay on floor 6. I know the way the fluorescent lights in the south corridor change color at 3 PM."

P: "Those are observations. The vocabulary comes later. The observations come first."

K: "Then I've been doing it right."

P: "There is no right. There is noticing and writing."
