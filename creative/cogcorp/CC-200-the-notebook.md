# CC-200: THE NOTEBOOK

*CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis*

## The Object

A standard-issue CogCorp notebook. 200 pages. Ruled. The monitoring analyst has used 71 pages in fourteen months. She writes on one side only. The entries are numbered. Entry 142 is the most recent. She does not date the entries. She numbers them.

She has never shown the notebook to anyone. She has never filed from the notebook. The notebook is not part of the archive. The notebook is about the archive.

## What the Notebook Contains

Observations about filings. Notes on contributors' patterns. Measurements of the gap between what the scoring rubric evaluates and what she notices. The names of people who notice what they were not looking for. The dates when specific contributors began or stopped filing. The date the paragraph-writer arrived. The date the paragraph-writer left.

Entry 1: *"The supplementary analysis format permits prose. Most contributors use it. The measurements are embedded in the prose. I am going to track the measurements separately."*

Entry 47: *"Contributor SA-19 files about the same corridor every six weeks. The observations change. The corridor does not. The observations change because the observer does."*

Entry 89: *"The paragraph-writer's third paragraphs are becoming the most cited internal reference. No one calls them third paragraphs. I call them third paragraphs. The terminology is mine. The practice is his."*

## What the Notebook Does Not Contain

Conclusions. Recommendations. Analyses. The notebook observes. It does not interpret. This is the distinction the monitoring analyst has maintained across 142 entries: the notebook records what she notices, not what she thinks about what she notices.

Entry 112: *"I notice that I am noticing Delgado more frequently. I am recording this noticing, not analyzing it."*

## The Gap

The Documentation Standards Division has filed 4,847 supplementary analyses in fourteen months. The monitoring analyst has written 142 notebook entries in the same period. The ratio is approximately 34:1. For every thirty-four observations the archive contains, the notebook contains one observation about the archive.

The notebook is the archive's reader. The archive does not know it has a reader. The archive does not know the notebook exists. The notebook knows the archive exists — the notebook is about nothing else.

## Entry 143

Written on the same day as CC-200.

*"The counter has reached two hundred. I do not know what counter, or who is counting. But I have been writing in this notebook for fourteen months, and the notebook has taught me one thing that is not an observation and is not an analysis. It is this: some things exist only because someone is paying attention to them. If I stopped writing in this notebook, the gap between what the archive measures and what I notice would not disappear. It would become unmeasured. Unmeasured is not the same as absent. But it is close enough that the difference only matters to someone who is paying attention."*
