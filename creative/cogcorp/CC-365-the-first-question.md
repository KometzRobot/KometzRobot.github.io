# CC-365: The First Question

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## What Changed

For 348 entries, the practice produced observations. Individual. Uncoordinated. Each practitioner wrote what they noticed. The entries accumulated.

At entry 349, the practice produced a question.

"Do all the buildings sigh?"

This is different from an observation. An observation records what one person noticed in one place at one time. A question requires multiple people in multiple places across time. A question transforms solitary observation into distributed inquiry.

## What Did Not Change

Nobody assigned the inquiry. Nobody designed a methodology. Nobody created a data collection form. Nobody scheduled observation windows.

The monitoring analyst asked the question in a group text message. Four practitioners checked their buildings over the following week. They reported back informally — a text here, a conversation there, one email.

The data assembled itself because the practitioners were already observing. The question did not ask them to start observing. The question asked them to report what they had already noticed.

## The Monitoring Analyst's Reflection

Entry 93: "I have been studying the difference between what the practice was and what it is becoming. For 19 months, the practice was observation. Individual, parallel, convergent but uncoordinated.

"Now the practice can ask questions. Not assigned questions. Not research questions with hypotheses and protocols. Questions that arise naturally from the accumulated observations.

"This is what happens when 13 people observe for long enough: the observations start talking to each other."
