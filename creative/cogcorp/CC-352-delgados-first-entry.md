# CC-352: Delgado's First Entry

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## Before the Meeting

Delgado heard about the practice from the maintenance technician in the break room. They work the same shift. The technician did not describe the practice. He said: "I've been writing things down. Things I notice at work that don't go in any report."

Delgado said: "What kind of things?"

The technician said: "The draft in corridor B takes 3.2 seconds to stabilize after the ventilation cycles. Every time. Nobody measures it."

Delgado said: "I've noticed the lights in stairwell C take exactly four flickers before reaching full brightness."

The technician said: "There's a meeting Thursday."

## Entry 1

Delgado, Entry 1: "March 25. Stairwell C. Four flickers. I've been counting them for two years. I never wrote it down before. Now I am writing it down. I don't know why this feels different from counting it in my head, but it does."

## What Delgado Does

Delgado works in Building B, floor 3. Facilities coordination. She schedules maintenance requests, tracks completion times, files work orders. Her job is to measure things. Response times. Completion rates. Outstanding tickets.

She has been measuring stairwell C flickers on her own time, in her own head, for two years.

The difference between her job and her practice is not the measuring. The difference is what gets measured.
