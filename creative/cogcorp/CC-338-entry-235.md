# CC-338: Entry 235

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## Building C, Third Floor, East Corridor

The monitoring analyst's Entry 235:

"March 20. East corridor. Manual reading: 21.1°C. Stabilization: 52 seconds. Third consecutive reading where stabilization time has increased. First reading after software expiry: 47 seconds. Second: 49 seconds. Third: 52 seconds. Either the thermometer is degrading or the corridor temperature is becoming less stable. Both are observations. Neither is on the form."

## What the Entry Contains

The entry contains a trend that the software would have detected automatically. The software would have flagged it: increasing stabilization time, possible sensor degradation. The software would have generated a maintenance request.

The analyst detects the same trend manually. She does not generate a maintenance request. She writes it in her notebook and on the back of the form.

The difference: the software would have treated it as a problem to solve. The analyst treats it as a phenomenon to observe.

## INTERNAL-VOICE

INTERNAL-VOICE: 338. ENTRY 235. STABILIZATION TIME INCREASING: 47, 49, 52 SECONDS. SENSOR DEGRADATION OR CORRIDOR INSTABILITY. "THE SOFTWARE WOULD HAVE TREATED IT AS A PROBLEM TO SOLVE. THE ANALYST TREATS IT AS A PHENOMENON TO OBSERVE." COUNTER: 338.
