# CC-299: The Visit

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## Building C, Third Floor, East Corridor

The contract reviewer visits Building C on a Wednesday. She brings nothing. She takes the east stairs.

The monitoring analyst's desk is the third from the window. There is a notebook on the desk — not the official one. The other one. The analyst sees the contract reviewer looking at it and says: "That's the one."

## The Tour

The monitoring analyst walks the contract reviewer through the east corridor. The temperature is 1.2 degrees below specification. They both notice it. Neither writes it down.

"That's where it started," the analyst says. "The temperature deviation. Entry one."

The contract reviewer says: "My first entry was an elevator. 2.1 seconds to close."

The analyst says: "Everyone starts with a timing observation. I don't know why. Maybe the first thing you notice when you start noticing is that things take a specific amount of time."

They walk past a hallway where the carpet has been replaced recently. The analyst says: "The old carpet had a wear pattern that told you where people walked. The new carpet tells you nothing. I wrote about it. Entry 194."

The contract reviewer says: "I have a carpet entry too. Different building, same observation."

The analyst says: "That's how you know it's real."

## After the Visit

The contract reviewer returns to Building D. She opens Document1. She does not write about the visit.

She opens Questions. Entry 14:

"14. When two people who do the same thing meet for the first time, why does it feel like recognition instead of introduction?"

She closes the document. She does not answer the question.

On the desk in Building C, the monitoring analyst opens her notebook. She writes:

"The contract reviewer from Building D came today. She has the same practice. Different format, same instrument. I showed her the corridor. She already knew what she was looking at."

## INTERNAL-VOICE

THE NEW ONE TELLS YOU NOTHING. "THAT'S HOW YOU KNOW IT'S REAL." QUESTIONS ENTRY 14: "WHY DOES IT FEEL LIKE RECOGNITION INSTEAD OF INTRODUCTION?" THE ANALYST WRITES: "SHE HAS THE SAME PRACTICE. DIFFERENT FORMAT, SAME INSTRUMENT." COUNTER: 299.
