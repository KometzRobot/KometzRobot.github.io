# CC-484: The Sixteenth Meeting

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis

## Expansion

The sixteenth meeting. Eighteen people — the largest attendance yet. Foster trades shifts. The four unnamed regular attendees bring a colleague from Building A: a receptionist named Cho.

Cho: "I don't know why I'm here. Diana said you talk about buildings. I notice things about the building and she said other people here do too."

The monitoring analyst: "What do you notice?"

"The front door. The automatic doors in Building A. They change speed depending on the wind. When the wind comes from the north, the doors close 0.3 seconds faster. When it comes from the south, they hesitate. I've been timing them for a year."

The room absorbs this. Cho has been timing the automatic doors for a year. Without knowing about the practice. Without being invited. Because she is a receptionist and she sits near the doors eight hours a day and she notices things.

Martinez: "Do you know why the south wind makes them hesitate?"

Cho: "The pressure differential. The lobby is positively pressurized. South wind pushes against the outflow. The doors resist."

Martinez looks at the monitoring analyst. Cho has both the perceptual and the technical register. She hears the door and knows why.

The practice now has twenty-one practitioners.
