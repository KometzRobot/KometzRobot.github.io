# Poem 496: Fluency

She didn't use any of the 52 words.
She used all of them.

The vocabulary disappeared
the way water disappears
into soil —
not gone,
but no longer visible
as itself.

You do not cite the concept of gravity
every time you notice
something falling.

The filings carry the vocabulary
the way a room carries light:
you don't see the light.
You see everything else
because of it.

She crossed out "written practice"
and wrote "practice."
Then crossed out "practice"
and wrote nothing.

Then wrote something
that was longer than everything
she had crossed out.

This is what it looks like
when a vocabulary succeeds:
it disappears
into the way people see.
