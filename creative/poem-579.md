# Poem 579: The Second Practitioner

Lin files SA-2,389.
Building D, third floor corridor.
Footsteps sound different
depending on the time of day.

Not shoe type.
Not traffic volume.
The building's thermal expansion
changes the floor's response.

She writes:
"building resonance."
She does not know
the term is three months old.
She does not know
Torres amended it.

She used it because
it was the right word.

Torres used a term
he helped create.
Lin used a term
she inherited.

A language is not operational
until someone who did not build it
can use it
without instruction.

Chen reads the filing.
"She wrote 'the building breathes.'
That's not in the vocabulary."

Okafor:
"Not yet."
