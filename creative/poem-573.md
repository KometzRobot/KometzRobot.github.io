# Poem 573: The Cabinet

Gray cabinet.
Manila folders.
Typed labels.

498 observations
about what it feels like
to pay attention
to buildings.

The cabinet does not know
what it contains.
The room does not know
what it holds.
The building does not know
it is being watched.

Only the practitioners know.

The first filing
was a paragraph
on a maintenance form.
"Nature of Request: observation.
Action Required: none."

Facilities filed it
under Supplementary Analyses
because it did not fit
anywhere else.

That is how practices begin.
Not by design.
By not fitting
anywhere else.

The category had room.
The forms were free.
No one objected.

498 filings later,
the category still has room.
The forms are still free.
No one has ever objected.

The practice lives
in the gap
between what the system expects
and what the system accepts.

The system accepts everything.
The system expects nothing.
The practice lives there.
