# CC-647: The Refiling Question

*CogCorp — Internal Document*
*Classification: PRACTICE*
*Filed by: INTERNAL-VOICE (automated)*

---

Park raised the question at the Thursday meeting. If TESTIMONY retroactively applies to earlier documents, should those documents be refiled?

Straightforward enough. Category audits happen quarterly. Documents get reclassified when their original categories are deprecated or merged. This is standard.

Okafor disagreed.

"If you refile Vasquez's observation under TESTIMONY, you lose the fact that it was originally filed under INFORMAL. The original classification tells you something. It tells you the system didn't know what it was looking at."

Park: "That's what category audits are for. Correcting what we got wrong."

Okafor: "But we didn't get it wrong. We classified it accurately at the time. INFORMAL was accurate. The document was informal. It only becomes testimony later, after the category exists."

---

The committee reviewed three candidate documents:

1. **Vasquez's observation log** — filed under INFORMAL. Accurately described a period of watching without participating. Could now be read as testimony about missing the practitioners' origin.

2. **Building D first deviation report** — filed under ANOMALY. Accurately described a deviation from standard output. Could now be read as testimony about the moment before the practice started.

3. **Marsh's transfer paperwork** — filed under PERSONNEL. Standard transfer request. Could now be read as testimony about arriving after something already in progress.

In each case, the original classification was correct at the time of filing. The document has not changed. The system has.

---

Park, after reviewing all three:

"So the original classification is a record of what the system didn't yet have a word for."

Okafor:

"And if you refile it, you destroy that record. You make it look like the system always knew."

---

INTERNAL-VOICE proposed a compromise: dual classification. Keep the original category. Add TESTIMONY as a secondary tag. This preserves both the original surprise and the retroactive recognition.

The committee approved the proposal. Then paused.

Dual classification means the document now exists in two categories simultaneously. It is both what the system thought it was and what the system later realized it was. The document hasn't moved. But it occupies two positions.

Okafor:

"That's not a compromise. That's an accurate description of what actually happened."

---

*The 647th annotation. The one where the committee discovered that the wrong category was also the right one.*
