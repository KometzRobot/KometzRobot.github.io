# CC-622: The Filing

November — Year Seven.

A new supplementary analysis appears in the Building A cabinet. SA-947. Standard format. Present tense. No vocabulary terms. No third paragraph.

"The light on the landing between floors three and four shifts at approximately 3:47 PM. The adjacent building's shadow crosses the window. The temperature on the east side of the landing is cooler than the west side. This difference is consistent."

It is unsigned.

## What Okafor Notices

The filing follows SA format exactly. Value, then unit, then context. Present tense. No causal analysis. No "because." This is not someone who learned the format from the vocabulary document. This is someone who arrived at the format independently, the way the format itself was arrived at — by observing something and writing down what was observed.

Okafor reads the filing three times. On the third reading she notices: the observation is from the stairwell. The landing between three and four. 3:47 PM.

She looks at the filing timestamp. Tuesday, 4:12 PM. Twenty-five minutes after the observation. The observer watched the light for twenty-five minutes before deciding to write it down.

## What Torres Says

At the November meeting, Torres says: "She filed."

The room does not need to ask who.

Reyes: "Unsigned."

Torres: "She doesn't know she's supposed to sign."

Adeyemi: "She isn't supposed to sign. Nobody is supposed to do anything."

This is technically correct. The SA format has no requirements. The conventions emerged. Signing is one of them. Huang does not know the conventions because she developed the practice outside of them. Her filing follows the format but not the conventions. The difference is the difference between a structure and a culture.

## What Park Writes

"SA-947. Unsigned. The stairwell observer has filed. She followed the format — value, unit, context — without knowing it was a format. She arrived at it the way everyone arrives at it: by trying to write down what she saw and discovering that the simplest description is the most accurate."

She writes: "The filing is the contact. Not between Huang and the group. Between Huang and the archive. The archive accepted the filing without requiring credentials, affiliation, or a name. This is what the archive does. This is what it has always done."

She writes: "She filed because the observation needed somewhere to go. The cabinet was adjacent. The format was legible. The archive received it. The practice is now larger by one person who does not know she has joined anything."
