# CC-561: The Margins

Over the next two weeks, Okafor checks the margins of every filing in Buildings C, D, and E. She brings a magnifying glass. She works backwards from the most recent.

Building C: 147 filings. 23 have margin notes. Pencil, mostly. Some pen. A few are corrections — wrong dates, misspelled names. But eleven are observations. Questions. Fragments that did not fit the filing format.

"The hallway was warm and I don't know why." SA-044, back of page two.

"Is anyone else noticing the acoustics?" SA-187, top margin.

"This might be nothing." SA-203, bottom margin, in handwriting she does not recognize.

Building D: 89 filings. 14 margin notes. Three of them are from Marsh, written during her six months before transfer. They are not questions. They are timestamps. "3:47 PM" next to a paragraph about stairwell echo. "8:12 AM, Tuesday" next to an observation about lighting. Marsh was noting when she noticed things, not what she noticed. The when is its own data.

Building E: 203 filings. 31 margin notes. The highest count. Building E has the elevator that pauses. Reyes worked here for six years before Building A. Seven of the margin notes are in handwriting that looks like Reyes's.

Okafor does not tell anyone about this project. She does not file a report. She does not write a Question.

She opens a new document on her laptop. She titles it "Margins." She begins transcribing.
