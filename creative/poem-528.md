# Poem 528: The Speed of a Building

She counts. I water a fake plant.
The building doesn't care
which one of us is paying attention.

Eighteen years is not a long time
to a building. Six before that
in the building with the elevator pause.
She knows what buildings do.
She knows it by staying still.

What is the speed of a building?
Slower than counting.
Slower than watering.
Exactly the speed of sitting
in a corridor long enough
for the nothing to develop texture.

They don't exchange names.
The notebook doesn't ask for names.
The building has been here
longer than either of them
and does not know their names
and does not need to.
