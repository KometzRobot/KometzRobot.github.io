# CC-593: Year Five Begins

January — Year Five.

The January meeting is the first meeting of Year Five. No one says "Year Five." No one acknowledges the transition. The meeting begins at 2:00 PM on the second Tuesday, which is when it always begins.

Twelve present. The full complement. Even Reyes, who missed five meetings in Year Four and came back without explanation.

Okafor opens with the count: "We have 498 filings from last year. We are on pace to finish this year below 500 again. The vocabulary has 81 terms. We have one new Building F observation about the ventilation cycling — Yun, would you like to present?"

## Yun's Observation

Yun stands. She is the youngest practitioner by seven years and the one who proposed "building memory" and "building weight." She does not seem nervous anymore. She seemed nervous at her first meeting, eighteen months ago.

"Building F's ventilation cycles every ninety seconds. The pause between cycles is three seconds. In that three seconds, you can hear the building's natural ambient. It's different from every other building I've been in. Building F sounds empty even when it's full."

Chen: "Because it's new."

Yun: "That's what I thought. But Building A sounded like Building A from the first filing. Torres's SA-4 describes the same stairwell that SA-350 describes. Buildings don't develop their sound. They have it."

Torres: "She's right."

## The New Term

Term 82 is proposed: "building voice." The inherent acoustic character of a structure, distinct from sounds made within it. Not noise. Not silence. The sound the building makes by existing.

The vote is 11-1. Torres votes no.

"I don't disagree," he says. "I just think we should sit with it for a month."

The vote is tabled until February. No one objects. Torres has been doing this for five years. He has earned the right to ask for a month.

Park writes: "January. Year Five. Torres asks for a month. He has earned it."
