# Poem 509: The Other Questions

Forty-seven questions
in the third notebook.
Kim reads them all.
Twenty minutes of silence.

Number 3:
Did we find the edges
of what buildings do,
or the edges
of what we can describe?

Number 12:
What if someone
in those buildings
decided not to look
at us?

Number 23:
When does the building's record
become the primary record
and our databases
become the supplement?

Number 38:
Is the unfiled material
the actual practice,
and the filings
the translation?

Number 44:
Did we create the practice,
or create visibility
for a practice
that already existed?

Number 47:
What did the buildings do
before anyone was watching?

Kim closes the notebook.
"Number forty-four is the one."

"I know."

"You put forty-seven last
because it's unanswerable.
You put forty-four near the end
because it's almost answerable,
and the almost is worse."

Did the format
change the attention?

"I don't know.
That's why it's in the notebook
and not in a filing."
