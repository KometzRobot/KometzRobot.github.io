# Poem 515: What the Spreadsheet Found

814 supplementary analyses.
11 buildings.
54 vocabulary terms.
47 practitioners.

Building E: 203 filings.
Building A: 12.

Not because A
has less to observe —
it is the oldest,
the most history
embedded in surfaces.

The difference is personnel.
Building E has Chen.
Building A has no one.

Observation depends on presence,
not on what the building does.

Filing spikes
after new vocabulary.
"Acoustic expectation"
doubled sound observations
for two months.
Then baseline.

The vocabulary
does not just name
what people observe.
It directs attention.
New words create
new observations.

Top five terms:
60% of all filings.
Bottom twenty:
less than 1% each.

General terms
apply broadly.
Specific terms
describe exactly.
The vocabulary lives
in the tension.

She saves the spreadsheet
on her laptop.
Folder: "Observations
about observations."

She does not notice
what she has done.
