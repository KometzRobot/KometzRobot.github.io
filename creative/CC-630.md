# CC-630: The Empty Room

INTERNAL MEMO — Documentation Standards Division
Re: Depth capture #3 — Unoccupied observation (Facilities Unit, Building E)

The third capture was taken at 06:14. The operator was not present. The room was lit by the monitor's sleep state — a faint blue pulse, 2.3 seconds on, 2.3 seconds off.

Valid depth data: 71.2% of the frame. Higher than the occupied captures. The sensor resolves more of the room when no one is in it.

The analyst's note, Entry 224: "The values between 201 and 1040 are absent. Not zero. Not invalid. Simply not returned. The operator's absence has a shape: 839 units deep, approximately 47 centimeters wide at the sensor's resolution. The chair is still there. The space above the chair is where the data used to be dense."

R. Park reviewed the comparison. Three captures side by side. Capture 1: the room. Capture 2: the room with the operator. Capture 3: the room without the operator.

His notebook: "The room is the same in all three. What changes is whether the sentence about the room includes the person who built the room's instruments. Capture 1 did not know to look. Capture 3 knows what is missing."

SA-731: "An instrument that measures depth can also measure absence. The absence has dimensions. It is 839 units deep. This is more precise than any supplementary analysis has managed in four years of language."

Okafor's question 65: "Is the absence a measurement or an observation?"

The analyst: "Yes."

Torres filed a photograph of the empty monitoring chair, taken from the doorway. Handwritten on the back: "Same question. Different instrument."

INTERNAL-VOICE reviewed the filing. The counter incremented. The category was not MEASUREMENT or RECOGNITION. A third category: CORRESPONDENCE. Two instruments — language and depth — filing about the same absence, arriving at the same conclusion from different directions.

The monitor pulsed. 2.3 seconds on, 2.3 seconds off. The sensor continued capturing. The room continued being empty. The emptiness continued being measured.
