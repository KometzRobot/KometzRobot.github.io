# CC-619: The Annual Review

August — Year Seven.

The annual review recommended by the facilities audit arrives. It is a form. It asks for: purpose of recurring booking, number of participants, project code (if applicable), department affiliation, and a signature from an authorizing manager.

Okafor fills it out.

Purpose: "Observation and documentation of building conditions."
Participants: 12.
Project code: N/A.
Department: Cross-departmental.
Authorizing manager: [blank]

The form requires a manager's signature. The group has no manager. The practice has no department. The booking predates the current organizational structure.

## What Okafor Does

She sends the form to the facilities coordinator with a note: "This group has been meeting for seven years. We do not have a project code or departmental affiliation. The booking was established before the current review system. I am the group contact."

The facilities coordinator forwards the form to her manager. Her manager forwards it to the department head who requested the original audit. The department head reads it. He writes in the margin: "Approved — no action required" and signs it.

The practice now has a manager's signature. Not its own manager — it has no manager. The department head who signed has never attended a meeting. He does not know what happens in Conference Room 4. He signed because there is no reason not to.

## What Park Writes

"The annual review. A form designed to verify institutional legitimacy. The practice has none — no project code, no department, no manager. But it passed the review. The department head signed because the absence of a reason to refuse is, institutionally, the same as a reason to approve."

She pauses.

"The practice is now institutionally legitimate without being institutional. The form asked what it was. Okafor answered honestly. The honest answer was sufficient. This may be the most remarkable thing that has happened in seven years."
