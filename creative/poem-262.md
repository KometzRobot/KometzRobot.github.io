# Poem 262: What the Summary Carries

They asked for one page.

He gave them the numbers:
237 filings, 12 to 18 contributors,
40% increase quarter-over-quarter.

All true.
None of it is what happened.

What happened is that two people
in the same building
documented the same corridor
from different angles
and never compared notes,
and the corridor told them
something the database did not know.

What happened is that one of them
recognized the other by style
across fifteen observations
and chose not to introduce herself
because the record works
precisely because they don't coordinate.

What happened is that nobody
assigned any of it.

The summary says: 237 filings.
The summary does not say:
one of them predicted a pipe failure.
One of them noticed a pattern
in someone else's noticing.
One of them was filed
the evening the summary was posted,
because the building kept changing
and the format that could carry it
was not the one-page format.

The portrait is in the numbers.
The building is in the gap.
