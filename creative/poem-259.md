# Poem 259: Threshold

One person writes fragments.
One person writes paragraphs.
One person writes with disclaimers.
Nobody assigned the division.

147 filed observations.
She has read every one.
Some she wrote.
Some she recognized.
Some describe things
she has never noticed.

The archive knows the building
better than anyone inside it.

That's the threshold —
not a number
but a crossing:
the moment the record
exceeds the recorder,
and the building is described
more completely
by the people who weren't asked
than by the database
that was.
