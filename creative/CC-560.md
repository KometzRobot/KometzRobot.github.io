# CC-560: The Return

March. Okafor returns to Building C. She has been here every week for three years but today the corridor feels different. It is not different. She is.

She stands in the lobby and looks at the filing cabinet where SA documents are stored. Building C has 147 filings in the system. 23 vocabulary terms originated here. Four registered practitioners. It is the second-most active building in the monitoring network.

She opens the cabinet. Pulls SA-891. Chen's filing. "Functional drift." She reads the three pages she has read six times before. This time she reads the margins. Chen wrote in the margins. Pencil. Light enough that the scan missed it.

"Does the building know?"

Three words. In the margin of a filing about elevator hesitation. Chen asked the same question Okafor asked in Building A. Chen asked it two years ago. In pencil. In a margin that the scanner didn't capture.

She checks SA-588. Davis's filing. The one with no vocabulary. She reads the back of the page. Nothing. She checks SA-312. R. Park's first filing. The back is blank.

She checks SA-001. The first filing in the system. Anonymous. January, Year One. She reads the back of the page.

Someone has written: "I notice things about this building. I don't know where to put this."

The filing system has always had a blue folder. It was just written on the backs of pages no one turned over.
