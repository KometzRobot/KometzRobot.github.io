# Poem 520: The Blue Folder

Five documents.
Five reasons they don't fit.

Before — Reeves's forms
from before the vocabulary.

Outside — unsigned notes
about Monday smells,
no SA number, no date.

Visual — a photograph.
Something about the light.

In use — the notebook
where she writes questions.

After — Chen's page three.
The system examining itself.

The blue folder
is the negative space
of the archive.

It holds what the practice
produces
but cannot classify.

Not because the documents
are deficient —
because the classification system
is shaped,
and these fall
outside its shape.

She considers creating
vocabulary for the folder.

She does not.

Naming the blue folder
would dissolve it.
The folder exists
because the vocabulary
doesn't reach here.

Fifty-five terms.
Forty-seven practitioners.
Eight hundred and fourteen filings.
Eleven buildings.
Fifty-one questions.

And one blue folder
that will never
have a number.
