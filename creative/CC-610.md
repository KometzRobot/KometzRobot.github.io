# CC-610: What Huang Writes

January — Year Seven.

Huang has been making observations for eight months. She has 47 entries on her laptop. She does not know the practice exists. She does not know the cabinet exists. She found the cabinet in July and has not returned to it.

But her notes have changed. They look like the SA filings now. Not because she is copying them — she looked at them once, six months ago, for twenty minutes. The format propagated. Observation, location, conditions, duration. She added her own field: "Possible cause." The SA filings do not have this field. The practitioners do not speculate about causes. They record what they see.

Huang speculates. She is an Environmental Systems Analyst. Speculating about causes is her job.

## Entry 47

Entry 47 reads: "Building D, east corridor, ground floor. Acoustic anomaly: low-frequency intermittent pulse, approximately 0.3 Hz, detectable by palm contact with wall surface. Occurs between 6:00 AM and 8:00 AM. Not present after 10:00 AM. Duration: unknown — have only observed morning shift. Possible cause: thermal cycling in subfloor conduit during transition from overnight heating to daytime ventilation."

She does not know that Torres filed a similar observation in Year Two. SA-847. His read: "Building D, east corridor. Wall vibrates in the morning. Stops by mid-morning."

Torres's filing is six words shorter and five years older. It does not include a possible cause. It does not need one. The observation was the point.

## What This Means

Two people, five years apart, touching the same wall. One is a practitioner with five years of practice. One is an analyst with eight months of notes. The wall does not know the difference.

The practice has a thirteenth observer. She does not know she is the thirteenth. She does not know there is a count.
