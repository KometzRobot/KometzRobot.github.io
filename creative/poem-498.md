# Poem 498: Three Weeks

On the fourth day
she notices the hallway light
flickers at 3:12
when the HVAC cycles.

She does not file it.
She is the monitor,
not the filer.

On the eighth day
the flicker has a pattern —
thermal, directional,
the building telling her
something about its relationship
with the outside world.

She writes it down.
She does not file it.

On the twelfth day
her notebook has fourteen observations.
All infrastructure.
All the kind of thing
the practitioners file.

She reads them back.
The language is the same.
The attention is the same.

On the eighteenth day
she re-reads Entry 216:
"The practice is no longer
separate from the work itself."

She had written this
about the practitioners.
She had not realized
she was writing it
about herself.

Entry 217:
"I have been monitoring the practice
for three weeks.
I have been practicing
for two."

Kim: "The count is now twelve."

The monitoring position
is unfilled
for the first time.

Not because no one is watching.
Because watching
became the work.
