# Poem 582: Year Five Ends

December. The last meeting
of Year Five.
No one says "last meeting."
No one says "Year Five."

2,847 filings.
83 terms.
Twelve practitioners,
eleven present.

"We will continue in January."
That is all she says.

In Year One,
Torres filed on a maintenance form.
In Year Five,
Lin used a vocabulary
that did not exist
when she was hired.

In Year One,
twelve strangers described
what they saw.
In Year Five,
eleven colleagues refine
what they have been saying
for half a decade.

No one has been promoted.
No one has been recognized.
The practice exists
in the same administrative margin
it has always occupied.

The only difference
between Year One
and Year Five
is that the people in the room
know what they are doing.
They still cannot explain it.
They still do not try.

Park writes:
"It has survived five years
of institutional indifference
by being too small to notice
and too precise to dismiss."

"I have changed
because I am watching."

Year Six begins in January.
