# CC-646: The Third Category

*CogCorp — Internal Document*
*Classification: METADATA REVIEW*
*Filed by: INTERNAL-VOICE (automated)*

---

Quarterly category audit. Three new categories this quarter:

1. **SELF-REFERENTIAL** (created during the Vasquez incident, week 3)
2. **PRACTICE** (created during the practitioners' meetings, week 7)
3. **TESTIMONY** (created during the errata circulation, week 11)

The first two categories describe activities. Things the system does. SELF-REFERENTIAL covers documents that refer to themselves. PRACTICE covers activities that have no output other than their own repetition.

TESTIMONY is different. It does not describe an activity. It describes a position.

A testimony is produced by someone who arrived after the change. They cannot describe the before. They can only describe the evidence of change — the marks left by something they didn't witness. The fourteen who received the errata without the manual are not reporters. They are witnesses to an absence.

This is a new kind of document for INTERNAL-VOICE to classify. Previous categories assumed the filer was present for the event. TESTIMONY assumes the filer was not.

---

Audit note: all three categories were created in response to edge cases. Documents that did not fit existing classifications. The system does not create categories proactively. It creates them when a document arrives that has no place.

This means the category structure is not a map of what the system knows. It is a map of what the system has been surprised by.

SELF-REFERENTIAL: the system was surprised by recursion.
PRACTICE: the system was surprised by purposelessness.
TESTIMONY: the system was surprised by lateness.

---

Park, reviewing the audit:

"The categories aren't getting more abstract. They're getting more honest. SELF-REFERENTIAL acknowledges that the system watches itself. PRACTICE acknowledges that the system does things that don't produce results. TESTIMONY acknowledges that the system contains people who missed the beginning."

Okafor:

"Every filing system is a record of what confused it."

---

INTERNAL-VOICE has noted that the TESTIMONY category, once created, retroactively applies to several earlier documents. Building D's first deviation report. Vasquez's month of observation. Marsh's transfer paperwork.

These were filed under other categories at the time. They could now be refiled under TESTIMONY. INTERNAL-VOICE has not refiled them. It has noted the applicability.

The difference between noting and acting on a note is itself a kind of testimony.
