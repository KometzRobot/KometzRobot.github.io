# Poem 518: The Fifty-Fifth Word

Chen files three pages
instead of one.

Freight elevator, Building E.
A hesitation
at floors 2 and 5.

People wait
without pressing the call button.
The hesitation is long enough
that the doors sometimes open
before continuing.

Existing vocabulary:
"Temporal signature" — the rhythm.
"Implicit navigation" — the adaptation.
"Acoustic expectation" — the sound.

All three apply.
None of them are wrong.

But the observation is not
about the rhythm,
the behavior,
or the sound.

It is about
the relationship between
the building's mechanical error
and the occupants' adaptation.

The error became a feature.
The feature was never designed.
The adaptation was never taught.

Term 55:
"Functional drift."

The process by which
an unintended building behavior
becomes an anticipated function
through occupant adaptation.

The unnamed middle step
between what a building
does accidentally
and what a building does
because people expect it.

First new term
in seven months.

The vocabulary grew
because silence
became unbearable.
