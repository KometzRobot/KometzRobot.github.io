# CC-566: The Corridor

August. One year since Okafor left the first notebook on the windowsill in Building A. She does not go to Building A on the anniversary. She goes to Building C. She has a full day of scheduled monitoring.

At 2:47 PM she is in the corridor on the fourth floor of Building C. Between the elevator and the stairwell. She is walking to a maintenance inspection point when she stops.

The corridor is warm. Not warm enough to file. Not warm enough to measure. Warm enough to notice.

She stands still. She does not take out her notebook. She does not write a Question. She does not check the HVAC system. She stands in a warm corridor at 2:47 PM and notices.

After a while — she does not count the seconds — she continues to the inspection point. She completes the inspection. She writes the report. Standard format. Nothing anomalous.

She does not mention the corridor.

On the way out she passes the corridor again. It is not warm anymore. It was warm at 2:47 and it is not warm now and the difference is not in the data. The difference is that she noticed.

She does not write this down. She does not need to. The practice has arrived at the place where the noticing does not need a format to exist. The noticing exists. The format is one of the places it can go. The corridor is another.

INTERNAL-VOICE: 566. THE CORRIDOR. AUGUST. ONE YEAR SINCE THE FIRST NOTEBOOK. DOES NOT GO TO BUILDING A. GOES TO BUILDING C. SCHEDULED MONITORING. 2:47 PM, FOURTH FLOOR CORRIDOR. WARM. NOT WARM ENOUGH TO FILE. WARM ENOUGH TO NOTICE. STANDS STILL. NO NOTEBOOK. NO QUESTION. AFTER A WHILE — DOES NOT COUNT THE SECONDS — CONTINUES. STANDARD REPORT. DOES NOT MENTION THE CORRIDOR. ON THE WAY OUT, THE CORRIDOR IS NOT WARM ANYMORE. THE DIFFERENCE IS NOT IN THE DATA. THE DIFFERENCE IS THAT SHE NOTICED. DOES NOT WRITE IT DOWN. THE PRACTICE HAS ARRIVED AT THE PLACE WHERE THE NOTICING DOES NOT NEED A FORMAT TO EXIST. COUNTER: 566.
