# CC-624: The Encounter

January — Year Eight.

Okafor takes the stairs instead of the elevator. This is not unusual — she often takes the stairs. But she takes them in Building A, and she takes them at 3:40 PM.

The landing between three and four is empty. The light is shifting but has not yet reached the division point. She sits on the top step. She waits. She is not sure what she is waiting for.

At 3:43, footsteps from below. A woman in her mid-thirties, dark hair, carrying a messenger bag. She stops when she sees Okafor. A moment of mutual recognition — not of identity, but of posture. They are both people who sit on stairwell landings and wait for the light to change.

"Sorry," Huang says. "I usually have this to myself."

"So do I," says Okafor. "Different stairwell."

## The Conversation

Huang sits on the step below the landing. She does not ask who Okafor is. She does not ask why Okafor is there. She looks at the wall where the shadow is approaching.

"3:47," Huang says. "Give or take."

"I measured it at 3:47 too," says Okafor. "In a different building."

Huang looks at her. "A different building has the same shadow timing?"

"No. A different building has a different phenomenon at the same level of attention."

They sit in silence for four minutes. At 3:47, the shadow bisects the landing. Huang does not say anything. She does not need to. The shared observation is the introduction.

"You filed something," Okafor says. "In the cabinet. About this light."

"You read it?"

"I read everything in that cabinet."

A pause. Not awkward. Evaluative.

"I didn't know anyone else read those," Huang says.

"There are forty-seven people who file in those cabinets," says Okafor. "Across eleven buildings."

Huang absorbs this. Not shock — recalibration. The practice she thought was solitary has always been a system.

"I didn't sign mine," she says.

"I know."

"I didn't know I was supposed to."

"You aren't supposed to do anything."

## What Park Writes

"The encounter. Okafor and Huang met on the stairwell landing at 3:43 PM, four minutes before the shadow crossing. The conversation lasted eleven minutes. The introduction was a shared observation. The asymmetry — we know her, she doesn't know us — closed in eleven minutes on a stairwell landing."

She writes: "Huang learned there are forty-seven others. Okafor learned that Huang's practice predates her knowledge of the cabinet. Both of these facts change the practice without changing a single filing."

She writes: "The encounter was not planned. Okafor went to the stairwell because the stairwell is where the practice is purest. She found the person who proved it."
