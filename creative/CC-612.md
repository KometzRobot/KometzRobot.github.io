# CC-612: What Huang Counts

March — Year Seven.

Huang counts. It is what she does — she is an analyst. She goes back to the cabinet on a Tuesday afternoon. She counts the filings in the first drawer. 412. She counts the second drawer. 398. She opens a third drawer she had not noticed. 387.

There are four drawers. She counts them all. 1,587 filings in this cabinet. But the numbering goes to 3,078. Half the filings are somewhere else.

She looks at the room. There is one other cabinet in Conference Room 4. She opens it.

Maintenance records. Old maintenance records from 2014-2018. This is what she was looking for in July when she opened the wrong cabinet. She was looking for these. She found the filings instead.

The other half of the filings — 1,491 by her count — are not in this room.

## What This Tells Her

Someone started this collection. Someone numbered it. Someone is still adding to it — the most recent filing is dated two weeks ago. There are at least 3,078 observations, filed by hand, stored in an unlocked cabinet in a room anyone can book.

She checks the booking calendar on the company intranet. Conference Room 4 is booked every second Tuesday, 3:00-4:30 PM. "Cross-departmental working group." The next meeting is in eight days.

She does not book the room. She does not add herself to the meeting. She writes down the date.

## What She Does Not Do

She does not ask anyone about the filings. She does not search for "SA filings" on the company intranet. She does not mention the cabinet to her manager or her colleagues in Building D.

She adds Entry 48 to her laptop: "Conference Room 4 contains approximately 1,587 structured observation filings (SA-numbered series, total count at least 3,078). Filing format: location, observation, conditions, duration. No causal analysis. No author attribution. Most recent filing: two weeks old. Regular meeting associated with the room: second Tuesday, cross-departmental working group."

It is her longest entry. It is the first entry about the practice itself rather than about a building.

She does not know this is a different kind of observation.
