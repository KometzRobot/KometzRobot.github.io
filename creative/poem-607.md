# Poem 607: Unsigned

A new filing appears
in the cabinet.
Standard format.
Present tense.
Unsigned.

The light on the landing
between floors three and four
shifts at approximately 3:47 PM.

She watched for twenty-five minutes
before deciding to write it down.

She followed the format
without knowing it was a format.
She arrived at it the way
everyone arrives at it:

by trying to write down what she saw
and discovering
that the simplest description
is the most accurate.

The filing is the contact.
Not between the observer
and the group.
Between the observer
and the archive.

The archive accepted it
without requiring
credentials, affiliation,
or a name.

This is what the archive does.
This is what it has always done.
