# CC-623: The Second Filing

December — Year Seven.

SA-961. Unsigned.

"The radiator in the north corridor activates fourteen seconds before the thermostat reading changes. The building anticipates the temperature."

Shorter than the first. More precise. The twenty-five minutes between observation and filing has become four minutes. The practice is compressing. Not because Huang is hurrying but because the distance between noticing and writing is shrinking.

## SA-968. Unsigned.

"The elevator on the east side pauses 0.7 seconds longer at the ground floor than at any other floor. The pause is independent of passenger load."

She has started measuring. Not with instruments — she has no instruments. She counts. She has counted the elevator pause enough times to know it is 0.7 seconds and not 0.5 or 1.0. The precision is not technical. It is attentional. She has watched the elevator enough times that the number arrived.

## What Changes

Three unsigned filings in six weeks. The monitoring analyst notes them in her log. She does not flag them. Unsigned filings are not violations — there is nothing to violate. But they are unusual. In 4,960 filings over seven years, 4,947 are signed.

The thirteen unsigned filings include: Huang's three, plus ten from Year One and Year Two, when the convention of signing had not yet solidified. The unsigned filings bracket the archive — at the beginning, before the culture formed, and now, from outside it.

## What Okafor Does Not Do

Okafor does not go to the stairwell. She does not leave a note in the cabinet. She does not ask facilities for the name of anyone with regular access to floors three and four.

She reads SA-968 again. The elevator observation. She has measured the same elevator. Her measurement was 0.8 seconds. The difference is real — it depends on where you stand, which tells you when you start counting.

She writes in her notebook: "Her 0.7 is as correct as my 0.8. The difference is not error. The difference is position."

## What Park Writes

"Three unsigned filings. The practice continues to grow from the stairwell. Huang does not know the conventions because she is developing her own. Unsigned. Uninstructed. The archive holds her observations alongside ours without distinction. This is the test: does the archive treat her filings differently? It does not. The format is democratic. The culture is not. We know her observations. She does not know ours. The asymmetry is the last distance."
