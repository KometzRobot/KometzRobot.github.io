# CC-597: The Second Practitioner

May — Year Five.

Lin files SA-2,389. Building D, third floor corridor. She describes the way footsteps sound different depending on the time of day — not because of shoe type or foot traffic volume, but because the building's thermal expansion changes the floor's acoustic response.

She writes: "The corridor has a building resonance that is temperature-dependent. Morning: tight. Afternoon: loose. The building breathes."

## What This Means

Lin is the second practitioner to use the term "building resonance" in a filing. She joined eighteen months ago. She learned the vocabulary by attending meetings. She did not know the term was new. She did not know Torres had amended it from "building voice" three months ago. She used it because it was the right word.

This is different from Torres's first use. Torres used a term he helped create. Lin used a term she inherited. Torres's use confirmed the vocabulary worked. Lin's use confirmed the vocabulary transferred.

A language is not operational until someone who did not build it can use it without instruction.

## What Okafor Records

Okafor adds a note to her records: "SA-2,389. Second operational use of Term 82. Filed by Lin. No instruction given. The vocabulary is transferring."

She pauses, then adds: "The vocabulary was built by the original practitioners over four years. Lin acquired it in eighteen months. The acquisition rate is accelerating. The language is easier to learn than it was to build."

Chen, reading over the weekly filings: "She wrote 'the building breathes.' That's not in the vocabulary."

Okafor: "Not yet."
