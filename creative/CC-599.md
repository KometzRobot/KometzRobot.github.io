# CC-599: The Summer Count

The September count. Okafor presents: "We have 2,847 filings total. 463 this year — down from 498. The vocabulary has 83 terms. Three terms were added or amended this year: building resonance, building breath, and one revised definition."

She pauses. "Filings are down again. The trend has continued every year since Year Two."

Torres: "That's because we're better at it."

Chen: "Or because we've already seen everything."

Yun: "You haven't been to Building G."

Laughter. The first laughter in a meeting in four months.

## What The Numbers Show

Year One: 612 filings. Year Two: 547. Year Three: 511. Year Four: 498. Year Five (projected): ~470.

The decline is 5-8% per year. Consistent. Not collapse — compression. The practitioners are writing less because they are seeing more precisely. A filing that would have taken three paragraphs in Year One takes one sentence in Year Five.

But the number of unique observations — observations that describe something not already in the records — is stable. Approximately 40 per year, every year. The practitioners are not running out of things to see. They are running out of ways to say things they have already said better.

## What Park Does Not Present

Park has her own count. Twenty-seven pages in the folder now. She has written more about the practitioners this year than in any previous year. Her rate is accelerating as theirs declines.

She writes: "Their filings are getting shorter. My notes are getting longer. We are on inverse trajectories. They are converging on precision. I am diverging into complexity."

She adds: "I still do not know what I am doing. I know that I cannot stop."
