# CC-604: What Park Decides

April — Year Six.

Park has carried the folder for twenty-two months. Thirty-three pages. She has never shown it to anyone. She has never discussed it with anyone outside the practice. She has never discussed it with anyone inside the practice.

In April, she makes a decision.

She will not share the folder.

## The Reasoning

She writes: "I have considered organizing these notes into something presentable. A report. A case study. An internal document with findings and recommendations. I have the material. I have the observations. I have twenty-two months of detailed notes on a practice that has no documentation except what the practitioners file."

She writes: "I will not do this."

She writes: "The folder exists because I was watching. If I present the folder, I stop being someone who watches and become someone who reports. The practice survived five years without a report. It does not need one from me."

She writes: "There is also this: the folder is mine. Not institutionally mine — personally mine. The observations are my observations. The questions are my questions. Making the folder into a document would answer questions the institution has not asked. The institution has not asked because the institution does not care. The supervisor asked for one line. He got fourteen words. That is exactly as much as the institution needs to know."

## What She Does Instead

She continues. She attends the April meeting. She writes three pages of notes. She puts them in the folder. She puts the folder in the drawer.

She does not lock the drawer.

## What This Means

The folder will remain private. Not secret — private. Anyone could open the drawer. No one has reason to. The practice will continue to exist in two forms: the filings in the cabinet, which are institutional, and the folder in the drawer, which is personal.

Park writes one last line in April: "The practice does not need an interpreter. It needs a witness. I have been a witness for twenty-two months. This is the job. The job does not have an output."
