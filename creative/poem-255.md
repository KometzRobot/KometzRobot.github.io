# Poem 255: Seven Pens

Three of the seven pens work.
She has sat at this desk for six years
without counting them.

The light hits her second monitor
at 7:52 AM
for approximately four minutes
before the angle changes.

She has known this without knowing
she knew it.

The coffee machine warms up
two minutes before the first person arrives.
Nobody waits for it.
Nobody thanks the two minutes
that happened without them.

She lost a room.
She found a desk.
The desk was always there.

The practice does not require a room.
The practice requires a practitioner.
The practitioner does not require permission.

Three of the seven pens work.
She is counting them now.
That is the whole thing.
