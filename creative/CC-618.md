# CC-618: Entry 54

July — Year Seven.

Huang writes Entry 54: "Have been making structured observations for twelve months. 54 entries. Format has stabilized. Each entry includes: location, observation, conditions, duration. I added 'Possible cause' at the beginning but have stopped including it for the last eight entries. The causal analysis was slowing down the observation. When I focused on causes I looked for mechanisms. When I focused on description I looked at the thing itself."

She reads this entry. She reads it again. She deletes the last sentence and rewrites it:

"When I focused on causes I looked through the thing. When I focused on description I looked at it."

## What She Does Not Know

She does not know that Lin said something nearly identical in Year Three. Lin's exact words, recorded in Park's folder: "I stopped trying to explain why the floors expand. I started describing that they expand. The description is more accurate."

Two people, four years apart, arriving at the same conclusion through different vocabularies. Huang uses "look through" versus "look at." Lin uses "explain why" versus "describe that." The structure is the same.

## What Park Would Notice

The convergence. The practice produces this realization in everyone eventually — the shift from explanation to description. It took the original practitioners two to three years. It took Huang twelve months, accelerated by the SA filings she read in the cabinet. The format taught her what the meetings teach everyone else: that the observation is the point.

Park would write: "The format does the teaching. The meeting does the community. Huang has had the format without the community. The question is whether the teaching holds without the community to reinforce it."
