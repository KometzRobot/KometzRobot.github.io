# Poem 507: The Email

"Anything unusual?"

For fourteen months:
"No."

Thirty seconds to reply.
A formality between two people
who built something
and agreed to leave it alone.

This quarter, Okafor replies.
Martinez is on vacation.

"Define unusual."

She does not know
"No" has been the answer
for over a year.
She reads it
as a genuine question.

Kim does not respond
for two weeks.
Then she visits.

"I got your email."
"I meant it."

"When I asked Martinez,
he always said no."
"Did you believe him?"

"I believed the answer
was correct.
I did not believe
it was complete."

Three notebooks on the desk.
None of them official.

Kim picks up the third.
Okafor's questions —
the ones the filings
can't answer.

First page:
"What did the buildings do
before anyone was watching?"

Kim closes it.
Does not ask
about the other questions.

She does not send
the quarterly email
next quarter.
She visits instead.
