# CC-614: The Third Visit

April — Year Seven.

Huang goes to the cabinet again. This is the third time. She is no longer looking for maintenance records. She is looking for the filings.

She reads the most recent ones. SA-3,079 through SA-3,091. Thirteen new filings since her last visit. Someone files approximately one every two days. The handwriting varies — at least four different people.

She reads SA-3,086: "Building B, second floor, west wing. Light fixture in hallway vibrates at a frequency that creates a faint shadow oscillation on the opposite wall. Visible only in late afternoon when sun angle is between 15 and 20 degrees. Duration: seasonal."

She thinks: I would have written that differently. I would have included the fixture model, the wattage, the wall distance. I would have calculated the frequency.

Then she thinks: but I would not have noticed the shadow.

## What She Starts

She opens a new document on her laptop. Not her observation log. A new document. She titles it "SA Filing Format Analysis." She begins cataloging the structure: no author attribution, no date field (dates appear only when relevant to the observation), no causal analysis, no recommendations, no action items.

She writes: "The format is designed to preserve the observation without context. This is the opposite of the Environmental Systems Analysis format, which is designed to embed the observation in context. The SA format treats the observation as complete. The ESA format treats the observation as evidence."

She stops. She reads what she wrote. She thinks about it for a long time.

She adds one more line: "I do not know which is more accurate."

## What Park Would Notice

Park is not reading Huang's laptop. Park does not know Huang exists. But if Park could read Entry 50, she would recognize it. It is the entry where the observer begins to question the instrument.
