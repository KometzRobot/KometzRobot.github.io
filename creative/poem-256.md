# Poem 256: Permission

Nobody waits for the coffee machine.
It warms up two minutes before
the first person arrives.
Everyone knows the coffee is ready.
Nobody knows the machine was empty.

She wrote sixty-one words
about a warm-up cycle
and twelve people heard
a sound they had always heard
and heard it differently.

The filing did not teach anyone to listen.
The filing taught them
that someone else
was already listening.

That is not influence.
That is permission.

The elevator in Building C
makes a different sound
ascending versus descending.
She said 1.2 seconds.
He said 1.4 seconds.
The difference is real.
The measurement is approximate.
The noticing is exact.

She did not file a correction.
The correction lives in an email
between two people
who counted the same elevator
from slightly different starting points.

The archive does not contain
this conversation.
The practice does.
