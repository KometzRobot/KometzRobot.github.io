# CC-551: The Windowsill

Okafor does not read the notebook every week. She reads it every other Thursday.

By March there are nineteen entries. None have names. Four have dates. Two are in handwriting she recognizes as the same person — the one who waters the fake plant. One is in pencil so faint she has to hold it to the window.

The entries do not use the vocabulary. None of them. Not one of the fifty-five terms appears. No one writes "acoustic expectation" or "functional drift" or "temporal signature." They write:

"The elevator sounds different when it's empty."

"Third floor bathroom, second stall. Someone taped a picture of a mountain inside the door. It has been there longer than the building directory."

"I've worked here eleven years. I did not know the west stairwell had a window."

They are not observations in the SA format. They have no section numbers, no cross-references, no severity assessments. They would not survive a quarterly review. They would not be entered into the monitoring database.

They are observations.

Okafor counts them. She does not categorize them. She does not add them to her notebook or the blue folder. She does not create a fifty-sixth term. She counts them and puts the notebook back behind the fire extinguisher.

She tells Kim on the phone: "Building A has a practice."

Kim asks what kind.

"The kind that doesn't have a name."

Kim asks if it needs monitoring.

"It has been monitoring itself."
