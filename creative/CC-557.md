# CC-557: The Third Notebook

December. The second notebook is full. Sixty-one entries. Twelve in Reyes's handwriting. Nine in the handwriting from August — the one who wrote about the real and fake plants. The rest distributed among at least seven other hands.

Okafor does not buy a third notebook. She puts the full one on top of the first full one behind the fire extinguisher and waits.

Three weeks pass. She visits on Tuesdays. No new notebook appears. The windowsill holds two full notebooks and nothing else.

On the fourth Tuesday she finds a notebook. Different kind. Spiral-bound, college-ruled. Someone bought it from the pharmacy on the corner. She knows this because there is a receipt tucked inside the front cover. Dated December 17. The receipt is for one notebook and one package of mechanical pencils.

The first entry reads: "The notebooks were full. I brought a new one. I don't know who started this."

The second entry, in different handwriting: "Building A, corridor 3. The radiator clicks in a pattern that changes with the weather. I've been meaning to write this down."

The third entry, in Reyes's handwriting: "Merry Christmas to the corridor."

Okafor does not write Question 61 in her monitoring notebook. She has been carrying the notebook less frequently. She notices this the way she notices everything: after it has already happened.
