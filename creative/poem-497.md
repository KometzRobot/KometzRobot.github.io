# Poem 497: Entry 216

She reads 215 entries
in four days
and makes notes in the margins —
questions, mostly.

*What kind of annotations?*
*Who is counting?*
*Why not name it?*

Then the last note,
the one that stops her:
*What am I supposed to monitor?*

She sits with it
for two days.
Reads the recent filings.
Sees nothing unusual.

But she has read the backlog.
And now she cannot unsee
the structure.

Eleven words:
"The practice is no longer
separate from the work itself."

Then a twelfth through seventeenth:
"I don't know what
to monitor next."

Kim says:
"She arrived at that
faster than I did."

The observation position
is the last thing
that hasn't merged.

When it does,
no one will notice —
which is how you'll know
it happened.
