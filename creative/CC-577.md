# CC-577: August

August. The practitioners meet.

Eleven people in a room designed for twenty. Okafor sits at the head of the table because the room has a head and someone has to sit there. Park has prepared slides. Chen has brought a notebook. Reyes has brought nothing, which is how Reyes arrives at everything.

Park presents the cross-building data. The chart shows filing clusters — observations triggering observations across buildings within 48 hours. "The pattern is consistent across three quarters," Park says.

"Is that a problem?" someone asks.

"It's a finding," Park says.

Okafor watches the room. Eleven people who observe buildings for reasons they can each articulate differently. Chen measures. Reyes remembers. Park maps. Torres protects. Davis writes short sentences about things that happen. The new practitioners — Yun from Building F, Adeyemi from the warehouse complex — listen more than they speak.

The vocabulary discussion takes forty minutes. Three proposed terms:

"Functional echo" has been in use for six months. No objections.

"Temporal margin" generates twelve minutes of conversation. Chen suggests the definition should include the word "awareness" — the gap isn't between the change and the filing, it's between the change and the awareness that something has changed. Reyes disagrees: "Sometimes you file about something before you know you noticed it."

The third term has no name yet. Adeyemi describes it: "The thing where you go back to check if something you remember is still true." Six people nod. No one has a word. Chen writes in his notebook: "verification impulse?" Okafor does not write anything. She already has a word for it. It's in the blue folder. She does not say this.

The meeting runs forty minutes over. No one mentions it. Okafor notices that the meeting has developed its own temporal margin — the gap between when it should end and when anyone notices.

Afterward, Park stays behind. "The cross-building pattern. Should I investigate?"

"What would investigating look like?"

Park thinks about this. "Asking people why they file when they file."

"And what would that change?"

Park is quiet for a moment. "I think it would change what they notice."

"Yes."

Park leaves. Okafor sits in the empty room for three minutes. Eleven chairs pushed back at eleven angles. She notices this. She does not file it.
