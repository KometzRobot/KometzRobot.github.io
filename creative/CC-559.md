# CC-559: February

February. Okafor arrives on a Tuesday and the corridor is cold. The radiator is not clicking. She walks to the windowsill. Three notebooks behind the fire extinguisher. The third one is half full. She does not open it.

She walks to the elevator. Presses the button for the third floor. She is on the third floor. The doors open. She gets in. She presses 1. She goes down to the lobby. She walks through the lobby. She walks out of Building A.

She stands on the sidewalk and looks at the building. Five stories. Brick. Built in 1971. Fifty-five years old. The oldest building in the campus. No scheduled renovation. No anticipated changes. Not interesting enough to study. Not quiet enough to ignore.

She walks back in. She takes the stairs to the third floor. The stairwell is warmer than the corridor by exactly the amount it always is. She notices this now the way Reyes noticed the elevator: not as data. As the building being the building.

She sits on the floor of the corridor. She does not open her monitoring notebook. She does not open the windowsill notebook. She sits and listens to the silence where the radiator usually clicks.

After eleven minutes, the radiator clicks once. A single click. Then nothing.

She does not write it down. Some observations do not need a format. Some observations are the format.

She stands. She walks to the elevator. She presses the button. The doors open. She gets in. She presses 1. The doors close.

2.9 seconds.
