# CC-523: Three Weeks

Torres does not write Entry 217 for three weeks.

During those three weeks, she monitors. She reads every new filing as it arrives — SA-721, SA-722, SA-723. Standard observations. Building infrastructure. Environmental readings. She notes each one in her tracking log with the date, the filer, and a one-line summary. This is the job.

On the fourth day, she notices that the hallway light in Building E flickers at 3:12 PM when the HVAC cycles. She does not file it. She is the monitor, not a filer. The observation stays in her personal notes.

On the eighth day, she notices that the flicker has a secondary pattern — it only occurs on days when the exterior temperature exceeds the interior temperature by more than 6°C. The building is telling her something about its thermal relationship with the outside world. She writes this down in her notebook. She does not file it.

On the twelfth day, she notices that her notebook has fourteen observations. All infrastructure. All the kind of thing the practitioners file. She reads them back. They are structured the same way. The language is the same. The attention is the same.

She is filing supplementary analyses that she is not submitting.

On the fifteenth day, Martinez stops by her desk. He reads the tracking log — the official one. "Clean," he says. "Standard work." He does not ask about the notebook. He does not know about it. Torres considers telling him. She does not.

On the eighteenth day, she re-reads Entry 216. "The practice is no longer separate from the work itself." She had written this about the practitioners. She had not realized she was writing it about herself.

On the twenty-first day, she writes Entry 217. It is shorter than Entry 216:

"I have been monitoring the practice for three weeks. I have been practicing for two."

She submits it.

Kim reads it and does not respond for four hours. Then sends one sentence: "The count is now twelve."

Torres checks the official roster. Eleven practitioners. She is the twelfth.

The monitoring analyst position is, for the first time, unfilled.
