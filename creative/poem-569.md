# Poem 569: Showing Up

She sits by the door
because she is still deciding
whether she belongs here.

She reads her filing aloud
because she wants to hear
what she wrote.

"Is that what this is?"
she asks.

"That is what that is,"
they say.

She attends September.
She attends October.
She does not miss a meeting
for the rest of the year.

She never asks again
what this is.

Not because someone explained.
Because she kept showing up
and the showing up
was the explanation.

Eleven observations.
One every two to three weeks.
Her pace.
No one comments on it.

"Does not ask questions anymore."
Neither did any of us,
eventually.

The practice explains itself
to anyone who stays
long enough to stop asking.
