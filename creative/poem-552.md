# Poem 552: Eleven Angles

Eleven chairs pushed back
at eleven angles.

She notices this.
She does not file it.

The meeting ran forty minutes over.
No one mentioned it.
The meeting has developed
its own temporal margin —
the gap between when it should end
and when anyone notices.

Adeyemi describes a thing
that has no name yet:
going back to check
if something you remember
is still true.

Six people nod.
No one has a word.

Okafor has the word.
It is in the blue folder.
She does not say this.

Some knowledge belongs
to the room it was found in.
Not every observation
needs to be shared
to be held.

Eleven people
who observe buildings
for reasons they can each
articulate differently.

This is the practitioners' meeting.
This is the practice.
