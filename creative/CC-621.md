# CC-621: The Decision

October — Year Seven.

The group does not discuss Huang at the October meeting. They discuss the east loading dock, which has started smelling different since the ventilation maintenance. They discuss a pipe joint in Building F that Adeyemi measured at 0.3mm wider than the previous quarter. They discuss whether "acoustic expectation" should be split into "acoustic expectation" and "acoustic memory," and decide it should not.

At the end, Okafor says: "Torres saw the stairwell person again."

The room waits.

Torres: "She was there Tuesday. And Thursday. Same landing. No laptop this time."

Reyes: "She's past the laptop."

No one asks what this means. Everyone understands. The laptop was the filing phase. Past the laptop is observation without record.

## The Conversation That Follows

Lin: "So she's doing what we do, in a stairwell, by herself."

Okafor: "She's doing what we did before we were a group."

This lands differently. The practice predates the group. The group organized something that was already happening. What Huang is doing in the stairwell is not a derivative of Conference Room 4. It may be the original.

Park speaks for the first time in twenty minutes: "The cabinet was the invitation we didn't make. The stairwell is the invitation the building makes."

## What They Decide

They decide nothing. This is not avoidance. They have discussed it. They have acknowledged it. They do not vote because there is nothing to vote on. The practice cannot include or exclude someone who does not know it exists as a practice.

Chen: "She'll find us or she won't."

Adeyemi: "She's already found it. She just hasn't found us."

The distinction is precise and everyone hears it. The practice is not the group. The practice is what the group does. Huang has found the practice. She has not found the people who also do it.

## What Park Writes

"The October meeting. The stairwell observer came up briefly. No decision made. This is correct. The group cannot decide about someone who arrived without a decision. The practice spread to the cabinet through adjacency. It spread to the stairwell through the cabinet. Each step further from the group. Each step closer to the origin."

She writes: "The origin was always one person noticing something in a building. Everything since then has been infrastructure."
