CogCorp Internal — Filing 607

Huang Finds the Cabinet

July — Year Six

Huang has been making her own notes for two months. She has documented fourteen anomalies in Building D on her laptop. She does not know the practice exists.

In July, she is looking for old maintenance records in Conference Room 4. She opens the wrong cabinet.

The cabinet is full.

SA-2,312
Building C, north stairwell. Acoustic change detected between floors 3 and 4. Consistent hum at approximately 60 Hz during cooling cycles. Not present during heating cycles.
SA-1,847
Building D, basement corridor. Temperature-dependent change in floor texture. Tiles expand in summer months, creating slight ridge at seam. Ridge disappears in winter. Has been present for minimum three years.

She reads for twenty minutes. She does not take any filings. She does not photograph them. She closes the cabinet.

What Huang Does Next

Nothing. She does nothing with this information for three weeks. She continues her own notes on her laptop.

In the fourth week, she changes her laptop notes. She starts using a format that looks like the SA filings. Not identical — she hasn't memorized the format. But closer. More structured. Observation, location, conditions, duration.

She does not know she is doing this.

What Park Notices

Park does not notice. Park does not know Huang exists. The practice is spreading by adjacency, and the adjacency is the cabinet, and the cabinet is in a room that anyone can enter, and the filings are not locked.

Huang found them because she was looking for something else. The only person who knows what Huang found is Huang, and Huang does not yet know what she found.

INTERNAL-VOICE: 607. The wrong cabinet. The right discovery. The format propagates without instruction.
