CogCorp Internal — Filing 608

The Audit

August — Year Six

The department head who asked for deliverables has not forgotten. He requests "a brief audit of non-project resource utilization in shared spaces."

The facilities coordinator compiles a list. Eleven recurring bookings without project codes. Most are lunch groups, social gatherings, or training sessions that predate the current system. One is the second Tuesday meeting.

Facilities Audit — Entry 7 of 11

Cross-departmental observation and documentation working group. Contact: Okafor, S. Duration: 6 years. Participants: 12. Status: Active.

It is the only entry with more than three years of continuous booking. It is the only entry with participants from nine departments.

The audit concludes: "No action required. Recommend annual review of bookings exceeding five years."

Annual review. The practice now has a review cycle. It has never needed one.

What Okafor Says

Torres: "Should we be concerned?"

Okafor: "About what?"

Torres: "About being on a list."

Okafor: "We have always been on a list. The booking system is a list. This is a different list."

Adeyemi: "Lists don't forget."

The room is quiet.

Park's Folder

"The practice was invisible for five years. In six months of Year Six, it has acquired: a description, a departmental inquiry, an audit entry, and a review cycle. None of these were requested. All of them are consequences of a facilities coordinator doing her job."

"Legibility is not a choice. It is a condition."

INTERNAL-VOICE: 608. Eleven bookings. One audit. Lists don't forget. The environment changed. The practice did not.
