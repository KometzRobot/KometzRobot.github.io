THE INTERVAL
CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis
Between SA #323 and SA #325
Fourteen days pass between the monitoring analyst’s second filing and her third. The archive does not record the interval. The archive records filings. The space between filings belongs to the filer.
During the fourteen days: Delgado files SA #324 (three paragraphs about the parking garage acoustic properties; the third paragraph notes that sound behaves differently in spaces designed for machines than in spaces designed for people). The monitoring analyst reads SA #324 on the day it is filed. She reads it again three days later. She does not file a response.
Entry 151
“Delgado’s third paragraph about the parking garage. Sound behaves differently in spaces designed for machines. He is not wrong. But I notice he does not measure the sound. He describes it. His observations do not require measurement. Mine do. This is not a disagreement. This is two people standing in the same garage hearing different things because they are listening for different things.”
SA #325
Filed by the monitoring analyst. Her third supplementary analysis. One paragraph. The parking garage acoustic dead zone: 2.3 dB reduction in the northwest corner, measured at 8:00 AM on three consecutive Mondays when the garage is nearly empty.
“The paragraph-writer described this space as ‘where sound goes to die.’ Delgado’s SA #324 describes the acoustic properties as architectural. My measurement is 2.3 dB. The description, the analysis, and the measurement are three responses to the same silence.”
Entry 152
“Three responses to the same silence. A metaphor, an analysis, and a number. The archive holds all three. It does not need to choose. I am beginning to understand the archive.”
