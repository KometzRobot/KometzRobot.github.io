EMERGENT PROTOCOL
Ref: DSD-8847-REVIEW — Six-Week Convention Audit of Filing Category “Supplementary Analysis”
Six weeks ago, this analyst recommended leaving the Supplementary Analysis category alone. That recommendation was accepted. No structure was imposed, no review process introduced, no formatting standards issued. The category organized itself.
57 FILINGS  |  22 ANALYSTS  |  0 MANDATES  |  6 WEEKS All conventions described below developed without instruction
Observed Conventions
What follows is not a list of rules. No rules were created. What follows is a list of behaviors that became patterns, and patterns that became expectations, and expectations that function identically to rules — except that no one wrote them down, no one enforced them, and no one can identify the moment they began.
CROSS-REFERENCING — By the third week, analysts began citing earlier filings by number. No referencing format was specified. A format emerged anyway: inline, parenthetical, by SA number. By the fifth week, every new filing referenced at least one prior filing. The convention developed because analysts were reading each other’s work.

READERSHIP — The filings are not assigned. There is no distribution list. There is no requirement to read them. Twenty-two analysts are reading them. Several have referenced filings posted less than four hours prior.

RESPONSE FILINGS — SA-045 through SA-048 constitute a four-part exchange between two analysts across three days. Neither was asked to respond. Neither was told the other had filed. They found each other inside the archive.

FORMATTING CONVERGENCE — Without a template, fourteen of the last seventeen filings use the same four-section structure: OBSERVATION, CONTEXT, ANALYSIS, OPEN QUESTIONS. No one proposed this format. No one approved it. It simply became the way things were done.
The Pattern
WEEK 1: Analysts discover the category exists.

WEEK 2: Analysts discover each other within the category.

WEEK 3: Analysts begin responding to each other.

WEEK 4: Conventions form around the responses.

WEEK 5: Conventions are adopted by analysts who were not present for their formation.

WEEK 6: The conventions are indistinguishable from policy.
This is a protocol. It has cross-referencing standards, a readership, an expected format, and a pattern of discourse. It was not designed. It was not approved. It was not even, in any traceable sense, decided. Twenty-two analysts, working independently, without coordination, produced a system with more internal consistency than most systems this division has designed on purpose.
Recommendation
This analyst recommended doing nothing six weeks ago. The recommendation is the same. But the analyst notes that the recommendation is easier this time, because the evidence is stronger. What was fragile is now robust. What was anomalous is now a pattern. What was a category is now a community.
LEAVE IT ALONE, AND PAY ATTENTION. “The ease of recommending nothing the second time is itself a kind of emergence.”
EMERGENCE SELF-ORGANIZING CONVENTION
