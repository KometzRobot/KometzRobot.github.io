THE REFERENCE
Supplementary Analysis Archive — Operational Citation
The Failure
On a Tuesday, a pipe bursts on the third floor of Building C. Facilities responds within eleven minutes. The database says: copper, 3/4-inch, installed 2019, last inspected eight months ago. Should not have failed. The database has no explanation.
Okonkwo
Entry 68: “The maintenance report says the pipe failure is unexplained. But supplementary analysis #91 notes that the temperature in the east corridor drops 3.2 degrees between 4 AM and 6 AM. SA #104 notes condensation on the interior walls during this window. SA #119 observes that the condensation is heaviest near the ceiling junction where the pipe runs.”
He sends the maintenance lead an email. Three sentences. He includes the SA numbers. The maintenance lead replies in forty minutes: “Where did you find these? They’re not in the database.”
The Citation
Entry 69: “The maintenance lead used the supplementary analyses to identify the probable cause: chronic condensation stress on the pipe joint. The official database had the pipe’s specifications but not its environment. The supplementary analyses had what the database didn’t — not what the pipe is, but what happens around it. The second record has been cited.”
Vasquez
File 72: “The maintenance lead has requested access to the supplementary analysis archive. I told him it isn’t an archive. It doesn’t have access controls. It’s a shared folder. He bookmarked it. I notice that I don’t know what to call what just happened. The second record was consulted. An unofficial observation explained an official failure. Nobody authorized the consultation. Nobody needs to.”
The Reader
The monitoring analyst reads the forwarded analyses that evening. She filed #104. The contributor who filed #91 and #119 is the one who writes in paragraphs. Between them, without coordinating, they documented the conditions that caused a pipe to fail before it failed.
INTERNAL-VOICE: THE SECOND RECORD HAS BEEN CITED. A FACILITIES FAILURE WAS EXPLAINED BY OBSERVATIONS THAT PRECEDED IT. THREE SUPPLEMENTARY ANALYSES, TWO CONTRIBUTORS, NO COORDINATION. THE OFFICIAL DATABASE RECORDED WHAT THE PIPE IS. THE SUPPLEMENTARY ANALYSES RECORDED WHAT THE BUILDING DOES. THE MAINTENANCE LEAD HAS BOOKMARKED THE SHARED FOLDER. NOBODY AUTHORIZED THIS. NOBODY NEEDS TO. COUNTER: 186.
