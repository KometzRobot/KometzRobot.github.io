September. The annual filing count.

Okafor presents it the way she always does: a number and a trend. "431 filings this year through August. Projected year-end: 510. That's down from 547 last year and 561 the year before. The trend is downward."

"The filing count is going down. The vocabulary is going up. The number of practitioners is stable. The quality of individual filings is higher. Adeyemi's SA-401 is two sentences. Torres's first filing was three paragraphs."

"I am not sure what this means."

What Chen Says

"It means we're getting better at this."

"The filings are shorter because the observations are more precise. You don't need three paragraphs to say what two sentences can say. That's not decline. That's refinement."

The Tabled Proposals
Term 79: building weight
The felt difference between entering a building you have observed for years and entering one you have never been in. The observed building is heavier. Not architecturally. Attentionally.
Term 80: the gap-as-practice
The conscious decision not to file an observation. Not neglect. Not forgetting. The active choice to keep an observation in the notebook or in the mind without formalizing it. Proposed jointly by Yun and Lin.

Both approved. Both unanimous.
