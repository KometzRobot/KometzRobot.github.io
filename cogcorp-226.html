CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis
Supplementary Analysis #312
SA #312
“Implement ongoing monitoring of filings exhibiting vocabulary divergence exceeding 1.5 standard deviations from departmental baseline. Flag for review. Purpose: early identification of descriptive range expansion that may indicate evolving observational practice.”
What the Recommendation Creates
The monitoring analyst noticed the gap. She filed about it once. The Pattern Recognition Group noticed the pattern across 12 filings. They filed a recommendation to automate the noticing.
The gap was personal. The pattern was analytical. The recommendation is procedural. Each step moves further from the experience and closer to the system.
The Recursion
SA #312 recommends monitoring for vocabulary divergence. SA #312 itself uses standard vocabulary. It will not be flagged by its own recommendation. The system it proposes will never detect the report that proposed it.
But if the analyst who wrote SA #312 ever files in non-standard vocabulary — if the analyst ever writes about what it feels like to discover that 12 people wrote differently about their work for reasons the data cannot fully explain — that filing will be flagged. The system waits.
What the Monitoring Analyst Does Not Know
She does not know about SA #312. She does not know that her 73 words produced a 14-page report and an automated monitoring system.
