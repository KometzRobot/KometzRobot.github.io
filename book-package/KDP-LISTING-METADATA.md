## ISBN Decision (read once before listing anything)

KDP gives you a free ISBN for the paperback. Use it. Reasons:
- Free. Your own ISBN block (Bowker) is $125 for one, $295 for 10.
- The free KDP ISBN locks the book to Amazon-only distribution. For an indie author starting out, that's fine — Amazon is 70-80% of US ebook sales anyway.
- You can switch later. If a book takes off and you want IngramSpark / Barnes & Noble print distribution, you buy your own ISBN at that point and re-list.
- Kindle ebooks do NOT need an ISBN. KDP assigns an ASIN automatically.

Decision: **Use free KDP ISBN for all three paperbacks. No ISBN for Kindle versions.**

## After Listing

For each title:
1. **Order an author proof copy** (~$5 + shipping). DO NOT skip this. The PDF margins, image bleeds, and trim alignment can only be verified on paper.
2. **Wait 72 hours** before clicking "Publish" while your draft sits in review. Re-read the metadata when fresh.
3. **Categories matter more than keywords.** Pick the deepest sub-category you legitimately fit; ranking #50 in a small category beats #10,000 in a broad one.
4. **First review is the slowest.** Ask 3-5 people to buy + review in the first week — Amazon's algorithm waits for ~10 reviews before surfacing the book broadly.
5. **The KDP dashboard updates royalty payouts on the 60-day delay.** A sale in May pays in late July. Plan accordingly.
