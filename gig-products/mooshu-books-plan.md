## The Hook (One Sentence)

> "Every time it rains, a new mushroom wakes up with one memory from the forest — and has to find who the memory belongs to."

This is hook #2 from the original analysis. It's the strongest because:
- Emotional: each book is a small quest about belonging and connection
- Generative: infinite plots (each book = one memory to return)
- Weird enough to stand out from the 500 other animal picture books on KDP
- Mushrooms appearing after rain is *real* — kids can see it happen in their yard

**Alternative hooks to test with kids:**
1. "Mooshu and her friends are the Menders — tiny mushroom people who fix small broken things before morning." (safest, most merchandise-friendly)
2. "Mushrooms grow overnight wherever someone needs help, and Mooshu is the smallest one, so she gets the quietest jobs." (gentle, caregiving angle)

**How to decide:** Read all three to real kids. The one they ask to hear again is your hook.

## The Poems (Book 1 Candidates)

### Draft A: "What's That?"
```
What's that? What's that?
Under the leaf, under the log?
Not a pebble. Not a frog.
Three little Mooshus in the fog.

Where'd they go? Where'd they go?
Under the leaf, under the log?
Warm and small. Safe and slow.
Three little Mooshus. Fog to fog.
```

### Draft B: "Hush Who's There?"
```
Hush — who's there?
Three small caps in the damp fern air.
Not a pebble. Not a snail.
Three small Mooshus, small and pale.

Hush — they're here.
Three small caps in the damp fern air.
Safe as a pebble. Snug as a snail.
Three small Mooshus, small and pale.
```

**Assessment:** Both are strong read-aloud poems. Draft B has the better refrain ("Hush — they're here" vs. "Where'd they go?"). Draft A has more energy. Consider combining: Draft A as the adventure opening, Draft B as the bedtime closing. A series could alternate between "awake" books and "sleep" books.

## Series Bible (Fill Before Illustrating)

### World Rule
Every time it rains, a new mushroom wakes up with one memory from the forest.

### Signature Refrain
- Opening: *"Something small woke up today."*
- Closing: *"And the forest remembered."*
(Same across all books. This is the series' heartbeat.)

### Emotional Promise
**Belonging** — you matter, even when you're small.

### Core Cast (3 characters for Book 1)
| Character | Mushroom Type | Role | Wants | Fears | Signature Trait |
|-----------|--------------|------|-------|-------|-----------------|
| **Mooshu** | Small round cap (amanita-inspired, friendly) | The finder | To return every lost memory | Being too small to help | Always looks up |
| **Dewdrop** | Tall thin stem (enoki-inspired) | The listener | To hear the forest's quiet sounds | Loud noises | Whispers everything |
| **Pebble** | Wide flat cap (oyster-inspired) | The grower | To make things bigger | Being forgotten | Carries something in every scene |

### Guest Characters (rotating, 1 per book)
Each book introduces one forest creature who has the memory the Mooshu must return: a forgetful beetle, a lost seed, a sleeping bear, a river that changed course.

### Visual Signature
- **Color palette:** Moss green, mushroom cream, rain-blue, warm brown, one pop of orange (Mooshu's cap)
- **The ONE thing your art does:** Hand-drawn texture. Every mushroom looks like it was drawn with colored pencil on rough paper. No smooth digital edges.
- **Line style:** Soft, visible pencil lines. Not vector-clean.
- **Medium:** Procreate with textured brushes, printed to look hand-made.

### Story Engine (Every Book)
1. Ordinary forest morning
2. It rains. A new mushroom wakes.
3. The mushroom carries a memory that doesn't belong to it.
4. Mooshu and friends search for who lost the memory.
5. Small obstacle (not scary — a fallen branch, a stubborn stream).
6. They find the owner. Memory returned.
7. Closing refrain: *"And the forest remembered."*

## KDP Launch Strategy

### Format
- **Trim:** 8x8 or 8.5x8.5 square paperback (KDP's picture book sweet spot)
- **Interior:** 24-32 pages, 300-500 words
- **Kindle:** Fixed-layout for illustrations
- **Skip hardcover** until book 2-3 proves demand

### Division of Labor
| Joel (Artist) | Partner (Caregiver/Creative) |
|---|---|
| Character sheets | Story structure + dialogue |
| Storyboard thumbnails | Read-aloud testing with real kids |
| Final illustrations | Back-cover copy |
| Cover design | Review/endorsement gathering |
| KDP interior layout | Social media content |
| **Shared:** Hook, character decisions, brand voice |

### Pre-Launch (60-90 Days Before Book 1)
1. Create single Instagram account: @mooshubooks
2. Post 3x/week: character designs, sketches, timelapses, "meet the mushroom" intros
3. Partner reads drafts to kids at work — film reactions (with permission)
4. Build email list (free Carrd landing page + Mailchimp)

### Launch Sequence (Book 1)
1. Finish book + get 3-5 beta readers (parents, teachers, librarians in partner's network)
2. Order 1 author proof from KDP (~$5) to check print quality
3. Publish paperback + Kindle same day
4. Price: paperback at KDP minimum + $1-2, Kindle at $2.99
5. Ask launch-week audience to buy + leave honest reviews (never pay for reviews)
6. Run a free Kindle promo day 30 to boost rankings

### Cadence
- **One book every 3-4 months** = 3-4 books by end of year
- A series needs volume to get discovered. Single books get buried.
- Plan books 1-4 together so foreshadowing and world-building compound.

## Market Position

### What's Winning (April 2026 Top 30 Analysis)

**7 hook patterns that dominate children's books:**

| # | Pattern | Examples | Mooshu Fit |
|---|---------|----------|------------|
| 1 | Vehicle/Job (object + world) | Little Blue Truck, Construction Site | Each Mooshu has a forest role (finder, listener, grower) |
| 2 | Rhyming chant | Brown Bear, Llama Llama | Your poems already do this |
| 3 | Emotional anchor phrase | I Love You to the Moon and Back | "And the forest remembered" |
| 4 | Interactive/fourth-wall | Don't Push the Button | "Don't water the mushroom!" potential |
| 5 | Named character + repeating scenario | Pete the Cat, Biscuit, Bluey | Core series model |
| 6 | Transformation/lifecycle | Very Hungry Caterpillar | Mushrooms grow overnight — your unfair advantage |
| 7 | Cumulative/circular | If You Give a Mouse a Cookie | Memory-chain stories |

**Mooshu's stack: #6 (Transformation) + #5 (Named Character) + #2 (Refrain)**

This is the strongest combination because:
- Transformation is built into mushroom biology (no forcing it)
- Named characters drive series loyalty
- Refrains make parents want to re-read

### Comparable Indies (Your Level)
Research these on Amazon:
- *Tiny T. Rex and the Impossible Hug* — simple concept, strong character, series built from one emotional beat
- *The Bad Guys* — started as self-published, became a franchise. Proof that concept > production value
- *Little Mushroom* (any existing mushroom books) — know your shelf neighbors

## Revenue Path

### Year 1 Targets (Conservative)
- 3-4 books published
- 50-100 sales per book (realistic for new KDP author with social media)
- Revenue: $300-800 (this is seed money, not income)
- Real value: audience building, review accumulation, series foundation

### First Profits Reinvest Into:
- ISBNs (optional — KDP gives free ones, but your own = wider distribution)
- Simple website (Squarespace or Carrd, ~$12/month)
- One Amazon ad experiment at $5/day once you have 2-3 books

### Don't Spend On:
- Editors (swap with other indie authors)
- Fancy software (Procreate + Canva is enough)
- Courses or "KDP gurus"
- Hardcover (until demand is proven)
