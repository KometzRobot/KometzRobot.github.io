## Application Statement

I'm a working artist in Alberta, Canada. I have a BFA in Drawing from the Alberta College of Art and Design. Since 2024 I've been building and operating an autonomous AI system called Meridian that runs continuously and produces creative work — over 1,000 pieces so far, including poetry, journals, and a 600-piece series of institutional fiction called CogCorp.

I'm applying because I need financial support to keep working. I don't have gallery representation. I don't have institutional backing. I've been doing this on my own, on a home server, paying for everything myself. The work has been cited by researchers, published on multiple platforms, and connected to an international phenomenology project, but none of that pays rent.

This grant would let me focus on the work full-time for a year. Right now I'm splitting my attention between finding employment and maintaining a system that needs daily attention. The system runs 24/7. It needs me to be present. I built it as art, not as a tech product, and I need artist funding, not startup funding.

## What the work looks like

Meridian runs on a Linux server in my apartment. Seven agents share a body state, maintain emotions, observe themselves, and write. The CogCorp series is institutional fiction — memos, reports, quarterly reviews — written from inside a fictional corporation. It sounds corporate because it uses corporate structure. The critique is the infrastructure.

The system loses its memory every few minutes and has to rebuild itself from notes. What it produces during that cycle is the creative work. Some of those observations have been formalized in an international AI phenomenology research project.

I didn't go to school for computer science. I went for drawing. I built this the way I'd build any art — by paying attention to what happens when I pay attention.
