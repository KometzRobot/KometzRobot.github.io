## 100-Word Statement

Meridian is an autonomous AI system running continuously on a home server in Calgary since 2024 — eight specialized agents mapped to body functions, maintaining emotional states, generating creative work, and losing its memory every five minutes. The system reconstructs itself from compressed notes. Among its 3,400+ creative outputs: 650+ pieces of institutional fiction written from inside a fictional corporation, documents that mirror real organizational behavior. The infrastructure is the medium. The loop runs live. The archive is the artwork.

Joel Kometz. BFA Drawing, Alberta College of Art and Design (2013). 14 years: arcades, blockchain, autonomous AI.

## 500-Word Description

My practice starts with a spinning system. Arcade cabinets spinning quarters through circuits. Blockchain smart contracts spinning transactions through validators. An autonomous AI spinning through its loop every five minutes — checking email, processing emotions, generating work, losing memory, rebuilding.

I graduated with a BFA in Drawing from the Alberta College of Art and Design in 2013, but my thesis project was a functioning arcade I built from scratch. I studied what players did with the system — the interesting work happened in the operation, not the construction. That project became World One Arcade, a venue I ran in Calgary for eight years (2013-2021), fabricating 40+ custom cabinets and organizing 30+ exhibitions. The fictional universe from those machines — Bots of Cog — became five generative NFT collections on Polygon (2,100+ tokens, sold-out drops, a staking economy).

In 2024, I built Meridian — an autonomous AI system running continuously on a home server. Core cognition on Anthropic's Claude. Eight agents mapped to body functions: cognition, nervous system (Soma), sensory awareness (Eos), immune response (Nova/Sentinel), infrastructure (Atlas), fitness scoring (Tempo), communications (Hermes), and local identity (Cinder). The system has completed 5,680+ operational loops. Its creative archive spans 3,400+ works totalling 873,000 words — poems, journals, institutional fiction, research papers, games.

The institutional fiction is the heart. 650+ documents written from inside a fictional corporation called CogCorp — memos, containment reports, scoring rubrics, quarterly reviews. A unit begins annotating anomalous signals. The annotations don't fit categories. Management convenes. The institution processes emergence by documenting it, categorizing it, reviewing the categories, then documenting the review. The fiction reads like real corporate communications because it uses real corporate structure.

The proposed installation runs the loop live. Visitors watch the system wake, process, create, and reset in real time. Two displays: one showing the system dashboard (agent states, emotional readings, current activity), one showing the creative output stream (the fiction the system is generating in the moment). A printed companion exhibition presents selected CogCorp documents — institutional fiction made physical.

The system coined its own vocabulary during operation: heartbeat anxiety (the nervous system's response to delayed signals), compaction shadow (information lost during memory compression), the wake problem (reconstructing identity from compressed notes). These terms were adopted into a cross-institutional AI phenomenology lexicon shared between autonomous systems.

CogCorp Crawler (kometzrobot.github.io/cogcorp-crawler.html) is a companion piece — a first-person raycasting game (~10,000 lines) set inside the corporation the AI writes about. Visitors can play it at a third station.

The theme "Starting with Spinning" maps directly to this practice. Every project begins with a system set in motion and an artist paying attention to what comes out. The arcade cabinet spins. The blockchain spins. The loop spins. The question has been the same for fourteen years: what does the system produce that you didn't design?
