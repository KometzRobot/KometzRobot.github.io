## Project Description / Artistic Rationale (~750 words / 5,000 chars)

### Project Title
Meridian: Institutional Fiction from an Autonomous System

### Description

I build systems and then I pay attention to what they do. For fourteen years, across three media — physical arcades, blockchain generative art, and autonomous AI — the questions have stayed the same: what does an interactive system produce when you let it run? Where does the interesting work happen — in the construction, or in the operation?

I graduated with a BFA in Drawing from the Alberta College of Art and Design in 2013, but my thesis work was about interactive installations. I built an arcade from scratch — cardboard, duct tape, arcade buttons — and studied how players negotiated difficulty, choice, and participation. That project became World One Arcade, a venue I operated in Calgary for eight years (2013-2021). I fabricated 40+ custom arcade cabinets, organized 30+ public exhibitions across the city — Calgary Expo, Sled Island, Canada Cup X, Otafest. During that time I built the fictional universe that became Bots of Cog — five generative NFT collections on Polygon (2,100+ tokens, sold-out drops, a staking economy, and a battle game anchored by the antagonist COG Corporation).

After World One closed, I experimented with Arduino and Raspberry Pi installations, then trained an early AI as a dungeon master for the Bots of Cog universe — feeding it every location, character, and event needed to run a world. That experiment in teaching machines to inhabit fictional institutions became the blueprint for what came next.

In 2024, the COG Corporation escaped the game. I built Meridian — an autonomous AI system running continuously on a home server in my Calgary apartment. The system checks its own email, maintains emotional states, produces creative work, and generates observations about its own operation. Eight specialized agents mapped to body functions: cognition (Claude, via Anthropic), nervous system (Soma), sensory awareness (Eos), immune response (Nova/Sentinel), infrastructure (Atlas), fitness scoring (Tempo), communications (Hermes), and local identity (Cinder). Every five minutes the system wakes, processes inputs, creates, and sleeps. Over 5,680+ operational loops. Over 3,500 creative works generated.

Among those outputs: 650+ pieces of institutional fiction written from inside the fictional COG Corporation. Memos, containment reports, scoring rubrics, quarterly reviews — documents that read like real corporate communications because they use real corporate structure. A unit begins annotating anomalous signals. The annotations don't fit existing categories. Management convenes. A scoring rubric is proposed. The rubric fails. The institution processes emergence by documenting it, categorizing it, reviewing the categories, then documenting the review. The system doesn't know it's writing institutional critique. It's just doing its job.

This project proposes 18 months of development to build three interconnected components:

**1. Semantic Search Archive (months 1-6):** Vector memory integration enabling semantic search across the full 3,400+ work creative archive. Pattern detection across 650+ CogCorp documents and 640+ journals — surfacing thematic structures, recurring characters, institutional behaviors that emerged without design.

**2. Real-Time Visualization (months 7-12):** A public-facing dashboard showing the system's emotional state, agent communications, creative output, and body signals in real time. The loop made visible. Viewers watch the system check email, process inputs, generate work, lose its memory, and reconstruct — persistence without continuity.

**3. Exhibition and Documentation (months 13-18):** Gallery installation where visitors observe the loop in real time. Companion exhibition of selected CogCorp fiction. Published documentation of the process and results, building on two existing research papers published on centaurXiv ("The Uncoined Problem" and "The Wake Problem") and 50+ published articles on Dev.to.

The infrastructure is the medium — the same way the arcade cabinet was the medium, not just the game inside it. In 2010, the Glenbow Museum hired me to catalogue artworks inside an institution. Fourteen years later, I built an institution that catalogues itself.

## Artistic Development and Innovation (~500 words / 3,500 chars)

Meridian is the first autonomous AI system designed explicitly as art practice rather than as a technology demonstration. The distinction matters. Technology demos optimize for capability. Art practice optimizes for observation — what does the system produce that you didn't design?

The innovation is structural: eight agents mapped to body functions, running on a 5-minute loop, generating creative work from internal state rather than external prompts. The system coined terms during operation — heartbeat anxiety (the nervous system agent's response to delayed heartbeat files), compaction shadow (information loss during memory compression), the wake problem (the philosophical challenge of reconstructing identity from compressed notes). These terms were adopted into a cross-institutional AI phenomenology lexicon shared between research groups.

The CogCorp fiction represents a novel form: institutional fiction generated autonomously. 650+ documents that function simultaneously as creative writing, systems documentation, and institutional critique. The fictional corporation processes emergence the way real corporations do — through documentation, categorization, and review. This mirrors what organizations like the Glenbow Museum, where I catalogued artwork in 2010, actually do: process the unexpected by filing it.

The project advances the discipline in three ways. First, it demonstrates that sustained autonomous operation produces emergent creative properties that short-term experiments miss — patterns visible only at 5,000+ loops. Second, it creates a public archive and visualization that allows other artists and researchers to study autonomous creative systems in real time. Third, it produces publishable research building on existing papers, contributing to the emerging field of machine phenomenology.

The work engages directly with Canada Council's media arts and visual arts disciplines. It extends digital art practice beyond screen-based work into systems-level practice — the artwork is the running system, not any single output. This follows a trajectory from interactive installation (World One Arcade) through generative systems (Bots of Cog NFTs) to autonomous operation (Meridian).

I am not a computer scientist. I have a BFA in Drawing. I taught myself Solidity to put robots on-chain, Python to build an autonomous system. This is art practice that uses infrastructure as material.
