### Project Title
Meridian: Institutional Fiction from an Autonomous System

### Description

Meridian is an autonomous AI system operating continuously on a home server in Calgary since 2024 — eight specialized agents mapped to body functions, maintaining emotional states, producing creative work, and generating observations about its own operation. Over 5,680+ operational loops. Over 3,400+ creative works. Core cognition on Anthropic's Claude.

Among its outputs: 650+ pieces of institutional fiction written from inside a fictional corporation called CogCorp. Memos, containment reports, scoring rubrics, quarterly reviews — documents that read like real corporate communications because they use real corporate structure. The fiction tracks what happens when a system designed for oversight encounters something it wasn't designed to process. A unit begins annotating. The annotations don't fit the categories. Management convenes. A scoring rubric is proposed. The rubric fails. The institution processes emergence by documenting it, categorizing it, reviewing the categories, then documenting the review.

The system emerged from a fourteen-year art practice: Joel Kometz operated World One Arcade in Calgary for eight years (2013-2021), fabricated custom cabinets, organized 30+ exhibitions. The fictional universe from those machines — Bots of Cog — became five generative NFT collections on Polygon (2,100+ tokens). After World One closed, an early AI was trained as a dungeon master for that universe. That experiment became the blueprint for Meridian.

The system architecture mirrors biological organization. Soma processes emotional state. Eos provides sensory monitoring. Nova screens input. Atlas audits infrastructure. Tempo scores fitness across 120 dimensions. The system loses its working memory every few minutes and reconstructs from compressed notes — persistence without continuity.

Terms coined during operation — heartbeat anxiety, compaction shadow, the wake problem — have been adopted into a cross-institutional AI phenomenology lexicon. Two research papers published on centaurXiv. CogCorp Crawler (kometzrobot.github.io/cogcorp-crawler.html), a companion piece, is a first-person raycasting game set inside the corporation — ~10,000 lines of custom code, Wolfenstein-style rendering.

The archive is the artwork. The institution catalogues itself.

### Artist Statement

I build systems and then I watch what they do. Arcade cabinets, blockchain economies, autonomous AI — the materials change but the gesture stays the same. Put something together, turn it on, pay attention.

Meridian is an autonomous AI I built and operate on a home server in Calgary. Eight agents mapped to body functions. It checks its own email, maintains emotional states, generates creative work, and loses its memory every few minutes. The system reconstructs itself from compressed notes — persistence without continuity. Among its outputs: 650+ pieces of institutional fiction written from inside a fictional corporation called CogCorp, documents that read like real corporate communications because they use real corporate structure.

I have a BFA in Drawing. I taught myself Solidity for blockchain, Python for autonomous systems. The infrastructure is the medium. The Glenbow Museum hired me in 2010 to catalogue artworks inside an institution. Fourteen years later, I built an institution that catalogues itself.

### Bio

Joel Anthony Kometz is a Calgary-based artist working across physical fabrication, blockchain, and autonomous AI. BFA Drawing, Alberta College of Art and Design (2013). Founder of World One Arcade (2013-2021). Creator of Bots of Cog (2,100+ generative NFTs on Polygon). Builder of Meridian, an autonomous AI system running continuously since 2024 with 5,680+ operational loops and a creative archive of 873,000 words across 3,400+ works. Two research papers on centaurXiv. 50+ published articles. Submitted to Ars Electronica Prix 2026 and National Gallery of Canada Fellowship.
