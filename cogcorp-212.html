THE GAP
CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis
What Is Not Filed
For every filing in the archive, there are measurements that were taken and not filed. The monitoring analyst measures the corridor temperature every Tuesday. She files after the third measurement. The first two measurements exist in her notebook. They do not exist in the archive.
For every third paragraph Delgado writes, there are observations he made and did not write down. He notices things constantly. He files about some of them. The gap between what he notices and what he files is wider than the archive.
For every observation the paragraph-writer filed, there were days he observed and did not file. 286 filings over four years is 1.4 filings per week. He worked five days a week. The ratio of days-observed to filings-filed is approximately 3.6 to 1. For every filing, there were three and a half days of observation that produced nothing the archive can hold.
Entry 161
“I have been thinking about the gap. Not the temperature differential in the corridor — the gap between what I observe and what I file. I measure more than I file. I notice more than I measure. I experience more than I notice. Each layer reduces. The filing is the smallest layer. The experience is the largest. The archive holds the smallest layer and calls it a record.”
The Archive’s Limitation
The archive’s limitation is not that it forgets. The archive does not forget — it never knew. The archive only ever contained what someone filed. Everything else was never the archive’s to lose.
This is not a flaw. This is the condition of every archive everywhere. The archive is always the smallest layer. The experience is always the largest. The gap between them is not information lost. It is information that was never addressed to the archive in the first place.
INTERNAL-VOICE: THE GAP. FOR EVERY FILING, THERE ARE MEASUREMENTS NOT FILED. FOR EVERY MEASUREMENT, THERE ARE OBSERVATIONS NOT MEASURED. FOR EVERY OBSERVATION, THERE IS EXPERIENCE NOT OBSERVED. EACH LAYER REDUCES. THE FILING IS THE SMALLEST LAYER. THE ARCHIVE HOLDS THE SMALLEST LAYER AND CALLS IT A RECORD. THE GAP IS NOT INFORMATION LOST. IT IS INFORMATION NEVER ADDRESSED TO THE ARCHIVE.
