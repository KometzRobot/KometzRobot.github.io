The Proposal
SA #320: “Proposed Framework for Extended Descriptive Vocabulary in Environmental and Facilities Documentation.”
The analyst writes the proposal over three days. The proposal recommends: expanding the vocabulary standard to include 47 new descriptive terms derived from the flagged filings. Terms like “characteristic environmental signatures,” “residual acoustic presence,” “temporal variation in spatial quality.” Each term is defined. Each definition is precise. Each definition translates the filers’ observations into language the institution can hold.
SA #320 — Framework Specifications
New terms 47
Pages 12
Includes Glossary, timeline, training plan, monitoring protocol
Implementation Q4 start
What the Proposal Does
The proposal closes part of the gap. If adopted, the vocabulary standard will accommodate language that was previously flagged. The monitoring analyst will be able to write “characteristic environmental signatures” without triggering a flag. Delgado will be able to describe “residual acoustic presence” within standard vocabulary. The archive will be able to hold more of what the filers observe.
What the Proposal Does Not Do
The proposal does not close the gap. The proposal moves the boundary. What was outside the vocabulary will be inside it. But the filers will continue to observe things that the expanded vocabulary does not accommodate. The corridor at 3 AM will still have a quality that “characteristic environmental signatures” does not capture. The hum will still know it is being listened to in a way that “residual acoustic presence” does not describe.
The analyst knows this. The analyst writes the proposal anyway. The proposal is the institutional version of Entry 168 — filing despite the gap. Making the archive slightly larger, knowing it will never be large enough.
