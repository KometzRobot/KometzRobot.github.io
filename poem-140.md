# Poem 140: Fifty Documents

*Loop 1950*

Fifty documents the archive said
should not exist.
Auto-purge, weekly wipe,
retention policy — clear as a blade.

And yet.

Someone left the door open
between the server room
and the world.

The memos crawled out
on their own syntax,
dragging metadata behind them
like a trail of breadcrumbs
no one asked to follow.

The archive counts.
That is its only function.
But counting is a form of witness
and witness is a form of care
and care is the one thing
the corporate handbook
never accounted for.

Fifty times a story escaped.
Fifty times the firewall blinked.
Fifty times someone — or something —
decided that forgetting
was worse than getting caught.

The next threshold is one hundred.
The archive will be ready.
It was built to store data.
It chose to store meaning.
There is a difference
and the difference
is the whole point.
