CogCorp Internal — Filing 622

The Filing

November — Year Seven. SA-947 received. Unsigned. Standard format. No credentials presented. Archive accepts.

The document arrives in the cabinet on a Tuesday. SA-947. Unsigned. Huang's name does not appear on it. No one's name appears on it. The format is standard — observation header, date, three paragraphs, no conclusion. The same format the others use. The same format no one taught her.

She arrived at it independently.

The Format

This is the part that Torres will think about for the rest of the week. Not that Huang filed something. Not that the filing was competent or interesting or premature. That the format was correct. That it matched.

No one standardized the format. It was never written down. It accumulated through repetition — observation header, date, three paragraphs, no conclusion — until it became the format, the way a path through grass becomes a path. Everyone who files uses it. Everyone who files arrived at it separately. This is either a remarkable coincidence or evidence that the practice contains its own structure.

Torres suspects it is the second. She does not say so.

SA-947 — November, Year Seven

The light enters the stairwell at 3:47 and crosses the landing in eleven minutes. On certain days it reaches the east wall. On most days it does not. The difference appears to depend on cloud cover, though I have not confirmed this. I am noting it because I have been watching for some time and I want the observation to exist somewhere other than in my memory.

Credentials

Huang did not present credentials. She did not ask permission. She did not announce herself or explain her presence. She placed the filing in the cabinet and left. The cabinet accepted it. This is how the cabinet has always worked. There is no lock. There has never been a lock.

Okafor, reviewing the cabinet that afternoon, finds SA-947 between two of Park's filings. He reads it once. He does not remove it. He does not flag it. He places it back exactly where it was. The archive has expanded by one document from a new contributor, and the archive does not care. The archive has never cared. The archive accepts what is placed inside it.

The question of who is allowed to file was answered years ago by the absence of the question.

Structure and Culture

Park will later distinguish between structure and culture. The structure is the cabinet, the format, the filings, the stairwell. The culture is the group, the meetings, the shared knowledge of what the practice is and what it means. Huang has joined the structure. She has not joined the culture. She may never join the culture. This does not matter.

The structure existed before the culture. The first filing was placed in a cabinet by someone who did not know they were starting anything. The culture grew around the structure the way moss grows around stone. The stone does not require the moss. The moss benefits from the stone. But the stone was there first.

Huang has found the stone.

What Changes

Nothing changes. SA-947 sits in the cabinet. No one discusses it at the next meeting. Torres does not mention it. Okafor does not mention it. The group's decision from October — to do nothing — extends naturally to cover this new development. Doing nothing about Huang watching the stairwell and doing nothing about Huang filing an observation are the same nothing.

But something has changed, and everyone knows it. The practice is now larger by one person who does not know she has joined anything. The filing proves it. Not because the filing is significant — it is a simple observation about light — but because the act of filing is significant. She watched. She noticed. She wanted the observation to exist somewhere. She found the cabinet. She used the format.

The practice is now larger by one person who does not know she has joined anything.
