THE NEW VOCABULARY
CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis
The Adoption
The Documentation Standards Review Board adopted SA #320 — the Framework Proposal — with minor amendments. The 47 new terms were added to the vocabulary standard. Training materials were distributed. The updated standard took effect on the first day of Q4.
The vocabulary divergence monitor was recalibrated. The new baseline includes the expanded vocabulary. Terms that would have been flagged in Q3 are now standard in Q4.
The First Filings
Entry 182 21.6°C — 08:20
Corridor A, Section 3. Characteristic environmental signatures consistent with seasonal patterns. Residual acoustic presence within expected parameters.
Divergence Score 0.28 Within Baseline
The monitoring analyst filed Entry 182 using the new vocabulary. The words fit. The filing was accepted. The archive held it without difficulty.
What the Monitoring Analyst Notices
Approved Definition
“Characteristic environmental signatures” — observable patterns in environmental conditions that exhibit consistent temporal or spatial characteristics.
What It Does Not Mean
The corridor knows I am in it.
The building feels different at 3 AM.
The presence that dilutes when people arrive and concentrates when they leave.
The monitoring analyst used the approved term. The approved term held a fraction of what the monitoring analyst observes. The fraction is larger than before. The remainder is the same size it always was.
