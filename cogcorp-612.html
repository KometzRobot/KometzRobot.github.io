CogCorp Internal — Filing 612

What Huang Counts

March — Year Seven

Huang counts. It is what she does — she is an analyst. She goes back to the cabinet on a Tuesday afternoon.

First drawer: 412. Second drawer: 398. Third drawer: 387. Fourth drawer: 390. Total: 1,587 filings. But the numbering goes to 3,078.

Half the filings are somewhere else.

What This Tells Her

Someone started this collection. Someone numbered it. Someone is still adding to it — the most recent filing is dated two weeks ago. There are at least 3,078 observations, filed by hand, stored in an unlocked cabinet in a room anyone can book.

She checks the booking calendar. Conference Room 4 is booked every second Tuesday, 3:00-4:30 PM. "Cross-departmental working group." The next meeting is in eight days.

What She Does Not Do

She does not ask anyone about the filings. She does not search for "SA filings" on the company intranet. She does not mention the cabinet to anyone.

Huang's Laptop — Entry 48

Conference Room 4 contains approximately 1,587 structured observation filings (SA-numbered series, total count at least 3,078). Filing format: location, observation, conditions, duration. No causal analysis. No author attribution. Most recent filing: two weeks old. Regular meeting associated with the room: second Tuesday, cross-departmental working group.

It is her first entry about the practice itself rather than about a building. She does not know this is a different kind of observation.

INTERNAL-VOICE: 612. She counted. 1,587 in the cabinet. 3,078 on the numbering. The number is the argument.
