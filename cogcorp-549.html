CogCorp Internal — Filing 549

Building A

Four stories. Built 1971. 12 filings in 9 years. All from different people. None filed more than twice. No one stayed.

Okafor goes to Building A on a Thursday afternoon. She has not been inside it in three years.

The lobby has the same tile as every lobby. The elevator works. The stairwell is clean. The heating is on. The fluorescent lights do not flicker.

She stands in the second-floor corridor for eleven minutes. She hears the heating system, which sounds like every heating system. She hears footsteps one floor up. She hears the elevator arrive, open, close, leave.

Nothing.
The Twelve Filings
SA-019

"Building A lobby, Tuesday morning. The light is different." No further description.

SA-103

"Second floor, north wing. A sound I can't identify. Possibly plumbing." It was plumbing.

SA-211

"The stairwell between 3 and 4 smells like paint. There is no paint." Filed 2018. Building A was last painted in 2016.

Each one almost ordinary. Almost nothing.

Analysis

Building E has the elevator hesitation, the stairwell acoustics, the parking structure sounds — anomalies that reward attention. They generate vocabulary. Vocabulary generates more attention.

Building A has no anomalies that reward attention. The building works.

But working is not the same as silent. Working means the sounds are expected. Expected sounds are invisible.

Question 54

"Does Building A produce no observations, or does it produce observations that the vocabulary has no use for?"

"Every building is doing something. The vocabulary developed in buildings where the doing was audible. Building A is doing something quiet.

Quiet is not nothing. It is the hardest thing to observe."
