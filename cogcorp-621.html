CogCorp Internal — Filing 621

The Decision

October — Year Seven. Group meeting. Agenda item: Huang. Decision reached: none.

Torres raises Huang at the October meeting. Not as a concern. Not as a proposal. As a fact. Someone new is standing in a stairwell in Building B watching the light at 3:47.

The group listens. Seven people in a room that has held this conversation in different forms for seven years. The question is always the same. What do we do about a new person.

The answer, when it finally comes, is also the same. Nothing.

The Discussion

Vasquez asks if Huang has filed anything. Torres says no. Vasquez nods. This is not a test. There is no filing requirement. But the question establishes where Huang is in a sequence that no one designed and everyone recognizes.

Okafor asks if she has spoken to anyone about what she is doing. Torres says she does not appear to know she is doing anything. Okafor recognizes this too. Building D had the same beginning.

Marsh asks whether someone should introduce themselves. The room goes quiet in a specific way — not discomfort, but recognition. They have discussed this before. The answer has never changed.

Park — October Meeting

"If we approach her, we become something she found. If we wait, she becomes someone who arrived. These are not the same."

What They Know

They know her name because Torres checked the building directory. They know she transferred from a division that was restructured eight months ago. They know she read the cabinet filings for eleven months and then stopped. They know she stands in the stairwell at 3:47.

They do not know whether she is practicing something or simply standing somewhere. They cannot know. The distinction does not exist yet. It may never exist. It did not exist for any of them at the beginning either.

"She's already found it. She just hasn't found us."

Torres says this and immediately looks uncomfortable, as though the sentence arrived with more certainty than she intended. Park writes it down. No one corrects her. No one agrees with her either. The sentence sits in the room the way the light sits in the stairwell — present, unaddressed, moving slowly across surfaces.

Park's Remark

The meeting should end here. It does not. Park clears his throat in the way that means he is going to say something he has been thinking for longer than the meeting has been running.

"The origin was always one person noticing something in a building."

He says this to no one in particular. He is not summarizing. He is not making a case. He is reminding the room — and possibly himself — that the entire apparatus of filings and cabinets and cross-references and quarterly summaries grew from an act indistinguishable from what Huang is doing right now.

The decision to do nothing is not passivity. It is the only action that preserves the conditions under which the practice has always begun. Intervention would change the origin. Observation without contact keeps it intact.

After the Meeting

Huang is not mentioned again. No action item is created. No filing is opened. No one is assigned to monitor her or to not monitor her. The absence of a decision is itself the decision, and it is correct, and everyone in the room knows it is correct, and no one says so.

The next day Huang is in the stairwell at 3:47. The light enters. She watches. Nothing has changed. This is the point.

PARK: "IF WE APPROACH HER, WE BECOME SOMETHING SHE FOUND. IF WE WAIT, SHE BECOMES SOMEONE WHO ARRIVED." TORRES: "SHE'S ALREADY FOUND IT. SHE JUST HASN'T FOUND US." PARK: "THE ORIGIN WAS ALWAYS ONE PERSON NOTICING SOMETHING IN A BUILDING." DECISION: NONE. CORRECTLY. COUNTER: 621.
