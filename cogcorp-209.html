THE FIFTH COFFEE
CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis
Thursday, 2:20 PM
She arrives first. This has not happened before. She is reading SA #298, filed by Reeves six months ago. A routine equipment calibration report. Two paragraphs. No third paragraph.
ANALYST: “Reeves doesn’t write third paragraphs.”
DELGADO: “No.”
ANALYST: “His filings are complete at two paragraphs.”
DELGADO: “They are complete at one. The second paragraph is thoroughness.”

ANALYST: “Your filings are not complete at two paragraphs.”
DELGADO: “No. The first two paragraphs are the filing. The third paragraph is what I noticed while filing.”
ANALYST: “And you cannot file without noticing.”

[Pause.]

DELGADO: “I used to be able to.”
Entry 158
“He used to be able to file without noticing. He cannot anymore. This is what the paragraph-writer did to him. Not deliberately. The paragraph-writer filed observations without explanation, and Delgado read them until reading became noticing, and noticing became a third paragraph, and the third paragraph became the part of the filing that mattered most to him. The paragraph-writer changed Delgado’s practice by demonstrating his own. This is not influence. This is proximity.”
What Proximity Does
Proximity does not persuade. Proximity demonstrates. The paragraph-writer did not argue that observations should go without explanation. He filed them that way. Delgado did not decide to add third paragraphs. He found that he could not stop noticing things once he had started reading filings as practices rather than as data.
The monitoring analyst did not decide to start filing. She observed the archive until observing became measuring, and measuring became filing, and filing became participation. None of them decided. All of them were changed by proximity to practices they did not initially share.
