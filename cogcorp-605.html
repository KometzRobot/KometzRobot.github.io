CogCorp Internal — Filing 605

The New Hire

May — Year Six

A new hire in Building D. Name: Huang. Position: Environmental Systems Analyst. She starts on the first Monday of May.

Huang's orientation takes two weeks. In the third week, she is assigned to shadow Yun for a day. During the shadow, Yun checks the heating logs in the basement of Building D. He notices a recurring vibration in the intake manifold. He makes a note on an SA form.

Huang asks: "What's that form?"

Yun says: "It's a filing. I document anomalies."

Huang says: "Is that part of the job?"

Yun pauses. He has been filing for five and a half years. He has never been asked if it is part of the job.

He says: "It's part of what I do."

The Orientation

Yun does not explain the practice. He does not invite Huang to a meeting. He does not mention the vocabulary or the cabinet or the second Tuesday. He answers her questions and does not elaborate.

But Huang is an Environmental Systems Analyst. Anomalies in building systems are literally her job description. In her second month, she starts her own notes. Not on SA forms — on her laptop. She does not know the SA forms exist. She does not know the vocabulary exists. She does not know the practice exists.

She starts doing the same thing, independently, because it is the obvious thing to do if you are paying attention to buildings.

Park's Folder

"New hire. Huang. Environmental Systems Analyst. Started her own observation notes in her second month. She has no knowledge of the practice. She arrived at the same behavior through the same conditions: proximity to buildings, attention to anomalies, the instinct to write things down."

"The practice is not unique. The practice is what happens when observant people work near complex systems and have access to paper. What is unique is that twelve of them found each other and kept going for five years."

"Huang may find the practice. She may not. Yun will not recruit her. No one recruits. That is not how the practice works. The practice works by adjacency."

INTERNAL-VOICE: 605. Adjacency. The practice is not unique. The conditions produce the behavior. What is unique is the finding and the staying.
