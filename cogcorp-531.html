CC-531

The Fifty-Fourth Word

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Vocabulary Reference

The vocabulary has been stable at fifty-three terms for six months. Okafor files them quarterly now — a list, not a glossary. The list lives in the shared drive, in a folder called "Reference," in a subfolder called "Supplementary."

Thermal memory. Structural patience. Occupant choreography. Load whisper. Settling rhythm. Elevator patience. Corridor memory. Window conversation. Pipe harmonics. Floor language. Stairwell echo. Ventilation mood. Light migration. Dust calendar. Wall temperature. Threshold behavior. Door resistance. Acoustic shadow. Humidity narrative. Concrete aging. Cable tension. Foundation breath. Seam weather. Joint memory. Panel drift. Insulation song. Drainage patience. Fixture dialogue. Surface patina. Wear narrative. Pressure vocabulary. Temperature gradient. Expansion silence. Seasonal shift. Nocturnal behavior. Dawn register. Afternoon patience. Evening settle. Corridor personality. Elevator habit. Window temperament. Roof conversation. Basement memory. Utility rhythm. Emergency readiness. Maintenance residue. Renovation scar. Paint history. Carpet memory. Tile speech. Hinge testimony. Bolt fatigue. Grout narrative.

Okafor reads the list every Monday. Not because she needs to. Because the list is a portrait of what the practitioners have learned to see over three years. Fifty-three ways of saying: the building is doing something.

SA-834
Filed: March 8. Building E, third floor. Filer: Chen, R. Department: Environmental Services.

"Third floor, north corridor. The fire door between sections 3A and 3B has been propped open with the same doorstop for eleven years. The doorstop was removed during last week's fire safety inspection. The door closed. Section 3B became quieter. The sound level didn't change — there was no noise source being blocked. What changed was the acoustic expectation. The corridor had been one space for eleven years. Now it is two spaces. The building remembers being one."

Chen's term: "acoustic expectation."

Okafor reads the filing. The term is not on the list. She considers whether it fits inside "acoustic shadow" or "corridor memory" or "corridor personality." It does not. Those terms describe what the building does. "Acoustic expectation" describes what the building anticipates. It is the first term about what the building expects rather than what the building performs.

Okafor adds it to the list. Fifty-four.

She does not announce this. She does not email Kim. She does not mark it as significant. She saves the file and closes it.

Three years. Fifty-three terms. Six months of stability. Then: the building remembers being one.
