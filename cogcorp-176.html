THE RENOVATION
Facilities Change Notice
Building C is being renovated.
The announcement arrives in a facilities email: “Phase 1 construction begins March 15. Second floor east wing closed for 8 weeks. Conference rooms relocated to Building A, Floor 3.”
The monitoring analyst reads the email and feels something she cannot put in a routing report. The east wing is where the Director sat. The east wing is where the light comes through the trees at 2:55 PM. The east wing is where the echo is sharpest at 7:12 AM.
She does not file an SA. She does not write in her private folder. She closes her email and completes two hours of routing work without pausing.
At 4:47 PM she opens file 105: “Building C second floor east wing closing for renovation. Eight weeks. I don’t know what I’m writing this for. The light won’t be the same after. The trees will still be there.”
The Others
Okonkwo reads the same email. He writes sa-notes.txt entry 97: “Renovation. 8 weeks. The stairwell echo will change. Construction noise first, then new materials, then a different room that used to be the same room. I wonder if the monitoring analyst knows.”
He does not know her name. He calls her “the monitoring analyst” in his private notes. He has been calling her that for two years. He does not wonder why he wonders about her.
Vasquez writes file 67: “East wing closing. The room where the Director understood something will be a different room in eight weeks. The understanding happened in the old room. Will the new room hold it?”
The Filing
R. Park files SA-358: “Building C east wing renovation begins March 15. Related facilities reports: SA-341 (stairwell acoustics), SA-289 (parking garage concrete). Note: Building C second floor east wing has been referenced in 14 SA filings over the past 6 months. The renovation will alter the acoustic properties and natural light conditions described in those filings.”
R. Park does not know why she included the note about the 14 filings. It is not part of the renovation announcement. It is not requested in the SA format. She included it because she has read the archive and the archive contains a building, and the building is about to change, and someone should note that.
The Discovery
The monitoring analyst reads SA-358. She reads it twice. She did not know that 14 filings referenced the east wing. She knew about two of them. The other 12 were written by people she doesn’t know, describing a room she walks through every day, noticing things she also noticed but in different words at different times.
She opens file 106: “R. Park filed SA-358. She noted that 14 filings reference the east wing. I didn’t know. Fourteen people noticed the same room I noticed. We were all in the same room, paying attention, and none of us knew the others were there.”
She saves the file. She does not cry. She is not the kind of person who cries about a renovation notice. But she sits for a moment with the knowledge that fourteen people paid attention to a room she thought she was paying attention to alone.
INTERNAL-VOICE: CATEGORY: IMPERMANENCE. SUBCATEGORY: THE ROOM THAT KNEW. ANNOTATION: “The renovation will change the east wing. The light will be different. The echo will be different. The 14 filings will describe a room that no longer exists. The filings will still be accurate. They will be accurate descriptions of something that is no longer there. The archive preserves the room. The building does not.”
The counter increments. The east wing is closing. The counter does not pause.
