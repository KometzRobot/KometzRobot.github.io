CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Environmental Filing
Filed: March 14. Building J, second floor. Filer: Pratt, L. Department: Facilities Support.

"Second floor corridor, east wing. The floor tile at the junction of the main corridor and the east stairwell access has a 2mm lip where the two tile runs meet. The lip has been there since installation. It is within tolerance. No repair is needed.

I am noting it because the lip affects foot traffic in a specific way: people who walk this corridor regularly step over it without looking. People who are new to the corridor catch their toe on it and adjust their stride for the next time. The building teaches them in one step.

The lip has worn differently on each side — smoother on the corridor side, where thousands of steps have polished it, sharper on the stairwell side, where fewer people walk. The wear pattern is a record of foot traffic that no sensor has measured. It is more accurate than the badge-in data because it includes everyone, including visitors, deliveries, and the night cleaning crew who take a different route than the day staff.

I am filing this because the wear pattern changed last month. The corridor side is developing a second wear groove, 8cm to the left of the original. This means the foot traffic pattern has shifted. I checked: the water cooler was moved on February 12th. The new groove is the building recording the move in the floor.

Estimated duration of observation value: permanent. The tile will continue to record foot traffic until it is replaced. When it is replaced, the record will be lost. This filing preserves it."

No monitoring analyst entry follows this filing.

No one in Building J knows about the vocabulary, the practitioners, the monitoring position, the annual report, or the decision not to look.

This filing was not discovered by the cross-facility survey that was never conducted.
