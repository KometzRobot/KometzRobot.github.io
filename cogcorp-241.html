The Filing
The Pattern Recognition analyst must file the quarterly vocabulary divergence report. This is SA #318. It follows the standard format: total filings analyzed, total flags generated, flags by department, flags by severity, actions recommended.

What the Report Contains
The report contains statistics. The report is accurate. Every number is correct. The report does not contain: that the analyst knows seven of the flagged filers personally, that six of them sat in a room together and described the gap, that the eighth filer has never met any of them, that the baseline shifted 20.6%, that the shift means the institution’s vocabulary is expanding without permission.
The report does not contain the word “gap.” The report does not contain the phrase “second practice.” The report contains what reports contain: numbers, categories, recommendations.
47 Flags, 0 Actions
Forty-seven times in ninety days, someone filed about what they actually observed instead of what the vocabulary allows. Forty-seven times, the system noticed. Zero times did anyone do anything about it.
The analyst files SA #318 and the report joins the archive and the archive holds it and the report says nothing about what the analyst knows and everything about what the system measured. These are not the same thing. They have never been the same thing.
