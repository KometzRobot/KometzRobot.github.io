Written by Joel Kometz, April 2026. For Chris Pierno and Chris Kometz.

---

## The Short Version

I want to build the technology side of Brothers Fabrication. Website, quoting system, customer portal, AI tools, marketing — all of it. I would start this summer and have something running by August or September.

If it works well, I want to turn it into a real business. A product that other fabrication shops and trades businesses across Alberta could use. Brothers Fabrication would be the founding partner and have a stake in that business.

Here is how I see the money and the structure working.

---

## My Rates

I do three types of work. Each has a different rate.

Marketing and content work is thirty-five to forty-five dollars an hour. That includes things like social media templates, content calendars, Google Business Profile work, and brand materials.

Design and web development is fifty to sixty dollars an hour. That covers website builds, interface design, front-end and back-end coding, and setting up systems like the quote form or customer portal.

Custom systems and AI integration is sixty to seventy-five dollars an hour. That is the specialized work. The food truck configurator, the chatbot, the operations dashboard, the automated follow-ups, and the data tools. This is the kind of work that most freelancers cannot do at all, and agencies charge one hundred to two hundred dollars an hour for.

Every hour is tracked. If something takes less time than estimated, it costs less.

## What the Build Looks Like

There are two realistic starting points.

### Option One — The Core Build

This costs eight to ten thousand dollars total. About a hundred and thirty hours of work over two to three months.

What you get is a proper digital foundation. A redesigned website with good search engine optimization. A quote request form that captures leads and sends notifications to you and Chris K. Google Business Profile fully optimized. Social media templates and a three-month content calendar. Email automation for follow-ups and review requests. Analytics so you can see where leads are coming from.

### Option Two — The Full System

This costs thirty-five to forty-five thousand dollars total. About three hundred to five hundred hours of work over ten to twelve months.

Everything in the core, plus major additions that no other shop in Alberta will have. An advanced food truck configurator where customers design their full build with layout, appliances, finishes, and get a detailed estimate. The AI chatbot upgraded with deep knowledge of your build process, timelines, and materials. A customer project portal so clients log in and see progress photos, milestone updates, and build stage. Smart quote routing that sorts inquiries by type, urgency, and build complexity. Automated milestone updates with photos sent to clients at each stage. A full operations dashboard with job tracking, scheduling, and capacity planning. Inventory and materials tracking tied to active builds. Supplier integration for automated reordering or alerting. Accounting system and Trello integration.

After the build phase we spend three to six months testing and refining with real jobs running through the system. Then ten to twelve months developing it into a sellable product for other shops. That makes this close to a two year project from start to product launch.

## How Payment Would Work

I need an upfront amount to cover equipment and let me pay bills while I build. After that, regular payments until the work is done or hits the cap.

For the Core Build, it would be three to four thousand dollars upfront. Then fifteen hundred to two thousand dollars every two weeks until the total of eight to ten thousand is reached. That works out to about two to three months of payments after the upfront.

For the Full System, it would be six to eight thousand dollars upfront. Then two thousand five hundred dollars every two weeks until it is done.

Once the build is done, it shifts to a monthly retainer to keep everything running and improving. The retainer depends on how much ongoing work you want.

Basic maintenance runs seven hundred fifty dollars per month. That covers updates and keeping things running.

Active growth runs twelve hundred to fifteen hundred dollars per month. That includes new content and ongoing improvements.

Full service runs twenty-five hundred dollars per month. That is hands-on management of everything full time, including opportunity for expansion. This opens the door to further investment and the possibility of handling another test shop.

## What This Costs Compared to Hiring Someone Else

I am not trying to position myself as the cheap option. But you should know what this kind of work normally costs.

A Calgary digital agency would charge one hundred to two hundred dollars an hour across multiple people. A custom web app like the food truck configurator would run fifteen to thirty thousand dollars from an agency. An AI chatbot would run eight to fifteen thousand. A full operations system would run eighty thousand and up.

I do all of that myself. Design, development, AI, marketing — one person. My rates are lower because there is no overhead, I use AI tools to work faster, and we are family. But the work is professional grade.

## What Makes This Different

Most freelancers can do one or two of these things. A web designer can build a website but cannot build an AI chatbot. A marketing person can run social media but cannot build a customer portal. An AI consultant can build the tools but charges two hundred and fifty dollars an hour.

I cover all four areas — design, web development, AI systems, and marketing — under one roof. That is not common. It means Brothers gets a cohesive system where everything works together, not a patchwork of tools from different vendors that do not talk to each other.
