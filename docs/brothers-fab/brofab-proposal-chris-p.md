# Brothers Fabrication — Technology Partnership Proposal

Prepared by Joel Kometz, April 17, 2026.

---

## What This Is

This is a proposal to build a custom technology system for Brothers Fabrication. The system would handle quoting, project tracking, customer communication, and marketing automation. It would be built specifically for how your shop works, not a generic off-the-shelf product.

The goal is to start with Brothers Fabrication as the first client, prove it works over two to three months, and then turn it into a product that other fabrication shops and trades businesses across Alberta can use.

## What I Bring to the Table

I cover design, web development, AI systems, and marketing under one roof. That is not common. Most shops would need to hire a web designer, a developer, a marketing person, and an AI consultant separately. I do all four.

My background is a BFA in Drawing from the Alberta College of Art and Design, eight years running World One Arcade in Calgary, and three years building AI systems. I am self-taught on the tech side. My prices reflect real experience, not agency overhead.

## How the Rates Work

There are three types of work involved in a project like this.

Marketing and content work runs thirty-five to forty-five dollars an hour. That includes social media templates, content calendars, Google Business optimization, and brand materials.

Design and web development runs fifty to sixty dollars an hour. That covers website builds, user interface design, frontend and backend coding, and setting up the quote system and customer portal.

Custom systems and AI integration runs sixty to seventy-five dollars an hour. That is the configurator, the chatbot, the operations dashboard, the automated follow-ups, and the data analysis tools. This is the specialized work that most freelancers cannot do at all.

For reference, Calgary agencies typically charge one hundred to two hundred dollars an hour. AI consultants usually charge two hundred to two hundred fifty per hour. My rates are competitive but fair for the quality of work you get.

## The Packages

There are three levels depending on how far you want to go.

### Option One — The Core Build — Eight to Ten Thousand Dollars

This is the foundation package. About one hundred thirty hours of work over two to three months.

Here is what you get. A fully redesigned website with a built-in AI assistant that answers customer questions twenty-four seven. Search engine optimization so people can find you. A simple truck quote building tool so customers can spec out a basic build and get a ballpark. Google Business profile optimization. Social media templates ready to go. A content calendar so you always know what to post. Email automation for follow-ups and updates. Analytics so you can see what is working.

Payment works like this. Three to four thousand dollars upfront to get started. Then fifteen hundred to two thousand dollars every two weeks until it is done.

### Option Two — The Full System — Thirty-Five to Forty-Five Thousand Dollars

This is the complete package. Three hundred to five hundred hours of work over ten to twelve months.

You get everything from the Core Build plus major additions that no other shop in Alberta will have. An advanced food truck configurator where customers design their full build with layout, appliances, finishes, and get a detailed estimate. The AI chatbot upgraded with deep knowledge of your build process, timelines, and materials. A customer project portal so clients log in and see progress photos, milestone updates, and build stage. Smart quote routing that sorts inquiries by type, urgency, and build complexity. Automated milestone updates with photos sent to clients at each stage. A full operations dashboard with job tracking, scheduling, and capacity planning. Inventory and materials tracking tied to active builds. Supplier integration for automated reordering or alerting. Accounting system and Trello integration.

After the build phase we spend three to six months testing and refining with real jobs running through the system. Then ten to twelve months developing it into a sellable product for other shops. That makes this close to a two year project from start to product launch.

Payment works like this. Six to eight thousand dollars upfront to get started. Then two thousand five hundred dollars every two weeks until it is done.

Full service runs twenty-five hundred dollars per month. That is hands-on management of everything full time, including opportunity for expansion. This opens the door to further investment and the possibility of handling another test shop.
