What I need from you

Five things. Most take under 5 minutes.

  1. 1

    Connect your real Trello board

    Open the demo, hit Connect, follow the 3-step setup. Takes 2 minutes. Your jobs appear on screen in real time.

  2. 2

    Do you use Trello labels?

    Like "framed" or "plumbing done" — if yes, the system auto-advances job stages when you label. Zero extra work.

  3. 3

    Can you set up build@brothersfab.ca?

    This is the cornerstone. Crew email or text build photos to one address. Photos auto-attach to the right job. Customers see progress without anyone "updating" anything.

  4. 4

    Is there a crew photo group chat?

    If they already share build photos somewhere, we pull from there instead of asking anyone to change habits.

  5. 5

    Comfortable with wifi-based time tracking?

    Optional. Phone detects shop wifi = clocked in. No buttons, no app. If not, we skip it entirely.

The rollout

Each step earns the next. You change nothing until it's already working.

Week 1 — Mirror

Zero work for anyone

Connect Trello, tablet goes on the wall. Customer portals turn on for every active job. Chris changes nothing. Crew changes nothing.

Result: Customers stop texting "where's my truck?"
Week 2 — Reflect

Capture what's already happening

Set up the build@ email. Crew emails or texts build photos (same habit, new recipient). Photos auto-attach to jobs. Customer portal shows real progress.

Result: Portal feels alive without anyone "updating" it
Month 1 — Notice

The money feature

Morning email to Chris: "Here's what I'd do today — tap to confirm." Budget alerts. Material reorder suggestions. All single-tap — never data entry.

Result: Save Chris 30+ minutes every morning
Month 2 — Augment

Passive data collection

Tablet asks "Andrew, is this you?" when a job moves to Plumbing (one tap). System drafts customer ETAs; Chris hits send. Wifi time tracking opt-in.

Result: Schedule data and crew assignment without anyone "tracking" anything
Month 6 — Replace

It becomes the source of truth

Tablet becomes drag-to-move for admins. Chris notices he hasn't opened Trello in a week. The system IS the source of truth.

Result: Unified operations system running Brothers Fab

Your crew already takes photos, Chris already uses Trello, customers already ask for updates — this system connects those three things and eliminates the busywork in between.
