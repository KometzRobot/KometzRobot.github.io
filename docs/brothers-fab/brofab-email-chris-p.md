## The Idea

I want to build the technology side of Brothers Fabrication. That means the website, the quoting system, a customer portal, AI tools for the shop floor, and marketing. All of it, built specifically for how your shop works.

I am forming an LLC to do this properly. Brothers Fabrication would be the founding client and development partner. You would pay an upfront amount so I can cover equipment costs and pay my bills while I build. After that, you would pay every two weeks until the work is done or we hit the agreed cap.

If the system works well after two or three months of real use in the shop, we would start turning it into a product. A platform built for fabrication shops and trades businesses across Alberta. Brothers Fabrication would have an ownership stake in that business as the founding partner.

## What I Charge

I do three types of work. Each has a different rate.

Marketing and content work runs forty to fifty dollars an hour. That includes social media templates, content calendars, Google Business optimization, and brand materials. A marketing agency in Calgary would charge eighty to one hundred and fifty dollars an hour for the same work.

Design and web development runs fifty-five to sixty-five dollars an hour. That covers the website build, user interface design, frontend and backend coding, and setting up systems like the quote form or customer portal. A Calgary web agency would charge one hundred to one hundred and eighty dollars an hour across a team of two or three people.

Custom systems and AI integration runs sixty-five to eighty dollars an hour. That is the specialized work. The food truck configurator, the AI chatbot, the operations dashboard, the automated follow-ups, and the data tools. A specialized AI consultant would charge one hundred and fifty to two hundred and fifty dollars an hour for this kind of work. Most freelancers cannot do it at all.

Every hour is tracked. If something takes less time than I estimated, it costs less. No padding.

To put it simply, my rates run about forty to sixty percent below what an agency or consultant would charge for the same work. That is because there is no overhead, I use AI tools to work faster, and we are family. But the work itself is professional grade. One person doing what normally takes a team.

## The Packages

There are two realistic starting points.

### The Core Build

This costs nine to eleven thousand dollars total. That is about one hundred and forty hours of work over six to eight weeks.

What you get is a proper digital foundation. A redesigned website with good search engine optimization. A quote request form that captures leads and sends notifications to you and Chris K. Google Business Profile fully optimized. Social media templates and a three month content calendar. Email automation for follow-ups and review requests. Analytics so you can see where your leads are coming from.

Payment for the core build would work like this. Four thousand dollars up front. Then two thousand dollars every two weeks until the total is reached. That works out to about two to three months of payments after the upfront.

An agency would charge twenty to thirty thousand dollars for the same scope.

### The Full System

This costs twenty to twenty-four thousand dollars total. That is about three hundred to four hundred hours of work over three to five months.

You get everything in the core build, plus the tools that set Brothers apart from every other shop. The food truck configurator where customers choose their layout, appliances, and finishes and get a rough estimate. A twenty-four seven AI chatbot trained on your services, pricing, and frequently asked questions. A customer project portal where clients can log in and see progress photos and milestone updates. Smart quote routing that sorts inquiries by type and urgency. Automated milestone updates with photos sent to clients at each stage.

Payment for the full system would work like this. Six thousand dollars up front. Then twenty-five hundred dollars every two weeks until the total is reached. That works out to about six to eight months of payments after the upfront.

An agency would charge fifty to eighty thousand dollars for this scope, and it would take a team of five or six people a year to deliver.

## Monthly Costs After the Build

Once the system is built, it needs to be maintained and improved. There are three levels. You can cancel any of them at any time with no contracts.

Basic maintenance runs seven hundred and fifty dollars a month. That covers website updates, security patches, system monitoring, one to two social posts a week, and a monthly check-in.

Active growth runs thirteen hundred dollars a month. That adds more social media content, search engine work, blog posts, analytics reports, and system improvements.

Full service runs twenty-seven fifty a month. That adds paid advertising management, video content support, ongoing feature development, and monthly strategy calls.
