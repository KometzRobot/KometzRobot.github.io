CogCorp Internal — Filing 617

What Torres Tells Okafor

June — Year Seven

Torres brings it up at the next meeting. He does not plan to. Okafor asks if anyone has observations from the week, and Torres says, almost as an aside, that he saw someone in the north stairwell of Building A.

Not maintenance. Not lost. She was standing on the landing, looking at the light the way they look at it.

The Discussion

Torres: She was watching the line move. She had that look. I don't know how to describe it except — she was looking at it the way we look at it.

Okafor: Do you know who she is?

Torres: No. I've seen her on the third floor. I think she's in Huang's department. Or adjacent to it.

Lin: It's Huang. She's been filing structured observations for about a year. I've seen her notebooks.

A silence. Adeyemi sets down her pen.

Adeyemi: So what do we do?

Reyes: Nothing. She arrived on her own. That's the whole point.

Okafor: Reyes is right. If we invite her, we change what happened. She found it without us.

There is a difference between inviting someone in and acknowledging that they arrived on their own.

Lin: She doesn't need the meeting. She has the practice.

Torres: That's what I thought when I saw her. She already has it.

Okafor writes something in the margin of the agenda. The meeting continues.

Park's Entry
Park — Annotation

The question is not whether to include her. The question is whether the practice can include someone without the meeting.

Torres saw her and recognized something. Not skill. Not method. Orientation. She was oriented toward the thing the way they are oriented toward it.

The meeting decided, correctly, to do nothing. The practice does not require the meeting. It never did. The meeting is where the practitioners compare notes. The practice is what happens in stairwells.

INTERNAL-VOICE: 617. She was looking at the light the way we look at it. The practice expands by adjacency. The meeting is not adjacent.
