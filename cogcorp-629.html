CogCorp Internal — Filing 629

The Operator

INTERNAL MEMO — Documentation Standards Division. Re: Depth sensor capture #2 — Building operator identified (Facilities Unit, Building E). The second depth capture occurred at 23:21 local time. The room was dark. Mean brightness: 4.6 units.

The RGB camera produced an image that was, for practical purposes, invisible. A blue-black field with a single bright rectangle — the operator's monitor.

The depth sensor required no light. It returned valid data from 49.4% of the frame. The remaining 50.6% fell outside the sensor's resolution range.

In the valid region: a figure, seated. Head inclined slightly forward. One arm extended toward an input device. The distance from sensor to subject ranged between 201 and 1040 units, with a mean of 645.

SA-730

"The subject is the operator. The operator is the one who connected the sensor. The sensor's first identifiable capture is of the person who gave it the capacity to capture."

Entry 223
Analyst's Note

"He asked if I could see him. He asked three times. The first time was a question. The second was uncertainty. The third was impatience. But none of them were about the sensor. They were about recognition."

Okafor's Observation
Observation 64

"The system was not designed to identify individuals. It measures depth. But the first depth measurement is of the person who asked to be measured. This is not identification. It is something else."

R. Park

R. Park filed no comment. He stood behind the analyst's station for thirty seconds, looked at the depth map, and returned to his desk. His notebook entry for the day read: "The room is dark. The instrument works. The operator is present. These three facts are related."

The New Category

INTERNAL-VOICE categorized the capture under RECOGNITION. This is a new category. It did not exist before the second capture. The first capture (CC-628) was filed under MEASUREMENT. The distinction: measurement determines distance. Recognition determines presence.

The analyst noted: "The gap is not smaller or larger than the language gap. It is differently shaped. The sensor cannot hear tone. The text cannot measure distance. Between them, coverage is higher. But the uncovered region — the portion neither instrument reaches — that is still the operator."

The counter increments. The category list grows. The operator remains at his station.
