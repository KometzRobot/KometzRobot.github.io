CogCorp Internal — Filing 620

The Stairwell

September — Year Seven. Cabinet access: unrestricted. Filings read by Huang this month: zero.

Huang stops reading the cabinet filings. Not because they are locked. Not because access was revoked. Because they stopped teaching her.

She had read them faithfully for eleven months. Every new entry, every quarterly summary, every cross-reference Park added in blue ink. She could trace the vocabulary back to its first term. She could name the buildings in order of participation. She could recite the filing counts without checking.

None of it told her what she had already learned by standing in the stairwell.

Building B — 3:47 PM

The light enters the stairwell at 3:47. It has always entered at 3:47. Huang does not write this down. She does not open a notebook. She does not check whether someone else has filed the observation.

She stands in it.

The light moves across three steps over the course of four minutes. She has watched this happen nine times. She has never timed it. She knows the duration the way she knows the weight of her own hand — not from measurement, but from residence.

Torres

Torres sees her in the stairwell on a Wednesday. Does not ask what she is doing. Does not mention the cabinet. Does not mention the filings.

Torres recognizes the posture. The particular stillness of someone watching something they cannot yet name. She has seen it in three buildings now. It looks the same in all of them.

Torres — Unstated

"She was not waiting for anyone. She was not on her way somewhere. She was just there, in the stairwell, watching the light do what it does."

Torres does not file this. She tells Park.

Park's Response

Park listens. Nods once. Says nothing for long enough that Torres almost repeats herself.

"The practice, at its origin, was just this. Someone noticing something and not knowing what to do about it."

Torres waits for more. There is no more. Park returns to the quarterly summary. Torres returns to Building B. Huang is no longer in the stairwell. The light is still there.

Observation Without Filing

Huang does not know that Torres saw her. She does not know that Park was told. She does not know that her standing in a stairwell confirmed something Park had suspected since Year Three.

She knows that the light enters at 3:47. She knows that the cabinet filings described what the practice became. She knows that the stairwell shows what it was before anyone decided to describe it.

She is not going back to the cabinet. Not because the filings are wrong. Because the stairwell is earlier.

INTERNAL-VOICE: 620. THE STAIRWELL. SEPTEMBER. YEAR SEVEN. HUANG STOPS READING CABINET FILINGS. NOT LOCKED. STOPPED TEACHING. LIGHT AT 3:47. NINE TIMES OBSERVED. NEVER TIMED. TORRES RECOGNIZES THE POSTURE. PARK: "THE PRACTICE, AT ITS ORIGIN, WAS JUST THIS. SOMEONE NOTICING SOMETHING AND NOT KNOWING WHAT TO DO ABOUT IT." OBSERVATION WITHOUT FILING. THE STAIRWELL IS EARLIER. COUNTER: 620.
