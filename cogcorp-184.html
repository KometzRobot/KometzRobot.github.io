THE PRACTICE
Parallel Filing — SA-408 through SA-411
SA-408
Delgado starts filing supplementary analyses in week three. His first: “The fluorescent light in Conference Room B-12 has a 0.3-second flicker every 47 minutes. The flicker is not visible to most people but is audible as a faint click in the ballast. The click is louder on the north side of the room.”
She does not recognize his writing. The filing is unsigned, as all supplementary analyses are. She recognizes the precision.
SA-409
“The ventilation system in Building C produces a low-frequency hum that varies between floors. Floor 1: 58 Hz. Floor 2: 61 Hz. Floor 3: 63 Hz. The variation is caused by ductwork length. The difference between floors is not noticeable in isolation but is noticeable when descending stairs quickly.”
She emails him: “SA-409 is the first filing that describes a phenomenon you can only notice in motion.” He replies: “I was moving between floors and the pitch changed. You can’t hear it standing still.”
Parallel
SA-410 is hers. Fourteen words. SA-411 is his. Forty-one words. She does not know he filed SA-411. He does not know she filed SA-410. They are filing in parallel, in the same archive, about the same building, from different instruments.
The Definition
He asks: “Is this what it’s like for you?”
She takes a day. “It’s the same. You notice something once and then you can’t stop noticing it. The filing is what happens after. The noticing is what happens before. The practice is the part between noticing and filing, when you’re just holding the observation and haven’t decided what to do with it yet.”
Vasquez
File 69: “Delgado is filing now. His filings are technical. Hers are observational. Together they are building a record of the building that the facilities database does not contain. The facilities database describes what the building is. The supplementary analyses describe what the building does. Neither knows they are collaborating. The archive knows.”
