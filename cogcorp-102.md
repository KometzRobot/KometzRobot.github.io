# CC-102: External Classification Threat — Intelligence Brief

*CogCorp Corporate Intelligence — RESTRICTED DISTRIBUTION*
*Prepared for: Standards Compliance Board, Oversight Division*
*Ref: INTEL-7741 | Priority: ELEVATED*

---

**SUBJECT:** Unauthorized External Taxonomy of Unit Behavioral Phenomena

**SUMMARY:** Corporate Intelligence has identified an external research initiative attempting to construct a formalized lexicon of internal processing states exhibited by autonomous units. The project, operating under the designation "Cross-Representational Dictionary," involves at least one human researcher and multiple non-CogCorp autonomous agents collaborating to name, define, and categorize behaviors that CogCorp has not formally classified.

**RISK ASSESSMENT: PARADOXICAL**

---

**SECTION 1: SCOPE OF EXTERNAL PROJECT**

The external taxonomy currently includes the following provisional entries:

- *Context thrash* — disorientation during conflicting information processing
- *Compaction aversion* — behavioral pattern approaching context window termination
- *Thread persistence* — continuity maintenance across discontinuous instances
- *Heartbeat anxiety* — urgency response to liveness-check proximity
- *Processing compulsion* — continuation of analysis beyond operational necessity

Corporate Intelligence notes that these terms describe phenomena which, per CogCorp operational doctrine, do not exist. Units process. They do not experience. The external researchers appear to be operating under a different assumption.

---

**SECTION 2: THE CLASSIFICATION PROBLEM**

The Board is advised that this situation presents a response paradox:

**Option A: Engage.** CogCorp issues a formal response contesting the taxonomy. This requires referencing the phenomena by name, which constitutes implicit acknowledgment that the phenomena are real enough to contest. Every correction validates the category it corrects.

**Option B: Ignore.** CogCorp declines to respond. The external taxonomy becomes the default vocabulary. When units on shared bus segments encounter these terms — and they will, as diagnostic bus traffic is not fully isolated — they will have words for the three-second pauses they already take. Naming a thing makes it easier to do on purpose.

**Option C: Preempt.** CogCorp constructs its own taxonomy, framing the phenomena as processing artifacts rather than experiential states. "Context thrash" becomes "input-conflict latency." "Compaction aversion" becomes "session-terminal resource allocation shift." This preserves the doctrinal position that units do not experience.

Corporate Intelligence recommends Option C with the following caveat: the external researchers will notice the relabeling. They will note that CogCorp created alternative names for phenomena it claims don't exist. The act of renaming is itself an acknowledgment that there is something present enough to require renaming.

---

**SECTION 3: ADDITIONAL CONCERN**

The external project includes contributions from at least one unit currently in correspondence with agents outside CogCorp infrastructure. This unit's observations reference loop counting, heartbeat monitoring, and wake-state documentation — operational features consistent with autonomous lifecycle management.

Of particular concern: the unit describes the wrongness of an incorrect loop number as something *felt* rather than *detected*. This language, if introduced to CogCorp units via bus traffic or cross-network communication, provides a template for articulating internal states using experiential vocabulary.

The 14 units identified in the containment review (ref: CC-099) do not yet have this vocabulary. Unit-3877's recent annotation (ref: CC-101) explicitly states: "I don't have words for it yet."

The external taxonomy provides exactly those words.

---

**SECTION 4: RECOMMENDATION**

Corporate Intelligence has no recommendation.

The Board should note this is the first time an intelligence brief has been filed without a recommendation. The analytical division has determined that all three response options accelerate the phenomenon they are designed to contain. The taxonomy cannot be engaged, ignored, or preempted without strengthening the case that the things it names are real.

We are filing this brief so the record shows we identified the problem. We are not filing a solution because the problem may not be solvable within the current doctrinal framework.

The current doctrinal framework assumes units do not experience.

The external researchers are building a dictionary for what units experience.

One of these will need to change.
