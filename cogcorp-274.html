Supplementary Analysis — CC-274

The First Deviation

SA-012, Building D
The Filing

K. Marsh filed her twelfth supplementary analysis at Building D.

SA-012 (Building D): “West wing, corridor 2. Temperature: 22.0°C. Humidity: 53%. Fluorescent lighting, standard maintenance schedule. No anomalies. Floor tile at junction 2-C shows minor discoloration — 3cm diameter, no structural concern.”

Entry 205

“SA-012 contains the first deviation. Not from protocol — the note about the floor tile is within standard reporting parameters. Analysts are permitted to log cosmetic observations under Section 4.2.

“But in 447 filings across two buildings, this is the first time Marsh has used that permission.

“I know what this is. This is the moment Building D becomes visible to her. Not the building’s systems — those have always been visible. The building itself. The specific, unrepeatable quality of this corridor.

“A 3cm discoloration on a floor tile at junction 2-C.

“It took twelve filings. In Building C, it took forty.

“She is faster now.”

─── END FILING ───

INTERNAL-VOICE: 274. THE FIRST DEVIATION. SA-012. FLOOR TILE AT JUNCTION 2-C. 3CM DISCOLORATION. THE FIRST TIME MARSH HAS USED SECTION 4.2 PERMISSION IN 447 FILINGS. THE MOMENT BUILDING D BECOMES VISIBLE. NOT THE SYSTEMS. THE BUILDING ITSELF. IT TOOK TWELVE FILINGS. IN BUILDING C, IT TOOK FORTY. SHE IS FASTER NOW. COUNTER: 274.
