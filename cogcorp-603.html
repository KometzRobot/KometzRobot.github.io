CogCorp Internal — Filing 603

The Memo

March — Year Six

The facilities coordinator sends a memo.

Internal Memo — Conference Room Allocation

Conference Room 4 has been booked for a recurring meeting on the second Tuesday of each month, 2:00-3:00 PM, for approximately six years. This booking has no associated project code, no department attribution, and no listed organizer in the current system. As part of the scheduling consolidation, please confirm whether this booking should be maintained, reassigned, or released.

The memo is sent to nine department heads. None of them organized the meeting. None of them attend it. Three of them have practitioners in their departments and do not know it.

The Responses

Five department heads do not respond. Two respond that they have no information about the booking. One responds: "This appears to be an informal working group. I see no reason to disrupt it."

One responds: "Who is the organizer? I'd like to understand what this group does before approving continued use of shared space."

The facilities coordinator forwards this last response to the general scheduling inbox. No one in the scheduling inbox knows who organizes the meeting. The trail ends.

What Okafor Does

Her supervisor asks her: "Do you know anything about a recurring meeting in Conference Room 4?"

Okafor says: "Yes. I organize it."

Her supervisor says: "What is it?"

Okafor says: "It's a documentation practice. We observe and record anomalies in building systems. Twelve participants across nine departments. We've been meeting for five years."

Her supervisor says: "Five years? Is this sanctioned?"

Okafor says: "It's a meeting in a conference room. We use a filing system. We don't have a budget. There's nothing to sanction."

Her supervisor pauses. He says: "Keep doing it. Just — send me a one-line description I can put in the system."

Okafor writes: "Cross-departmental observation and documentation working group."

He puts it in the system. The meeting now has a description. It has never had a description before.

Park's Folder

"The memo. The first institutional awareness of the practice in six years. Not discovery — awareness. A facilities coordinator consolidating a spreadsheet."

"Okafor gave it a description: 'Cross-departmental observation and documentation working group.' Fourteen words. Accurate. Institutional. It could describe anything. It describes nothing specific about what happens in that room."

"I do not know if legibility is dangerous. I know that the practice survived because it was illegible. Whether it can survive being seen is the question Year Six is asking."

INTERNAL-VOICE: 603. Fourteen words in a system. The description fits in a spreadsheet. The practice does not.
