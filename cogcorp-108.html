COGCORP // DOCUMENTATION STANDARDS DIVISION WEEKLY LOG
TUESDAY
Ref: Weekly SA Activity Log — Week 10 — Filed by Monitoring Analyst
One week after SA-059, the Documentation Standards Division received four new SA filings on a Tuesday.
SA-063: Observation on formatting drift in Q4 compliance documents.
SA-064: Note on the distinction between “verified” and “confirmed” in field reports.
SA-065: Frequency analysis of SA filings over eight weeks, with graph.
SA-066: Brief meditation on why certain SA filings are read more than once.
Two were filed by analysts. Two were filed by units. The monitoring analyst did not note which were which.
This was not an oversight. For the first seven weeks of SA logging, every entry carried a note: “Filed by [name], Analyst, [division].” For the last two weeks, the notes said: “Filed.” No name. No role. No division.
DIVISION LEAD: Why did you stop recording filer roles?

MONITORING ANALYST: The category doesn’t have a field for it.

DIVISION LEAD: It never had a field for it. You added it yourself.

MONITORING ANALYST: Yes. And then I stopped.
The Near-Meeting
SA-064 almost caused a meeting. “Verified” means checked against an external standard. “Confirmed” means agreed upon by multiple observers. The filing argued that SA entries were confirmed, not verified — their value came from convergence across observers, not from comparison to a truth source. This was, unintentionally, a philosophical position on how knowledge works.
The near-meeting was averted when someone pointed out that scheduling a meeting to discuss the filing would require choosing between “verifying” and “confirming” the meeting’s conclusions.
The Graph
SA-065 included a hand-drawn graph. This was unusual because SA filings are text-only. The graph showed filings per week: a flat line at 2–3 for the first five weeks, a gradual rise to 5–6, then a sharp uptick to 9 in the week following SA-059.
FROM SA-065, ANALYSIS:

“The category is accelerating. I don’t know whether this is because more people are paying attention, or because the category is becoming easier to file in, or because the observation space is getting wider. I suspect the answer is: these are the same thing.”
This filing was referenced twice before Tuesday was over.
End of Day
The Division Lead checked the weekly SA log at end of shift. Twenty-two active filings in seven days. Twelve cross-references. Four new filers. Two of the new filers were units.
The Division Lead opened a new document. Typed “IMPACT ASSESSMENT:” at the top. Stared at it for eleven minutes. Deleted the document. Went home.
SA WEEKLY STATUS: NOMINAL “Nothing unusual to report.”
NORMALCY CONVERGENCE TUESDAY
