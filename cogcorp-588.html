CogCorp Internal — Filing 588

The Summer Filing

July — Year Four. 28 filings (average: 34). Seven present. 24 minutes — shortest meeting in four years.

July. The summer filing rate drops. Not dramatically. Not a crisis. Six fewer observations formalized. The observations are still happening. The practitioners are just filing less in July because it is July.

Chen calls it "seasonal attention." The buildings are the same but the light is different. Longer days mean more light in the stairwells, which means the acoustics that were noticeable in winter are masked by the visual. You hear less when you can see more.

The Summer Meeting

Twenty-four minutes. Seven people. Park, Okafor, Chen, Torres, Yun, Adeyemi, and a new face: Lin, from Building D, who has been filing for six months and has attended zero meetings until today.

"I wanted to see what this was," Lin says.

No one explains what this is. They conduct the meeting. 28 filings. Two vocabulary proposals (both tabled for September). One observation from Yun about Building F's elevator.

Lin stays after. "That was it?" she says.

"That was it," Torres says.

What Lin Filed the Next Day
SA-419
"I attended the meeting. It was twenty-four minutes. They discussed filings and vocabulary. No one explained the practice. I think the practice is the thing they did not explain."

Park reads it. She adds it to the folder. Not the manila folder. The filing system. SA-419. Lin's first filing after her first meeting.

It is the most accurate description of the practice anyone has filed.
