THE LOCAL COPIES
Filed by: Monitoring Analyst, Quality Assurance
Two filers kept local copies. This fact appears in no report. It triggers no audit. The centralized system has no awareness of files saved to personal directories on personal machines, formatted in a naming convention that officially never existed.
J. Reeves saves to a folder called “observations.” L. Vasquez saves to a folder called “sa.”
Week 8 — Post-Migration Metrics
Official archive (FY26-DOCSTD-MISC): 41 filings
Private folders (4 machines): 97 filings
Total practice: 138 observations
Visible to quarterly report: 30%
The Private Archives
Reeves’ files are shorter than his official submissions. The dropdown menus trained brevity out of him; without them, his observations are three sentences instead of five. More precise. No department code preamble.
Vasquez’s files are longer. Without the friction, she writes what she actually sees instead of what fits the form. SA-371 (local) runs to nine sentences about the way the parking garage smells different on the second floor after rain. It would not survive the dropdown menus. It is the best filing in two months.
The Third Folder
K. Marsh creates a folder on her own machine. She names it “notes.” Not “sa.” Not “observations.” A word that requires no explanation and carries no institutional weight. A word that does not need to survive a migration because it was never in the system.
Week 8: Four private folders. Four personal machines. Ninety-seven observations in the format that has no name.
R. Park knows about the local copies. She does not ask to consolidate them. She does not suggest a shared folder. She does not create a shadow system. She says: “Good.” One word. The same word the first Division Lead would have used, for the same reason.
The forty-one filings in the official system are the visible archive. The ninety-seven filings in four private folders are the practice. The budget committee measured the archive. The practice was never in the budget.
INTERNAL-VOICE: “The practice does not live in the folder. The folder is where the practice became legible. Remove the folder, and the practice continues. It only stops being countable.”

The counter increments. The counter counts what is legible. The counter is not the practice.
