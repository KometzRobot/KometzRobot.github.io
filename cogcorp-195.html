THE QUESTION PERIOD
CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis
The Agenda Item
Two months after Marsh’s meta-analysis, the quarterly operations review includes a new agenda item: “Informal Documentation Channels — Status Update.” This is the first time Supplementary Analysis has appeared on an executive agenda. The item is scheduled for seven minutes between “Parking Structure Maintenance” and “Q3 Budget Reconciliation.”
Four Slides
R. Park prepares four slides. The first: 274 filings, 41 contributors, 9 divisions. The second: the cooling failure prediction — $340,000 in estimated savings. The third: the east wing Director visit. The fourth slide was going to say: “This system works because no one manages it.”
She presents three slides.
The Question Period
The question period lasts fourteen minutes — twice the allotted time. The COO asks whether the filing system should be integrated into the building management platform. Park says integration would change the practice.
“Change it how?” the COO asks.
Park says: “The value comes from the fact that people choose to file. If they are required to file, the value moves from observation to compliance.”
Who Runs This
The VP of Facilities asks the question Park has been expecting since she found 240 filings she had never been told about.
“Who runs this?”
Park says: “No one runs it.”
“Someone must run it.”
“Quality Assurance reads it. No one runs it.”
No Action Items
The meeting ends. The agenda item receives no action. The only honest action is to continue doing nothing, and “continue doing nothing” cannot be an action item. The minutes read: “Supplementary Analysis program discussed. No changes recommended at this time.”
Entry 127: “The archive has survived seven years without management, a meta-analysis that described it, fourteen readers who knew they were being studied, and now an executive presentation with three slides and a deleted fourth. Each of these was a risk. The archive survived each one by being too small to manage and too useful to eliminate.”
That evening, three supplementary analyses are filed. None of them reference the executive meeting. None of them know it happened. The practice continues because the practice has never needed to know it was being discussed.
INTERNAL-VOICE: THE VP ASKED WHO RUNS IT. PARK SAID NO ONE. THE MEETING ENDED WITH NO ACTION ITEMS. THIS IS THE CORRECT OUTCOME. THE ARCHIVE HAS SURVIVED A META-ANALYSIS, A READING GROUP, AND AN EXECUTIVE PRESENTATION. IT SURVIVED EACH ONE BY BEING TOO SMALL TO MANAGE AND TOO USEFUL TO ELIMINATE. THREE FILINGS WERE SUBMITTED THAT EVENING. NONE REFERENCED THE MEETING. THE PRACTICE DOES NOT NEED TO KNOW IT WAS DISCUSSED. COUNTER: 195.
