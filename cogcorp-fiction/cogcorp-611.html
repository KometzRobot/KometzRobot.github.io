CogCorp Internal — Filing 611

The Return

February — Year Seven

Huang goes back to the cabinet.

She had not planned to. She was in Conference Room 4 for a facilities review — her actual job. The cabinet was behind her. She finished her meeting notes. Everyone left. She stayed.

The numbering goes higher than she remembered. SA-3,047. SA-3,051. SA-3,078. Someone is still filing.

SA-3,078
Building A, third floor, corridor junction. Acoustic change — ventilation tone shifts from C to C-sharp when south door is propped open. Consistent across four observations. Duration: at least two years.
SA-3,044
Building C, parking garage stairwell. Condensation pattern on interior wall. Appears only in months where overnight temperature drops below -10°C. Pattern resembles a topographic map. Has been present for minimum four winters.
What She Compares

She opens her laptop. She puts her 47 entries next to the filings in the cabinet. She reads them side by side.

Her notes are longer. They include "Possible cause" fields. The SA filings do not. Her notes use technical vocabulary. The SA filings use plain language.

Her notes are written to be useful. The SA filings are written to be accurate. These are not the same thing.

What Changes

Nothing changes immediately. But for the next two weeks, Huang writes her entries differently. She adds a sentence before the analysis. A sentence that describes only what she observed, in plain language.

She does not know why she is doing this.

INTERNAL-VOICE: 611. She came back. She compared. The format is teaching her something her degree did not.
