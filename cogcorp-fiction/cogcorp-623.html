CogCorp Internal — Filing 623

The Second Filing

December — Year Seven. Three unsigned filings in the cabinet. Huang measures with attention, not instruments. The difference is 0.1 seconds. The asymmetry is the last distance.

SA-948 appears in the cabinet three weeks after SA-947. Same format. Unsigned. Observation header, date, three paragraphs, no conclusion. Torres finds it on a Thursday morning, slipped between Park's October series and a filing from Okafor she has read four times. She reads SA-948 once and places it back.

Huang has filed again. This is no longer an event. This is a pattern.

The Measurement

SA-948 describes a timing difference. The stairwell light — the same light from SA-947, the light that crosses the landing in eleven minutes — reaches the east wall 0.7 seconds before it touches the railing on days when the cloud cover is partial. On fully clear days the interval is 0.8 seconds. Huang does not explain why the difference matters. She does not explain how she measured it.

Torres reads the filing a second time, which is unusual. The measurement is precise without instruments. Huang is timing with her attention. She is watching closely enough that a tenth of a second registers as a difference, and the difference registers as worth recording.

She is measuring with attention, not instruments.

SA-948 — December, Year Seven

On days with partial cloud cover, the light reaches the east wall 0.7 seconds before it touches the railing. On clear days the interval is 0.8 seconds. I cannot explain the difference. I am noting it because the difference is consistent and I do not want to lose it. The light does not know it is being timed. I am not sure I am timing it. I am watching, and the numbers appear.

The Difference

Park reads SA-948 that afternoon and says nothing for a long time. Then he says: the difference between 0.7 and 0.8 seconds is not a measurement. It is a position. You have to be standing in a specific place, watching in a specific way, for that tenth of a second to become visible. The numbers are not the observation. The position is the observation.

Torres asks what he means. Park says: anyone with a stopwatch could time the light. The stopwatch would give you the numbers. It would not give you the position. Huang is standing somewhere — not physically, though also physically — where 0.1 seconds is legible. That is not precision. That is proximity.

The difference is position.

Three Filings

By the end of December there are three unsigned filings in the cabinet. SA-947, SA-948, and SA-949. The third describes the sound the stairwell door makes at different temperatures. Huang does not identify herself in any of them. She does not need to. The filings identify her. Not by name — by attention. The quality of noticing is consistent across all three. Torres recognizes it the way she recognizes Park's filings, or Okafor's, or her own. Not by content. By grain.

Okafor says: we know her filings because we know what watching looks like when it is written down. Everyone who files long enough develops a grain. The grain is the residue of how they pay attention. It cannot be faked because it is not deliberate. It is what happens when watching becomes habitual and the habit shapes the prose.

The Asymmetry

There is an asymmetry now that did not exist before November. The group knows Huang. Huang does not know the group. She files in a cabinet that she found on her own, using a format she arrived at independently, measuring things with a precision that comes from proximity rather than instrumentation. She does not know that her filings are read. She does not know that her grain is recognized. She does not know that Park called her measurement a position rather than a number.

The group has discussed whether to close this asymmetry. Torres raised it. Park said nothing. Okafor said: the asymmetry is not a problem to solve. It is the last distance between Huang and the practice. When she is ready to close it, she will close it. If she never closes it, the filings are still in the cabinet. The archive does not require acquaintance.

The asymmetry is the last distance.
