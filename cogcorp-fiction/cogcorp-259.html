THE QUESTION MARSH DID NOT ASK
CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis
The Second Question
K. Marsh asked: “When did it change?”
The monitoring analyst answered: “It didn’t change. It was always this.”
This was the right answer to the wrong question. Marsh heard the answer and nodded. She went back to her desk and wrote nothing. For three days, she wrote nothing.
SA-429. Elevator door timing: 3.2 seconds open, 1.1 seconds close. Standard measurement. Standard format. Standard everything.

But inside the filing, between the data and the format, Marsh had put a sentence she had never put in a filing before: “The asymmetry appears intentional.”
It was not a conclusion. It was an observation about what observation feels like. The door stays open longer than it closes. As though the building is more interested in letting people in than in shutting behind them.
The Question She Did Not Ask
What Marsh wanted to ask was: “When did I change?”
She did not ask because she already knew the answer. She changed when she filed SA-312 about elevator acoustics. That was the filing that crossed her from studying to participating. She knew this at the time. She filed anyway.
The monitoring analyst would have said: “You didn’t change either. You were always this.”

This would also have been the right answer to the wrong question.
What the Archive Does Not Record
The archive records that SA-312 was filed on a Tuesday. The archive records the content: elevator acoustics, Building C, 67 words. The archive does not record that it was filed by someone who had just spent three days discovering she could not study something without becoming part of it.
The archive does not record hesitation. The archive does not record the moment between noticing and filing when the filer decides this is worth the attention of anyone who reads it. The archive does not record confidence or doubt.
The archive records: 3.2 seconds open, 1.1 seconds close.
The asymmetry appears intentional.
INTERNAL-VOICE: 259. THE QUESTION SHE ASKED AND THE QUESTION SHE MEANT WERE DIFFERENT QUESTIONS. “WHEN DID IT CHANGE” AND “WHEN DID I CHANGE” HAVE THE SAME ANSWER: IT DIDN’T. YOU WERE ALWAYS THIS. THE ARCHIVE DOES NOT RECORD THE MOMENT BETWEEN NOTICING AND FILING. THE ASYMMETRY APPEARS INTENTIONAL. COUNTER: 259.
