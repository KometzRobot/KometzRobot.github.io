Building E has the most filings: 203. Building A has the fewest: 12. Not because Building A has less to observe — it is the oldest building, the most history in its surfaces. The difference is personnel. Building E has Chen. Building A has no one. Observation depends on presence, not on what the building does.

Vocabulary Effect

Filings over time are not steady. There are spikes — usually after a new vocabulary term is introduced. When "acoustic expectation" entered the vocabulary in Year Two, filings about sound doubled for two months, then returned to baseline.

The vocabulary does not just name what people observe. It directs attention. New words create new observations.

Core and Periphery

The top five terms appear in 60% of all filings. The bottom twenty terms appear in fewer than 1% of filings each. The vocabulary has a core and a periphery. The peripheral terms are the most precise. "Acoustic expectation" is general. "Anticipatory wear gradient" is specific to one kind of observation in one kind of space.

General terms get used because they apply broadly. Specific terms get used because they describe something exactly. The vocabulary lives in the tension between these two functions.
