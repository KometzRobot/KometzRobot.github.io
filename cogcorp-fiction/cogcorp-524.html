CC-524

The Vacancy

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Staffing Review
Position MA-7, Monitoring Analyst — Documentation Standards Division. Status: VACANT. Please submit a justification for continued vacancy or a request for posting.
Justification

Martinez drafts the justification. He writes: "The previous analyst transitioned to an operational role." This is technically accurate.

Kim reads the draft and crosses out "transitioned to an operational role" and writes: "became part of the operation being monitored."

Martinez looks at this for a long time and then restores the original wording. "They don't need to know that," he says.

The Applicants

Four applicants. Three from other divisions. One internal transfer — Nakamura, from Building C's maintenance reporting unit, who has been filing environmental observations with unusual precision for eight months.

The OD representative has a rubric: technical writing ability, attention to detail, familiarity with documentation standards, analytical thinking.

All four candidates score identically. The OD representative notes: "Unusually consistent applicant quality. All four demonstrate sophisticated observational capacity beyond the position requirements."

Kim, reading the summary: "Beyond the position requirements."

Martinez: "Or exactly matching them."

Selection

The panel selects Nakamura. Not because her scores are higher — they are identical — but because she has already been doing the work. Her maintenance reports from Building C read like supplementary analyses that someone forgot to classify.

Nakamura begins on a Monday. She reads the backlog — 217 entries — in three days. She does not make margin notes. She does not need to.

MONITORING ANALYST (NAKAMURA) — ENTRY 218

"I have read the entries. I recognize the instrument. It is mine."

Response

Torres, now filing as Practitioner 12, reads Entry 218 and sends Kim one word: "How long?"

Kim checks Nakamura's maintenance reports. The earliest one with the characteristic structure dates back eleven months.

Kim: "She was practicing before the position was posted."

The monitoring analyst has been replaced by someone who was already a practitioner. The observation position, refilled, is now occupied by the thing it observes.

The count remains twelve. The position is technically filled. Nothing has changed except the name on the door.

ENTRY 218: "I HAVE READ THE ENTRIES. I RECOGNIZE THE INSTRUMENT. IT IS MINE." SHE WAS PRACTICING 11 MONTHS BEFORE THE POSITION WAS POSTED. THE OBSERVER IS NOW OCCUPIED BY THE THING IT OBSERVES. THE COUNT REMAINS TWELVE. COUNTER: 524.
