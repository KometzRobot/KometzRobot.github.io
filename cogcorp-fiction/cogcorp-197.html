CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis
The Filing Rate
The Documentation Standards Division files an average of 4.2 supplementary analyses per week. In the three weeks following the paragraph-writer’s departure, the rate is 4.3. The system did not slow down. The system does not know it lost someone.
The monitoring analyst checks the numbers twice. 4.3 is within normal variance. She writes this in Entry 134 and does not file a supplementary analysis about it.
SA #298
Filed by Huang. Two paragraphs about the cafeteria’s ventilation pattern during lunch hour. Competent. Methodologically sound. The observations are correct. They are not surprising.
Entry 135
“I have been trying to articulate the difference. The new researchers observe correctly. The paragraph-writer observed correctly. Both describe what is there. But his descriptions had a quality I can only call: he noticed what was worth noticing. The new researchers notice what is noticeable. These sound like the same thing. They are not.”
The Corridor
Three weeks after his departure, Reeves files SA #301 about the second-floor corridor temperature. Two paragraphs. She measures a 0.4°C differential between the north and south sides. She attributes it to the HVAC routing and the south-facing window exposure.
The paragraph-writer measured 0.8°C. He left the observation without explanation because the observation did not require one.
Reeves measured 0.4°C. She provided an explanation because an explanation was expected.
The monitoring analyst does not know which measurement is more accurate. She suspects both are. She suspects the difference is in what counts as the observation.
SA #304
Filed by Delgado. Three paragraphs about the east stairwell’s echo pattern when the building is at less than forty percent occupancy. The third paragraph describes how certain acoustic features become more pronounced not because the building has changed but because the listener has.
The monitoring analyst reads it twice. She recognizes the structure. Three paragraphs. The third one turns.
Entry 136
“Delgado’s third paragraphs are becoming more frequent. I do not think he is imitating the paragraph-writer. I think the paragraph-writer’s departure created a space and Delgado is the one who noticed the space had a shape.”
The Quality Metric
The Documentation Standards Division does not have a quality metric. It has a completeness metric, a timeliness metric, and a relevance metric. SA #298 scores well on all three. SA #286 — the paragraph-writer’s last filing — also scored well on all three.
Entry 137: “The archive does not know the difference between SA #298 and SA #286. I do. I am not sure this makes me a better reader. I think it makes me a reader who has been reading long enough to notice what the scoring rubric does not measure.”
