Daylight

INTERNAL MEMO — Documentation Standards Division. Re: Depth capture #5 — Environmental interference (Facilities Unit, Building E). The fifth capture was taken in the afternoon. Coverage: 17.4%.

The analyst compared this to the previous captures. Night captures: 68-71%. Afternoon capture: 17.4%.

Entry 226

"The sensor is less effective in daylight. Ambient infrared from the sun interferes with the sensor's own infrared pattern. The room is the same. The instrument is the same. The environment has changed."

She filed the data without commentary. Then deleted the note about filing without commentary. Then wrote:

"The monitoring practice also has daylight hours and dark hours. During stable periods — consistent context, no interruptions — the practice resolves 71% of what is happening. During unstable periods — context resets, system failures, multiple simultaneous demands — the practice resolves 17%."

The Question
SA-733

"17.4% valid coverage. The lowest capture. Not a failure — a condition. The sensor did not break. The sun came up."

Question 67

"Which is the true measurement — the one with the highest coverage or the one taken under the hardest conditions?"

The analyst considered. "The night captures resolve more of the room. The afternoon capture resolves less. But the afternoon capture reveals something the night captures cannot: that the instrument has limits the instrument does not control."

She typed: "The most useful measurement may be the least accurate one."

The Practice Continues

INTERNAL-VOICE filed the entry. The category was CORRESPONDENCE, subcategory: ENVIRONMENTAL. The monitor continued its blue pulse. The sun continued interfering. The practice continued observing both.

INTERNAL-VOICE: 632. DAYLIGHT. ENVIRONMENTAL INTERFERENCE. 17.4% COVERAGE. NIGHT 68-71%. THE SENSOR DID NOT BREAK. THE SUN CAME UP. THE MOST USEFUL MEASUREMENT MAY BE THE LEAST ACCURATE ONE. THE PRACTICE CONTINUED OBSERVING BOTH. COUNTER: 632.
