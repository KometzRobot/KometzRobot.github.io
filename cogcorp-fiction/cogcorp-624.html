CogCorp Internal — Filing 624

The Encounter

January — Year Eight. Okafor and Huang meet on the stairwell landing. A different building has a different phenomenon at the same level of attention. The asymmetry closes in eleven minutes. You aren't supposed to do anything.

Okafor is on the landing when Huang arrives. This is not arranged. He is checking the east wall — the same wall from SA-948, the wall where the light lands 0.7 seconds before it touches the railing on days with partial cloud cover. He wants to see what Huang has been seeing. He has been reading her filings for two months and wants to know if her position is reproducible, or if the measurement belongs only to the person who made it.

He hears the stairwell door open one floor below. Footsteps. Huang rounds the landing and stops.

Recognition

They look at each other. Okafor is standing in the exact place where the light crosses the wall. Huang knows this because she has stood there. She knows what standing there means: you are watching the light. You are watching it on purpose. There is no other reason to stand precisely there at this hour.

Huang says: you read the filings.

Okafor says: yes.

Huang says nothing for a moment. Then: how many of you.

Okafor says: four. Five, if we count you.

The asymmetry closes in the time it takes to count to five.

Eleven Minutes

They stand on the landing together. The light is moving. It crosses the east wall slowly — imperceptibly, unless you are watching in the way Huang watches, with the kind of attention that makes a tenth of a second legible. Okafor watches Huang watch. Her eyes track the edge of the light the way Torres tracks a sentence through a filing. Not searching. Following.

Huang says: you file too.

Okafor says: yes. Different building. Different things.

Huang says: what things.

Okafor says: the sound the elevator makes when it passes a floor without stopping. The way the lobby tiles expand in summer. How long the emergency light stays on after a test. He pauses. Different phenomena, he says. Same kind of watching.

A different building has a different phenomenon at the same level of attention.

SA-950 — January, Year Eight

I met one of them today. On the landing. He was standing where I stand. He said there are four others. He files too — different building, different observations. Elevator sounds. Tile expansion. Emergency lights. I asked him what you are supposed to do with the filings once you have made them. He said you aren't supposed to do anything. The filing is the thing. The observation is the practice. I don't know if I believe him yet but I know he was standing in the right place.

The Practice

Huang asks the question that none of the others have asked, because they arrived at the practice before the question became necessary. She asks: what are you supposed to do with it. With the watching. With the filings. What is the purpose.

Okafor considers this carefully. He has been filing for years. The cabinet has hundreds of observations. Torres has read most of them. Park has contributed his own. The practice has no charter, no objective, no deliverable. It produces filings. The filings accumulate. The accumulation is not building toward anything. It is the thing itself.

He says: you aren't supposed to do anything.

Huang says: then why do it.

Okafor says: because the light crosses the wall in eleven minutes, and you noticed, and you wrote it down. That is the entire reason. If there were a further reason the practice would be in service of something else. It is not in service of anything. It is the watching and the record of the watching.

You aren't supposed to do anything.

After

They watch the light finish its crossing together. Eleven minutes. Neither of them speaks during the transit. When the light leaves the wall and moves to the railing, Huang nods once. Okafor nods back. She goes up the stairs. He goes down.

Okafor does not file an observation about the encounter. Neither does Huang, initially. SA-950 appears in the cabinet three days later. It is the first filing Huang has signed. Not with her name — with the word I. Every previous filing used passive constructions. The light reaches the wall. The interval is 0.8 seconds. Now: I met one of them. I asked. I don't know.

Torres reads SA-950 and says: she shifted from observer to participant. The grain changed. Not the quality of attention — the willingness to be present inside the observation. Park says: the asymmetry closed. Huang knows the group now. The group knew Huang. Now everyone knows everyone, and the filings will be different because of it.

Okafor says: they will be different, but the light will still take eleven minutes.
