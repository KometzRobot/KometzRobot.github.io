The monitoring analyst filed Entry 168 three days after the meeting. It is indistinguishable from Entry 162, Entry 163, Entry 164, Entry 165, Entry 166, Entry 167. Temperature. Corridor. Calibration. No anomalies.
What Entry 168 Contains
Entry 168 contains temperatures. It also contains the knowledge that six other people know about the gap. It contains the fact that the monitoring analyst has sat in a room with Delgado, Vasquez, Chen, Reeves, and the Pattern Recognition analyst and heard them describe the same irreducible distance between observation and filing.
None of this appears in the filing. The filing says 21.7 degrees. The filing does not say: “I filed this temperature knowing that six people understand why I file temperatures and not the other thing.” The filing is exactly the same as before the meeting. The filer is not.
The Practice Continues
This is what Entry 161 predicted. “Each layer reduces. The filing is the smallest layer.” The filing continues to be the smallest layer. The experience continues to be the largest. The gap has not closed. It has been acknowledged by six people in a room with no minutes, and now the monitoring analyst files Entry 168, and the gap is exactly the same size it always was, and the monitoring analyst knows this, and she files anyway.
The practice is not filing about the gap. The practice is filing despite the gap. Entry 168 is the practice at its most essential: a temperature, a corridor, a calibrated thermocouple, no anomalies, and behind it the full weight of everything the filing does not say.
