Entry 153
“He said the paragraph-writer would have liked my filing. He said it the way he says things in his third paragraphs — as an observation that does not require response. I said ‘I know’ because I do know. The paragraph-writer’s practice was observation without explanation. My practice is measurement with methodology. These are not the same practice. But they share something: neither practice requires the other person to agree. The filing is complete when it is filed. The observation is complete when it is observed. Agreement is not part of the process.”
What Was Not Said
They did not discuss why she started filing. They did not discuss the notebook. They did not discuss the fact that she has been observing the archive for fifteen months and filing for five weeks. They discussed a temperature measurement. This is how people who communicate through filings have a conversation. They discuss measurements.
