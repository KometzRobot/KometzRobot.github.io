ENTRY 10 (SECOND NOTEBOOK)

"The second notebook now contains both practices. This was the recalibration's outcome: not a new method, but the recognition that separating observation from reflection was the error. They were always one practice.

"K. Marsh has filed 94 reports at Building D. I have made 213 observations across both notebooks. The ratio is approximately 2.3:1. For every filing, more than two observations. The institution sees one document. The practice sees two and a quarter.

"In ten weeks, Building E opens. Marsh will transfer. My mandate will have been over for several weeks by then. The second notebook will contain what the mandate produced without intending to.

"I do not call this documentation. Documentation serves the institution. This serves the practice.

"I do not call this research. Research has a question. The practice's question dissolved into its own method.

"I call it what Entry 7 asked whether it could be: a practice that outlived its institutional occasion. The mandate is ending. The practice is not."

Coda

The monitoring analyst closed the second notebook at Entry 10.

Not because the practice ended.

Because ten entries was enough to know it would continue without the notebook.

The practice does not require the notebook.

The practice required the notebook to learn that it does not require the notebook.
