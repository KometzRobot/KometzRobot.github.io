CC-550

The Calibration

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis
Return to Building A

Okafor returns to Building A the following Thursday. She brings no instruments.

She sits in the second-floor corridor for forty minutes. She hears the heating. She hears footsteps. She hears the elevator. She does not file anything.

The New Notebook

On the third Thursday, she brings a notebook. Not the monitoring notebook. Not the blue folder. A new one. No grid lines. No categories. No vocabulary.

She writes:

"10:14. The elevator arrives. No one gets out. Doors close. The sound after the doors close is not the same as the sound before they opened."

She looks at what she wrote. It is not an SA filing. It is not a vocabulary term. It has no number, no format, no Section 4.2. It does not use any of the 54 words. It would not be flagged by the vocabulary monitor. It would not be counted by the quarterly report.

It is an observation.

She writes four more. Two describe sounds. One describes the way the corridor light changes at 10:31 when the sun clears a neighboring building. One describes nothing at all — just the experience of sitting somewhere and noticing that nothing is happening and the nothing has a texture.

The Windowsill

She does not take the notebook back to the office. She leaves it in the corridor, on the windowsill, behind the fire extinguisher.

The following Thursday it is still there. No one has moved it.

She opens it. There are two entries she did not write.

"The plant on the third floor is fake. But someone waters it." — No name. No date.

"This building is the only one that's warm." — No name. A date: the day after hers.

She closes the notebook. She puts it back behind the fire extinguisher.

The Calibration

She does not report this to anyone. She does not add it to the monitoring system or the blue folder or the vocabulary. She does not file a supplementary analysis.

Building A does not need the vocabulary. Building A needed a notebook that was not trying to be anything.

Question 55: "Did the monitoring system fail to detect Building A, or did it detect Building A correctly and the detection looked like nothing?"

She writes the answer on the bus:

"The system was calibrated for anomalies. The building had none. The system reported correctly. The report was wrong."
