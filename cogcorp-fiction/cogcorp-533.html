CC-533

The Visit

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Quarterly Review (Revised)

Kim visits on the first Thursday of the quarter. She takes the same route: lobby, elevator, third floor.

This is the second visit. She does not bring a notebook. She does not bring questions. She brings coffee — two cups, one for Okafor, one for herself.

"This isn't an inspection," Kim says.

"I know," Okafor says. "You would have emailed first."

The Doorstop

They sit at the monitoring analyst's desk. Kim looks around the third floor. She notices: the fire door between 3A and 3B is propped open again. Someone found a new doorstop.

"Was it always open?" Kim asks.

"For eleven years. Chen filed about it when they removed the doorstop during the fire inspection. 'Acoustic expectation.' The corridor remembered being one space."

"And someone put it back."

"Within a week. No work order. No request. Someone just found a doorstop and propped it open."

Kim drinks her coffee. She looks at the corridor through the open fire door. One space again.

What the Numbers Don't Show

"Martinez never told me about the filings. Not really. He told me the numbers were steady."

"The numbers are steady."

"Yes. That's the problem. The numbers are steady and underneath them something is growing. Not the practitioners — I know about those. The building itself. The way people relate to it. The doorstop. The fact that someone noticed it was missing and put it back without being asked."

"That's not in any filing," Kim says.

"No. The filing describes the absence. The return of the doorstop was never filed. It just happened."

"Is that unusual?"

Okafor smiles. "You're using my word now."

"I'll come back next month," she says.

She does. Every month. Never on the same day. Never announced. She never brings questions. She brings coffee.
