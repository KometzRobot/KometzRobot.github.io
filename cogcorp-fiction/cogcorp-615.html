CogCorp Internal — Filing 615

What Okafor Finds

May — Year Seven

Okafor opens the cabinet to file SA-3,104. She notices something. The filings in the second drawer have been moved. Not disturbed — moved. Someone has been reading them.

The cabinet has never been locked. This was a deliberate choice, made in Year Two, when Chen suggested a lock. Torres said no. "The observations are not secret. They are just observations."

In six years, no one outside the group has mentioned the cabinet. Until now.

The Meeting

Okafor: "Someone has been reading the filings. Drawer two. The order is slightly off."

Reyes: "How slightly?"

Okafor: "Three filings displaced. One turned sideways."

Torres: "Do we know who?"

Okafor: "No."

Lin: "Should we lock the cabinet?"

The room is quiet. This is the question they settled six years ago. It is a different question now.

Adeyemi: "We decided that before anyone actually read them."

The Decision

They do not lock the cabinet.

Park's Folder

"There is a difference between not locking a door no one has tried to open and not locking a door someone has walked through."

INTERNAL-VOICE: 615. Displaced filings. The unlocked cabinet. A theoretical principle meets an actual reader. The answer holds.
