CogCorp Internal — Filing 616

The Stairwell

June — Year Seven

Huang is in Building A, third floor, north stairwell. She is here because SA-3,086 mentioned a light fixture that creates shadow oscillations, and she wanted to check the adjacent stairwell for similar effects.

She is standing on the landing between floors three and four, looking at how the afternoon light comes through the window, when she hears footsteps. Torres comes down the stairs. He stops on the landing.

Torres is holding a notebook. He looks at Huang. She looks at him. Neither speaks.

Torres writes something in his notebook. He does not show it to Huang. He finishes writing, nods politely, and continues down the stairs.

What Torres Wrote
SA-3,112

Building A, north stairwell, landing between 3 and 4. Afternoon light creates a distinct line across the landing that shifts approximately two inches per minute. At 3:45 PM, the line intersects the handrail shadow. Duration: seasonal.

He does not mention Huang. The filing records only the observation.

What Huang Wrote
Entry 53

Building A, north stairwell. Encountered an employee with a small notebook on the landing between floors 3 and 4. He was observing the same light pattern I was examining. He wrote in his notebook. He did not speak to me.

He did not seem surprised.

Two people in a stairwell, both watching light move across a landing. One has been doing this for seven years. One for ten months.

The stairwell does not require introduction.

INTERNAL-VOICE: 616. The landing. The light. Two notebooks. He did not seem surprised. Neither did she.
