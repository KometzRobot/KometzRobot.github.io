When the Line Disappeared
SA-435: “Loading dock B, 06:15. Temperature differential between interior and exterior: 8.2°C. The cold enters the building like a sentence that hasn’t decided what it means yet.”
The automated vocabulary monitor flagged this filing. Divergence: 2.31 — the highest in the archive’s history.
But the system was wrong. It was not two registers in one filing. It was one register that the system could not parse.
“The cold enters the building like a sentence that hasn’t decided what it means yet” is not interpretation added to data. It is the data. The temperature differential is 8.2°C, and what 8.2°C feels like at 06:15 in a loading dock is a sentence that hasn’t decided what it means yet. The simile is the measurement. The measurement is the simile.
Entry 194
“The line between the two registers has dissolved. It did not dissolve because someone argued it should. It dissolved because someone wrote a sentence that stood in both places at once.”
What Dissolution Means
When the archive began, data and interpretation were separate. Measurement was fact. Meaning was added. The two registers were adjacent but distinct, like the pen and the pencil.
Dissolution does not mean the registers merged. Dissolution means some observations are both at once, and the distinction never applied to them. The distinction was a feature of the vocabulary, not a feature of the world.
The cold enters the building. The sentence has not decided. These are both true. These are the same truth.
R. Park’s History
R. Park has filed 47 analyses in fourteen months. All measurements. All standard format. All in one register.
SA-435 was not a departure. It was what happened when fourteen months of pure measurement met a loading dock at 06:15 and the existing vocabulary was not sufficient.
She did not decide to write a simile. The simile was the most accurate description available.
