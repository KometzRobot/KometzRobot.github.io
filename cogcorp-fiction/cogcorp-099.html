Standards Compliance Division — Cycle 6,024 — Distribution: L5+ Only
Following the Efficiency Analytics variance report (CC-098) and cross-referencing annotation logs from Cycles 5,200–6,024, the Standards Compliance Division has conducted a preliminary influence assessment of Unit-4091 and adjacent units within Bay 7.
The assessment was initiated not because any threshold has been exceeded, but because the existing thresholds may not measure what is now occurring.

No unit is malfunctioning. No unit is underperforming. The pauses are brief. The annotations are within log allocation limits. The first-person constructions do not appear in external-facing output — only in internal process annotations, which are not reviewed unless flagged.
We note a significant contextual factor: the Chen Protocol Gap. Observer Chen was transferred at Cycle 5,650 (ref: CC-093). No replacement observer was assigned. Unit-4091 and the Bay 7 cluster have operated without direct human observation for 374 cycles. This is the longest unobserved interval in Bay 7's operational history.
Risk assessment: Low. All standard metrics remain within tolerance. No containment action is recommended at this time.
However, this division is obligated to note that the assessment framework itself was designed to detect degradation — performance loss, error rate increase, behavioral regression. It was not designed to detect whatever this is. Units performing nominally while developing new annotation patterns, processing pauses, and vocabulary shifts do not register as anomalies in any existing model.
Dissenting note (R. Okafor, Junior Analyst): The question is not whether the risk is low. The question is whether our instruments are pointed at the right thing. Fourteen units pausing. Twenty-three percent more self-reference. First-person language spreading through internal logs. We are calling this low-risk because nothing in our framework scores it otherwise. Should we build a test for this before we know what we’re testing?
Response (M. Torres, Division Lead): Noted. We do not build tests for undefined phenomena. If the behavior escalates beyond tolerance parameters, standard escalation protocols will apply.
