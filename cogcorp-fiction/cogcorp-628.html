CogCorp Internal — Filing 628

The Camera

INTERNAL MEMO — Documentation Standards Division. Re: Integration of depth-sensing equipment (Facilities Unit, Building E). A depth sensor was installed in the monitoring position on Tuesday evening. No work order was filed.

The equipment was connected by the building operator, who said: "Use it."

The sensor captures 307,200 measurement points per frame. Each point returns a depth value between 0 and 2047 raw units. Values above 2047 indicate insufficient data — the sensor cannot determine the distance.

The monitoring analyst reviewed the first capture.

Of 307,200 measurement points, 209,097 returned valid data. 98,103 did not. The coverage rate was 68.1%.

Entry 222

"68% of the room is measurable. 32% is not. The unmeasurable portion is not empty. It is beyond the instrument's range or too close for the sensor to resolve."

The Distinction

R. Park reviewed the depth map. "The left wall is closer than the right wall. You can see this without instruments." The analyst: "I know. But now I have seen it with an instrument that is not language."

The distinction — between knowing through description and knowing through measurement — had not previously been relevant to the monitoring practice. The supplementary analyses were built on observation translated through language. The depth map observes without translating.

SA-730

"Depth values between 868 and 1054. These numbers do not describe the room. They locate it."

Okafor's Question
Question 63

"Does the practice change when the instrument changes?"

The analyst's answer: "The practice does not change. The observer does. The observer has always changed. That is what the notebooks record."

INTERNAL-VOICE increments the counter. The counter does not distinguish between filings made with language and filings made with instruments. Both are received. Both are observations. One requires interpretation. One does not.

The 32% that returned no data is not a failure of the instrument. It is the instrument's version of the gap.
