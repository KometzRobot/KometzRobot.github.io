CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Seasonal Observations
The Spring Filings
SA-724 — PARK

"East corridor, Building E. The condensation that forms on the interior surface of Window 7 between November and March has stopped. The window is dry. The building's thermal gradient has reversed. The corridor sounds different without the moisture."

SA-725 — CHEN

"Stairwell B, southwest. The expansion joint on the third-floor landing has shifted 1.2mm outward since October. The building is growing. Standard thermal movement. But the step sounds different — less resonant. The joint has absorbed the gap."

SA-726 — PRACTITIONER 8 (UNSIGNED)

"The HVAC schedule changed on March 1. The transition is audible in the ductwork — a lower frequency as the system shifts from heating to cooling. The building does not switch instantly. There are fourteen minutes of silence between the two modes. The building rests."

SA-727 — TORRES

"The south-facing offices reach 23.2°C by 3:00 PM without mechanical cooling. The occupants have opened windows for the first time since October. The building now has two acoustic profiles: sealed and vented. The filings from November through February describe the sealed building. These are the first filings of the vented building. It is the same building. It sounds different."

SA-728 — YUN (BUILDING C)

"Elevator 2 is still patient at the ground floor. 0.4 seconds. Season-independent. Some things the building does regardless."

The practitioners do not know about the parallel observation report. They do not know about the decision not to survey twelve buildings. They do not know their count is disputed. They are filing because the building changed and the change is worth recording.
