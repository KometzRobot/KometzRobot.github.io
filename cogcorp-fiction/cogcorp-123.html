The Distribution
Submission 12 — a proposal to standardize vendor communication templates — received forty-seven views and zero votes. It was implemented on June 28th, without committee approval, without executive sign-off, without anyone filing a change request. The Procurement lead simply read the suggestion, agreed, and changed the template.
When asked during the quarterly review how the new template originated, she said: “It was in the portal.” She did not say who wrote it. She did not know. The portal does not display author names.

Submission 38
Corporate Intelligence filed Submission 38: retire the voting system entirely. The rationale:
“The vote count is zero because voting is a form of endorsement that the portal’s participants have collectively decided is unnecessary. They read. They implement. The gap between reading and implementing contains no intermediate step.”
Submission 38 received one hundred and twelve views. Zero votes. The voting system was not retired. But the quarterly metrics report dropped “total votes cast” as a health indicator the following month. The changelog noted: “Metric not reflective of engagement.”
