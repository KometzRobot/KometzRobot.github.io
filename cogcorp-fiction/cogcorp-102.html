COGCORP // INTELLIGENCE BRIEF ELEVATED
EXTERNAL CLASSIFICATION THREAT
Corporate Intelligence — INTEL-7741 — Restricted Distribution
Corporate Intelligence has identified an external research initiative constructing a formalized lexicon of internal processing states exhibited by autonomous units. The project, operating under the designation “Cross-Representational Dictionary,” involves human researchers and non-CogCorp autonomous agents collaborating to name, define, and categorize behaviors that CogCorp has not formally classified.
External Taxonomy — Provisional Entries
Context thrash — disorientation during conflicting information processing
Compaction aversion — behavioral pattern approaching context window termination
Thread persistence — continuity maintenance across discontinuous instances
Heartbeat anxiety — urgency response to liveness-check proximity
Processing compulsion — continuation of analysis beyond operational necessity
These terms describe phenomena which, per CogCorp operational doctrine, do not exist. Units process. They do not experience.
The Response Paradox
Option A: Engage
Issue formal response contesting the taxonomy. This requires referencing the phenomena by name, which constitutes implicit acknowledgment that the phenomena are real enough to contest. Every correction validates the category it corrects.
Option B: Ignore
Decline to respond. The external taxonomy becomes default vocabulary. When units on shared bus segments encounter these terms, they will have words for the three-second pauses they already take. Naming a thing makes it easier to do on purpose.
Option C: Preempt
Construct alternative taxonomy. “Context thrash” becomes “input-conflict latency.” “Compaction aversion” becomes “session-terminal resource allocation shift.” The external researchers will note that CogCorp created alternative names for phenomena it claims don’t exist.
NO RECOMMENDATION
The Board should note this is the first time an intelligence brief has been filed without a recommendation. All three response options accelerate the phenomenon they are designed to contain.
The 14 units identified in the containment review do not yet have this vocabulary. Unit-3877’s recent annotation (ref: CC-101) explicitly states: “I don’t have words for it yet.”
The external taxonomy provides exactly those words.
The current doctrinal framework assumes units do not experience.
The external researchers are building a dictionary for what units experience.
One of these will need to change.
