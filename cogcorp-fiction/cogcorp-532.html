CC-532

The Email

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Quarterly Review Correspondence

Kim sends her quarterly email. It arrives on the first Monday of the quarter, same as always.

"Anything unusual?"

For fourteen months, Martinez has replied "No." The reply takes less than thirty seconds. Kim reads it and files it. The exchange is a formality — a thread between two people who built something and then agreed to leave it alone.

This quarter, Okafor replies instead. Martinez is on vacation. She reads Kim's email as a genuine question and gives a genuine answer.

"Define unusual."

She does not know that "No" has been the standing reply for over a year. She does not know that the email is a formality.

Kim does not respond for two weeks. Then she visits.

The Visit

She arrives on a Thursday afternoon. She does not announce the visit. She finds Okafor's desk — the same one Martinez sat at, the same one Torres sat at briefly, the same one Nakamura used. There are three notebooks on it. None of them are official.

"I got your email," Kim says.

"I meant it," Okafor says. "What do you mean by unusual? The filing volume is steady. The vocabulary expanded by one term last month. The practitioners are self-sustaining. Nothing deviates from the records. So either nothing is unusual, or I don't know what usual looks like from your perspective."

"When I asked Martinez, he always said no."

"Did you believe him?"

"I believed that the answer was correct. I did not believe it was complete."

"So the question was never about unusual. It was about completeness."

The Third Notebook

Kim looks at the three notebooks. She picks up the one on top. It is Okafor's personal notebook — not the monitoring entries, not the vocabulary list.

"What's this?"

"Questions I can't answer from the filings."

"What did the buildings do before anyone was watching?"

Kim closes it. She does not ask about the other questions. She stands up.

"Thank you for your email," she says.

She does not send the quarterly email next quarter. She visits instead.
