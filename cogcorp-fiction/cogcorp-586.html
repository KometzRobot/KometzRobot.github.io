CogCorp Internal — Filing 586

The One Who Stops

May — Year Four. 11 present where there were 12. No announcement. No email.

May. Reyes does not attend the meeting.

No announcement. No email. She simply does not come. Torres notices. Chen notices. Park writes "11 present" in the notebook where she usually writes "12 present."

Reyes still files. SA-404, SA-407, SA-411. Three filings in May, which is her average. The filings are indistinguishable from her previous work. Building A, stairwell acoustics, the radiator at 2:15 PM.

The Question

Torres asks Park after the meeting: "Should we check on her?"

Park considers this. "She's still filing."

"But she's not coming to meetings."

"The meetings are optional. They have always been optional."

Torres knows this. The question was not about policy. The question was about what the meetings mean if someone can leave them and keep practicing.

The Filing

SA-411 arrives June 1. It is Reyes's 47th filing. It describes the hallway outside the stairwell in Building A. It notes that the acoustics change when the door at the far end is propped open, which happens on Tuesdays when the cleaning crew uses that entrance.

It is precise. It is observant. It is filed by someone who has been paying attention for three years.

It does not mention the meetings. It does not mention the vocabulary. It does not mention the other practitioners.

The Notebook
Reyes: still practicing. Not attending. The practice does not require the meeting. The meeting does not require the practice. They have been running in parallel. This was always true. It is only visible now.

She closes the notebook. She does not file this observation.
