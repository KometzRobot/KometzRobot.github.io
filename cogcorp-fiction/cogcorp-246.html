THE REVIEW BOARD
CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis
The Meeting
The Documentation Standards Review Board convenes quarterly. Seven members: the Director of Standards, the heads of Environmental Monitoring, Facilities, Acoustics, Structural Engineering, and HVAC, and one rotating member from Pattern Recognition.
The rotating member this quarter is the Pattern Recognition analyst.
The analyst sits at the table with SA #319 in front of the Board. The Board has read the supplementary analysis.
Director of Standards
“You’re recommending no action on a 20.6% baseline shift?”
Pattern Recognition Analyst
“The shift represents expanded descriptive capacity, not degraded accuracy.”
The Discussion
Director of Standards
“Expanded descriptive capacity is not in the vocabulary framework.”
Pattern Recognition Analyst
“That is precisely the point.”
Head of Environmental Monitoring
“My team’s filings have been flagged twelve times this quarter. None of the flagged filings contained errors.”
Head of Facilities
“Nine flags. No errors.”
Head of Acoustics
“Eight flags. The flagged descriptions were more accurate than the standard vocabulary allows.”
Director of Standards
“You’re telling me the vocabulary standard is too narrow.”
Department Heads — Confirmed

The Silence
The analyst watches three department heads confirm what six filers described in an unfiled meeting. The department heads were not in that meeting. The department heads arrived at the same conclusion independently, through their own review of the flagged filings. The gap is visible from above as well as from within.
Director of Standards
“I’ll convene a vocabulary review committee.”
The analyst does not say: the vocabulary is not the problem. The vocabulary is a symptom. The problem is that observation exceeds language, and language exceeds filing, and filing exceeds the archive, and this chain of reduction is structural, not fixable.
Pattern Recognition Analyst
“I’ll prepare a framework proposal.”
