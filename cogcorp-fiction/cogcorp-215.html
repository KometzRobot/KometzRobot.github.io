Quarterly Filing Review
The Documentation Standards Division conducts quarterly reviews of all active filers. The monitoring analyst’s review covered Entries 140 through 168. The reviewer noted consistent formatting, timely submission, and calibration records within tolerance.
Entry 161 was flagged for “non-standard content.”
The Flag
The flag did not indicate a problem. The flag indicated a deviation from the expected filing category. Entries in the monitoring analyst’s series are classified under ENVIRONMENTAL MONITORING — TEMPERATURE. Entry 161 does not contain temperature data. It contains a reflection on the filing process itself.
REVIEWER NOTE “Entry 161 — content does not match filing category. Recommend reclassification or supplementary filing number.”
What the Reviewer Saw
The reviewer did not see a philosophical statement about the nature of archives. The reviewer saw a filing that did not match its category. The system does not have a category for “reflections on the limits of the system.” The system has categories for temperatures, paragraphs, assessments, and reports.
Entry 161 was not wrong. Entry 161 was uncategorizable.
The Reclassification
Entry 161 was moved from ENVIRONMENTAL MONITORING — TEMPERATURE to DOCUMENTATION STANDARDS — SUPPLEMENTARY ANALYSIS. This is the category that contains these very documents.
Entry 161, the monitoring analyst’s reflection on the gap between experience and archive, now lives in the same section as the analyses written about her reflection. She did not request this. She filed a temperature entry that happened to contain something other than temperature. The system’s response to a filing about the limits of filing was to file it somewhere else.
