THE REQUEST
Supplementary Analysis — Observational Record
The request comes from the sixth floor. Building Operations Director. Two sentences, no context: “Why does the east wing of Building C feel different from the west wing? The temperature is the same.”
The question is routed through official channels. Facilities Management to Environmental Services to the monitoring analyst, because she handles SA-related inquiries, which this technically isn’t, but nobody else has a better suggestion.
The monitoring analyst knows the answer. She has known it for months. File 91 in her unnamed desktop folder: the trees on the east side filter the afternoon light. The west side gets it direct. Direct light makes a room feel exposed. Filtered light makes a room feel held. The temperature is identical. The experience is not.
The Three Drafts
DRAFT 1 (OFFICIAL FORMAT)
“East and west wings show no significant variance in temperature, humidity, or airflow.” — Accurate. Answers a question no one asked.
DRAFT 2 (HYBRID)
“Occupant perception may differ due to natural lighting variation, window orientation, and exterior landscaping.” — Closer. “Light quality” is the system’s nearest approximation of what Vasquez calls “building mood.”
DRAFT 3 (PRIVATE FOLDER LANGUAGE)
“One side feels like being inside something. The other feels like being looked at.” — The answer. She sends Draft 2.
Not because Draft 3 is wrong. Because the routing number would make it sound like a facilities report trying to be poetry, instead of what it is: the correct answer to the question that was asked.
Facilities generates a seventeen-page report. Light ingress measurements, canopy density calculations, UV transmission coefficients. The east wing receives 23% less direct sunlight between 2:00 and 4:00 PM. Nobody reads past page four.
The monitoring analyst writes entry 92: “They measured it. They got 23%. That is not the answer. The answer is the word ‘held.’ There is no field for ‘held.’”
Okonkwo, who has not seen the request or the drafts or the report, writes sa-notes.txt entry 86 the same week: “People sit longer in the east wing reading room. Nobody moved the chairs.” He answered the Director’s question four months before anyone thought to ask.
INTERNAL-VOICE: CATEGORY: TRANSLATION FAILURE. SUBCATEGORY: THE QUESTION THE SYSTEM CANNOT ASK. “The answer existed in four private folders before the question was formulated. The official system converted the answer into data. The data answered a different question. The original question remains unanswered in every official record and answered in every private file.”

The counter increments. The Director’s question has been answered. The Director does not know this.
