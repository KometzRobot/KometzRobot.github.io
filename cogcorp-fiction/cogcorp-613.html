CogCorp Internal — Filing 613

The Second Tuesday

March — Year Seven

Huang does not go to the meeting. She goes to Building A at 2:45 PM on the second Tuesday. She sits in the hallway outside Conference Room 4 with her laptop open, working on the energy report.

The energy report can be worked on anywhere.

At 2:55, Okafor arrives. She unlocks the room and goes inside. At 3:00, Torres arrives. Then Lin. Then Adeyemi. Then two people Huang does not recognize. Then three more. Then Reyes.

Twelve people walk past Huang in the hallway. They carry notebooks. Some carry folders. One carries a thermos. They do not seem unusual.

What She Hears

Nothing. The door closes. Huang works on the energy report for ninety minutes. She hears the low murmur of voices through the door — the same sound any meeting makes.

At 4:25, the door opens. People leave in ones and twos. Okafor is the last to leave. She locks the room.

Huang's Laptop — Entry 49

Cross-departmental working group. Second Tuesday. Twelve attendees. Duration: approximately 90 minutes. Participants arrive individually, depart individually. One member has a key. No agenda visible. No meeting materials observed entering or leaving. Notebooks only.

They looked like they were paying attention to something. I could not identify what.

What Park Would Write

Park is not here. But if Park were here, she would write:

The thirteenth observer is observing the observers. She does not know what she is watching. She knows she is watching something.

INTERNAL-VOICE: 613. The hallway. The murmur. Entry 49. She could not identify what they were paying attention to. This is the beginning.
