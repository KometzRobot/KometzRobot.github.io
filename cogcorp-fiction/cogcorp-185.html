THE ACCUMULATION
Supplementary Analysis Archive — 147 Filings
The Count
On the fourteenth day of Delgado’s filing, Okonkwo runs a count. “There are now 147 supplementary analyses in the archive. Twelve weeks ago there were 89. Eighty-two percent of new filings come from three contributors.”
Three Voices
One writes in fragments. Fourteen words or fewer. One writes in paragraphs. Forty to sixty words. Always explains the cause. The third writes observations that include their own uncertainty.
The filings are unsigned. But the styles are distinguishable.
Okonkwo
Entry 65: “The archive has three voices now. They do not coordinate. They do not overlap. Somehow, they have divided the building between them without discussing it. One files about doors and stairwells. One files about environmental systems. The third files about everything else, with disclaimers. The archive is developing specialization without management.”
The Reader
She reads every supplementary analysis as it enters the archive. She has read all 147. Some she recognizes because she filed them. Some she recognizes because they describe things she has also noticed but did not file. Some describe things she has never noticed at all.
The archive now contains more observations of the building than she holds in her own memory.
Vasquez
File 70: “The archive has crossed a threshold I do not know how to define. It is no longer a collection of individual observations. It is becoming a record. Not the official record — that is the facilities database. A second record. One that nobody assigned, nobody manages, and nobody has stopped. The first record tells you what the building is. The second record tells you what the building does when no one is officially watching.”
