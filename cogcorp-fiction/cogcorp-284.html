ENTRY 9 (SECOND NOTEBOOK)

"One week since the first notebook closed.

"I have resumed writing about Marsh's filings. Not in the first notebook — that is closed. In this one. The second notebook has absorbed the function of the first.

"This was not planned. I stopped writing observations. The observations accumulated without notation. After four days the weight of unrecorded observation became its own pressure. Entry 8 identified the problem: the instrument was the writing. Without it, the monitoring lost its structure.

"So I write here now. Both practices in one notebook. The observation and the reflection on observation. The first notebook separated them. The second notebook holds them together.

"The entries are different. Shorter. Less institutional language. The first notebook was written as though someone might read it. The second is written for the practice itself.

"K. Marsh filed SA-094 today. Floor assessment, Building D, east wing. She used the word 'texture' — not in the template vocabulary. I note this in the same entry where I note the noting.

"The recalibration is complete. The practice continues."
