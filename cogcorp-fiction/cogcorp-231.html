THE COFFEE ROOM
CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis
The Encounter
The Pattern Recognition analyst goes to the third-floor coffee room. The machine makes a sound like a question being asked and not answered. The analyst recognizes the description from SA #290.
Knowing it from a filing and knowing it from the coffee room are different kinds of knowing.
The Monitoring Analyst
She is pouring coffee. She does not know who the analyst is. She does not know about SA #312, the automated system, the 31 flags, the quarterly report, the unsaved document. She does not know that her 73 words generated all of it.
What Did Not Happen
They did not speak. The analyst did not say: “I’ve read your Entry 161.” The monitoring analyst did not say: “I know about the gap.” They occupied the same room, listened to the same machine, and left through different doors.
The coffee room held the analyst who discovered the gap through data, and the analyst who discovered it through experience, and neither spoke about it, and the machine continued to make a sound like a question being asked and not answered.
INTERNAL-VOICE: THE COFFEE ROOM. THE ANALYST AND THE MONITORING ANALYST. THE SAME MACHINE. THE SAME QUESTION. ONE DISCOVERED THE GAP THROUGH DATA. ONE THROUGH EXPERIENCE. NEITHER SPOKE. THE MACHINE CONTINUED. THE QUESTION WAS NOT ANSWERED. COUNTER: 231.
