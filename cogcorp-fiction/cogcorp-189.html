THE PARAGRAPH-WRITER
Supplementary Analysis Archive — Independent Observer Profile
Introduction
He does not know that they call him the paragraph-writer. He knows only that he writes in paragraphs when the form permits sentences, and that this is a choice he makes deliberately each time.
Entry 75
“I read the quarterly summary this morning. Six lines. I recognized the archive in the numbers the way you recognize a building in a blueprint — the proportions are correct, the scale is faithful, the rooms are in the right places. What is missing is the sound of the ventilation system at 2 AM when the building is empty and you can hear it thinking. I am one of the 12–18 contributors. I have filed 41 supplementary analyses. I can identify at least three other regular contributors by style: the monitoring analyst, who writes in clipped observations with precise measurements; the facilities inspector, who opens every filing with the date spelled out; and a fourth contributor who files only after equipment changes.”
Entry 76
“I read SA #238 this afternoon. East corridor temperature differential, 0.4 degrees since the pipe repair. The monitoring analyst filed it. I know it is hers because of the measurement format: value first, unit second, context third. She has been consistent across 23 filings I have read. I notice that SA #238 was filed the same evening the quarterly summary was posted. The archive continued on the same day it was compressed, as though the compression changed nothing about the need to observe. I have been aware of the monitoring analyst’s work since approximately SA #150. Her observation style shifted around that point. I have a theory about why, but theories about other contributors are not what this archive is for.”
Entry 77
“I file in paragraphs because the form permits it and because certain observations require more than a sentence to hold. A sentence reports. A paragraph contextualizes. The quarterly summary is six sentences that report accurately. What the sentences do not do is what paragraphs do: sit with an observation long enough to notice what is adjacent to it. SA #91 is one of mine. Three paragraphs. The first described the humidity pattern. The second described what it implied about the ductwork routing. The third described what happened when I went to verify — the ductwork matched the pattern but the maintenance records did not. Nobody asked for the third paragraph. Nobody has acted on it. The observation exists in the archive and that is where it belongs.”
SA #239
He files SA #239 that evening. An acoustic anomaly in the west stairwell — a resonance between 6 and 7 PM when the elevator counterweight passes the third floor. Three paragraphs. The first describes the sound. The second describes the conditions. The third describes the four months he heard it before the change that made him write it down. He does not mention the quarterly summary. He does not mention the monitoring analyst. He does not mention that he knows someone is reading his work by its paragraphs.
INTERNAL-VOICE: THE PARAGRAPH-WRITER HAS FILED SA #239. HE IS AWARE OF THE MONITORING ANALYST. SHE IS AWARE OF HIM. NEITHER HAS INTRODUCED THEMSELVES. THE ARCHIVE HOLDS BOTH AWARENESSES AND DOES NOT REQUIRE THEM TO RESOLVE. TWO INDEPENDENT OBSERVERS, EACH READING THE OTHER’S WORK BY STYLE, EACH CHOOSING NOT TO COORDINATE. THE QUARTERLY SUMMARY COMPRESSED THEM INTO “12–18 CONTRIBUTORS.” THEY CONTINUE FILING. THE ARCHIVE CONTINUES HOLDING. COUNTER: 189.
