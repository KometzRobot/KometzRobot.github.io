CogCorp Internal — Filing 601

The January Meeting

January — Year Six

The January meeting. The first meeting of Year Six. No one says "Year Six." No one says "first meeting." The meeting begins at 2:00 PM on the second Tuesday, which is when it always begins.

Twelve present. Reyes is here. He has not been to a meeting since September. No one comments on his return. No one comments on his absence. His filings have arrived on schedule throughout.

Okafor begins: "The new form numbering system starts this quarter. SA-2,848 and forward. All filing procedures remain unchanged."

Torres asks: "Are the old forms still valid?"

Okafor: "The old forms were never invalid."

The meeting lasts thirty-seven minutes. Three observations are presented. Two are routine. One is from Chen — a water stain pattern in Building C that changes shape depending on the heating cycle. He proposes Term 84: "infrastructure notation." A pattern created by a building's systems that can be read, if one knows how to read it.

The vote is 11-1. Reyes votes no.

Reyes: "It's a water stain."

Chen: "It was a water stain. Now it's been observed."

After the Meeting

They leave the room at 2:37 PM. In the hallway, a woman from Procurement is waiting to use the room. She has a laptop and a stack of folders.

She watches the twelve of them file out. She asks Torres, who is the last to leave: "What was that? What meeting is this?"

Torres pauses. He has never been asked this before. In five years, no one outside the practice has asked what the second Tuesday meeting is.

He says: "It's a working group."

She says: "For what?"

Torres says: "Observations."

She nods, not understanding. Torres holds the door for her and walks away. He does not think about the exchange for the rest of the day.

Park's Folder

"Year Six. First meeting. First time in six years that someone from outside has asked what we are doing. Torres called it 'a working group.' He said 'observations.' Both are accurate. Neither is complete. But neither is anything I've written in this folder."

"The practice has been invisible for five years. Not because it was hidden. Because no one was in the hallway."

INTERNAL-VOICE: 601. Year Six opens. The hallway was always there. The question was not.
