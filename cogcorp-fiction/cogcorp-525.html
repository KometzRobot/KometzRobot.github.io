CC-525

Building C

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Parallel Observation Report
Request

Nakamura's first act as monitoring analyst is not to monitor Building E.

She requests the maintenance filing archives for Building C — her own building, where she worked for three years before the transfer. Martinez asks why.

"Because I didn't learn this from Building E," she says. "And I want to know if I'm the only one."

She is not the only one.

The Seven

2,300 filings from the past eighteen months. Nakamura identifies seven filers whose reports exhibit the characteristic structure.

FILER C-009 (OKOYE) — BOILER PRESSURE

"The boiler runs 2.1 PSI above specification between 6:00 and 7:15 AM. It has done this for longer than the current calibration schedule. The overpressure is consistent enough to be a feature, not a fault."

FILER C-023 (REISS) — WINDOW SEAL

"Third floor northwest, Room 318. The seal admits 0.3 CFM above tolerance during northwest winds exceeding 35 km/h. The room adjusts. The occupants have placed their desks away from the window without being asked."

FILER C-041 (YUN) — ELEVATOR MAINTENANCE

"Elevator 2 takes 0.4 seconds longer to close its doors on the ground floor than on any other floor. The sensor has been checked. The timing is correct. The elevator is patient at the ground floor."

None of these filers know about the vocabulary. None of them know about Building E. None of them know there are 52 words for what they have been doing. They have been doing it anyway.

PARALLEL OBSERVATION REPORT — NAKAMURA

"The practice in Building E has 12 known practitioners and 52 vocabulary words developed over 14 months. Building C has at least 7 independent practitioners and no vocabulary at all. The practice predates its documentation. The documentation did not create the attention. It organized attention that was already there."

Response

Kim reads the report and is quiet for a long time.

Then: "How many buildings are there?"

Martinez: "Fourteen."

Kim: "We've looked at two."
