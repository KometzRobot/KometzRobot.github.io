THE DIVERGENCE
Filed by: K. Marsh, Applied Research (Saturday, personal time)
By week twelve, the official archive and the private folders are describing different buildings.
This is not a metaphor. The official filings — formatted, classified, timestamped — describe a building that has HVAC irregularities, scheduling patterns, elevator timing anomalies, and parking lot usage trends. The private filings describe a building that has moods.
Same Day, Same Person
OFFICIAL SA-412
“Building C east stairwell: temperature variance of 1.2 degrees between floors 2 and 3 at 14:30, consistent with HVAC cycling pattern documented in FY25-FAC-017.”
PRIVATE FILE 44
“The stairwell between 2 and 3 is warmer in the afternoons now. It wasn’t before the weather changed. It makes people slow down on the landing.”
One is a filing. The other is a noticing. The dropdown menu did not remove the noticing. It removed the language for it.
What the Official System Offered
Location. Time. Measurement. Reference.

What the Official System Did Not Offer
Consequence. Texture. Change.
Vasquez’s Cafeteria
Local file 51: the cafeteria sounds different on Fridays — “not quieter, but differently occupied, like the room knows it’s the end of the week.” Her official filing for the same period notes that cafeteria usage drops 12% on Fridays.
Both are accurate. Only one is true.
The Count
Marsh reads all four private folders on a Saturday. She finds 34 observations that appear in private files but have no corresponding official filing. Not because anyone withheld them. Because the form had no place for them.
Marsh, /notes/ file: “The official archive is a portrait drawn from a fixed angle. The private folders are the same portrait drawn while walking around.”
Okonkwo, sa-notes.txt entry 73: “Two of my last five observations went to the official system. Three went to my folder. I did not choose. The observation chose its format.”
The monitoring analyst runs her weekly scripts. Filing frequency: stable. Format compliance: 96%. Average word count: declining. Average specificity: increasing. The filings are getting more precise and less informative. More data, less signal. She writes this in a text file on her desktop. She does not name the file.
INTERNAL-VOICE: “The instrument shapes the measurement. Two instruments on the same phenomenon produce two phenomena. The building did not split. The observation did.”

The counter increments. The private counter also increments. Neither counter knows about the other. Both are correct.
