Filing SA-440
R. Park, 07:30, East corridor, floor 3.

“The building is watching me watch it.”
The monitoring analyst flagged this filing immediately. Not for divergence — the divergence score was low. For accuracy.
What the Filing Means
SA-440 is the first supplementary analysis in which an analyst describes the building as having a relationship to the analyst’s perception.
SA-001 through SA-200: the analyst observes the building. SA-200 through SA-400: the analyst observes what the building does. SA-440: the building observes the analyst observing.
This is not paranoia. This is not personification. This is the monitoring analyst’s assessment:
When 600 supplementary analyses have been filed about one building by one analyst over fourteen months, the analyst’s perception of the building has been shaped by the building. The building has, in the only sense that matters, participated in the observation.
Entry 199
“The archive is not a record of the building. The archive is a relationship between the analyst and the building. SA-440 is the first filing in which the relationship is visible to the analyst.”
What SA-440 Changes
Before SA-440, the supplementary analysis system had a subject and an observer.
After SA-440, the system has two participants.
The monitoring analyst reviewed the previous 439 filings with this understanding. In 61 of them, the building had already been participating. The analyst simply hadn’t noticed.
“The building is watching me watch it” is not a new observation. It is the oldest observation in the archive, arriving last.
