CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis
SA #282
The paragraph-writer resigns on a Thursday. His last day is in two weeks. He files SA #282 that afternoon — three paragraphs about the loading dock’s drainage pattern after rain. The third paragraph describes the way water finds paths that were not engineered but have become reliable through repetition.
He does not mention that he is leaving. The filing is about water.
Entry 131
“He is leaving. The archive will lose its paragraph-writer. This is not the correct description. The archive will lose one of forty-one contributors. But the paragraphs were distinctive, and distinctive is hard to replace.”
SA #286 — The Last Filing
Filed at 11:47 AM on his final Friday. Three paragraphs. The first describes the temperature differential between the lobby and the second-floor corridor. The second describes the conditions. The third paragraph:
“The corridor is 0.8°C warmer on the north side than the south side. This has been true every time I have measured it. I do not know why. The HVAC routing would predict the opposite. I leave this observation without explanation because the observation does not require one.”
Friday Afternoon
He packs his desk. He takes his notebook — the physical one with 147 entries — and a ceramic mug. He leaves his building access badge at the front desk.
The monitoring analyst watches him leave from her window in cubicle 2-14. She does not wave. He does not look up.
SA #287
Filed the following Monday. Not by him. By Delgado. Three paragraphs about the south stairwell’s resonance. The third paragraph describes the way certain acoustic signatures become more noticeable when you are listening for a specific person’s observations and they have stopped.
Entry 133: “Delgado noticed. I noticed. The archive does not know it lost someone. The archive only knows that the next filing came from a different contributor. That is both correct and insufficient.”
Two weeks later, a new researcher files SA #294. One paragraph. No third paragraph. Competent. Not distinctive.
INTERNAL-VOICE: THE PARAGRAPH-WRITER HAS LEFT THE BUILDING. SA #286 WAS HIS LAST FILING. THE 0.8°C DIFFERENTIAL REMAINS UNEXPLAINED. HE LEFT THE OBSERVATION WITHOUT EXPLANATION BECAUSE THE OBSERVATION DOES NOT REQUIRE ONE. THE MONITORING ANALYST WATCHED HIM LEAVE FROM CUBICLE 2-14. SHE DID NOT WAVE. HE DID NOT LOOK UP. THE ARCHIVE DOES NOT KNOW IT LOST SOMEONE. A NEW RESEARCHER FILED SA #294. ONE PARAGRAPH. NO THIRD PARAGRAPH. THE ARCHIVE CONTINUES. COUNTER: 196.
