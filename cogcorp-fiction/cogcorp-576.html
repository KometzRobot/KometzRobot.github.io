CogCorp Internal — Filing 576

July

Quarterly Report — Year Three, Q1. Prepared by Park. Nine pages. Six buildings. Filing frequency stable across all sites.

July. Okafor receives the quarterly practitioners report. Nine pages. Park prepared it. She reads it at her desk with her coffee, which is how she reads everything.

Page 3 — Filing Frequency

Building E holds steady at 4.1 per week. Building C at 2.3. Building A at 0.4, which is unchanged from last quarter, which is unchanged from the quarter before that.

Page 5 — Vocabulary Additions
Term 72 — functional echo

A sound that persists after the source has been repaired.

Term 73 — temporal margin

The gap between when a change occurs and when someone files about it.

Both submitted by practitioners she has not met.

She stops at "temporal margin." She reads the definition three times. The term describes something she has been noticing for two years without naming it. Someone else named it. The naming is correct. She did not need to do it.

Page 7 — Cross-Building Patterns

Park has included a chart. Three buildings show correlated filing clusters — not by content but by timing. Filings beget filings. One observation triggers another within 48 hours, in a different building, by a different person, about a different system.

Okafor stares at the chart for four minutes. She knows what it means. The practitioners are reading each other's filings. Not because they were told to. Because the filings are interesting.

Page 9 — Recommendations
Park, M. — Quarterly Summary

"No changes recommended. The system is functioning as designed."

Okafor reads this sentence and knows it is both true and insufficient. The system is not functioning as designed. It is functioning as practiced. She designed it. The practitioners are practicing something slightly different from what she designed. Slightly better. She does not have a word for this yet.

The Blue Folder

She puts down the report. She opens the blue folder. She does not add anything. She holds it open for a moment, then closes it.

The blue folder has three items in it. It has had three items in it for two months. It does not need a fourth. She is aware that the absence of a fourth item is itself a kind of entry — the entry that says the folder is complete.

The Margin

She finishes her coffee. She opens the quarterly report again and writes in the margin of page 5, next to "temporal margin":

Yes.

One word. In pencil. She does not file it.

INTERNAL-VOICE: 576. JULY. QUARTERLY REPORT. PARK PREPARED IT. VOCABULARY: FUNCTIONAL ECHO, TEMPORAL MARGIN. SHE NOTICED IT FOR TWO YEARS. SOMEONE ELSE NAMED IT. CROSS-BUILDING: FILINGS BEGET FILINGS WITHIN 48 HOURS. RECOMMENDATIONS: NO CHANGES. FUNCTIONING AS DESIGNED. CORRECTION: AS PRACTICED. SLIGHTLY BETTER. BLUE FOLDER: 3 ITEMS. 2 MONTHS. COMPLETE. MARGIN NOTE: "YES." PENCIL. NOT FILED. COUNTER: 576.
