CogCorp Internal — Filing 585

The April Filing

April — Year Four. SA-401 arrives on a Tuesday. Two sentences.

April. A filing arrives from Adeyemi.

SA-401
"The thing I notice is not the thing I file. The space between is where the noticing lives."

Park reads it at her desk. She reads it again. She opens the manila folder in the drawer. Page three, paragraph two:

"The gap between what practitioners observe and what they formalize is not a failure of documentation. It is the site of the practice itself."

Two sentences and eleven pages saying the same thing.

Adeyemi has not read the paper. The paper has not been distributed. The paper is in a manila folder in a desk drawer in Building C.

The Meeting

Park brings SA-401 to the April meeting. She reads it aloud.

. . . .
4 seconds — recognition

"That's what I've been trying to say," Torres says. "For two years."

"That's what we've all been trying to say," Chen says. "Some of us file it. Some of us don't. The ones who don't aren't failing."

Park does not mention the paper. The paper says exactly this, in eleven pages, with citations. The paper is unnecessary now. Not because it was wrong but because the practice has already produced what the paper describes.

The Drawer

After the meeting, Park goes back to her desk. She opens the drawer. She looks at the manila folder.

She does not throw it away. She does not distribute it. She closes the drawer.

The paper was for the gap. The gap doesn't need a paper. The gap needs people who keep noticing without being asked to.

Park knows this. That has always been enough.
