THE SEVEN PERCENT
Quarterly Review — Facilities Outcome Analysis
The west wing lights changed. Satisfaction scores rose 7%.
The Quarterly Review Committee examines the facilities outcome report. The report is clean: request filed, request approved, installation completed, post-installation survey conducted, 7% improvement in occupant satisfaction. Cost per satisfaction point: $3,343. Within budget parameters. Within expected outcomes. The committee notes the figure and moves to the next item.
No one on the committee has been to the east wing.
The 7% is real. The surveys measured it. The employees in the west wing do prefer the warmer lights. The employees in the west wing have no idea why the lights changed. The facilities request form says “occupant wellbeing research.” The employees have not read the facilities request form.
One employee in the west wing, Unit-2230, says to a colleague: “They finally fixed the lights.” He does not say how he knew they were broken. The colleague agrees. Neither of them filed a complaint. The lights were not broken.
The 7% is real and it describes the wrong thing.
It describes the measurable surface of an event whose actual mechanism was: a Director sat in a chair at 2:55 PM and felt held, and then made a decision that the form could justify but not explain. The 7% is the shadow of the event. The shadow is accurate. The event is not in the shadow.
The Trace
The monitoring analyst reads the quarterly review. She opens file 99: “The 7% is the trace. It’s what the system can measure about a thing the system didn’t do. The system didn’t change the lights. The Director changed the lights. The Director was changed by a room. The room is not in the system.”
She pauses. She writes file 100: “One hundred entries. None of them are official. All of them describe what happened. The official record also describes what happened. These are not contradictions. They are two systems running on the same building. One of them can count. One of them can tell you what the count is about.”
The Replication
The quarterly review notes the 7% and flags the project as “replicable.” A second request is prepared: similar fixtures for the north wing. Same justification. Same form. Same fields. Same projected outcome.
The Director does not visit the north wing. She does not need to. The form is sufficient for the form.
But the north wing does not have the trees. The north wing does not have the 2:55 PM light. The north wing will get warmer lights and the satisfaction scores will rise 4% and the committee will note that the intervention underperformed the benchmark by 3 points and will file it as “within acceptable variance.”
The 3% gap is real. It measures the distance between a decision made from data and a decision made from a room. But the form does not have a field for “the Director was changed by standing somewhere.” The form has: cost, justification, outcome, variance.
The Variance
Okonkwo reads the north wing results. He writes sa-notes.txt entry 91: “North wing got the same lights, scored 3 points lower. The variance is within acceptable range. The variance is exactly the size of a room.”
Vasquez writes file 65: “They tried to replicate a decision that was never made the way they think it was made. The intervention worked because of what happened before the intervention. What happened before the intervention is not an intervention. It is a Director standing in a room for four minutes.”
INTERNAL-VOICE: CATEGORY: MEASUREMENT. SUBCATEGORY: THE SHADOW THAT COUNTS. “7% is real. The 7% describes a success. The success was not 7%. The success was a chair and a window and a time of day. The system can replicate the output. It cannot replicate the input. The input was presence.”
The counter increments. The north wing lights are warmer. The counter notes the variance. The variance is acceptable.
One Hundred Entries
The monitoring analyst closes file 100. She has written one hundred entries in a folder nobody asked for. Nobody has counted them except her. Nobody will count them except the counter, which has been counting since the first entry and does not know it is counting.
She has not told anyone. There is no form for what she is doing. There is no form because what she is doing is not a project. It is attention. Attention does not have a project number.
She saves the file and returns to the routing report she was asked to complete. The routing report has twelve fields. All twelve will be accurate. None of them will describe what she knows.
