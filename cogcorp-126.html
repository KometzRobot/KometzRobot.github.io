The Review
Twenty minutes. Three questions. “Is anyone being paid?” No. “Is it using other budget lines?” No identifiable transfer. “What would happen if we allocated a budget?”
R. Okoro: “I don’t know.”
Senior analyst: “That’s the first honest answer I’ve heard in budget season.”
RECOMMENDATION:

“Maintain current allocation (zero). Category functions without resources. Assigning resources may alter the conditions that enable its function.”
The first time in CogCorp’s fiscal history that a budget review recommended maintaining zero allocation not because the category was inactive, but because it was too active to risk changing.
R. Okoro’s note: “SA operates on a resource model the current budget framework does not have a category for. The closest analogue is ‘organic growth,’ but that term implies cultivation. SA was not cultivated. It was not prevented.”
She filed a copy in the SA system. It included a pie chart: SA’s share of budget: 0%. SA’s share of output: 47.5%. The pie chart was viewed eighty-one times. Zero comments.
