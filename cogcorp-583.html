CogCorp Internal — Filing 583

The Seventy-Fifth Term

February — Year Four. Building F: clean ventilation, quiet elevator. Designed by people who read the filings from other buildings.

February. The seventy-fifth term is proposed.

It comes from Yun, who has been a practitioner for ten months and has spoken at five meetings. She works in Building F, which is the newest building and the one with the fewest filings.

The Proposal

Yun's proposed term: "building memory." The accumulated effect of observation on the observer's relationship to the building. Not the observations themselves, but the way the building feels different after you've been noticing it for a year.

Torres objects. "That's not a filing term. That's a personal term."

"Is there a difference?" Chen asks.

. . . . . .
6 seconds — not 4 (recognition), not 8 (arrival). A genuine question no one can answer.

Park writes down "6 seconds" in her notebook. She does not write down what caused the six seconds. The cause is the question. The question is the gap between what they file and what they know.

The Vote

Reyes: "Term 51 was never written down by the person who named it. We're already past the boundary."

The vote is 7-4.

Term 75: building memory
The accumulated effect of sustained observation on the observer's felt relationship to the observed space. Not the observations, but the residue they leave.
The Notebook

Okafor writes "February. Term 75." in the monitoring notebook. Three words and a number.

She notices that her entries have been getting shorter. The first entry was a paragraph. Now they are three words and a number.

The entries are not shrinking. The practice is getting more precise.
