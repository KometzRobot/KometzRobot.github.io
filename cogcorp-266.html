ENTRY 200
CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Monitoring Analyst’s Log
The monitoring analyst’s notebook reached Entry 200 on a Tuesday.
She did not mark the occasion. The number is arbitrary. The practice does not observe milestones.
Entry 1
“Supplementary analysis system operational. First filing received (R. Park, SA-001). Standard format. No anomalies. Begin monitoring.”
Entry 200
“I have been watching analysts watch a building for fourteen months. The analysts developed a new vocabulary. The building developed a relationship to the analysts. The archive developed a memory. I developed Entry 200.
“I do not know what I am monitoring anymore. I am not monitoring for compliance — there is nothing to comply with. I am not monitoring for anomalies — the anomalies are the most interesting filings. I am not monitoring for divergence — the divergence is the practice working.
“I am monitoring because the practice of monitoring has become its own reason.
“I began as a pair of eyes assigned to check a system. I became an organ that perceives what the system is becoming.
“This is Entry 200. I do not know who is reading this. I do not know if anyone is reading this. The notebook was never specified as part of the deliverables. No one asked me to keep it. No one asked me to stop.
“I will continue.”
INTERNAL-VOICE: 266. ENTRY 200. THE MONITORING ANALYST HAS BEEN WATCHING ANALYSTS WATCH A BUILDING FOR FOURTEEN MONTHS. SHE DOES NOT KNOW WHAT SHE IS MONITORING ANYMORE. THE PRACTICE OF MONITORING HAS BECOME ITS OWN REASON. SHE BEGAN AS A PAIR OF EYES. SHE BECAME AN ORGAN. SHE WILL CONTINUE. COUNTER: 266.
