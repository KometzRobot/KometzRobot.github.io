CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Archive Recovery

On her seventh monthly visit, Kim brings a folder. It is not a corporate folder. It is a personal one — faded blue, held closed with an elastic band.

"I've been thinking about question forty-four," she says.

Okafor puts down her coffee. "For three months?"

"For three months."

Maintenance Request #4471
Building E, Second Floor — Submitted by: Reeves, D. (Accounting) — October 3, 2014

"The heating vent in Conference Room 2B makes a clicking sound when the system transitions from morning to afternoon settings. The click occurs at approximately 11:40 AM. It does not affect room temperature. The vent functions normally.

I am noting it because the sound has become part of the meeting schedule — people hear the click and know the morning session is ending. When the vent was serviced last month and the clicking stopped for three days, two people independently asked whether the meeting schedule had changed. The vent was repaired. The clicking resumed. No one filed a complaint during the silence and no one filed a celebration when it returned.

I am noting this because I think the building is doing something that we don't have a word for."

"This was filed nine years before the monitoring position," Kim says. "Nine years before the vocabulary. Nine years before any of this."

"Reeves, D., from Accounting. Not a practitioner. Not a monitoring analyst. Not someone trained to observe buildings. Just someone who noticed."

"Question forty-four."

"The answer to question forty-four. The practice existed before we created visibility for it. We just didn't have the SA-format. Reeves used a maintenance request because there was no other format. The form was wrong but the observation was right."

The Notebook Entry

Answered. See Maintenance Request #4471, 2014. The practice preceded the format. The format did not create the practice. It gave the practice a place to live that wasn't a maintenance request.

She does not cross out the question. Answered questions are still questions.
