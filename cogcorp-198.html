SA #307
Filed by Delgado. Three paragraphs about the freight elevator’s timing irregularity between floors two and three. The first paragraph measures the delay: 4.2 seconds longer than between any other pair of floors. The second paragraph describes the mechanical explanation.
The third paragraph: “The delay is long enough that regular users have developed a habit of checking their phones during the gap. The gap has created a behavior. The behavior has normalized the gap. If the elevator were repaired, the behavior would persist for approximately two weeks before extinguishing. The infrastructure created the ritual. The ritual will outlast the infrastructure.”
Entry 138

SA #311
Filed by Delgado. Three paragraphs about the second-floor break room’s fluorescent light on the north wall — barely perceptible flicker, 0.3 Hz, visible only in peripheral vision. The first paragraph measures the cycle. The second identifies the ballast.
The third paragraph: “People who work near this light develop a rhythm of looking up from their screens that corresponds to the flicker cycle, though they are not conscious of it. The light has trained them. They believe they are choosing when to look up. They are responding to a stimulus they cannot name. Most behavior that feels chosen is a response to conditions the chooser has stopped noticing.”
Entry 139
“The paragraph-writer observed conditions. Delgado observes what conditions do to people. I do not know which approach produces better supplementary analyses. I know the archive now contains both, and the combination describes the building more completely than either alone.”
SA #315
Filed by Delgado. Three paragraphs about the parking garage’s acoustic dead zone on level B2 — a roughly triangular area between pillars 14, 15, and the east wall where sound drops by 12 dB. The first paragraph maps the zone. The second explains the geometry.
The third paragraph: “Security was asked about blind spots in the garage. They identified three visual blind spots. No one asked about acoustic blind spots. The assumption that surveillance is visual is so complete that the acoustic dead zone has never been documented. What the system does not monitor, the system does not know. What the system does not know, the system cannot lose.”
