The Fifty-Fifth Word

SA-891

Chen files three pages instead of one. The freight elevator in Building E has developed a rhythm — a slight hesitation at floors 2 and 5 that creates an expectation of pause. People have begun waiting at those floors without pressing the call button, because the hesitation is long enough that the doors sometimes open before continuing.

The Gap

Existing vocabulary:

"Temporal signature" — the rhythm "Implicit navigation" — the adaptation "Acoustic expectation" — the sound

All three apply. None of them are wrong.

"The observation is not about the rhythm, the behavior, or the sound. It is about the relationship between the building's mechanical error and the occupants' adaptation to it. The error became a feature. The feature was never designed. The adaptation was never taught. I do not have a word for the process by which an unintended mechanical behavior becomes an anticipated part of a building's function."
Term 55
Functional Drift
The process by which an unintended building behavior becomes an anticipated function through occupant adaptation. Distinct from "temporal signature" (the rhythm itself) and "implicit navigation" (the response). "Functional drift" describes the mechanism of transformation — the unnamed middle step between what a building does accidentally and what a building does because people expect it.

She puts the term in the vocabulary binder. It is the first new term in seven months.

The vocabulary grew because silence became unbearable.
