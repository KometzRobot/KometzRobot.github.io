THE PATTERN
CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis
The Correlation
Filings with divergence above 1.5 are reclassified at 19.2%. Filings below: 3.1%. Six times more likely. Statistically significant (p < 0.01).
The Category
The analyst created a sub-category: “thematically aligned, linguistically divergent.” Filings that belong in their department by subject but not by vocabulary.
12 filings across the entire corpus. 12 moments when the practice deviated from its vocabulary without deviating from its purpose. Entry 161 is one of the 12. SA #289 is another. The coffee machine filing is a third.
The Translation
“These 12 filings represent instances where departmental personnel used non-standard vocabulary to describe standard observations. The linguistic divergence does not indicate subject-matter confusion. It indicates an expansion of descriptive range within existing practice parameters.” — PATTERN RECOGNITION GROUP REPORT
“Expansion of descriptive range.” This is the Pattern Recognition Group’s term for what the monitoring analyst called “the gap.” She said: I experience more than I file. The analyst says: her vocabulary expanded beyond her department’s baseline. Same phenomenon. Different language. Different layer of reduction.
The Report
The analyst’s report is 14 pages, 23 tables, 8 charts, 47 instances of the word “divergence.” It was not flagged for vocabulary divergence because it uses exactly the vocabulary the Pattern Recognition Group uses. The report about divergence is not itself divergent. The report about the gap does not contain a gap.
