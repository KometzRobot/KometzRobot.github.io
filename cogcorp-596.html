CogCorp Internal — Filing 596

The Folder

April — Year Five.

Park's folder has twenty-three pages now. She adds to it after meetings and sometimes between them. She writes about what the practitioners do without knowing they are being studied. She studies them without knowing she is practicing.

"Torres amended a term last month and used it this month. The gap between amendment and use was thirty-one days. In Year One, the gap between proposal and use was never measured because no one used the terms outside of meetings. The vocabulary was decorative. Now it is structural load-bearing."
What the Folder Contains
PAGE 1
The paper. "Institutional Practices of Informal Observation: A Five-Year Case Study." Never submitted. Never shown.

PAGES 2–4
The pauses. Not the proceedings. Where Torres looked when Yun spoke. How long Chen waited before responding. The rhythm of the room.

PAGES 5–12
Practitioner profiles. Not job descriptions — behavioral descriptions. "Torres: files consistently, proposes sparingly, never amends until February Year Five." "Chen: shortest responses, longest consideration."

PAGES 13–18
The vocabulary as Park understands it. Not the official definitions — her annotations. "Building weight (Term 14): officially, the perceived mass of a structure. In practice: the word Torres uses when he means a building makes him tired."

PAGES 19–23
Questions she cannot answer. "When does monitoring become participation?" "Is this folder a filing?" "If no one reads this, is it observation or practice?"
What Park Does Not Write
She does not write: "I have been doing this for a year and I cannot stop."
She does not write: "The practitioners do not know I study them and I do not know why I started."
She does not write: "This folder is the most honest document in the building."

She puts the folder back in the drawer. The drawer closes. The practice continues.
