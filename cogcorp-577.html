August. The practitioners meet.

Eleven people in a room designed for twenty. Okafor sits at the head of the table because the room has a head and someone has to sit there. Park has prepared slides. Chen has brought a notebook. Reyes has brought nothing, which is how Reyes arrives at everything.

Cross-Building Data

Park presents the cross-building data. The chart shows filing clusters — observations triggering observations across buildings within 48 hours. "The pattern is consistent across three quarters," Park says.

"Is that a problem?" someone asks.

"It's a finding," Park says.

Okafor watches the room. Eleven people who observe buildings for reasons they can each articulate differently. Chen measures. Reyes remembers. Park maps. Torres protects. Davis writes short sentences about things that happen.

In use for six months. No objections. Approved.

Term 73 — temporal margin

Twelve minutes of discussion. Chen: the gap isn't between the change and the filing, it's between the change and the awareness. Reyes: "Sometimes you file about something before you know you noticed it."

Term 74 — [unnamed]

Adeyemi: "The thing where you go back to check if something you remember is still true." Six people nod. No one has a word. Chen writes: "verification impulse?"

Okafor does not write anything. She already has a word for it. It's in the blue folder. She does not say this.

After

The meeting runs forty minutes over. No one mentions it. Okafor notices that the meeting has developed its own temporal margin — the gap between when it should end and when anyone notices.

Park stays behind.

"The cross-building pattern. Should I investigate?"

"What would investigating look like?"

"Asking people why they file when they file."

"And what would that change?"

"I think it would change what they notice."

"Yes."

Park leaves. Okafor sits in the empty room for three minutes. Eleven chairs pushed back at eleven angles. She notices this. She does not file it.
