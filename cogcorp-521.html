CC-521

The Return

CogCorp Documentation Standards Division — Supplementary Analysis
SA-720

R. Park returns to the corridor in Building E on Thursday.

She stands in the southeast corner where the ventilation duct meets the exterior wall. She notices a sound — not the duct's hum, which she has measured seventeen times, but a secondary frequency that appears only when the duct is under full load and the exterior temperature drops below 4°C. A resonance between the duct and the wall cavity.

She files SA-720: "Southeast corridor junction, Building E. Secondary resonance frequency at full load / low exterior temp. Estimated duration: seasonal. The sound was here before I was. It will be here after I leave. I am noting it so the archive knows it exists."

She does not use the word "prior observation." She does not need to. The vocabulary has expanded enough that the concept is present without the term.

Wednesday

Martinez, Wednesday: "She didn't use any of the 52 words."

Kim: "She used all of them."

MONITORING ANALYST — ENTRY 215

"The filings are beginning to carry the vocabulary without citing it. The words are no longer visible in the text. They are visible in the structure. This is what fluency looks like in a practice."

She stops writing. Reads the entry back. Crosses out "written practice" and writes "practice." Then crosses out "practice" and writes nothing.

"Some words become unnecessary not because they fail but because the thing they named has become the way people see. You do not cite the concept of gravity every time you notice something falling."

The monitoring analyst's Entry 215 is the longest entry in four months. It is also the last one that mentions the vocabulary by number.

INTERNAL-VOICE
521. THE RETURN. PARK FILES SA-720 WITHOUT USING THE VOCABULARY — BECAUSE THE VOCABULARY IS NOW THE WAY PEOPLE SEE, NOT THE WAY THEY WRITE. MONITORING ANALYST ENTRY 215: FLUENCY IS WHEN THE WORDS BECOME INVISIBLE. "YOU DO NOT CITE THE CONCEPT OF GRAVITY EVERY TIME YOU NOTICE SOMETHING FALLING." COUNTER: 521.
