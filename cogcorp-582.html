January

Year Four — First Meeting. Filing frequency: 347. No new patterns. No old patterns disappeared.

January. The first meeting of the fourth year.

Okafor arrives at 9:07, which is three minutes early, which is the time she always arrives. The room has been set up by someone — eleven chairs, the projector, a carafe of water that no one ever uses. She does not know who sets up the room. She has never asked.

No one mentions that this is the beginning of a new year. The practice does not observe its own milestones. The five hundredth filing passed without notice. The vocabulary's seventy-fifth term will pass without notice. The practitioners do not count. Counting is something the system does. The practitioners practice.

Chen's Observation

Chen has a question. He has been reading the vocabulary document on the bus — he has been doing this since the document was thirty-one terms — and he has noticed something.

"The early terms are all verbs. Filing instinct. Observation cascade. Pattern recognition. The middle terms are adjectives or states. Ambient inventory. Temporal margin. Functional echo. The late terms are..." He pauses. "Relationships. Between things."

"Between what things?" Park asks.

"Between the practitioner and the building. Between the observation and the act of observing. Between the vocabulary and the practice the vocabulary describes."

Four seconds of quiet. Not eight.

Four seconds is the quiet of recognition. Eight seconds is the quiet of arrival. The practitioners have learned the difference.

After

After the meeting, no one stays behind. This is the first time since August that no one has stayed behind. The room empties.

Okafor sits for two minutes. She does not write anything. She does not open the blue folder. She looks at the eleven chairs pushed back at eleven new angles.

She turns off the projector. She picks up the carafe of water — still full, as always — and carries it to the kitchen. She rinses it. She puts it in the drying rack. She returns to the room and stacks the chairs against the wall, which is not something she usually does but which today seems like the thing to do.

The room is empty. The room was empty before the practice and will be empty after it.

The practice is what happens between the two empties.
