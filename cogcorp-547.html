CogCorp Internal — Filing 547

Two Descriptions

Okafor finds the match by accident. She is refiling SA-891 when she notices Davis filed SA-588 three days later. Same elevator. Same hesitation at floors 2 and 5.

Chen
SA-891

"The freight elevator in Building E exhibits a consistent hesitation pattern at floors 2 and 5, lasting approximately 3.2 seconds. This behavior constitutes functional drift (Term 55) — an unintended mechanical pattern that has been incorporated into occupant navigation through implicit adaptation. The hesitation generates a distinct temporal signature (Term 12) that regular users have internalized, creating an acoustic expectation (Term 31) at each pause point."

Davis
SA-588

"The freight elevator pauses at 2 and 5. People wait without pressing the button. The pause has been there longer than I have."

Assessment

Chen's filing tells her what the building is doing. It categorizes, measures, connects the observation to three vocabulary terms, identifies a process, and names it. It is the vocabulary working exactly as intended.

Davis's filing tells her what the building is like. It describes without categorizing. It locates the observation in time — not measured time, but personal time. "Longer than I have."

Question 52

"Which description is the building?"

"Both. But only one of them would survive if the vocabulary disappeared."
